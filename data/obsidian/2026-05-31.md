# 2026-05-31

**CAN-2409 + definitive 78Gy RT: DFS HR 0.7 (p=0.0155), 2yr biopsy pCR 80 vs 64%; a local-control gain without dose escalation or ADT, OS/PCSM still immature.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate carried the RT news, and the recurring lesson is that intensifying on top of definitive RT mostly fails hard endpoints: ENZARAD (enza add-on) and COMPPARE (proton vs IMRT) both null, while CAN-2409 bought local control but not yet OS. Outside RT, RASolute 302 hit mOS 13.2 vs 6.6mo (HR 0.40) in 2L pancreatic.

## 🌰 Prostate

Four prostate reads probing intensification on definitive RT: enzalutamide and protons added nothing on hard endpoints, an oncolytic virus moved only local control, and PROTEUS stays an unreadable preview.

### COMPPARE

> No proton advantage over IMRT: bowel urgency 5.7 vs 6%, GI G2+ tox 5.2 vs 5.6%, 3yr biochemical control 98.0 vs 97.9%, all NS.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Not proton vs photon but the rectal spacer is the toxicity lever: it cut 2yr GI G2+ toxicity (4.4% IMRT, 4.7% proton with spacer vs 7.2% and 8.7% without, p=0.009), dwarfing modality. Both arms fell far below hypothesized rates, so paying the proton premium for less GI toxicity isn't supported.

![COMPPARE](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJrbdsMW8AYkCpV.jpg)

| Endpoint | IMRT | Proton | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bowel urgency (EPIC) | 6% | 5.7% | 0.28 |
| Bowel frequency (EPIC) | 4% | 3.5% | 0.43 |
| GI tox (CTCAE ≥G2) | 5.6% | 5.2% | 0.60 |
| 3yr freedom from progression | 97.9% | 98.0% | 0.90 |

![COMPPARE](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJrbduxXoAMiv-c.jpg)

| Group | 2yr GI G2+ toxicity (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| IMRT, no spacer | 7.2% (5.0%, 9.9%) |
| Proton, no spacer | 8.7% (5.0%, 14%) |
| IMRT, spacer | 4.4% (2.8%, 6.4%) |
| Proton, spacer | 4.7% (3.6%, 6.0%) |

- 🔍 Prospective comparative-effectiveness cohort (non-randomized), PCORI-funded; 51 centers, 2524 accrued Jul 2018–Oct 2022
- 🔍 Cohorts: 1500 proton vs 1000 photon; all de novo localized prostate excl very-high-risk and metastatic
- ⚠️ Realized GI/bowel toxicity ~5-6% in both arms, far below the 15-29% hypothesized for IMRT
- ⚠️ Low realized event rates leave the comparison likely underpowered to detect a true proton benefit
- 📊 Rectal spacer cut 2yr GI G2+ toxicity more than modality choice (p=0.009); proton vs IMRT negligible within spacer strata
- 🔗 vs PARTIQoL (randomized proton vs IMRT, localized prostate): concordant null for patient-reported bowel/GI endpoints
- ⚠️ Early results; late GI/GU toxicity (the main proton rationale) and long-term control not yet mature
- ⚠️ Non-randomized cohorts: baseline differences between proton and IMRT pts may confound the read

### ENZARAD

> MFS HR 0.88 (0.67-1.15) p=0.34, OS HR 0.87 p=0.40: adding enzalutamide to high-dose RT + 2y ADT did not improve high-risk localised prostate.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is the pelvic-nodal-RT interaction: enzalutamide helped only pts who received pelvic RT (MFS HR 0.47 vs 1.25 without, interaction p<0.001) and N1 pts (HR 0.43). Hypothesis-generating, but it flags node-positive/whole-pelvis pts as the subgroup where ARSI intensification with definitive RT might still pay off.

![ENZARAD](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G3nCbXlW8AARLSS.jpg)

| Arm | MFS events/N | HR (95% CI) | 2p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enzalutamide | 98/401 | 0.88 (0.67-1.15) | 0.34 |
| Control (NSAA) | 109/401 | ref | — |

![ENZARAD](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G3nCbXoXQAAmSWJ.jpg)

![ENZARAD](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/G3nCbXnW0AALcpc.jpg)

| Arm | OS events/N | OS 8y | HR (95% CI) | 2p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Enzalutamide | 69/401 | 83% | 0.87 (0.63-1.20) | 0.40 |
| Control (NSAA) | 77/401 | 80% | ref | — |

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT (ANZUP), N=800, high-risk localised prostate, all RT-treated.
- 🔍 Shared backbone both arms: 2y LHRHa + high-dose EBRT, brachy boost and pelvic nodal RT permitted, RT starting after 16wk.
- 💊 Randomised add-on: enzalutamide 160mg ×24mo vs conventional NSAA ×6mo.
- 📊 Negative trial: primary MFS not met and OS not improved at 8y median follow-up.
- ⚠️ Benefit seen only in predefined subgroups: cN1 disease and pts receiving pelvic nodal RT (MFS interaction p<0.001 for pelvic RT). Hypothesis-generating.
- 📐 ICECAP surrogacy held: MFS and OS hazard ratios well-correlated in the trial's analysis.
- 🔗 vs EMBARK (enzalutamide in nmHSPC biochemical recurrence): intensification improved MFS there but adds nothing layered on definitive RT + 2y ADT here.
- ⚠️ Pelvic-RT subgroup reflects treatment received, not randomisation; interaction is exploratory and needs prospective confirmation before changing practice.

### CAN-2409

> DFS HR 0.7 (0.52-0.94, p=0.0155), median NR vs 86.1mo, adding intra-prostatic oncolytic virus to definitive 78Gy RT; OS/PCSM immature.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT-relevant read is local control: 2yr positive-biopsy fell to 19.6% vs 36.4% (p=0.0015) without dose escalation or added ADT, a new local-intensification lever. But the control RT (78Gy, no boost or ADT) predates modern intensification, leaving its place vs brachy boost or ADT unsettled.

| Method (trial) | Endpoint | Control | Intensified |
|---|---|---|---|
| +ADT (RTOG 9408) | 2yr +biopsy | 40% | 20% |

- 🔍 Intra-prostatic oncolytic-viral therapy (CAN-2409) added to definitive EBRT 78Gy, vs RT + placebo, randomized
- ⚠️ DFS driven mainly by 2yr post-RT biopsy pCR, a surrogate endpoint
- ⚠️ OS and PCSM still flat, so clinical benefit unproven
- ⚠️ Per discussant: control RT 78Gy lacked modern dose intensification (brachy boost, SIB) or ADT, blunting relevance to current SOC
- 🔗 Local-control gain reached without dose escalation or ADT, a different lever than brachy boost or hormones
- 📊 ≥Gr 3 toxicity <1% with CAN-2409 added, no major added RT toxicity

### PROTEUS

> Preview only; most distant mets found by PSMA PET (53.0% apalutamide vs 60.7% ADT), so MFS events were PET- not conventionally-driven.

- 📊 Preview release; primary MFS effect size (HR, medians) not reported in source tweets.
- 📊 Distant mets identified by PSMA PET: 53.0% apalutamide vs 60.7% ADT.

## 🌀 GI Lower

Upfront RT folded into 1L systemic + PD1 for unresectable MSS met rectal ca, but the single-arm design leaves RT's contribution unmeasurable.

### MIRACLE-2

> ORR 68%, mOS 23.2mo, 18% reached NED with upfront RT then systemic + PD1 in 1L unresectable MSS met rectal ca.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is upfront priming: HFRT to primary plus HFRT/SBRT to mets precedes systemic + PD1, and 18% (9/50) of unresectable pts reached NED, an unusual signal in immune-cold MSS. RT dose/fractionation isn't in source and no omission arm isolates its effect, so adding RT to a systemic backbone stays hypothesis-only.

![MIRACLE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJpxlR1X0AMyvn4.jpg)

- 📊 Efficacy outcomes (single-arm, N=50)

  | Endpoint | Value | 95% CI |
  |---|---|---|
  | ETS rate (1°) | 76.0% | 62.4-86.8% |
  | ORR | 68.0% | 53.6-80.0% |
  | DCR | 88.0% | 76.0-95.2% |
  | Median PFS | 9.3 mo | 7.1-11.5 |
  | Median OS | 23.2 mo | 15.1-31.3 |
  | Median DOR | 8.0 mo | 5.2-10.8 |

- 📊 18% (9/50) reached NED; 1-yr OS 93.3%, 1-yr PFS 33.4%, 1-yr DOR 20%
- 🔍 Prospective single-arm phase I, N=50, 1L MSS unresectable met rectal ca, primary ≤10cm from anal verge
- 🔍 RT given upfront: HFRT to primary, HFRT or SBRT to mets. Dose/fractionation not reported in source
- 💊 Then FOLFOX-bev-tislelizumab (RAS/BRAF-mut) or FOLFIRI-cetux-tislelizumab (WT); RAS/BRAF mut in 56.0%
- ⚠️ Grade 3/4 TRAEs, heme-dominant
  - Lymphopenia 36.7%
  - Neutropenia 26.5%
  - Leukopenia 20.4%
- ⚠️ RT-associated lymphopenia a plausible driver of the 36.7% G3/4 lymphopenia
- ⚠️ RT contribution unmeasurable: no RT-omission arm, effect confounded with systemic + PD1
- ⚠️ 1° EP is ETS (≥20% shrinkage at 8wk), an early surrogate not OS/PFS; mFU only 19.9mo (16.4-23.4)
- ⚠️ Durability thin: 1-yr DOR 20%, mDOR 8.0mo despite high 1-yr OS
- 🔗 MSS ~95% of mCRC and intrinsically IO-resistant; single-agent PD1 inactive in MSS

## 🍽️ GI Upper

A systemic standout with no RT relevance: daraxonrasib nearly doubled OS in 2L RAS-G12 metastatic pancreatic.

### RASolute 302

> mOS 13.2 vs 6.6mo, HR 0.40 (0.30-0.54), P<0.001 for daraxonrasib vs chemo in RAS G12 2L metastatic pancreatic cancer.

![RASolute 302](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJprw8yWkAQlNuV.jpg)

| Population | Daraxonrasib mOS (mo) | Chemo mOS (mo) | HR (95% CI) | P |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| RAS G12 | 13.2 (10.0-NR) | 6.6 (5.4-8.2) | 0.40 (0.30-0.54) | <0.001 |
| Overall | 13.2 (10.0-NR) | 6.7 (5.8-8.0) | 0.40 (0.30-0.53) | <0.001 |

- 🔍 Randomized 2L metastatic PDAC: daraxonrasib vs investigator's-choice chemotherapy
- 🔍 'Overall' population = RAS G12/G13/Q61-mutant or no RAS mutation detected; G12 the enriched cohort
- 📊 Deaths 32% daraxonrasib vs 55% chemo in the RAS G12 cohort
- ⚠️ Daraxonrasib mOS upper CI not reached (10.0-NR); OS data still maturing
- ⚠️ Investigator's-choice comparator pools heterogeneous 2L regimens; no uniform control arm
- 🔗 An OS benefit of this magnitude in 2L metastatic PDAC is highly unusual for a historically chemo-refractory setting
