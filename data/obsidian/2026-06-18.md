## 🌰 Prostate

Diagnostic biopsy-triage question, not a therapy signal: where PSMA-PET slots into the pre-biopsy pathway.

### PRIMARY2 · [[NCT05154162]]

> Non-inferior csPCa detection (12% vs 16%, diff -3.7%) with PSMA-PET biopsy triage, avoiding biopsy in 49% of equivocal/negative-MRI men.

- 📊 Co-1° csPCa (Gleason ≥3+4, ≥10% pattern 4): 12% (39/331) PSMA-PET vs 16% (51/329) systematic biopsy
- 📐 Risk difference -3.7% (95% CI -8.9 to 1.5), p=0.0093; non-inferiority margin 10% met
- 📊 Co-1° biopsy avoided in 49% (163/331) of PSMA-PET arm (95% CI 44-55), p<0.0001 vs 20% threshold
- 📊 Post-biopsy adverse events (among those biopsied)

  | AE | PSMA-PET | Systematic biopsy |
  |---|---|---|
  | Pain | 33 (21%) | 62 (21%) |
  | Haematuria | 60 (38%) | 126 (43%) |
  | Haematospermia | 77 (48%) | 133 (45%) |

- 🔍 Phase 3 non-inferiority RCT, 7 Australian sites, N=660 biopsy-naive men, 1:1, unmasked
- 🔍 Eligible: non-suspicious/equivocal MRI (PI-RADS 2 in 51%, 3 in 49%) + high clinical risk, PSA ≤20, ≤cT2
- 🔍 PET-positive (PRIMARY score 3-5) → targeted transperineal biopsy; PET-negative (1-2) avoided biopsy
- 🔗 Extends the earlier PRIMARY diagnostic-accuracy study into a randomised biopsy-triage design
- ⚠️ Open-label; no masking of participants or investigators
- ⚠️ Follow-up ongoing: long-term safety of avoided biopsies (missed csPCa) not yet confirmed
- ⚠️ Single-country (Australia), one tracer ([68Ga]PSMA-11); health-economics + other radiopharmaceuticals unvalidated
