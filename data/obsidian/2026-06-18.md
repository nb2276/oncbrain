## 🌰 Prostate

Diagnostic-pathway data: PSMA-PET as a biopsy-avoidance gate in biopsy-naive high-risk men with equivocal MRI.

### PRIMARY2 · [[NCT05154162]]

> PSMA-PET avoided biopsy in 49% while staying non-inferior for csPCa detection (12% vs 16%) vs systematic biopsy in high-risk equivocal-MRI men.

- 🔍 Phase 3 non-inferiority RCT, 7 Australian sites, N=660, randomised 1:1 PSMA-PET-CT vs systematic transperineal biopsy, no masking
- 🔍 Biopsy-naive, high clinical risk, PI-RADS 2 (51%) or 3 (49%) MRI, PSA ≤20 (median 5·2), ≤cT2
- 🔍 PRIMARY score 3-5 on PSMA-PET → targeted biopsy; score 1-2 → biopsy avoided
- 📊 Co-primary endpoints, PSMA-PET vs systematic biopsy

  | Endpoint | PSMA-PET | Control | Effect |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | csPCa detected | 39/331 (12%) | 51/329 (16%) | diff −3·7% (95% CI −8·9 to 1·5), p=0·0093 |
  | Biopsy avoided ≤6mo | 163/331 (49%) | n/a | 95% CI 44-55%, p<0·0001 |

- 📊 Post-biopsy morbidity by arm (among biopsied)

  | Symptom | PSMA-PET arm | Control |
  |---|---|---|
  | Pain | 33 (21%) | 62 (21%) |
  | Haematuria | 60 (38%) | 126 (43%) |
  | Haematospermia | 77 (48%) | 133 (45%) |

- 🔗 Phase 3 confirmation of the PRIMARY (Emmett et al) PSMA-PET PRIMARY-score pathway for csPCa detection
- ⚠️ Non-inferiority design with a wide 10% margin; csPCa detection point estimate favoured control, raising missed-cancer vs avoided-overdiagnosis question
- ⚠️ Follow-up ongoing; whether biopsy-avoided men later develop csPCa is not yet established
- ⚠️ Open-label, single-country, Ga-PSMA-11 only; generalisability to F-18 PSMA tracers and other settings unproven
