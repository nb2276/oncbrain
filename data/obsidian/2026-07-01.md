# 2026-07-01

**NRG/RTOG 1112: adding SBRT to sorafenib mOS 15.8 vs 12.3mo (HR 0.77, 1-sided P=.06, ns 1°); stratification-adjusted OS HR 0.72 P=.04, mPFS 9.2 vs 5.5mo.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Two RT-forward reads. RTOG 1112 gives SBRT its first randomised PFS signal (HR 0.55) added to sorafenib in locally advanced HCC, though 1° OS just missed and the sorafenib comparator is now obsolete. Alongside, a ReCOG/ESTRO/ASTRO consensus formalizes reRT selection for recurrent HNSCC on a thin, mostly retrospective base.

## 👄 Head & Neck

Guidance where randomised data doesn't exist: a formal reRT consensus for recurrent/2nd-primary HNSCC in previously irradiated fields.

### ReCOG/ESTRO/ASTRO HNSCC Reirradiation Consensus

> Expert consensus on reRT for recurrent/2nd-primary HNSCC in previously irradiated fields; 17-panel vote, guidance on selection, delineation, dose accumulation, toxicity.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The dose-accumulation and target-delineation domains are the real value: reRT toxicity is gated by cumulative OAR dose from the first course, and this is the first multi-society attempt to standardize how you sum the two plans and draw the reRT target. It sequences salvage surgery first, then postoperative or definitive reRT, so it shapes selection, not a dose prescription.

- 🔍 Multi-society consensus (ReCOG + ESTRO Reirradiation Focus Group + ASTRO) on reRT for recurrent or 2nd-primary HNSCC in previously irradiated fields
- 🔍 Method: 10-expert core draft → single formal vote by an international panel of 17 radiation oncologists (agree/disagree/no-opinion)
- 📐 Consensus thresholds predefined: high ≥85%, moderate 70-84%, low <70%; no-opinion retained in the denominator, no second voting round
- 🔍 Domains covered: patient selection, imaging, target delineation, treatment planning, dose accumulation, toxicity management
- 🔍 Applies to definitive and postoperative reRT; salvage surgery preferred when feasible, then postop reRT for high-risk pts
- 📊 15-50% of HNSCC pts recur or develop a 2nd primary; few options once disease is in-field
- 🔗 Modern conformal technique (IMRT, VMAT, proton, SBRT) has widened reRT feasibility over ~20 yrs, but modern-era role remains undefined
- ⚠️ Low-level evidence base: most statements rest on Oxford level 3-4 (retrospective / expert consensus), not randomised data
- ⚠️ Excludes nasopharyngeal cancers (2021 recs) and rare histologies

> [!question] Open questions
> - Role of modern reRT (IMRT/proton/SBRT) undefined absent randomised trials
> - Optimal dose accumulation method across two RT courses
> - Selection for definitive vs postoperative reRT

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

SBRT's first randomised OS/PFS signal in locally advanced HCC, but the sorafenib-alone comparator predates current IO-based 1L.

### NRG/RTOG 1112 · [[NCT01730937]]

> Adding SBRT to sorafenib: mOS 15.8 vs 12.3mo (HR 0.77, 1-sided P=.06, ns primary); mPFS 9.2 vs 5.5mo, HR 0.55, P<.001.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is PFS, not the ns OS primary: mPFS 9.2 vs 5.5mo (HR 0.55, P<.001), a locoregional-control signal in a cohort 74% macrovascular-invasion, where liver-directed RT is hardest. Personalized 27.5-50Gy/5fx, no excess G3+ toxicity. Open decision: does SBRT still add over a modern IO backbone (sorafenib obsolete)?

- 📊 SBRT+sorafenib vs sorafenib alone, by endpoint

  | Endpoint | SBRT+sorafenib | Sorafenib | Effect (HR/P) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | mOS | 15.8 mo | 12.3 mo | HR 0.77 (90% CI 0.59-1.01), 1-sided P=.06 |
  | mPFS | 9.2 mo | 5.5 mo | HR 0.55 (0.40-0.75), P<.001 |
  | G3+ TRAE | 47% (39/83) | 42% (37/88) | P=.52 |

- 📐 Stratification-adjusted OS reached significance: HR 0.72 (95% CI 0.52-0.99), 2-sided P=.04
- 📐 SBRT personalized to 27.5-50 Gy in 5 fractions, liver-function adapted, delivered before sorafenib
- 🔍 Phase 3 open-label RCT, 193 randomized (177 eligible), 1:1 SBRT→sorafenib vs sorafenib
- 🔍 Pts: locally advanced HCC unsuitable for or refractory to locoregional therapy; 74% macrovascular invasion; 1L systemic candidates
- ⚠️ Primary OS missed prespecified significance (1-sided P=.06); benefit rests on adjusted OS plus PFS
- ⚠️ Accrual stopped early after 1L systemic SOC shifted; sorafenib-alone comparator now obsolete
- 📊 6-mo QoL improved in 6/17 (35%) SBRT+sorafenib vs 2/20 (10%) sorafenib

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 39699905]] **Stereotactic Body Radiotherapy vs Sorafenib Alone in Hepatocellular Carcinoma: The NRG Oncology/RTOG 1112 Phase 3 Randomized Clinical Trial.** — Dawson; Winter; Knox et al.. *JAMA oncology* (2025-02). [doi:10.1001/jamaoncol.2024.5403](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamaoncol.2024.5403)
  > IMPORTANCE: Most patients with locally advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) recur within the liver following systemic therapy.
  > OBJECTIVE: To determine whether stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) improves outcomes in patients with locally advanced HCC compared with sorafenib alone.
  > DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS: This multicenter phase 3 randomized clinical trial randomized patients with HCC 1:1 to sorafenib or SBRT followed by sorafenib, stratified by performance status, liver function, degree of metastases, and macrovascular invasion. Eligible patients had HCC unsuitable for or refractory to standard local-regional therapies and were candidates for first-line systemic therapy. Data were collected from April 2013 to March 2021, and data were analyzed from July 2022 to August 2023.
  > INTERVENTION: Personalized SBRT, 27.5 to 50 Gy in 5 fractions.
  > MAIN OUTCOMES AND MEASURES: The primary end point was overall survival (OS). Secondary end points were progression-free survival (PFS), adverse events, and quality of life.
  > RESULTS: Of 193 patients randomized, 177 were eligible. Accrual was stopped early due to a change in standard-of-care systemic therapy. Of 177 included patients, 150 (84.7%) were male, and the median (IQR) age was 66 (60-72) years. Macrovascular invasion was seen in 131 (74.0%). As of July 1, 2022, the median OS was 12.3 months (90% CI, 10.6-14.3) with sorafenib vs 15.8 months (90% CI, 11.4-19.2) following SBRT and sorafenib (hazard ratio [HR], 0.77; 90% CI, 0.59-1.01; 1-sided P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.06). Adjusting for stratification factors, OS was improved with SBRT (HR, 0.72; 95% CI, 0.52-0.99; 2-sided P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.04). Median PFS was improved from 5.5 months (95% CI, 3.4-6.3) with sorafenib to 9.2 months (95% CI, 7.5-11.9) with SBRT and sorafenib (HR, 0.55; 95% CI, 0.40-0.75; 2-sided P&#x2009;<&#x2009;.001). Treatment-related grade 3 or higher adverse events were seen in 37 of 88 (42%) and 39 of 83 (47%) of patients treated with sorafenib vs SBRT and sorafenib, respectively (P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.52). There were 2 treatment-related deaths in the sorafenib group (death not otherwise specified and liver failure) and 1 in the SBRT and sorafenib group (lung infection). At 6 months, improved quality of life was seen in 2 of 20 (10%) and 6 of 17 (35%) of patients treated with sorafenib and SBRT and sorafenib, respectively.
  > CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE: In this phase 3 randomized clinical trial, among patients with locally advanced HCC, SBRT was associated with a clinically important but not statistically significant improved overall survival compared with sorafenib alone.
  > TRIAL REGISTRATION: ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: [[NCT01730937]].
