# 2026-07-01

**RTOG 1112: adding SBRT to sorafenib lifts mOS 15.8 vs 12.3mo (HR 0.77, 1° not met at P=.06) and mPFS 9.2 vs 5.5mo (HR 0.55) in LA-HCC.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Hepatobiliary carries the day: RTOG 1112 is the first phase 3 to test SBRT added to systemic therapy in LA-HCC, with a strong PFS gain (HR 0.55) but an OS primary that missed (P=.06) against a now-obsolete sorafenib control. Head-neck adds a multi-society reirradiation consensus, no effect sizes.

## 👄 Head & Neck

First multi-society (ReCOG/ESTRO/ASTRO) framework for reirradiating recurrent or second-primary HNSCC within prior RT fields.

### ReCOG/ESTRO/ASTRO Reirradiation Consensus

> Multi-society expert consensus on reirradiation for recurrent or second-primary HNSCC in prior RT fields: selection, delineation, dose accumulation, toxicity guidance.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The practical value for reRT planning is the guidance on dose accumulation across the prior plan and target delineation, the two steps with the widest inter-center variation. Endorsement spans ReCOG, ESTRO, and ASTRO, so it works as a shared selection-and-replanning framework, not new efficacy data (underlying evidence is largely level 3-4).

- 🔍 Endorsed by ReCOG, the ESTRO Reirradiation Focus Group, and ASTRO; Lancet Oncol 2026
- 🔍 Scope: definitive + postoperative reRT for recurrent/second-primary HNSCC within prior RT fields
- 🔍 Excludes nasopharyngeal cancers (2021 recs) and rare histologies
- 📊 15-50% of HNSCC pts develop recurrence or a second primary; few options in prior-irradiated regions
- 🔍 Recommended algorithm when disease sits in a prior RT field
  - Salvage surgery preferred when feasible
  - Postoperative reRT for some high-risk resected pts
  - Definitive-intent reRT when curative surgery not feasible
  - Palliative systemic therapy if neither is feasible
- 🔍 Consensus process
  - International 17-radiation-oncologist panel, single formal voting round
  - Thresholds: high ≥85%, moderate 70-84%, low <70% agreement
  - Evidence graded by Oxford CEBM level 1 (randomised) to level 4 (expert consensus)
- 🔍 Domains covered: patient selection, imaging, target delineation, planning, dose accumulation, toxicity management
- 🔗 Conformal era (IMRT, VMAT, proton, SBRT) improved therapeutic ratio over 20yr; modern role still undefined
- ⚠️ Expert-consensus document; underlying evidence heterogeneous and low-level, no effect sizes reported in source

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

SBRT enters the first-line locally advanced HCC conversation via a randomised phase 3.

### NRG Oncology/RTOG 1112 · [[NCT01730937]]

> mOS 15.8 vs 12.3mo adding SBRT to sorafenib (HR 0.77, 1-sided P=.06, primary not met); mPFS 9.2 vs 5.5mo, HR 0.55.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is a large PFS gain (9.2 vs 5.5mo, HR 0.55) plus a clinically meaningful OS signal (adjusted HR 0.72) with no added grade 3+ toxicity over sorafenib alone (47 vs 42%). Moves the add-SBRT decision in macrovascular-invasion HCC, though the sorafenib control predates the atezo/bev era.

- 📊 SBRT + sorafenib vs sorafenib alone, primary + key secondary endpoints

  | Endpoint | SBRT + sorafenib | Sorafenib | Effect |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Median OS (1°) | 15.8 mo | 12.3 mo | HR 0.77 (90% CI 0.59-1.01), 1-sided P=.06 |
  | OS, adjusted | n/a | n/a | HR 0.72 (95% CI 0.52-0.99), 2-sided P=.04 |
  | Median PFS | 9.2 mo | 5.5 mo | HR 0.55 (95% CI 0.40-0.75), P<.001 |
  | Grade 3+ TRAE | 47% (39/83) | 42% (37/88) | P=.52 |

- 🔍 First phase 3 RCT of SBRT added to systemic therapy in locally advanced HCC
- 🔍 SBRT personalized 27.5-50 Gy in 5 fractions, delivered before sorafenib
- 🔍 Eligible: HCC unsuitable for or refractory to local-regional therapy, first-line systemic candidates
- 🔍 74% macrovascular invasion, 150/177 male, median age 66
- ⚠️ Accrual stopped early after first-line SOC systemic therapy changed → underpowered
- ⚠️ Prespecified OS primary formally negative (1-sided P=.06); significant only after stratification adjustment
- ⚠️ Sorafenib-alone control now obsolete; current 1L SOC is atezo/bev then durva+treme
- 📊 QoL improved at 6mo in 35% (6/17) SBRT vs 10% (2/20) sorafenib, small N
- 📊 Treatment-related deaths: 1 SBRT+sorafenib (lung infection) vs 2 sorafenib (liver failure, NOS)

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 39699905]] **Stereotactic Body Radiotherapy vs Sorafenib Alone in Hepatocellular Carcinoma: The NRG Oncology/RTOG 1112 Phase 3 Randomized Clinical Trial.** — Dawson; Winter; Knox et al.. *JAMA oncology* (2025-02). [doi:10.1001/jamaoncol.2024.5403](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamaoncol.2024.5403)
  > IMPORTANCE: Most patients with locally advanced hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) recur within the liver following systemic therapy.
  > OBJECTIVE: To determine whether stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT) improves outcomes in patients with locally advanced HCC compared with sorafenib alone.
  > DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS: This multicenter phase 3 randomized clinical trial randomized patients with HCC 1:1 to sorafenib or SBRT followed by sorafenib, stratified by performance status, liver function, degree of metastases, and macrovascular invasion. Eligible patients had HCC unsuitable for or refractory to standard local-regional therapies and were candidates for first-line systemic therapy. Data were collected from April 2013 to March 2021, and data were analyzed from July 2022 to August 2023.
  > INTERVENTION: Personalized SBRT, 27.5 to 50 Gy in 5 fractions.
  > MAIN OUTCOMES AND MEASURES: The primary end point was overall survival (OS). Secondary end points were progression-free survival (PFS), adverse events, and quality of life.
  > RESULTS: Of 193 patients randomized, 177 were eligible. Accrual was stopped early due to a change in standard-of-care systemic therapy. Of 177 included patients, 150 (84.7%) were male, and the median (IQR) age was 66 (60-72) years. Macrovascular invasion was seen in 131 (74.0%). As of July 1, 2022, the median OS was 12.3 months (90% CI, 10.6-14.3) with sorafenib vs 15.8 months (90% CI, 11.4-19.2) following SBRT and sorafenib (hazard ratio [HR], 0.77; 90% CI, 0.59-1.01; 1-sided P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.06). Adjusting for stratification factors, OS was improved with SBRT (HR, 0.72; 95% CI, 0.52-0.99; 2-sided P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.04). Median PFS was improved from 5.5 months (95% CI, 3.4-6.3) with sorafenib to 9.2 months (95% CI, 7.5-11.9) with SBRT and sorafenib (HR, 0.55; 95% CI, 0.40-0.75; 2-sided P&#x2009;<&#x2009;.001). Treatment-related grade 3 or higher adverse events were seen in 37 of 88 (42%) and 39 of 83 (47%) of patients treated with sorafenib vs SBRT and sorafenib, respectively (P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.52). There were 2 treatment-related deaths in the sorafenib group (death not otherwise specified and liver failure) and 1 in the SBRT and sorafenib group (lung infection). At 6 months, improved quality of life was seen in 2 of 20 (10%) and 6 of 17 (35%) of patients treated with sorafenib and SBRT and sorafenib, respectively.
  > CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE: In this phase 3 randomized clinical trial, among patients with locally advanced HCC, SBRT was associated with a clinically important but not statistically significant improved overall survival compared with sorafenib alone.
  > TRIAL REGISTRATION: ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: [[NCT01730937]].
