## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

A DLL3 BiTE with intracranial activity, the open RT question is whether it defers WBRT/PCI at relapse.

### DeLLphi-304

> CNS-PFS HR 0.54 ITT, 0.40 in brain-met subgroup (6.5 vs 4.2mo); tarlatamab delays intracranial progression vs chemo in 2L SCLC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The intracranial signal (brain-met CNS-PFS HR 0.40, CR 14.9% vs 5.4%) lands on a pretreated background: >70% of the subgroup had prior CNS-directed therapy. That reframes the RT question from replacement to sequencing, whether a DLL3 BiTE with CNS activity supports deferring WBRT at relapse rather than substituting for it.

- 🔍 Post-hoc intracranial analysis of DeLLphi-304 (phase 3, 2L relapsed SCLC, tarlatamab vs chemo); CNS endpoints not the registered primary
- 🔍 Brain-met subgroup n=67 vs 56; >70% had prior CNS-directed treatment, so the signal sits on a pretreated intracranial background
- 📐 ITT CNS-PFS by RECIST/investigator (stratified Cox); brain-met subgroup by mRANO-BM/BICR (unstratified Cox)
- 📊 CNS complete-response duration favored tarlatamab: median NE (1.3+ to 13.6+) vs 3.6mo
- ⚠️ Short follow-up (median 11.4mo); durability of intracranial control beyond ~1yr unproven
- ⚠️ Post-hoc small-N subgroup with unstratified model; hypothesis-generating, not prespecified
- 🔗 RT read: a DLL3 BiTE with intracranial activity raises deferral of WBRT/PCI at relapse, but most pts here had prior cranial RT, so it is not shown to substitute for it

## 🌀 GI Lower

ctDNA's low sensitivity for local regrowth keeps imaging plus clinical surveillance the backbone of nonoperative rectal management.

### ctDNA and Local Regrowth/Distant Mets in Nonoperative Rectal Cancer

> Sensitivity for local regrowth only 41% (vs 74% for distant mets); positive ctDNA is prognostic but can't replace NOM surveillance.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> In watch-and-wait after TNT, ctDNA can't replace pelvic surveillance: sensitivity for local regrowth is only 41% (12/29), so a negative result doesn't clear the pelvis. It reads better as a distant-relapse and adverse-biology flag (mets sensitivity 74%; ctDNA+ at regrowth carried more ypT3-4, 75% vs 21%).

![KM by ctDNA status. Log-rank p=0.0002 to <.0001 (Fig 1 longitudinal; Fig 2 first post-NAT within 180d).](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJvLPL_XgAATN04.jpg)
*KM by ctDNA status. Log-rank p=0.0002 to <.0001 (Fig 1 longitudinal; Fig 2 first post-NAT within 180d).*

- 📊 Per-sample diagnostic accuracy for events within ±90d of each draw (n=669 samples)

  | Target | Sensitivity | Specificity | Accuracy |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Local regrowth | 41% (12/29) | 94% (480/509) | 91% (492/538) |
  | Distant mets | 74% (31/42) | 97% (611/627) | 96% (642/669) |

- 🔍 INTERCEPT cohort, N=110 MSS rectal adenoca; cCR/nCR after NAT then NOM; serial tumor-informed ctDNA (Signatera Exome)
- 🔍 Baseline cT3 72/110, cN1-2 60/110; NAT was TNT in 104/110; median f/u 25 mo
- 📊 Events on NOM: local regrowth 23 (21%), distant mets 12 (11%)
- 📊 Salvage after ctDNA+ regrowth had more ypT3-4 (75% [6/8] vs 21% [3/14], p=0.01)
- ⚠️ 41% sensitivity for regrowth: a negative ctDNA can't clear the pelvis, imaging + clinical surveillance stays the backbone
- ⚠️ First post-NAT ctDNA claim rests on only 12 evaluable pts (Fig 2); single-institution cohort
- 🔗 ctDNA tracks distant relapse better than local regrowth, consistent with low shedding of localized luminal rectal disease
