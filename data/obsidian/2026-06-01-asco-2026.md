# 2026-06-01 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**DeLLphi-304: tarlatamab CNS PFS 6.5 vs 4.2mo (HR 0.40) in SCLC brain mets, first DLL3xCD3 intracranial signal but post hoc.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Thoracic and lower-GI led, both pitting molecular tools against RT-adjacent decisions. Tarlatamab's post-hoc CNS signal in SCLC (HR 0.40) is the first DLL3xCD3 intracranial read, historically WBRT/SRS/PCI territory. ctDNA in post-TNT rectal NOM misses 59% of local regrowths, augmenting but not replacing endoscopy/MRI.

## 🌀 GI Lower

ctDNA surveillance in organ-preservation rectal cancer: strong for distant mets, weak for the local regrowth watch-and-wait actually hinges on.

### ctDNA and Local Regrowth/Distant Mets in Nonoperative Management of Stage II-III Rectal Cancer

> ctDNA sensitivity 41% for local regrowth vs 74% for distant mets; positive result predicts both but can't replace endoscopy/MRI surveillance in rectal NOM.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The asymmetry is the actionable read: ctDNA caught only 41% of local regrowths vs 74% of distant mets, so for post-TNT organ-preservation surveillance it flags distant-met risk but can't replace endoscopy/MRI. ctDNA+ at regrowth also tracked more advanced ypT3-4 salvage pathology (75% vs 21%).

![ctDNA and Local Regrowth/Distant Mets in Nonoperative Management of Stage II-III Rectal Cancer](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJvLPL_XgAATN04.jpg)

- 📊 Per-sample diagnostic accuracy (669 serial samples)

  | Endpoint | Sensitivity | Specificity | Accuracy |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Local regrowth | 41% (12/29) | 94% (480/509) | 91% (492/538) |
  | Distant metastasis | 74% (31/42) | 97% (611/627) | 96% (642/669) |

- 🔍 INTERCEPT program: prospective single-center cohort, N=110 MSS rectal adenoca in cCR/nCR after NAT, tumor-informed Signatera Exome
- 📐 Ever-positive longitudinal ctDNA → worse regrowth-free and distant-met-free survival (Fig 1, log-rank p=0.0002 and p<0.0001)
- 📐 First post-NAT ctDNA (n=12 evaluable, within 180d): positive → worse regrowth-free and distant-met-free survival (Fig 2, p=0.0006 and p<0.0001)
- 🩻 ctDNA+ at local regrowth → more advanced salvage pathology: ypT3-4 in 75% (6/8) vs 21% (3/14), p=0.01
- ⚠️ Diagnostic estimates rest on small event counts: 29 regrowth-associated and 42 distant-met-associated samples
- ⚠️ NAT was almost entirely TNT (104/110); may not transfer to short-course-RT or chemoRT-only NOM pathways
- 🔗 The RT read: 41% sensitivity means ctDNA can't replace DRE/endoscopy/MRI surveillance in post-TNT watch-and-wait, only augment it

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

A systemic bispecific posts an intracranial signal in SCLC, RT's historic domain for CNS control.

### DeLLphi-304

> Intracranial: time-to-CNS-progression HR 0.54 ITT; in brain-mets pts CNS PFS 6.5 vs 4.2mo (HR 0.40), CNS CR 15% vs 5% for tarlatamab.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is intracranial control without new radiation: brain-mets CNS PFS 6.5 vs 4.2mo (HR 0.40) and CNS CR 15% vs 5%. But >70% had prior CNS-directed therapy, so this is salvage intracranial activity, not a defer-brain-RT signal in RT-naive disease. Informs sequencing systemic ahead of repeat cranial RT.

![DeLLphi-304](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwT67bW0AA1Hqe.jpg)

- 🔍 Post hoc intracranial analysis of DeLLphi-304 (phase 3, 2L SCLC tarlatamab vs chemo); CNS endpoints not a prespecified primary
- 🔍 ITT CNS cohort n=254 vs 255; brain-mets subgroup n=67 vs 56 (mRANO-BM by BICR)
- ⚠️ >70% of brain-mets pts had prior CNS-directed therapy: salvage intracranial activity, not de novo control
- ⚠️ Brain-mets HRs unstratified, small N, post hoc; hypothesis-generating
- 📊 CNS tumor shrinkage ≥30%: 56.3% (9/16) tarlatamab vs 38.5% (5/13) chemo
- 🔗 First DLL3xCD3 bispecific intracranial signal in SCLC; historically CNS control leaned on WBRT/SRS and PCI
