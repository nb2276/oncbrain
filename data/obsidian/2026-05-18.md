# 2026-05-18

**HCC EBRT IPD (n=4,913): mOS 6.8yr BCLC-0, 4.6yr BCLC-A; EBRT outcomes comparable to resection/ablation in early-stage disease.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> HCC and prostate carried the biggest signal: n=4,913 EBRT cohort strengthens EBRT as a resection/ablation alternative in early HCC, while PEACE 2 puts pelvic RT in direct conflict with POP-RT (both phase III). EXTEND adds randomized cross-histology proof for MDT in oligomets; NRG 1005 cuts breast boost to 15 fractions.

## 🌰 Prostate

Two phase III trials: HEAT confirms SBRT NI to IMRT (ADT-inclusive); PEACE 2 is null for pelvic RT, directly conflicting POP-RT.

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT interim: SBRT non-inferior to IMRT hypofractionation for biochemical control in localized PCa; BF 7% vs 7.4% at 4.25y.

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)

| Endpoint | AHRT | EHRT |
|---|---|---|
| BF at 4.25y | 7% | 7.4% |
| p_non-inferiority | 0.007 | — |
| n randomized / analyzed | 156 / 142 | 156 / 142 |
| Median FU | 59.7 mo | 59.7 mo |

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInK_RpW0AACNnC.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase III international RCT (interim analysis); n=156 randomized, n=142 analyzed; 82.4% intermediate-risk; 28% received ADT; median FU 59.7 mo
- 💊 AHRT: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx) + GTV SIB 40 Gy; EHRT: 70.2 Gy/26 fx via IMRT; ADT ≤6 mo permitted both arms
- 🔍 1° EP: BF by Phoenix definition; non-inferiority margin 12%; full enrollment target n=456 (n=420 evaluable)
- 📊 AE by domain
  - Acute G2+ GI: lower with AHRT
  - Late G2+ GI: no significant difference between arms
  - Acute/late G2+ GU: no significant difference
- ⚠️ Laxative/psyllium use scored as G2 GI toxicity; may inflate acute GI rate in EHRT arm
- 🔗 HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005: prior AHRT trials lacked IMRT, excluded ADT, or had heterogeneous controls; HEAT fills all three gaps
- ⚠️ Interim analysis only; primary non-inferiority conclusion deferred to prespecified final analysis (n=456)

### PEACE 2

> PEACE 2: pelvic RT did not improve cPFS (HR 0.81, p=0.088), MFS, OS, or PCSS vs prostate-only RT in very high-risk N0 PCa.

![PEACE 2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8Pa0XQAAYLXz.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6; 72.2] | 0.81 [0.63; 1.03] | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4; 68.1] | ref | — |

![PEACE 2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8JCvXYAASRBo.jpg)

- 📊 Secondary EPs all null: no effect on MFS, PCSS, or OS
- 🔍 Phase III 2×2 factorial; N=761 in RT comparison; ADT 3 yrs ± cabazitaxel 4 cycles; staging by conventional imaging or Choline PET-CT
- 🔍 Eligibility: very high-risk (≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20), N0M0
- 🔗 vs POP-RT (Jain, NEJM Evidence 2023): pelvic RT improved bFFS, MFS, OS in high-risk PCa — PEACE 2 is null on all clinical EPs; direct conflict
- ⚠️ Staged pre-PSMA-PET: occult N+ pts in 'N0' arms could dilute pelvic RT signal or spuriously benefit prostate-only arm
- ⚠️ Modest non-significant cPFS trend (HR 0.81) cannot exclude subgroup benefit; no biomarker-stratified analysis in source
- ⚠️ Side effects minimal with modern RT; toxicity not the limiting factor here

## 👄 Head & Neck

TORPEdO finds no HR-QoL benefit from IMPT over IMRT; pooled ENI-omission data show 0% solitary elective nodal failure at 5yr.

### TORPEdO (CRUK/18/010)

> TORPEdO (N=205): IMPT vs IMRT for OPSCC — UW-QoL physical composite null at 12 months; no HR-QoL difference at 3, 12, or 24 months.

![TORPEdO (CRUK/18/010)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HImrQwvWMAA-Laf.jpg)

![TORPEdO (CRUK/18/010)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HImrQv4WIAAKThu.jpg)

- 📊 PRO co-primary endpoint (UW-QoL physical composite: saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech): no difference between IMPT and IMRT at 3, 12, or 24 months post RT
- 📐 No effect size (mean difference, CI, or p-value) reported in source for PRO co-primary; null finding stated by investigators
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=205, 2:1 IMPT:IMRT; OPSCC requiring bilateral neck concurrent CRT; 70 Gy/56 Gy in 33 fractions; cisplatin 100mg/m² D1+D22
- 🔍 Stratified by T-stage, N-stage, p16 status, smoking history; UK multicentre (Christie + ICR CTU)
- 📊 HR-QoL declines at end of CRT then recovers; most stabilize from 12 months; some meaningful deterioration persists to 2 years
- ⚠️ Clinician-reported co-primary (CTCAE G3 weight loss ≥20% or gastrostomy dependence at 12 months) not reported in source; full trial verdict pending
- ⚠️ Novice UK proton centres + identical planning constraints likely compressed dosimetric advantage vs expert-centre IMPT; commentators note high-experience centres still report clinical benefit
- ⚠️ 2:1 allocation limits IMRT arm power; N=205 modest for a phase 3 PRO endpoint
- 🔗 McBride et al., Lancet 2026 ([[PMID 42134353]]): concurrent proton vs photon OPSCC publication — US authors (MSK/UCSD); separate dataset from CRUK TORPEdO

  - Regional recurrence: 4.3%

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

Largest EBRT IPD cohort yet; early-stage OS on par with resection and ablation across multinational sites and 5-yr median follow-up.

### HCC EBRT Multinational IPD Cohort

> Largest HCC EBRT IPD cohort (n=4,913): mOS 6.8yr BCLC-0, 4.6yr BCLC-A; outcomes comparable to resection and ablation.

- 🔍 Retrospective IPD meta-analysis; n=4,913, multinational, median f/u 5.0yr; systematic review search to Dec 2022
- 📊 OS by BCLC stage

  | Stage | All pts mOS (95% CI) | Treatment-naïve mOS (95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | BCLC-0 | 6.8yr (5.7-8.7) | NR (8.6-NR) |
  | BCLC-A | 4.6yr (4.1-5.1) | 5.4yr (4.5-6.7) |

- 📐 Random-effects Cox multivariable modeling; higher mortality risk: advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, poor PS, Child-Pugh B/C
- 📐 Lower mortality risk: ablative RT dose and more recent year of treatment (multivariable)
- 🔗 OS appears comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies for very early/early-stage HCC
- ⚠️ No randomized comparator within the study; cross-modality OS comparisons are contextual, not direct
- ⚠️ Retrospective selection effects likely: EBRT historically reserved for pts ineligible for resection/ablation

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS). We analyzed individual patient data (IPD) from an international cohort to assess OS among patients with HCC treated with EBRT.
  > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). Corresponding authors were invited to submit IPD for the study. We performed Kaplan-Meier survival analyses to determine OS and restricted mean survival time (RMST) stratified by Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) stage and treatment status (ie, treatment-na&#xef;ve and experienced). We performed random effects Cox proportional hazards modeling to assess clinical characteristics associated with OS.
  > RESULTS: Data were provided on 4,913 patients treated with EBRT with a median follow-up time of 5.0 years. The median OS was 6.8 years (95% CI, 5.7 to 8.7) for BCLC-0 and 4.6 years (95% CI, 4.1 to 5.1) for BCLC-A. Among treatment-na&#xef;ve patients, the median OS was not reached (95% CI, 8.6 to not reached) for BCLC-0 and was 5.4 years (95% CI, 4.5 to 6.7) for BCLC-A. In multivariable models, more advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse performance status, and Child-Pugh class B or C were associated with a higher risk of mortality. Ablative radiation dose and more recent year of treatment were associated with a reduced risk of death.
  > CONCLUSION: To our knowledge, this study represents the largest multinational cohort of patients with HCC treated with EBRT. OS outcomes with EBRT for very early- and early-stage HCC appear to be comparable with resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies. These data support the inclusion of EBRT in the BCLC HCC clinical decision-making process.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND provides phase II RCT proof of MDT benefit (HR 0.54) across 1-5 met solid tumor histologies.

### EXTEND Trial

> EXTEND phase II RCT: MDT+SOC (98% RT) improves PFS vs SOC in oligometastatic solid tumors, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001, N=350.

- 📊 PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001, MDT+SOC vs SOC across all baskets
- 📊 PFS excluding prostate baskets: HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.40-0.89)
- 🔍 Phase II multicenter RCT; N=350 randomized, 334 per-protocol; 1-5 mets; MDT=RT in 98% of mets
- 📊 Basket-level PFS superiority: pancreas, prostate, Other; inconclusive: breast, kidney
- 🔗 Consistent with SABR-COMET (Lancet 2019), ORIOLE, STOMP; adds multi-histology randomized evidence
- ⚠️ Per-basket underpowering likely explains inconclusive breast/kidney results; basket-specific N not reported
- ⚠️ No OS data in abstract; PFS endpoint only; authors explicitly call for phase III per histology
- 🔍 ctDNA clearance at 3-mo post-enrollment correlated with improved survival (exploratory biomarker signal)

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Sherry AD, Haymaker C, Wang S, Liu S, Bathala TK, Medina-Rosales MN, Seo A, Hara K, Reddy J, Chun SG, Mayo LL, Walker G, Pant S, Zhao D, Kovitz CA, Ramirez D, Ha CS, Smith BD, Gomez D, Cohen L, Koong AC, Reuben A, Tannir N, Corn PG, Tran PT, Siddiqui BA, Subudhi SK, Msaouel P, Ludmir EB, Tang C. ## 🫘 Kidney

FASTRACK II: longest published SABR follow-up for primary RCC (62mo), 100% local control at 84mo in 70 non-surgical pts.

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 84 months with SABR for primary RCC in 70 non-surgical pts (median f/u 62mo).

- 📊 1° EP: 100% freedom from local progression at 36, 60, and 84 months (ITT, N=70)
- 🔍 Non-randomised phase 2, 8 sites (Australia/Netherlands), N=71 enrolled, 1 withdrew pre-treatment
- 🔍 Eligible: medically inoperable, high surgical risk, or surgery-declined; ECOG PS ≤2; tumor ≤10cm; N0-N1
- 🔍 Tx: 26 Gy × 1 (≤4cm) or 42 Gy × 3 @ 48h (>4cm)
- 📊 Predominantly T1b (56%), median age 77 (70-82), median tumor 46mm; no cancer-related deaths
- 📊 G3 AEs within 9 months (possibly/probably/definitely related): 7 pts (10%); no G4, no treatment-related deaths
  - Abdominal/flank/tumor pain: 4 events (6%)
  - Nausea/vomiting: 3 events (4%)
  - Colonic obstruction: 2 events (3%)
  - Diarrhea: 1 event (1%)
- ⚠️ Single-arm; no randomised comparator vs partial nephrectomy or ablation
- 🔗 Extends STARS phase I/II data; longest published median f/u (62mo) for SABR in primary RCC

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42144018]] **Long-term outcomes of stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy for primary kidney cancer (TROG 15.03 FASTRACK II): a multicentre, non-randomised, phase 2 study.** — Siva; Pryor; Martin et al.. *The Lancet. Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4)
  > BACKGROUND: Stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy (SABR) is an emerging, non-invasive alternative for primary renal cell carcinoma. We aimed to provide the final long-term trial outcomes of TransTasman Radiation Oncology Group (TROG) 15.03 FASTRACK II, the first phase 2 trial investigating SABR for primary renal cell carcinoma to our knowledge.
  > METHODS: FASTRACK II was a non-randomised, phase 2 study conducted in eight hospitals in Australia and the Netherlands by TROG and the Australian and New Zealand Urogenital and Prostate (ANZUP) Cancer Trials Group. Here, we report the final pre-planned follow-up results. Adult patients (aged &#x2265;18 years) with histologically confirmed primary renal cell carcinoma, who were medically inoperable, high risk, or declined surgery, had an Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status of 2 or less, had tumours 10 cm or less in size, and had N0-N1 disease were included. Patients underwent either a single fraction SABR of 26 Gy for tumours 4 cm or less in maximum diameter, or 42 Gy in three fractions delivered 48 h apart for tumours more than 4 cm in maximum diameter. The primary outcome was freedom from local progression to assess local control after SABR evaluated with the Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours. The primary endpoint and safety were evaluated in the intention-to-treat population. A patient representative was involved in the study design and conduct. The trial was registered with ClinicalTrials.gov ([[NCT02613819]]) and is closed to enrolment.
  > FINDINGS: Between July 28, 2016, and Feb 27, 2020, 71 patients were enrolled and one withdrew consent before treatment. Median follow-up was 62 months (IQR 60-72), median age was 77 years (70-82). 49 (70%) of 70 patients were male and 21 (30%) were female. Race and ethnicity data were not collected. The median tumour size was 46 mm (37-55), with 24 (34%) patients with T1a disease, 39 (56%) with T1b disease, six (9%) with T2a disease, and one (1%) with T3a disease. One patient (1%) had nodal involvement (N1). SABR resulted in 100% local control at 36 months, 60 months, and 84 months. Seven (10%) patients had at least one grade 3 adverse event within 9 months of SABR that was designated possibly, probably, or definitely related to treatment: nausea and vomiting (three [4%] events); abdominal, flank, or tumour pain (four [6%]); colonic obstruction (two [3%]); and diarrhoea (one [1%]). No new long-term safety signals, grade 4 events, or treatment-related deaths were noted.
  > INTERPRETATION: Long-term follow-up supports the safety and local control of SABR for non-surgical patients with renal cell carcinoma, with no observed local recurrences or cancer-related deaths in this cohort, which had predominantly T1b disease or higher.

## 🎀 Breast

NRG 1005 provides phase III US/international validation of concurrent SIB boost, cutting total fractions from 22-31 to 15.

### NRG/RTOG 1005 · [[NCT01349322]]

> NRG/RTOG 1005: concurrent breast boost non-inferior to sequential for IBR (HR 1.31, 90% CI 0.84-2.04), 15 vs 22-31 total fractions.

- 📊 IBR by timepoint and arm

  | Timepoint | Sequential | Concurrent |
  |---|---|---|
  | 5-yr IBR | 2.1% | 1.9% |
  | 7-yr IBR | 2.2% | 2.6% |

- 📐 HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04); upper bound 2.04 < NI margin 2.12; NI p=0.037
- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority RCT; N=2,255 eligible (2,354 randomized); 278 sites; median f/u 7.3yr
- 🔍 Treatment arms
  - Sequential: WBI 50/25F or 42.7/16F, then boost 12-14 Gy/6-7F (total 22-31F)
  - Concurrent: WBI 40 Gy/15F + boost 8 Gy at 0.53 Gy/day simultaneous (total 15F)
- 🔍 High-risk features: 52.7% G3, 29.6% ER-neg, 16.7% LVI, 16.3% node-pos; 61.8% received chemo
- 📊 Secondary oncologic endpoints: no arm differences
  - DFS, DDFS, OS: no significant differences between arms
  - 7-yr RNR 1.2% (95% CI 0.8-1.7); no arm difference
- 📊 Cosmesis: concurrent non-inferior at 3yr
  - BCTOS change 0.18 (concurrent) vs 0.16 (sequential); NI p<0.0001
  - Physician-rated excellent/good 82.4% vs 85.9%; p=0.34
- 📊 G3-4 AEs: no significant arm difference (p=0.81)
- 🔗 SIB/concurrent boost practiced at European centers pre-trial; NRG 1005 provides phase III US/international validation
- ⚠️ 56 IBR events over 7.3yr; low absolute count limits subgroup analysis power
- ⚠️ Open-label; primary cosmesis endpoint (BCTOS) is patient self-report

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02465](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02465)
  > PURPOSE: For patients with breast cancer undergoing breast conservation, escalating the dose (boost) of radiation to the lumpectomy cavity after whole-breast irradiation (WBI) reduces ipsilateral breast recurrence (IBR) but extends treatment duration. This phase III trial investigated whether boost delivery during WBI versus after WBI provides noninferior IBR and preserves cosmetic appearance.
  > METHODS: NRG/RTOG 1005 randomly assigned patients at higher risk for IBR after lumpectomy and axillary surgery to either a sequential boost of 12 Gy in six fractions(F) or 14 Gy in 7F after WBI of 50 Gy in 25F or 42.7 Gy in 16F (sequential arm) or a concurrent boost of 8 Gy in 15F of 0.53 Gy per day with WBI of 40 Gy in 15F (concurrent arm) using 3-dimensional conformal radiation therapy (RT) or intensity-modulated RT. Based on 1.59% 5-year IBR for the sequential arm, defining the noninferiority margin as a hazard ratio upper limit on the 90% CI of 2.12, 2,312 patients provide 80% power for noninferiority of IBR as first recurrence for the concurrent arm. Secondary end points included disease-free survival and overall survival, adverse events (AEs), and cosmetic outcomes.
  > RESULTS: Between May 24, 2011, and June 20, 2014, 2,354 patients were randomly assigned, with 2,255 eligible for analysis (sequential arm, n = 1,118; concurrent arm, n = 1,137). With median follow-up of 7.3 years, there were 56 IBR events; 5- and 7-year IBR were 2.1% and 2.2% on the sequential arm and 1.9% and 2.6% on the concurrent arm, respectively. The noninferiority criterion was met: HR (90% CI): 1.31 (0.84 to 2.04), P = .037. No differences were observed in AEs, cosmetic outcomes, or survival between arms.
  > CONCLUSION: Concurrent boost during WBI results in noninferior IBR compared with sequential boost without worsening toxicity or cosmetic outcomes and reduces overall treatment time.
