# 2026-05-18

**PEACE 2: pelvic RT adds no cPFS/MFS/OS benefit over prostate-only RT in very high-risk N0M0 PCa (HR 0.81, p=0.088), contradicting POP-RT.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> PEACE 2 nulls pelvic RT in very high-risk PCa (HR 0.81, p=0.088), contradicting POP-RT's positive bPFS/MFS signal. TORPEdO shows IMPT offers no HRQoL advantage over IMRT in OPSCC at 12 months. EXTEND adds 53-mo phase II randomised evidence for MDT across oligomets histologies (HR 0.54).

## 🌀 Lower GI

OPERA post-hoc raises whether W14 clinical exam can compress the W&W decision window by 10 weeks without compromising organ preservation rates.

### OPERA Trial (5-year, rectal preservation response assessment)

> OPERA post-hoc: W14 clinical exam (DRE + rectoscopy) reliably identifies cCR, enabling safe W&W with 5-yr OP ~75-83% in both arms.

![OPERA Trial (5-year, rectal preservation response assessment)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIm49InWMAAbrtG.jpg)

| Timepoint / Outcome | Arm A | Arm B | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| W14 good response (cCR+nCR) | 65% | 88% | 0.004 |
| 5-yr organ preservation | 75% | 83% | 0.24 |

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of OPERA trial; response assessed at W14 (DRE + rectoscopy ± MRI TRG) vs standard W24 three-modality assessment; N=141 with CTRE
- 📊 W14 good response (cCR + nCR): 76% overall; Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65% (p=0.004); MRI TRG1-2 concordance in CR pts 98% (80/82)
- 📊 5-yr organ preservation: Arm A 75% vs Arm B 83% (p=0.24); cCR vs nCR similar (81% vs 77%)
- 🔗 Original OPERA trial established boost-arm superiority for OP; this post-hoc asks whether W14 assessment can replace W24, condensing the decision window by ~10 weeks
- ⚠️ Post-hoc subgroup analysis only; W14 assessment protocol was not the prespecified primary decision point in original OPERA design; W&W selection bias cannot be excluded
- ⚠️ nCR (near-complete response) reflects radiation side effects per authors, not residual disease; grouping with cCR for OP eligibility is a clinical judgment call, not validated endpoint
- ❓ Prospective validation of W14 as primary decision timepoint needed; whether nCR-to-OP safely extends to all comers or requires MRI confirmation for equivocal DRE findings unclear

## 👄 Head & Neck

Two trials advance parallel de-escalation agendas: radiation field reduction (ENI omission) and modality selection (IMPT vs IMRT).

> INRT-AIR + DARTBOARD pooled (N=117): 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74% with ENI omission.

### TORPEdO

> TORPEdO: UW-QoL physical composite equivalent for IMPT vs IMRT in OPSCC; no patient-reported HRQoL advantage for proton at 12 months.

- 📊 PRO co-primary (UW-QoL physical composite: saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech): similar IMPT vs IMRT at 3, 12, and 24 months post RT; no effect size reported in source tweets
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=205, OPSCC bilateral neck CRT, 2:1 IMPT:IMRT, 70/56 Gy in 33f, cisplatin 100 mg/m² D1+D22; stratified by T/N-stage, p16, smoking history
- ⚠️ Clinician-reported co-primary (CTCAE G3 weight loss ≥20% or gastrostomy dependence at 12m) not reported in source tweets; full trial verdict pending that endpoint
- ⚠️ Commentary: identical planning constraints + novice UK IMPT centers may have attenuated proton's potential; experienced high-volume centers still report clinical gains
- 🔗 First phase 3 RCT powered for HRQoL comparison of proton vs IMRT in OPSCC; prior evidence base was single-arm series and dosimetric comparisons only
- ❓ 5-year HRQoL follow-up ongoing; meaningful deterioration in a subset at 2yr in both arms — durability question unresolved

## 🌰 Prostate

Prostate carried the day's sharpest null: pelvic RT adds nothing in very high-risk N0M0 PCa, directly contradicting POP-RT.

### PEACE 2 (pelvic RT, very high-risk localized prostate)

> PEACE 2: pelvic RT added no cPFS, MFS, or OS benefit over prostate-only RT in very high-risk localized PCa; HR 0.81 (p=0.088).

![PEACE 2 (pelvic RT, very high-risk localized prostate)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8Pa0XQAAYLXz.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% (57.4-68.1) | ref | — |
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% (61.6-72.2) | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |

- 📊 1° EP cPFS: HR 0.81 (95% CI 0.63-1.03), p=0.088; 7-yr rate 67.1% pelvic vs 62.9% prostate-only RT
- 📊 MFS, PCSS, OS all null (no effect sizes reported in source tweets)
- 🔍 Phase III 2x2 factorial; N=761 (380 prostate-only RT, 381 pelvic RT); ADT 3yr + high-dose RT ± pelvic field ± cabazitaxel x4 cycles; N0M0 staged by conventional imaging or Choline PET/CT
- 🔗 vs POP-RT (JCO 2021): pelvic RT significantly improved bPFS, cFFS, and MFS in high-risk PCa. PEACE 2 null across equivalent endpoints. Key differences: pre-PSMA staging, cabazitaxel co-treatment in 50%, and dose/fractionation.
- ⚠️ Conventional imaging staging predates PSMA PET era — occult nodal disease in subset undetected at enrollment. Cabazitaxel in half the cohort may have narrowed marginal benefit of adding nodal RT.
- ❓ Does PSMA PET-selected cN+ subgroup benefit from pelvic RT? Does cabazitaxel co-treatment dilute the pelvic RT signal in this design?

### HEAT Trial (AHRT vs EHRT, prostate) · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT phase III interim: AHRT (5fx) non-inferior to EHRT (26fx) for BF in localized PCa; 7% vs 7.4% at 4.25y.

![HEAT Trial (AHRT vs EHRT, prostate)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)

|  | AHRT (5fx) | EHRT (26fx) |
|---|---|---|
| BF at 4.25y | 7% | 7.4% |
| P(non-inferiority) | 0.007 |  |

- 📊 1° EP: BF (Phoenix) 7% AHRT vs 7.4% EHRT at 4.25y; P(non-inferiority)=0.007; NI margin 12%
- 🔍 phase III RCT, n=156 randomized / 142 analyzed (interim); 82.4% intermediate-risk; 28% received ADT; median f/u 59.7mo
- 💊 AHRT 36.25 Gy/5fx (7.25 Gy/fx) + GTV SIB to 40 Gy; EHRT 70.2 Gy/26fx IMRT; ADT ≤6mo permitted both arms
- 📊 Acute G2+ GI toxicity lower with AHRT; late G2+ GI and acute/late G2+ GU: no significant difference between arms
- 🔗 first head-to-head AHRT vs EHRT using IMRT with ADT in both arms; HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005 had heterogeneous controls or excluded ADT
- ⚠️ interim analysis; prespecified final primary analysis pending; accrual goal n=456, n=420 evaluable enrolled

## 🫘 Kidney

FASTRACK II provides the longest-follow-up SABR-for-primary-RCC dataset to date; the randomised comparator gap persists.

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03, SABR primary RCC long-term) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months for SABR in inoperable primary RCC (N=70, median f/u 62 mo).

- 📊 1° EP (freedom from local progression): 100% LC at 36mo, 60mo, and 84mo. No local recurrences observed.
- 🔍 Non-randomised phase 2, N=70 (1 withdrew pre-treatment), 8 centres AUS/NL. T1a 34%, T1b 56%, T2a 9%, T3a 1%. Median tumour 46mm. Medically inoperable, high-risk, or declined surgery.
- 💊 Single-fraction 26 Gy for tumours ≤4cm; 42 Gy in 3 fractions (48h apart) for >4cm.
- ⚠️ G3 AEs within 9mo in 10% of pts: nausea/vomiting 4%, abdominal/flank/tumour pain 6%, colonic obstruction 3%, diarrhoea 1%. No G4 events or treatment-related deaths.
- 🔗 Consistent with prior single-arm SABR-for-RCC signals (STAR-TRK, IROCK registry); no randomised comparator vs partial nephrectomy or thermal ablation exists.
- ⚠️ Single-arm design, no comparator arm. Predominantly older inoperable pts (median age 77); applicability to surgical candidates unclear. Cancer-related deaths not reported by count.
- ❓ Comparative efficacy vs partial nephrectomy or thermal ablation in operable pts remains unanswered; long-term renal function outcomes not reported in abstract.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

### EXTEND Trial (MDT + SOC, oligometastatic solid tumors)

> EXTEND phase II: MDT+SOC improves PFS vs SOC in oligomets, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001, 53-mo f/u.

- 📊 Overall PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001; excl. prostate baskets HR 0.60 (0.40-0.89)
- 🔍 Multicenter randomized phase II; N=334 per-protocol (MDT+SOC n=166, SOC n=168); 1-5 mets, 6 histology baskets; median f/u 53 months; 98% RT as MDT
- 📊 PFS by basket
  - Pancreas: superiority demonstrated
  - Prostate (both baskets): superiority demonstrated
  - Other basket: superiority demonstrated
  - Breast, kidney: inconclusive
- 🔗 vs SABR-COMET (Lancet 2019) and ORIOLE (JAMA Oncol 2020): EXTEND corroborates PFS benefit of MDT in oligomets with longer f/u and broader histology scope; basket-specific heterogeneity consistent with SABR-COMET subgroup signals
- ⚠️ Phase II, not powered for OS; basket-level results underpowered individually; breast and kidney inconclusive, limiting cross-histology generalizability; randomized within baskets but baskets combined for overall analysis
- 🔍 ctDNA detectable at enrollment correlated with shorter PFS/OS; ctDNA clearance at 3 months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival; MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation most pronounced in PFS-superior baskets
- ❓ Phase III warranted for pancreas and prostate baskets; optimal ctDNA threshold for oligomets definition unresolved; mechanism of systemic immune activation from locoregional MDT needs prospective validation

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

### HCC EBRT multinational IPD cohort (OS outcomes)

> EBRT for BCLC-0/A HCC: mOS not reached (tx-naive BCLC-0), 5.4yr BCLC-A; OS comparable to resection and thermal ablation.

- 📊 mOS by BCLC stage (N=4,913, median f/u 5.0yr)

  | Stage | All pts mOS (95% CI) | Tx-naive mOS (95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | BCLC-0 | 6.8yr (5.7-8.7) | NR (8.6-NR) |
  | BCLC-A | 4.6yr (4.1-5.1) | 5.4yr (4.5-6.7) |

- 🔍 IPD systematic review; prespecified technical standards for HCC EBRT; search through Dec 2022; random effects Cox modeling for OS predictors
- 📊 On multivariable analysis: ablative RT dose and more recent treatment year both independently associated with reduced mortality risk
- ⚠️ Advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse PS, Child-Pugh B/C all associated with increased mortality risk — expected confounders in a non-randomised cohort
- 🔗 Authors conclude OS comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies for BCLC-0/A; no direct head-to-head randomised comparison included
- ⚠️ Non-randomised IPD pooled cohort; selection bias for which institutions submitted data; no control arm; era effects (year of treatment as a significant covariate) limit contemporaneous inference
- ❓ Randomised comparison vs TACE, RFA, or resection for BCLC-A remains the critical unanswered question; dose-fractionation heterogeneity across contributing centres not fully characterised

- 📄 [[PMID 42139645]] **Overall Survival Among Patients With Hepatocellular Carcinoma Treated With External Beam Radiation Therapy: Individual Patient Data Outcomes From a Multinational Cohort.** — Moon; Yanagihara; Dawson et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS). We analyzed individual patient data (IPD) from an international cohort to assess OS among patients with HCC treated with EBRT.
  > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). Corresponding authors were invited to submit IPD for the study. We performed Kaplan-Meier survival analyses to determine OS and restricted mean survival time (RMST) stratified by Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) stage and treatment status (ie, treatment-na&#xef;ve and experienced). We performed random effects Cox proportional hazards modeling to assess clinical characteristics associated with OS.
  > RESULTS: Data were provided on 4,913 patients treated with EBRT with a median follow-up time of 5.0 years. The median OS was 6.8 years (95% CI, 5.7 to 8.7) for BCLC-0 and 4.6 years (95% CI, 4.1 to 5.1) for BCLC-A. Among treatment-na&#xef;ve patients, the median OS was not reached (95% CI, 8.6 to not reached) for BCLC-0 and was 5.4 years (95% CI, 4.5 to 6.7) for BCLC-A. In multivariable models, more advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse performance status, and Child-Pugh class B or C were associated with a higher risk of mortality. Ablative radiation dose and more recent year of treatment were associated with a reduced risk of death.
  > CONCLUSION: To our knowledge, this study represents the largest multinational cohort of patients with HCC treated with EBRT. OS outcomes with EBRT for very early- and early-stage HCC appear to be comparable with resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies. These data support the inclusion of EBRT in the BCLC HCC clinical decision-making process.
