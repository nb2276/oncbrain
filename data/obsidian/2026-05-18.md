# 2026-05-18

**EXTEND phase II: MDT+SOC vs SOC across oligomet histologies, PFS HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001, 53mo median f/u.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate and oligomets drove the sharpest tension: PEACE 2 (phase III null) diverges from POP-RT on pelvic RT in VHR CaP, while HEAT confirms SBRT 5fx non-inferiority to EHRT. EXTEND reinforces MDT benefit across histologies at 53mo. Head-neck, kidney, and HCC added non-randomized but direction-setting RT signals.

## 🌀 Lower GI

### OPERA Trial (5-year results)

> OPERA post-hoc: W14 clinical exam (DRE + rectoscopy) reliably identifies cCR, enabling safe W&W; 5-yr OP 75% vs 83% (p=0.24) across arms.

![OPERA Trial (5-year results)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIm49InWMAAbrtG.jpg)

| Outcome | Arm A | Arm B | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| W14 good response (cCR+nCR) | 65% | 88% | 0.004 |
| 5-yr organ preservation | 75% | 83% | 0.24 |

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of OPERA RCT; W14 CTRE (DRE + rectoscopy ± MRI) evaluated as earlier response assessment vs the trial's primary W24 three-modality approach; N=141 with CTRE data
- 📊 W14 good response (cCR + nCR): 76% overall; Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65% (p=0.004); MRI TRG1-2 in 98% of CR pts (80/82)
- 📊 5-yr organ preservation: 75% Arm A vs 83% Arm B (p=0.24); cCR vs nCR similar OP rates (81% vs 77%)
- 🔗 Key finding: nCR and cCR have equivalent OP outcomes at 5 years — nCR likely reflects radiation effect, not residual disease; justifies non-operative management for both
- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis only; CTRE at W14 not the primary trial endpoint; W14 assessment timing (1 month post-NAT) may not be universally adopted without prospective validation
- ❓ Prospective validation of W14 CTRE as decision point for W&W needed; role of MRI TRG alone vs combined CTRE at W14 unresolved

## 👄 Head & Neck

Two de-escalation signals in one day: proton vs photon QoL equivalence (TORPEdO) and ENI omission (INRT-AIR/DARTBOARD), each awaiting RCT validation.

### TORPEdO (CRUK/18/010)

> TORPEdO phase 3: IMPT vs IMRT shows no difference in HR-QoL at 12 months in oropharyngeal SCC.

![Conclusions: similar HR-QoL outcomes across multiple PRO questionnaires from week 6 post-CRT; HR-QoL stabilises from 12 months; follow-up ongoing to 5 years.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HImrQxQXoAAf4Fu.jpg)
*Conclusions: similar HR-QoL outcomes across multiple PRO questionnaires from week 6 post-CRT; HR-QoL stabilises from 12 months; follow-up ongoing to 5 years.*

- 📊 Co-primary patient-reported endpoint (UW-QoL physical composite: saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech): no difference between arms at 3, 12, or 24 months post-RT
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=205, oropharyngeal SCC requiring concurrent chemo-RT + bilateral neck treatment; 2:1 randomisation IMPT vs IMRT; 70/56 Gy in 33 fractions, cisplatin 100 mg/m² D1+D22; stratified by T-stage, N-stage, p16 status, smoking history
- 📊 HR-QoL trajectory similar from week 6 post-CRT onward; scores decline at end of treatment then recover, most stabilising from 12 months; 5-year follow-up ongoing
- ⚠️ Commentator (@AmadeoWals) notes identical planning constraints and inexperienced UK proton centres likely limited IMPT's potential benefit; centres with high proton expertise may still see clinical advantage not captured here
- ⚠️ Co-primary clinician-reported endpoint (CTCAE G3 weight loss ≥20% or gastrostomy dependence at 12 months) result not reported in source tweets; verdict constrained to HR-QoL arm only
- 🔗 vs prior IMPT rationale: dosimetric superiority of IMPT for salivary/mucosal sparing well-established; TORPEdO is the first powered phase 3 to test whether that translates to patient-reported QoL benefit at 12 months
- ❓ Whether centres with mature proton programs would show differential QoL or late-toxicity benefit remains open; 5-year HR-QoL and clinician-endpoint data pending

### ENI Omission HNSCC (INRT-AIR & DARTBOARD pooled)

> INRT-AIR/DARTBOARD pooled: 5-yr elective nodal recurrence 0% with ENI omission in 117 HNSCC pts, 5-yr OS 87%.

![5-yr OS 87%, 5-yr PFS 74%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, regional recurrence 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo; N=117, median f/u 3.4 yr](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl3v12W4AAZgD4.jpg)
*5-yr OS 87%, 5-yr PFS 74%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, regional recurrence 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo; N=117, median f/u 3.4 yr*

- 📊 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, regional recurrence 4.3%, DM 11%
- 📊 5-yr OS 87%, 5-yr PFS 74%; median f/u 3.4 yrs
- 📊 Mean composite MDADI score at 12 mo 84.9, no significant decline post-treatment
- 🔍 Patient-level pooled analysis, N=117, 2 prospective trials (INRT-AIR + DARTBOARD); oropharynx/larynx/hypopharynx, stage I-IVB (excl T1-2N0 larynx); required PET/CT + neck CT; AI-assisted suspicious node identification
- ⚠️ Non-randomized pooled analysis; no concurrent ENI control arm — can't isolate INRT effect from selection or era bias; authors explicitly call for RCT before standard adoption
- 🔗 Historic ENI standard: elective nodal recurrence rates after full ENI typically <5% in HNSCC — 0% here is reassuring but denominator is small and f/u is moderate
- ❓ RCT vs standard ENI chemoRT needed; HPV status breakdown and subgroup outcomes not reported in source

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

Largest IPD EBRT dataset in HCC to date (N=4,913): OS for BCLC-0/A comparable to resection/ablation by investigator cross-study comparison, no RCT arm.

### HCC EBRT Multinational IPD Cohort

> IPD cohort of 4,913 HCC pts treated with EBRT: mOS 4.6yr BCLC-A, not reached for treatment-naive BCLC-0.

- 📊 mOS 6.8yr (95% CI 5.7-8.7) BCLC-0, 4.6yr (95% CI 4.1-5.1) BCLC-A; treatment-naive BCLC-0 mOS not reached (95% CI 8.6-NR), BCLC-A 5.4yr (95% CI 4.5-6.7)
- 🔍 IPD meta-analysis, systematic review through Dec 2022, N=4,913, median f/u 5.0yr; KM + RMST stratified by BCLC stage and treatment-naive vs experienced; random-effects Cox modeling
- 📐 Multivariable: higher BCLC stage, tumor burden, worse PS, Child-Pugh B/C each associated with higher mortality; ablative dose and more recent treatment year associated with reduced death risk
- 🔗 Investigators conclude OS with EBRT for BCLC-0/A appears comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative LRT — no direct randomized comparator; cross-study comparison only
- ⚠️ IPD cohort, not RCT; selection bias unavoidable across 30+ contributing institutions spanning variable treatment eras and technique; no head-to-head arm against resection or RFA within dataset
- ❓ Whether EBRT OS advantage over ablation holds in prospective RCT (RTOG 1112 / ongoing trials) and how to integrate EBRT into BCLC decision-making alongside evolving systemic options remains open

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS).   > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). Corresponding authors were invited to submit IPD for the study. We performed Kaplan-Meier survival analyses to determine OS and restricted mean survival time (RMST) stratified by Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) stage and treatment status (ie, treatment-na&#xef;ve and experienced). We performed random effects Cox proportional hazards modeling to assess clinical characteristics associated with OS.
  > RESULTS: Data were provided on 4,913 patients treated with EBRT with a median follow-up time of 5.0 years. Ablative radiation dose and more recent year of treatment were associated with a reduced risk of death.

OS outcomes with EBRT for very early- and early-stage HCC appear to be comparable with resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies. These data support the inclusion of EBRT in the BCLC HCC clinical decision-making process.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

### EXTEND Trial

> EXTEND phase II: MDT+SOC improved PFS vs SOC across oligometastatic histologies, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001.

- 📊 1° EP PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001, median f/u 53 months, per-protocol N=334
- 📊 Excluding prostate baskets: HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.40-0.89), consistent benefit
- 🔍 Phase II multicenter RCT; 1-5 mets, 6 disease-site baskets; MDT was RT in 98% of mets; randomized 2018-2023, screened 521, analyzed 334
- 📊 Basket-level PFS superiority: pancreas, prostate, and Other baskets; breast and kidney inconclusive
- 🔗 vs SABR-COMET (Palma 2019 Lancet): similar HR (~0.47) but smaller N and shorter f/u; EXTEND adds basket-specific signal and 53mo f/u supporting durability
- ⚠️ Phase II single basket-level powering; breast and kidney baskets inconclusive; heterogeneous histologies limit cross-basket inference; no OS benefit reported in source
- ❓ Which baskets warrant phase III? ctDNA clearance as prospective eligibility biomarker; immune activation mechanism vs direct cytoreduction

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Sherry AD, Haymaker C, Wang S, Liu S, Bathala TK, Medina-Rosales MN, Seo A, Hara K, Reddy J, Chun SG, Mayo LL, Walker G, Pant S, Zhao D, Kovitz CA, Ramirez D, Ha CS, Smith BD, Gomez D, Cohen L, Koong AC, Reuben A, Tannir N, Corn PG, Tran PT, Siddiqui BA, Subudhi SK, Msaouel P, Ludmir EB, Tang C. Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial. J Clin Oncol. 2026 May 16:101200JCO2502856.

## 🫘 Kidney

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months with SABR for primary RCC in non-surgical pts (N=70, median f/u 62mo).

- 📊 1° EP (freedom from local progression): 100% LC at 36, 60, and 84 months; no local recurrences in the ITT population
- 🔍 Non-randomised phase 2, N=70 (1 withdrew pre-tx), 8 sites Australia/Netherlands; single fraction 26 Gy (≤4 cm) or 42 Gy/3 fx (>4 cm); median f/u 62 mo (IQR 60-72)
- 🔍 Eligibility: medically inoperable, high-risk, or surgery-declined; T1a-T3a, N0-N1, tumour ≤10 cm; 56% T1b, 34% T1a; median tumour 46 mm; median age 77
- 📊 G3 AEs within 9 mo (possibly/probably/definitely related): 10% (7/70); nausea/vomiting 4%, pain 6%, colonic obstruction 3%, diarrhoea 1%; no G4 events, no treatment-related deaths; no new long-term safety signals
- 🔗 No cancer-related deaths reported in cohort; consistent with prior single-arm SABR-for-RCC signals (SAFIR, STAR-TRK) suggesting durable LC is achievable non-surgically
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomised comparator vs partial nephrectomy or thermal ablation; cannot conclude equivalence or superiority to surgical standard; predominantly inoperable/older pts (median age 77) limit generalisability to operable population
- ❓ Randomised comparison vs nephron-sparing surgery or ablation still needed; OS and distant metastasis-free survival data not reported in source abstract

## 🌰 Prostate

Prostate delivered one NI confirmation and one major null: HEAT and PEACE 2 together define what SBRT and pelvic RT do and don't add in intermediate/VHR disease.

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT: AHRT (SBRT 5 fx) non-inferior to EHRT (70.2 Gy/26 fx) for biochemical control; BF 7% vs 7.4% at 4.25y.

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)

| Endpoint | AHRT | EHRT |
|---|---|---|
| BF rate at 4.25y | 7% | 7.4% |
| P non-inferiority | 0.007 | — |
| Median FU | 59.7 months | 59.7 months |

- 📊 1° EP biochemical failure (Phoenix): AHRT 7% vs EHRT 7.4% at 4.25y, P non-inferiority = 0.007
- 🔍 Phase III NI RCT (interim); n=156 randomized, 142 analyzed; 82.4% intermediate-risk; median FU 59.7 months; 28% ADT; NI margin 12%
- 💊 AHRT: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx) + GTV SIB 40 Gy; EHRT: 70.2 Gy/26 fx (2.7 Gy/fx) IMRT; ADT ≤6 months permitted both arms
- 📊 Acute G2+ GI toxicity lower with AHRT; late G2+ GI and acute/late G2+ GU toxicities comparable between arms
- 🔗 Prior AHRT trials (HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005) used heterogeneous EHRT controls or excluded ADT; HEAT is first 1:1 AHRT vs EHRT with IMRT + ADT
- ⚠️ Interim analysis of 156 of 420 enrolled; 1° EP is biochemical failure, not MFS or OS; final analysis pending
- ❓ Favorable vs unfavorable intermediate-risk subgroup outcomes and longer-term MFS not yet reportable

### PEACE 2

> PEACE 2: pelvic RT did not improve cPFS (HR 0.81, p=0.088) or MFS/OS/PCSS vs prostate-only RT in VHR localized CaP.

![PEACE 2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8Pa0XQAAYLXz.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6; 72.2] | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4; 68.1] | ref | - |

- 🔍 Phase III, 4-arm (ADT ×±cabazitaxel ×±pelvic RT), VHR CaP: Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, or PSA ≥20; N0M0 conventional imaging; ADT x3yr
- ⚠️ No benefit on MFS, OS, or PCSS; investigators conclude pelvic RT cannot be considered SOC in VHR pts without detectable nodal disease on imaging
- 🔗 Diverges from POP-RT (Lancet Oncol 2021), which showed cFFS and MFS benefit with pelvic RT in similarly high-risk CaP; PEACE 2 null across all endpoints
- ⚠️ Staged with conventional imaging (pre-PSMA PET era); true nodal burden unquantified, heterogeneous nodal risk may dilute pelvic RT effect
- ❓ Whether PSMA PET-staged node-positive pts represent a subgroup with genuine pelvic RT benefit remains unanswered
