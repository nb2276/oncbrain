# 2026-05-18

**TORPEdO: IMPT vs IMRT no HR-QoL benefit in OPSCC at 12 months (phase III, N=205); proton advantage not demonstrated under UK trial conditions.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Head-neck and oligomets led: TORPEdO phase III null for IMPT vs IMRT challenges proton ROI for OPSCC; EXTEND (PFS HR 0.54, p<0.001) is the largest randomized MDT signal across histologies. SBRT/hypofractionation equivalences stacked in breast (NRG 1005), prostate (HEAT), and kidney (FASTRACK II); HCC EBRT IPD (N=4,913) supports parity with resection.

## 👄 Head & Neck

Two paradigm questions: ENI omission via INRT shows 0% elective nodal failures at 5yr; TORPEdO finds no HR-QoL gain for proton over photon in OPSCC.

### INRT-AIR & DARTBOARD (ENI omission in HNSCC)

> ENI omission via INRT: 0% solitary elective nodal recurrence at 5 years, 5-yr OS 87% in pooled N=117.

- 📊 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%
- 📊 3-yr cumulative incidence by failure pattern
  - Local recurrence: 9.5%
  - Regional recurrence: 4.3%
  - Distant metastasis: 11%
- 🔍 Patient-level pooled analysis, N=117, oropharynx/larynx/hypopharynx, stage I-IVB (excl T1-2N0 larynx), median f/u 3.4 yrs; AI-assisted suspicious LN identification
- 💊 INRT omits ENI entirely; PET/CT + neck CT required for eligibility
- 📐 Mean composite MDADI score at 12 months 84.9, no significant decline post-treatment
- ⚠️ Pooled prospective cohort, not randomised; N=117 limits power for rare events; authors explicitly state RCT required before non-trial implementation
- 🔗 vs standard definitive CRT with ENI: reduced OAR dose is the claimed benefit, but no toxicity comparison arm reported in source

### TORPEdO

> TORPEdO phase III: no HR-QoL benefit for IMPT vs IMRT at 12 months in OPSCC (N=205).

- 📊 Co-primary PRO endpoint: UW-QoL physical composite (saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech) — no differences between arms at 3, 12, or 24 months post RT
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=205, 2:1 IMPT:IMRT; 70/56 Gy in 33 fractions over 6.5 weeks; cisplatin 100mg/m² D1+D22; bilateral neck treatment required
- ⚠️ Clinician-reported co-primary (CTCAE G3 weight loss or gastrostomy dependence at 12 months) not reported in source slides; only PRO data shown at ESTRO

## 🎀 Breast

NRG 1005 closes the concurrent vs sequential boost debate: noninferior oncologic and cosmetic outcomes, shorter overall treatment.

### NRG/RTOG 1005 · [[NCT01349322]]

> Concurrent boost during WBI noninferior to sequential boost for IBR (HR 1.31, 90% CI 0.84-2.04) with equivalent toxicity and cosmesis, shortening overall treatment.

- 📊 1° EP (IBR): 7-yr IBR 2.6% concurrent vs 2.2% sequential; HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04), p=0.037 for noninferiority (upper bound 2.04 < prespecified margin 2.12)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2,255 eligible; higher-risk early breast cancer post-lumpectomy; 278 sites across North America; median f/u 7.3 yrs; concurrent arm 40 Gy/15F + 8 Gy/15F simultaneous integrated boost vs sequential 50 Gy/25F or 42.7 Gy/16F + 12-14 Gy boost
- 📊 No differences in DFS, DDFS, OS, or regional nodal recurrence (7-yr RNR 1.2%) between arms
- 📊 Cosmesis (BCTOS at 3yr): concurrent arm noninferior to sequential by both patient-reported (mean change 0.18 vs 0.16) and physician rating (82.4% vs 85.9% excellent/good, p=0.34); central photo review 72.0% vs 64.2% (p=0.11, also noninferior)
- 📊 G3-4 toxicity: no significant differences between arms (p=0.81); radiation dermatitis, fatigue, and breast pain most common across both
- 🔍 Population: median age 55 yrs; 96.8% invasive, 52.7% grade 3, 29.6% ER-neg, 61.8% received chemotherapy; 16.3% node-positive
- ⚠️ Superiority analysis (prespecified secondary, triggered by meeting noninferiority) was not significant; 90% CI used throughout (not 95%), consistent with noninferiority trial convention but worth noting when interpreting HR precision

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42114027]] **Concurrent Versus Sequential Radiation Dose Escalation to the Surgical Cavity for Conservative Treatment of High-Risk Early Breast Cancer: NRG/RTOG 1005 Phase III Trial.** — Vicini; Winter; Freedman et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02465](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02465)
  > PURPOSE: For patients with breast cancer undergoing breast conservation, escalating the dose (boost) of radiation to the lumpectomy cavity after whole-breast irradiation (WBI) reduces ipsilateral breast recurrence (IBR) but extends treatment duration. This phase III trial investigated whether boost delivery during WBI versus after WBI provides noninferior IBR and preserves cosmetic appearance.
  > METHODS: NRG/RTOG 1005 randomly assigned patients at higher risk for IBR after lumpectomy and axillary surgery to either a sequential boost of 12 Gy in six fractions(F) or 14 Gy in 7F after WBI of 50 Gy in 25F or 42.7 Gy in 16F (sequential arm) or a concurrent boost of 8 Gy in 15F of 0.53 Gy per day with WBI of 40 Gy in 15F (concurrent arm) using 3-dimensional conformal radiation therapy (RT) or intensity-modulated RT. Based on 1.59% 5-year IBR for the sequential arm, defining the noninferiority margin as a hazard ratio upper limit on the 90% CI of 2.12, 2,312 patients provide 80% power for noninferiority of IBR as first recurrence for the concurrent arm. Secondary end points included disease-free survival and overall survival, adverse events (AEs), and cosmetic outcomes.
  > RESULTS: Between May 24, 2011, and June 20, 2014, 2,354 patients were randomly assigned, with 2,255 eligible for analysis (sequential arm, n = 1,118; concurrent arm, n = 1,137). With median follow-up of 7.3 years, there were 56 IBR events; 5- and 7-year IBR were 2.1% and 2.2% on the sequential arm and 1.9% and 2.6% on the concurrent arm, respectively. The noninferiority criterion was met: HR (90% CI): 1.31 (0.84 to 2.04), P = .037. No differences were observed in AEs, cosmetic outcomes, or survival between arms.
  > CONCLUSION: Concurrent boost during WBI results in noninferior IBR compared with sequential boost without worsening toxicity or cosmetic outcomes and reduces overall treatment time.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Vicini FA, Winter K, Freedman GM, Arthur DW, Rosenstein BS, Bentzen SM, Li XA, Halyard MY, Woodward WA, Bleicher RJ, Taghian A, Lyons J, Tomberlin JK, Seaward SA, Cheston SB, Hoover AC, Anderson BM, Perera FE, Poppe MM, Petersen IA, Jhawar S, Hijal T, Moughan J, Movsas B, White JR. ## 🌰 Prostate

SBRT equivalence consolidates (HEAT interim); PEACE 2 challenges pelvic RT benefit for very high-risk disease staged with conventional imaging.

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT (interim): SBRT non-inferior to moderate hypofractionation, BF 7% vs 7.4% at 4.25y (p-noninferiority=0.007), localized PCa.

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority RCT; n=156 randomized (interim), 142 analyzed; 82.4% intermediate-risk; median FU 59.7mo; 28% received ADT
- 💊 AHRT: 36.25Gy/5fx (7.25Gy/fx) + GTV SIB 40Gy; EHRT: 70.2Gy/26fx IMRT (2.7Gy/fx); ADT ≤6mo permitted both arms
- 📊 Acute G2+ GI toxicity lower with AHRT; late G2+ GI and acute/late G2+ GU comparable between arms (no effect sizes reported in source)
- ⚠️ Interim analysis only; full accrual goal n=456, n=420 evaluable enrolled; prespecified final primary analysis pending
- 🔗 First 1:1 AHRT vs EHRT RCT with modern IMRT + ADT; PACE-B, HYPO-RT-PC, NRG-GU005 excluded ADT or used heterogeneous control arms

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

N=4,913 IPD cohort makes a data-level case for EBRT inclusion in BCLC-0/A decision algorithms.

### HCC EBRT multinational IPD cohort

> Largest multinational EBRT cohort (N=4,913 HCC pts): mOS 6.8yr BCLC-0, 4.6yr BCLC-A; outcomes comparable to resection/ablation.

- 📊 mOS: 6.8yr (95% CI 5.7-8.7) BCLC-0, 4.6yr (95% CI 4.1-5.1) BCLC-A; treatment-naive BCLC-0 mOS not reached (95% CI 8.6-NR), BCLC-A 5.4yr (95% CI 4.5-6.7)
- 🔍 Systematic review + IPD from multinational cohorts; N=4,913; median f/u 5.0yr; search date Dec 2022; KM + RMST by BCLC stage/treatment status; random-effects Cox modeling
- 📊 Ablative RT dose and more recent treatment year both independently associated with reduced mortality risk in multivariable models
- 🔗 Authors conclude OS comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies for BCLC-0/A; directly supports EBRT inclusion in BCLC decision algorithm
- ⚠️ Non-randomised IPD cohort; era effects acknowledged (more recent tx year = better OS, mixing older lower-dose and newer ablative-dose cases); no direct head-to-head comparator arm within study; heterogeneous centers/techniques across multinational institutions

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS).   > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). Corresponding authors were invited to submit IPD for the study. We performed Kaplan-Meier survival analyses to determine OS and restricted mean survival time (RMST) stratified by Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) stage and treatment status (ie, treatment-na&#xef;ve and experienced). We performed random effects Cox proportional hazards modeling to assess clinical characteristics associated with OS.
  > RESULTS: Data were provided on 4,913 patients treated with EBRT with a median follow-up time of 5.0 years. ## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND (N=334) is the largest randomized MDT trial to date; PFS benefit across most histology baskets, breast and kidney inconclusive.

### EXTEND Trial

> EXTEND: MDT+SOC vs SOC in oligomets, PFS HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001 across all baskets.

- 📊 1° EP PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001 (per-protocol, all baskets); excluding prostate baskets HR 0.60 (0.40-0.89)
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, multicenter, N=334 analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC n=166, SOC n=168); 1-5 mets, 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, 2 prostate, Other); median f/u 53 months; RT used as MDT for 98% of mets
- 📊 PFS superiority by basket
  - Pancreas: PFS superiority identified
  - Prostate baskets: PFS superiority identified
  - Other basket: PFS superiority identified
  - Breast basket: inconclusive
  - Kidney basket: inconclusive
- 🔗 vs SABR-COMET (Lancet 2019, N=99) and STOMP (JCO 2018): EXTEND larger, longer f/u, basket-stratified, broader histology; consistent direction of benefit across non-RCC/non-breast histologies
- ⚠️ Phase II only, not powered for OS; basket-level results heterogeneous (breast + kidney inconclusive), so overall HR may overrepresent responder histologies; no effect sizes reported per basket beyond directionality
- 🔬 ctDNA detectable at enrollment correlated with shorter PFS/OS; ctDNA clearance at 3mo post-enrollment correlated with improved survival — exploratory, hypothesis-generating
- ⚠️ MDT-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced in baskets with PFS superiority — intriguing mechanistic signal but exploratory only

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Sherry AD, Haymaker C, Wang S, Liu S, Bathala TK, Medina-Rosales MN, Seo A, Hara K, Reddy J, Chun SG, Mayo LL, Walker G, Pant S, Zhao D, Kovitz CA, Ramirez D, Ha CS, Smith BD, Gomez D, Cohen L, Koong AC, Reuben A, Tannir N, Corn PG, Tran PT, Siddiqui BA, Subudhi SK, Msaouel P, Ludmir EB, Tang C. ## 🫘 Kidney

FASTRACK II: 84-month f/u confirms SABR durability for inoperable primary RCC; single-arm design limits comparator inference.

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months with SABR for inoperable primary RCC (N=70, median f/u 62 mo).

- 📊 1° EP local control (RECIST): 100% at 36, 60, and 84 months; no observed local recurrences or cancer-related deaths
- 🔍 Non-randomised phase 2, N=70 (1 withdrew pre-tx), 8 sites Australia/Netherlands; median age 77, 70% male; median tumour 46mm; 56% T1b, 34% T1a, 9% T2a, 1% T3a; 1 N1
- 💊 Single-fraction 26 Gy for ≤4cm; 42 Gy/3fx (48h apart) for >4cm
- ⚠️ G3 AEs in 10% within 9mo: nausea/vomiting 4%, abdominal/flank/tumour pain 6%, colonic obstruction 3%, diarrhoea 1%; no G4 events or treatment-related deaths
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomised comparator vs partial nephrectomy or thermal ablation; inoperable/high-risk/declined-surgery pts only; local control endpoint not OS or PFS
- 🔗 Consistent with prior single-arm SABR-RCC data (STAR-TRK, SAFIR cohorts); long f/u here is the differentiator but design limitations remain

- 📄 [[PMID 42144018]] **Long-term outcomes of stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy for primary kidney cancer (TROG 15.03 FASTRACK II): a multicentre, non-randomised, phase 2 study.** — Siva; Pryor; Martin et al.. *The Lancet. Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4)
  > BACKGROUND: Stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy (SABR) is an emerging, non-invasive alternative for primary renal cell carcinoma. We aimed to provide the final long-term trial outcomes of TransTasman Radiation Oncology Group (TROG) 15.03 FASTRACK II, the first phase 2 trial investigating SABR for primary renal cell carcinoma to our knowledge.
  > METHODS: FASTRACK II was a non-randomised, phase 2 study conducted in eight hospitals in Australia and the Netherlands by TROG and the Australian and New Zealand Urogenital and Prostate (ANZUP) Cancer Trials Group. Here, we report the final pre-planned follow-up results. Adult patients (aged &#x2265;18 years) with histologically confirmed primary renal cell carcinoma, who were medically inoperable, high risk, or declined surgery, had an Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status of 2 or less, had tumours 10 cm or less in size, and had N0-N1 disease were included. Patients underwent either a single fraction SABR of 26 Gy for tumours 4 cm or less in maximum diameter, or 42 Gy in three fractions delivered 48 h apart for tumours more than 4 cm in maximum diameter. The primary outcome was freedom from local progression to assess local control after SABR evaluated with the Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours. The primary endpoint and safety were evaluated in the intention-to-treat population. A patient representative was involved in the study design and conduct. The trial was registered with ClinicalTrials.gov ([[NCT02613819]]) and is closed to enrolment.
  > FINDINGS: Between July 28, 2016, and Feb 27, 2020, 71 patients were enrolled and one withdrew consent before treatment. Median follow-up was 62 months (IQR 60-72), median age was 77 years (70-82). 49 (70%) of 70 patients were male and 21 (30%) were female. Race and ethnicity data were not collected. The median tumour size was 46 mm (37-55), with 24 (34%) patients with T1a disease, 39 (56%) with T1b disease, six (9%) with T2a disease, and one (1%) with T3a disease. One patient (1%) had nodal involvement (N1). SABR resulted in 100% local control at 36 months, 60 months, and 84 months. Seven (10%) patients had at least one grade 3 adverse event within 9 months of SABR that was designated possibly, probably, or definitely related to treatment: nausea and vomiting (three [4%] events); abdominal, flank, or tumour pain (four [6%]); colonic obstruction (two [3%]); and diarrhoea (one [1%]). No new long-term safety signals, grade 4 events, or treatment-related deaths were noted.
  > INTERPRETATION: Long-term follow-up supports the safety and local control of SABR for non-surgical patients with renal cell carcinoma, with no observed local recurrences or cancer-related deaths in this cohort, which had predominantly T1b disease or higher.
