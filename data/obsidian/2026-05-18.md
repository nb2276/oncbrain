# 2026-05-18

**EXTEND: MDT+SOC cuts PFS risk across baskets, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72) p<0.001, with RT delivering 98% of the metastasis-directed treatment.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> RT's reach widened (EXTEND's multi-histology MDT signal, FASTRACK II's 100% 84-mo local control, 4,913-pt HCC EBRT cohort at resection-comparable OS) while volume and fractionation questions sharpened: PEACE 2 pelvic nodal RT missed cPFS, HEAT put 5fx on par with 26fx, NRG 1005 folded the boost into 15 fractions. Oligomet, prostate, and head-neck carried the day.

## 🌀 GI Lower

OPERA's post-hoc W14 read suggests near-complete response is good enough to select for watch-and-wait, moving the assessment 10 weeks earlier.

### OPERA Trial (5-year)

> 76% achieved a good clinical response at W14 (DRE+rectoscopy); nCR matched cCR for 5yr organ preservation (77% vs 81%), enabling early W&W selection.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> nCR carries the same 5yr organ-preservation prognosis as cCR (77% vs 81%) and reflects RT-related mucosal change, not residual tumor, so an nCR at reassessment shouldn't route a patient to salvage TME. Early W14 clinical exam (DRE+rectoscopy) matched MRI TRG1-2 in 98%, letting W&W selection move up from W24.

- 📊 W14 clinical response by arm and 5yr organ preservation

  | Endpoint | Arm A | Arm B | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | W14 good response (cCR+nCR) | 65% | 88% | 0.004 |
  | 5yr organ preservation | 75% | 83% | 0.24 |

- 📊 W14 CTRE feasible in 122/141 (87%): 76% good response (cCR+nCR), 24% PR
- 📊 5yr organ preservation similar for cCR vs nCR: 81% vs 77%
- 📐 Clinical CR concordant with MRI: TRG1-2 in 98% (80/82)
- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis testing W14 (~1mo post-NAT) vs protocol W24 assessment for W&W selection
- ⚠️ nCR reflects RT-related mucosal change, not residual tumor (per authors)
- ⚠️ Per authors, nCR should not justify radical surgery
- ⚠️ Post-hoc, non-randomised response comparison; W14 selection not prospectively validated vs W24

## 🌰 Prostate

Two volume-and-fractionation reads: PEACE 2 fails to justify elective pelvic coverage in non-PSMA-staged very-high-risk N0, while HEAT's interim puts 36.25 Gy/5fx on non-inferior footing against 70.2 Gy/26fx.

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> AHRT 5fx non-inferior to EHRT 26fx for biochemical failure, 7% vs 7.4% (P_NI=0.007) at 4.25y; less acute GI toxicity.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The head-to-head prior SBRT trials skipped: 5fx AHRT (36.25 Gy, SIB 40 Gy) vs 26fx EHRT, both IMRT with ADT allowed, isolating fractionation from technique and hormones. Acute G2+ GI toxicity favored the 5fx arm at equal biochemical control, moving the ultrahypofractionation choice for low- to intermediate-risk pts.

|---|---|---|
| AHRT | 7% | 0.007 |
| EHRT | 7.4% | ref |

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInK_RpW0AACNnC.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase III international non-inferiority RCT, 1:1; localized low- to intermediate-risk PCa, IPSS <12; ADT ≤6mo allowed both arms (NI margin 12%)
- 🔍 Radiotherapy arms

  | Arm | Dose / fractionation | Boost / technique |
  |---|---|---|
  | AHRT | 36.25 Gy / 5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx) | GTV SIB to 40 Gy |
  | EHRT | 70.2 Gy / 26 fx (2.7 Gy/fx) | IMRT all pts |

- 📊 AHRT non-inferior to EHRT for biochemical failure (Phoenix) at interim
- 📊 Acute G2+ GI toxicity lower with AHRT
- 📊 No significant difference in late G2+ GI or acute/late G2+ GU toxicity
- 🔍 Interim cohort
  - 156 randomized, 142 analyzed (accrual goal 456)
  - 82.4% intermediate-risk
  - Median FU 59.7 mo
  - 28% received ADT
- ⚠️ Interim analysis at ~1/3 of planned accrual; full pre-specified primary pending
- 🔗 First RCT comparing AHRT vs EHRT 1:1 with modern IMRT + ADT; prior ultrahypofx trials (HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005) used heterogeneous or no-ADT controls
- ⚠️ Supportive-med use (laxatives, psyllium) counted as G2 — may inflate GI event rates

### PEACE 2

> Pelvic nodal RT missed 1° endpoint: 7yr cPFS 67.1% vs 62.9% prostate-only, HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03) p=0.088; no MFS/PCSS/OS gain.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT decision is elective nodal coverage: a randomized head-to-head (n=380 vs 381) shows whole-pelvic RT adds no cPFS, MFS, PCSS or OS benefit over prostate-only in cN0 very-high-risk disease, with modern-technique toxicity minimal. Staging was conventional imaging/choline PET, not PSMA, so PSMA-defined N0 pts remain untested.

- ⚠️ Primary endpoint not met; cPFS trend favors pelvic but crosses 1

- ⚠️ Conference data (ESTRO26), not yet peer-reviewed

## 👄 Head & Neck

De-escalation by volume (INRT-AIR/DARTBOARD, 0% solitary elective nodal recurrence without a randomised contrast) and by modality (TORPEdO, no proton HR-QoL gain) both land short of practice-changing evidence.

### TORPEdO

> UW-QoL physical composite similar IMPT vs IMRT at 3/12/24mo in OPSCC; no proton QoL-toxicity benefit (no effect size reported in source).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The proton premise, less QoL toxicity, didn't show: UW-QoL physical composite tracked identically IMPT vs IMRT from 6 weeks through 24 months, both arms at 70/56 Gy in 33fx. That undercuts routing OPSCC pts to protons for QoL benefit, though identical planning constraints and novel UK proton centres may have capped IMPT's achievable dosimetric edge.

![TORPEdO](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HImrQwvWMAA-Laf.jpg)

![TORPEdO](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HImrQv4WIAAKThu.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 3 multicentre RCT (CRUK/18/010), N=205 OPSCC, 2:1 IMPT:IMRT; stratified by T-stage, N-stage, p16, smoking.
- 🔍 Both arms 70 Gy / 56 Gy in 33 fractions over 6.5 weeks; concurrent cisplatin 100 mg/m² D1+D22.
- 📊 PRO co-primary: UW-QoL physical composite (saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech) at 12mo post-CRT.
- 📊 No between-arm difference in UW-QoL physical composite at 3/12/24mo, similar from 6 weeks post-CRT.
- 📊 Both arms dip at end of RT then recover; most stabilise by 12mo, some deterioration persists to 2yr.
- ⚠️ No effect sizes, CIs, or p-values reported in source; equivalence stated but magnitude not quantified.
- ⚠️ Clinician co-primary (grade 3 weight loss ≥20% or gastrostomy dependence at 12mo) not reported in this HR-QoL readout.
- ⚠️ Late-effects primary not mature; HR-QoL follow-up ongoing to 5yr post-CRT.
- ⚠️ Critique (@AmadeoWals): identical planning constraints + novel UK proton centres may cap IMPT's advantage; experienced centres report clinical gains.

### INRT-AIR & DARTBOARD pooled analysis

> 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0% with ENI omission across 117 pts; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

Pooled IPD puts EBRT in the same OS range as resection and ablation for BCLC-0/A, though the comparison is cross-study, not randomised.

### Multinational HCC EBRT IPD Cohort

> Median OS 6.8y BCLC-0 and 4.6y BCLC-A across 4,913 EBRT-treated HCC pts, comparable to resection/ablation.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The treatment-naive BCLC-0 arm is the RT-relevant cell: median OS not reached (95% CI 8.6 to NR), i.e. first-line EBRT in a truly untreated very-early cohort, not salvage after failed ablation. Ablative dose independently predicted lower mortality, so this moves the dose-selection and BCLC-algorithm-placement decisions.

- 🔍 IPD pooled from published series meeting prespecified HCC technical standards; search date Dec 15 2022
- 🔍 N=4,913, median f/u 5.0 yrs, corresponding authors invited to contribute patient-level data
- 📊 Median OS by BCLC stage and treatment status

  | Cohort | BCLC-0 | BCLC-A |
  |---|---|---|
  | All pts | 6.8 yrs (95% CI 5.7-8.7) | 4.6 yrs (95% CI 4.1-5.1) |
  | Treatment-naive | NR (95% CI 8.6-NR) | 5.4 yrs (95% CI 4.5-6.7) |

- 📊 Ablative dose associated with reduced mortality risk on multivariable Cox; HR not reported in source
- 📊 More recent treatment year also associated with reduced mortality risk
- 📐 Higher mortality risk on random-effects multivariable Cox
  - More advanced BCLC stage
  - Higher tumor burden
  - Worse performance status
  - Child-Pugh B or C
- ⚠️ No comparator arm: 'comparable with resection and ablation' is a cross-study read, not a randomised one
- ⚠️ IPD contributed by responding corresponding authors only; centers with worse outcomes may be underrepresented
- ⚠️ Technical-standard filter selects modern-technique series, so results may not transfer to non-specialist EBRT

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS).   > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). ## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND is the largest randomised MDT dataset yet and the RT dose it used goes unreported, so transfer to your own SABR practice rests on the SABR-COMET-era schedules.

### EXTEND Trial

> MDT+SOC improved PFS across all baskets, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001; RT delivered 98% of MDT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT-relevant read is that this is essentially an SBRT trial (98% of MDT was radiotherapy) layered on top of continued systemic therapy, so the PFS gain is attributable to local ablation, not a systemic switch. The prostate-excluded HR 0.60 (0.40-0.89) is what matters for referrals outside prostate. Dose and fractionation are not in the source abstract, which gates transfer to practice.

- 🔍 Multicenter randomized phase II basket trial, 1-5 mets, 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, 2 prostate, Other) with basket-specific stratification + powering
- 🔍 RT was the MDT for 98% of treated metastases (370/379); dose/fractionation/technique not reported in source abstract
- 📊 1° EP PFS, per-protocol set. Overall HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001
- 📊 Excluding both prostate baskets: PFS HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.40-0.89), so the signal is not carried by prostate alone
- 📊 Per-basket PFS read (source states direction only, no per-basket HRs given)
  - Superiority identified: pancreas, prostate, Other
  - Inconclusive: breast, kidney
  - Per-basket HRs/CIs not reported in source abstract
- 📊 ctDNA: detectable at enrollment → shorter PFS and survival; clearance at 3mo → improved survival. No effect sizes reported in source
- 🔍 Screened 521 → randomized 350 → 334 analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC n=166, SOC n=168). Median f/u 53mo
- ⚠️ 1° analysis pre-specified in the PER-PROTOCOL set, not ITT; 16/350 randomized pts (4.6%) excluded from analysis
- ⚠️ Phase II, open-label, PFS primary with no OS claim in source. Multiple baskets tested → multiplicity risk despite basket-specific powering
- 🔗 vs SABR-COMET (Lancet 2019, mixed histology, N=99, OS HR 0.57): EXTEND is larger and PFS-only but concordant in direction
- 🔗 vs STOMP/ORIOLE (prostate, small randomized phase II): EXTEND's prostate baskets extend the same MDT signal alongside SOC systemic therapy

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.

## 🫘 Kidney

FASTRACK II gives SABR its first prospective long-term local-control benchmark in inoperable primary RCC, still without a surgical or ablative comparator.

### FASTRACK II · [[NCT02613819]]

> 100% freedom from local progression at 36, 60, and 84 months after single-fraction 26 Gy or 42 Gy/3fx SABR in inoperable primary RCC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The size-adapted prescription is what transfers: 26 Gy × 1 for ≤4 cm, 42 Gy/3fx at 48 h intervals above that, in a cohort that was 65% T1b or higher (median 46 mm), not a small-tumour-enriched series. Two colonic obstructions place bowel as the constraint for central lesions.

- 📊 1° EP freedom from local progression: 100% at 36mo, 60mo, and 84mo (RECIST, ITT)
- 🔍 Non-randomised phase 2, 8 hospitals in Australia + Netherlands (TROG/ANZUP); 71 enrolled, 1 withdrew pre-treatment, 70 treated
- 🔍 Size-adapted SABR prescription
  - ≤4 cm: 26 Gy single fraction
  - >4 cm: 42 Gy / 3 fx, delivered 48 h apart
- 🔍 Eligibility: medically inoperable, high surgical risk, or declined surgery; ECOG ≤2; tumour ≤10 cm; N0-N1
- 🔍 Baseline: median age 77 (70-82), median tumour size 46 mm (37-55), median f/u 62 mo (IQR 60-72)
  - T1a 24 (34%)
  - T1b 39 (56%)
  - T2a 6 (9%)
  - T3a 1 (1%)
  - N1 1 (1%)
  - Male 49 (70%), female 21 (30%)
- ⚠️ Treatment-related G3 AEs within 9 mo: 7 (10%) pts had ≥1 event
  - Abdominal, flank, or tumour pain: 4 (6%)
  - Nausea and vomiting: 3 (4%)
  - Colonic obstruction: 2 (3%)
  - Diarrhoea: 1 (1%)
- 📊 No grade 4 events, no treatment-related deaths, no new long-term safety signals
- ⚠️ Single-arm: no randomised comparator vs partial nephrectomy, cryoablation, or RFA in this operable-declining subset
- ⚠️ Colonic obstruction in 2/70 flags bowel as the dose-limiting OAR for larger central tumours on the 3fx schedule
- ⚠️ Median age 77 with competing mortality: 84-mo local control is the endpoint that matures fastest here; OS/cancer-specific survival not reported in source abstract beyond 'no cancer-related deaths'
- 🔗 Prior IROCK pooled retrospective series reported local control in the 97-98% range at 4-5 yr; this is the first prospective phase 2 confirmation with pre-planned long-term f/u

Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(26%2900091-4)
  > BACKGROUND: Stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy (SABR) is an emerging, non-invasive alternative for primary renal cell carcinoma. We aimed to provide the final long-term trial outcomes of TransTasman Radiation Oncology Group (TROG) 15.03 FASTRACK II, the first phase 2 trial investigating SABR for primary renal cell carcinoma to our knowledge.
  > METHODS: FASTRACK II was a non-randomised, phase 2 study conducted in eight hospitals in Australia and the Netherlands by TROG and the Australian and New Zealand Urogenital and Prostate (ANZUP) Cancer Trials Group. Here, we report the final pre-planned follow-up results. Adult patients (aged &#x2265;18 years) with histologically confirmed primary renal cell carcinoma, who were medically inoperable, high risk, or declined surgery, had an Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status of 2 or less, had tumours 10 cm or less in size, and had N0-N1 disease were included. Patients underwent either a single fraction SABR of 26 Gy for tumours 4 cm or less in maximum diameter, or 42 Gy in three fractions delivered 48 h apart for tumours more than 4 cm in maximum diameter. The primary outcome was freedom from local progression to assess local control after SABR evaluated with the Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours. The primary endpoint and safety were evaluated in the intention-to-treat population. A patient representative was involved in the study design and conduct. The trial was registered with ClinicalTrials.gov ([[NCT02613819]]) and is closed to enrolment.
  > FINDINGS: Between July 28, 2016, and Feb 27, 2020, 71 patients were enrolled and one withdrew consent before treatment. Median follow-up was 62 months (IQR 60-72), median age was 77 years (70-82). 49 (70%) of 70 patients were male and 21 (30%) were female. Race and ethnicity data were not collected. The median tumour size was 46 mm (37-55), with 24 (34%) patients with T1a disease, 39 (56%) with T1b disease, six (9%) with T2a disease, and one (1%) with T3a disease. One patient (1%) had nodal involvement (N1). SABR resulted in 100% local control at 36 months, 60 months, and 84 months. Seven (10%) patients had at least one grade 3 adverse event within 9 months of SABR that was designated possibly, probably, or definitely related to treatment: nausea and vomiting (three [4%] events); abdominal, flank, or tumour pain (four [6%]); colonic obstruction (two [3%]); and diarrhoea (one [1%]). No new long-term safety signals, grade 4 events, or treatment-related deaths were noted.
  > INTERPRETATION: Long-term follow-up supports the safety and local control of SABR for non-surgical patients with renal cell carcinoma, with no observed local recurrences or cancer-related deaths in this cohort, which had predominantly T1b disease or higher.

## 🎀 Breast

NRG/RTOG 1005 delivers the practical win: WBI plus boost in 15 fractions, cosmesis and G3-4 toxicity unchanged.

### NRG/RTOG 1005 · [[NCT01349322]]

> 7-yr IBR 2.6% vs 2.2%, HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04): concurrent boost noninferior to sequential, cutting WBI+boost to 15 fractions.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The transferable parameter is the boost itself: 8 Gy in 15F at 0.53 Gy per day, a lower cavity total than 12 Gy/6F sequential, with 7-yr IBR 2.6% vs 2.2%. Blinded photo cosmesis at 3y numerically favored concurrent (72.0% vs 64.2%, p=0.11). This moves boost technique, not whether to boost.

- 📊 1° EP IBR as first recurrence: noninferiority met, upper 90% CI 2.04 below the 2.12 margin
- 📊 IBR by arm (1° endpoint)

  | Endpoint | Sequential (n=1,118) | Concurrent (n=1,137) |
  |---|---|---|
  | 5-yr IBR | 2.1% | 1.9% |
  | 7-yr IBR | 2.2% (90% CI 1.5-3.0) | 2.6% (90% CI 1.9-3.5) |
  | IBR HR (90% CI) | reference | 1.31 (0.84-2.04), P=.037 |

- 🔍 Concurrent arm: WBI 40 Gy/15F with SIB 8 Gy/15F, 0.53 Gy per day to the cavity
- 🔍 Sequential arm: WBI 50 Gy/25F (52.4%) or 42.7 Gy/16F (47.6%), boost 12 Gy/6F in 84.9%
- 🔍 High-risk enrichment (2,255 analyzed, median age 55)
  - 52.7% grade 3
  - 29.6% ER-negative
  - 16.3% axillary nodal involvement
  - 16.7% close (<2 mm) or focally positive margins
  - 61.8% received chemotherapy
- 📊 3-yr BCTOS cosmesis mean change 0.16 sequential vs 0.18 concurrent, noninferiority met (1-sided p<0.0001)
- 📊 No significant difference in grade 3-4 events between arms, p=0.81
- ⚠️ Superiority test for the concurrent arm, triggered by meeting NI, was not significant
- ⚠️ NI margin (HR 2.12) set under a protocol redesign assuming 1.59% 5-yr IBR; observed 2.1% and 1.9%

- 📄 [[PMID 42114027]] **Concurrent Versus Sequential Radiation Dose Escalation to the Surgical Cavity for Conservative Treatment of High-Risk Early Breast Cancer: NRG/RTOG 1005 Phase III Trial.** — Vicini; Winter; Freedman et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02465](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02465)
  > PURPOSE: For patients with breast cancer undergoing breast conservation, escalating the dose (boost) of radiation to the lumpectomy cavity after whole-breast irradiation (WBI) reduces ipsilateral breast recurrence (IBR) but extends treatment duration. This phase III trial investigated whether boost delivery during WBI versus after WBI provides noninferior IBR and preserves cosmetic appearance.
  > METHODS: NRG/RTOG 1005 randomly assigned patients at higher risk for IBR after lumpectomy and axillary surgery to either a sequential boost of 12 Gy in six fractions(F) or 14 Gy in 7F after WBI of 50 Gy in 25F or 42.7 Gy in 16F (sequential arm) or a concurrent boost of 8 Gy in 15F of 0.53 Gy per day with WBI of 40 Gy in 15F (concurrent arm) using 3-dimensional conformal radiation therapy (RT) or intensity-modulated RT. Based on 1.59% 5-year IBR for the sequential arm, defining the noninferiority margin as a hazard ratio upper limit on the 90% CI of 2.12, 2,312 patients provide 80% power for noninferiority of IBR as first recurrence for the concurrent arm. Secondary end points included disease-free survival and overall survival, adverse events (AEs), and cosmetic outcomes.
  > RESULTS: Between May 24, 2011, and June 20, 2014, 2,354 patients were randomly assigned, with 2,255 eligible for analysis (sequential arm, n = 1,118; concurrent arm, n = 1,137). With median follow-up of 7.3 years, there were 56 IBR events; 5- and 7-year IBR were 2.1% and 2.2% on the sequential arm and 1.9% and 2.6% on the concurrent arm, respectively. The noninferiority criterion was met: HR (90% CI): 1.31 (0.84 to 2.04), P = .037. No differences were observed in AEs, cosmetic outcomes, or survival between arms.
  > CONCLUSION: Concurrent boost during WBI results in noninferior IBR compared with sequential boost without worsening toxicity or cosmetic outcomes and reduces overall treatment time.
