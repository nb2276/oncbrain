# 2026-05-18

**PEACE 2: pelvic RT did not improve cPFS (HR 0.81, p=0.088), MFS, or OS vs prostate-only RT in very high-risk localized PCa, conflicting with POP-RT.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate and oligomets carried the day's biggest signals: PEACE 2's phase III null across cPFS/MFS/OS in very high-risk PCa directly conflicts with POP-RT, while EXTEND (HR 0.54) adds the strongest phase II RCT evidence for MDT benefit. Head-neck and breast showed consolidation data, both pending randomised confirmation.

## 🌀 Lower GI

### OPERA trial (rectal preservation)

> OPERA post-hoc: W14 clinical response (DRE+rectoscopy) reliably identifies cCR candidates; 5-yr OP 75-83% across arms.

![OPERA trial (rectal preservation)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIm49InWMAAbrtG.jpg)

| Timepoint/Endpoint | Arm A | Arm B | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| W14 good response (cCR+nCR) | 65% | 88% | 0.004 |
| 5-yr OP rate | 75% | 83% | 0.24 |

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of OPERA trial; W14 CTRE (DRE+rectoscopy ± MRI) assessed in 122/141 pts (87%); CR defined as cCR+nCR
- 📊 W14 good response (cCR+nCR): 76% overall; Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65% (p=0.004); MRI TRG1-2 concordance in 98% (80/82) of CR pts
- 📊 5-yr OP: 75% Arm A vs 83% Arm B (p=0.24); OP similar between cCR and nCR (81% vs 77%)
- 🔗 Supports W&W eligibility assessment at W14 (3 mo post-NAT start, 1 mo post-NAT end) rather than waiting to W24 standard reassessment
- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis; W14 decision-making not prespecified primary endpoint; operational CTRE protocol varies from W24 three-modality standard used in OPERA primary analysis
- ❓ Whether W14 assessment can safely replace W24 reassessment in prospective protocol, and optimal salvage strategy for PR pts at W14, remain open

## 🌰 Prostate

Conflicting phase III RCT evidence on pelvic RT leaves delineation practice genuinely unresolved.

### HEAT trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT: AHRT (SBRT 5fx) non-inferior to EHRT (26fx IMRT) for biochemical control in localized low-intermediate risk PCa; 7% vs 7.4% BF at 4.25y.

![BF (Phoenix): 7% AHRT vs 7.4% EHRT at 4.25y, p(non-inferiority)=0.007. n=142 analyzed, median FU 59.7mo.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)
*BF (Phoenix): 7% AHRT vs 7.4% EHRT at 4.25y, p(non-inferiority)=0.007. n=142 analyzed, median FU 59.7mo.*

- 📊 1° EP biochemical failure (Phoenix): 7% AHRT vs 7.4% EHRT at 4.25y, p(non-inferiority)=0.007
- 🔍 Phase III randomized non-inferiority; n=156 randomized, n=142 analyzed (interim); 82.4% intermediate-risk, median FU 59.7mo, 28% received ADT; non-inferiority margin 12%
- 🔍 AHRT: 36.25 Gy/5fx (7.25 Gy/fx) + GTV SIB to 40 Gy; EHRT: 70.2 Gy/26fx IMRT; ADT ≤6mo permitted both arms; accrual goal n=456, n=420 evaluable
- 📊 Toxicity: acute G2+ GI lower with AHRT; no significant difference in late G2+ GI or acute/late G2+ GU toxicities
- 🔗 Prior randomized data (HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005) had heterogeneous control arms, no ADT, or limited IMRT; HEAT is first 1:1 AHRT vs EHRT with modern IMRT and ADT in both arms
- ⚠️ Interim analysis only; full accrual target n=456, only n=142 analyzed here; mature OS and late toxicity data not yet available
- ❓ Final primary analysis at full accrual pending; long-term GU/GI outcomes and QoL comparison needed; generalizability to high-risk pts unclear

### PEACE 2

> PEACE 2: pelvic RT did not improve cPFS, MFS, PCSS, or OS vs prostate-only RT in very high-risk localized prostate cancer.

![PEACE 2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8Pa0XQAAYLXz.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6; 72.2] | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4; 68.1] | ref | ref |

- 📊 1° EP cPFS: HR 0.81 (95% CI 0.63-1.03), p=0.088; 7-yr cPFS 67.1% pelvic vs 62.9% prostate-only RT
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=761 (380 prostate-only, 381 pelvic RT); 2x2 factorial with cabazitaxel arms; ADT 3yr + high-dose RT; very high-risk (Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, or PSA ≥20), N0M0 on conventional imaging or choline PET/CT
- 📊 No effect on clinically meaningful secondary endpoints: MFS, OS, PCSS all null per investigator conclusion
- 🔗 vs POP-RT (Lancet 2021): POP-RT showed bFFS HR 0.50 and MFS HR 0.72 favoring pelvic RT; PEACE 2 null across all endpoints — conflicting randomised evidence in same setting
- ⚠️ PEACE 2 enrolled conventional-imaging N0 pts; modern PSMA-PET staging would likely exclude most nodal-risk pts upfront, limiting contemporary applicability
- ⚠️ Modest non-significant cPFS trend (HR 0.81) leaves room for debate; trial may have been underpowered for small absolute differences, but OS and MFS null is a hard signal
- ❓ Does PSMA-PET-guided pelvic RT (treating only PET-detected nodes) outperform empirical whole-pelvis RT? PEACE 2 does not answer this.

## 👄 Head & Neck

Two prospective datasets challenge ENI necessity and proton superiority in OPSCC on the same day.

### INRT-AIR & DARTBOARD pooled analysis

> ENI omission via AI-assisted INRT in HNSCC: 5-yr elective nodal recurrence 0%, 5-yr OS 87%, 5-yr PFS 74% (N=117, median f/u 3.4 yr).

![Key Results: N=117, median f/u 3.4 yr. 5-yr elective nodal recurrence 0%. 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%. 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%. MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl3v12W4AAZgD4.jpg)
*Key Results: N=117, median f/u 3.4 yr. 5-yr elective nodal recurrence 0%. 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%. 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%. MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo.*

- 📊 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%
- 🔍 Patient-level pooled analysis, 2 prospective trials (INRT-AIR + DARTBOARD); N=117, median f/u 3.4 yr; oropharynx/larynx/hypopharynx, stage I-IVB (excl T1-2N0 larynx); required PET/CT + neck CT
- 💊 INRT approach: involved-nodal RT only, ENI omitted; AI model used to identify suspicious lymph nodes
- 📊 QoL: mean composite MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo, no significant decline post-treatment
- ⚠️ Pooled single-arm analysis, no randomised comparator vs standard ENI; N=117 limits subgroup inference; presenter acknowledges randomised evidence needed before implementation outside trial
- 🔗 vs historical CRT with ENI: regional failure rates broadly similar (4.3% at 3 yr), suggesting ENI omission does not increase nodal failure in this selected population
- ❓ Randomised trial required to confirm; optimal INRT eligibility criteria (AI threshold, stage cut-offs) unresolved

## 🎀 Breast

### NRG/RTOG 1005 · [[NCT01349322]]

> Concurrent boost during WBI noninferior to sequential boost for IBR in high-risk early breast cancer: HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04).

- 📊 1° EP (IBR): 7-yr IBR 2.6% concurrent vs 2.2% sequential; HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04), p=0.037 for noninferiority (upper bound 2.04 < prespecified margin 2.12)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2,255 eligible; high-risk early breast (grade 3, ER-, LVI, close margins, node+); 7.3-yr median f/u; 278 sites
- 💊 Concurrent: 40 Gy/15F WBI + 0.53 Gy/day concurrent boost (8 Gy/15F); Sequential: 50 Gy/25F or 42.7 Gy/16F WBI then 12 Gy/6F or 14 Gy/7F boost
- 📊 No differences in DFS, OS, DDFS, or RNR between arms; 7-yr RNR 1.2% (95% CI 0.8-1.7%) overall
- 📊 Cosmesis (BCTOS): mean 3-yr change 0.18 concurrent vs 0.16 sequential; noninferior (1-sided p<0.0001); physician-rated excellent/good 82.4% vs 85.9% at 3 yrs (p=0.34)
- ⚠️ Superiority test for concurrent arm (prespecified if NI met) was not significant; 3DCRT predominant technique (59%), limits generalizability to modern IMRT-dominant practice
- 🔗 Reduces total treatment fractions vs sequential schedule; aligns with hypofractionation trends post-START-B/FAST-Forward, though direct OS/DFS comparison not established here

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42114027]] **Concurrent Versus Sequential Radiation Dose Escalation to the Surgical Cavity for Conservative Treatment of High-Risk Early Breast Cancer: NRG/RTOG 1005 Phase III Trial.** — Vicini; Winter; Freedman et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02465](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02465)
  > PURPOSE: For patients with breast cancer undergoing breast conservation, escalating the dose (boost) of radiation to the lumpectomy cavity after whole-breast irradiation (WBI) reduces ipsilateral breast recurrence (IBR) but extends treatment duration. This phase III trial investigated whether boost delivery during WBI versus after WBI provides noninferior IBR and preserves cosmetic appearance.
  > METHODS: NRG/RTOG 1005 randomly assigned patients at higher risk for IBR after lumpectomy and axillary surgery to either a sequential boost of 12 Gy in six fractions(F) or 14 Gy in 7F after WBI of 50 Gy in 25F or 42.7 Gy in 16F (sequential arm) or a concurrent boost of 8 Gy in 15F of 0.53 Gy per day with WBI of 40 Gy in 15F (concurrent arm) using 3-dimensional conformal radiation therapy (RT) or intensity-modulated RT. Based on 1.59% 5-year IBR for the sequential arm, defining the noninferiority margin as a hazard ratio upper limit on the 90% CI of 2.12, 2,312 patients provide 80% power for noninferiority of IBR as first recurrence for the concurrent arm. Secondary end points included disease-free survival and overall survival, adverse events (AEs), and cosmetic outcomes.
  > RESULTS: Between May 24, 2011, and June 20, 2014, 2,354 patients were randomly assigned, with 2,255 eligible for analysis (sequential arm, n = 1,118; concurrent arm, n = 1,137). With median follow-up of 7.3 years, there were 56 IBR events; 5- and 7-year IBR were 2.1% and 2.2% on the sequential arm and 1.9% and 2.6% on the concurrent arm, respectively. The noninferiority criterion was met: HR (90% CI): 1.31 (0.84 to 2.04), P = .037. No differences were observed in AEs, cosmetic outcomes, or survival between arms.
  > CONCLUSION: Concurrent boost during WBI results in noninferior IBR compared with sequential boost without worsening toxicity or cosmetic outcomes and reduces overall treatment time.

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

### HCC EBRT multinational IPD cohort

> EBRT for BCLC-0/A HCC: mOS not reached (BCLC-0, tx-naive) and 5.4yr (BCLC-A, tx-naive) in 4,913-pt multinational IPD analysis.

- 📊 mOS by BCLC stage and treatment status

  | Population | BCLC-0 | BCLC-A |
  |---|---|---|
  | All pts | 6.8yr (95% CI 5.7-8.7) | 4.6yr (95% CI 4.1-5.1) |
  | Treatment-naive | NR (95% CI 8.6-NR) | 5.4yr (95% CI 4.5-6.7) |

- 🔍 Systematic review-derived IPD cohort; N=4,913; median f/u 5.0yr; BCLC staging; treatment-naive vs experienced strata analyzed separately
- 📐 Multivariable Cox: advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse PS, Child-Pugh B/C associated with higher mortality; ablative dose and more recent treatment year associated with reduced mortality risk
- 🔗 Authors conclude EBRT OS for BCLC-0/A appears comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies, supporting EBRT inclusion in BCLC decision-making
- ⚠️ Non-randomised retrospective IPD pooled cohort; no direct randomised comparison to resection or ablation; heterogeneous techniques across contributing institutions and treatment eras; era effect (year of treatment) as a significant covariate suggests substantial temporal confounding

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND adds the largest randomised phase II MDT dataset, with ctDNA and immune biomarker signals pointing toward biomarker-driven enrichment strategies.

### EXTEND trial

> EXTEND phase II: MDT+SOC improved PFS vs SOC across oligometastatic histologies, HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001.

- 📊 1° EP PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001 overall; HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.40-0.89) excluding prostate baskets
- 🔍 Phase II multicenter RCT, N=334 per-protocol (MDT+SOC n=166 vs SOC n=168); 1-5 mets, 6 histology baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, 2 prostate, Other); 98% MDT was RT; median f/u 53 mo
- 📊 Basket-level PFS superiority: pancreas, prostate, and Other baskets; breast and kidney inconclusive
- 🔗 Translational signal: ctDNA detectable at enrollment correlated with shorter PFS/OS; ctDNA clearance at 3 mo correlated with improved survival; MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation strongest in PFS-superior baskets
- ⚠️ Phase II, single randomisation across heterogeneous histologies; basket-level results underpowered individually; no OS benefit reported in this primary analysis; breast and kidney baskets inconclusive, limiting broad extrapolation
- ❓ Phase III testing flagged for histology-specific signals; optimal ctDNA threshold for oligometastatic selection and immune biomarker-driven patient enrichment still open

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Sherry AD, Haymaker C, Wang S, Liu S, Bathala TK, Medina-Rosales MN, Seo A, Hara K, Reddy J, Chun SG, Mayo LL, Walker G, Pant S, Zhao D, Kovitz CA, Ramirez D, Ha CS, Smith BD, Gomez D, Cohen L, Koong AC, Reuben A, Tannir N, Corn PG, Tran PT, Siddiqui BA, Subudhi SK, Msaouel P, Ludmir EB, Tang C. ## 🫘 Kidney

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months with SABR for inoperable primary RCC (N=70, median f/u 62 mo).

- 📊 1° EP (freedom from local progression): 100% LC at 36, 60, and 84 months; no local recurrences observed
- 🔍 Single-arm phase 2, N=70 (1 withdrew pre-tx), 8 sites AUS/NL; inoperable, high-risk, or surgery-declined; T1a-T3a, up to 10 cm; 26 Gy x1 (≤4 cm) or 42 Gy x3 (>4 cm, 48h apart); median age 77, 70% male; predominantly T1b (56%)

Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4)
  > BACKGROUND: Stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy (SABR) is an emerging, non-invasive alternative for primary renal cell carcinoma. We aimed to provide the final long-term trial outcomes of TransTasman Radiation Oncology Group (TROG) 15.03 FASTRACK II, the first phase 2 trial investigating SABR for primary renal cell carcinoma to our knowledge.
  > METHODS: FASTRACK II was a non-randomised, phase 2 study conducted in eight hospitals in Australia and the Netherlands by TROG and the Australian and New Zealand Urogenital and Prostate (ANZUP) Cancer Trials Group. Here, we report the final pre-planned follow-up results. Adult patients (aged &#x2265;18 years) with histologically confirmed primary renal cell carcinoma, who were medically inoperable, high risk, or declined surgery, had an Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status of 2 or less, had tumours 10 cm or less in size, and had N0-N1 disease were included. Patients underwent either a single fraction SABR of 26 Gy for tumours 4 cm or less in maximum diameter, or 42 Gy in three fractions delivered 48 h apart for tumours more than 4 cm in maximum diameter. The primary outcome was freedom from local progression to assess local control after SABR evaluated with the Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours. The primary endpoint and safety were evaluated in the intention-to-treat population. A patient representative was involved in the study design and conduct. The trial was registered with ClinicalTrials.gov ([[NCT02613819]]) and is closed to enrolment.
  > FINDINGS: Between July 28, 2016, and Feb 27, 2020, 71 patients were enrolled and one withdrew consent before treatment. Median follow-up was 62 months (IQR 60-72), median age was 77 years (70-82). 49 (70%) of 70 patients were male and 21 (30%) were female. Race and ethnicity data were not collected. The median tumour size was 46 mm (37-55), with 24 (34%) patients with T1a disease, 39 (56%) with T1b disease, six (9%) with T2a disease, and one (1%) with T3a disease. One patient (1%) had nodal involvement (N1). SABR resulted in 100% local control at 36 months, 60 months, and 84 months. Seven (10%) patients had at least one grade 3 adverse event within 9 months of SABR that was designated possibly, probably, or definitely related to treatment: nausea and vomiting (three [4%] events); abdominal, flank, or tumour pain (four [6%]); colonic obstruction (two [3%]); and diarrhoea (one [1%]). No new long-term safety signals, grade 4 events, or treatment-related deaths were noted.
  > INTERPRETATION: Long-term follow-up supports the safety and local control of SABR for non-surgical patients with renal cell carcinoma, with no observed local recurrences or cancer-related deaths in this cohort, which had predominantly T1b disease or higher.
