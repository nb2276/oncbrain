# 2026-05-18

**EXTEND: MDT+SOC vs SOC in oligomets, PFS HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72) at 53-month f/u — ablative therapy hardens its cross-histology case.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Oligomets and prostate dominated today. EXTEND phase II confirms durable PFS benefit from MDT across solid tumors (HR 0.54); PEACE 2 finds pelvic RT non-significant in very high-risk localized PCa (cPFS HR 0.81, p=0.088), diverging from POP-RT. Head-neck data (TORPEdO, INRT pooled) add RCT-level null for IMPT QoL advantage and prospective signal for ENI omission safety.

## 🌀 Lower GI

### OPERA Trial (5-year rectal preservation)

> OPERA post-hoc: W14 clinical exam (DRE+rectoscopy) reliably identifies cCR, supporting earlier watch-and-wait eligibility assessment.

![W14 good response: Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65%, p=0.004. 5-yr OP: 75% (A) vs 83% (B), p=0.24. cCR vs nCR 5-yr OP: 81% vs 77%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIm49InWMAAbrtG.jpg)
*W14 good response: Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65%, p=0.004. 5-yr OP: 75% (A) vs 83% (B), p=0.24. cCR vs nCR 5-yr OP: 81% vs 77%.*

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of OPERA RCT; N=141 evaluable at W14; response assessed by DRE + rectoscopy (CTRE) ± MRI at week 14, prior to standard W24 assessment
- 📊 W14 CTRE: 87% (122/141) assessable; 76% achieved good response (cCR+nCR), 24% PR
- 📊 Good response rate by arm: Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65%, p=0.004
- 📊 MRI concordance: TRG1-2 in 98% (80/82) of pts with CR at W14
- 📊 5-yr organ preservation: 75% Arm A vs 83% Arm B, p=0.24 (not significant); cCR vs nCR OP rates 81% vs 77% (similar)
- ⚠️ Post-hoc design limits causal inference; W14 assessment not prospectively powered; OP rates not statistically different between arms at 5y
- ❓ Whether W14 CTRE can replace W24 as primary decision point in prospective setting remains unanswered; nCR group should not trigger surgery per authors

## 👄 Head & Neck

TORPEdO provides the first phase III RCT PRO data for IMPT vs IMRT in OPSCC; INRT pooled analysis adds prospective ENI omission safety data.

### TORPEdO (CRUK/18/010)

> TORPEdO phase III: IMPT vs IMRT shows similar HR-QoL trajectories in OPSCC from 6 weeks post-CRT through 24 months.

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=205, OPSCC requiring concurrent chemo-RT with bilateral neck treatment; 2:1 randomisation IMPT vs IMRT; 70 Gy/56 Gy in 33 fractions + cisplatin 100 mg/m² D1+D22
- 📊 Co-primary patient-reported EP: UW-QoL physical composite (saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech) at 12 months — no difference between arms at 3, 12, or 24 months post-RT
- 📊 Co-primary clinician EP: CTCAE G3 weight loss (≥20% from baseline) or gastrostomy dependence at 12 months — no effect size reported in source tweets
- UW-QoL scores decline at end of treatment with subsequent recovery; most stabilise from 12 months post-CRT; some pts have meaningful deterioration up to 2 years; HR-QoL f/u ongoing to 5 years
- ⚠️ Commentator (@AmadeoWals) notes identical planning constraints and use of novice UK proton centres likely limited IMPT's differential potential; centres with high IMPT experience still show clinical advantages — institutional learning curve may have compressed the gap
- 🔗 vs prior single-arm and retrospective IMPT series in H&N: those suggested xerostomia/dysphagia benefit; TORPEdO's null PRO result in a rigorous RCT challenges extrapolation from non-randomised data
- ❓ whether high-volume IMPT centres with optimised dose-painting constraints would show QoL separation remains unresolved; 5-year PRO and late-effects data still pending

- 📊 3-yr cumulative incidence outcomes
  - Local recurrence: 9.5%
  - Regional recurrence: 4.3%
  - Distant metastasis: 11%
- 📊 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%; median f/u 3.4 yrs

## 🌰 Prostate

Two datasets today split on field irradiation: HEAT confirms SBRT NI at interim; PEACE 2 finds no benefit from pelvic RT in N0 very high-risk disease.

### PEACE 2

> PEACE 2: pelvic RT did not improve cPFS, MFS, OS, or PCSS vs prostate-only RT in very high-risk localized PCa.

![PEACE 2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8Pa0XQAAYLXz.jpg)

|  | Prostate-only RT | Pelvic RT |
|---|---|---|
| 7-yr cPFS | 62.9% [57.4; 68.1] | 67.1% [61.6; 72.2] |
| HR (95% CI) | ref | 0.81 [0.63; 1.03] |
| p | — | 0.088 |

- 📊 1° EP cPFS: HR 0.81 (95% CI 0.63-1.03), p=0.088 — non-significant; 7-yr cPFS 67.1% pelvic vs 62.9% prostate-only RT
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, 2x2 factorial (±pelvic RT, ±cabazitaxel), N=761 (Arm A+B vs C+D); very high-risk localized CaP (Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, or PSA ≥20), N0M0 on conventional imaging or choline PET/CT; ADT x3 yrs in all arms
- 📊 No effect of pelvic RT on MFS, PCSS, or OS per slide 4 conclusion — no effect sizes reported in source tweets for these endpoints
- 🔗 vs POP-RT (Lancet 2021): pelvic RT improved bFFS (HR 0.50) and MFS (HR 0.72) in high-risk pts; PEACE 2 population similarly staged but POP-RT used chemo-naïve arms — divergent results across trials in ostensibly similar settings raises question of era effects, imaging selection, or dose/fractionation differences
- ⚠️ Staging by conventional imaging (or choline PET/CT, not PSMA-PET) — occult nodal disease may have been present but undetected, diluting any pelvic RT benefit; PSMA-PET era would stratify this differently
- ⚠️ Trend in cPFS (4.2% absolute at 7 yrs) is non-significant but not trivially small; trial may be underpowered for modest effects given composite endpoint composition
- ❓ Whether PSMA-PET-selected node-positive pts would benefit from pelvic RT remains open — PEACE 2 excludes detectable nodal disease, so this is a different clinical question

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT: SBRT (36.25 Gy/5 fx) non-inferior to EHRT for biochemical failure at 4.25y (7% vs 7.4%, P-NI=0.007) in localized PCa.

![BF rate (Phoenix) at 4.25y: AHRT 7% vs EHRT 7.4%; P non-inferiority = 0.007; n=142 analyzed, median FU 59.7 mo](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)
*BF rate (Phoenix) at 4.25y: AHRT 7% vs EHRT 7.4%; P non-inferiority = 0.007; n=142 analyzed, median FU 59.7 mo*

- 📊 1° EP (BF by Phoenix definition) at 4.25y: AHRT 7% vs EHRT 7.4%; P non-inferiority = 0.007; NI margin 12% -- met
- 🔍 Phase III RCT (interim analysis); n=156 randomized, n=142 analyzed; 82.4% intermediate-risk, 28% received ADT, median FU 59.7 mo; trial design target n=456, n=420 evaluable enrolled
- 💊 AHRT: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx) + GTV SIB to 40 Gy; EHRT: 70.2 Gy/26 fx (2.7 Gy/fx) IMRT; ADT ≤6 mo permitted both arms
- 📊 Toxicity (clinician-reported)
  - Acute G2+ GI: lower with AHRT (no arm-level rates in source tweets)
  - Late G2+ GI: no significant difference
  - Acute/late G2+ GU: no significant difference
- 🔗 Prior AHRT NI trials (HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005) excluded ADT or used non-IMRT control arms; HEAT is first 1:1 AHRT vs EHRT with modern IMRT + ADT allowed in both
- ⚠️ Interim analysis only -- NI conclusion at 4.25y from n=142 of ~420 evaluable enrolled; full dataset and longer FU needed to confirm

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

### HCC EBRT multinational IPD cohort

> Largest multinational EBRT IPD cohort (N=4,913 HCC): mOS 4.6 yr BCLC-A, 6.8 yr BCLC-0; treatment-naive BCLC-A mOS 5.4 yr.

- 📊 mOS by BCLC stage: 6.8 yr (95% CI 5.7-8.7) for BCLC-0; 4.6 yr (95% CI 4.1-5.1) for BCLC-A
- 📊 Treatment-naive subgroup: mOS not reached (95% CI 8.6-NR) for BCLC-0; 5.4 yr (95% CI 4.5-6.7) for BCLC-A
- 🔍 IPD meta-analysis, N=4,913, systematic review methodology, median f/u 5.0 yr, stratified by BCLC stage and treatment-naive vs experienced
- 📊 Multivariable Cox: ablative RT dose and more recent treatment year associated with reduced mortality risk; advanced BCLC, higher tumor burden, worse PS, Child-Pugh B/C associated with increased mortality
- 🔗 Authors conclude EBRT OS for BCLC-0/A appears comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies — supports EBRT inclusion in BCLC decision pathway
- ⚠️ No randomized comparator arm; cross-modality OS comparisons are observational and subject to selection bias by era, institution, and candidacy
- ❓ Whether ablative EBRT (SBRT) achieves equivalent local control to resection in BCLC-A pts with preserved liver function — and how to define candidacy — remains the operative question for guideline uptake

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS).   > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). Corresponding authors were invited to submit IPD for the study. We performed Kaplan-Meier survival analyses to determine OS and restricted mean survival time (RMST) stratified by Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) stage and treatment status (ie, treatment-na&#xef;ve and experienced). We performed random effects Cox proportional hazards modeling to assess clinical characteristics associated with OS.
  > RESULTS: Data were provided on 4,913 patients treated with EBRT with a median follow-up time of 5.0 years. In multivariable models, more advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse performance status, and Child-Pugh class B or C were associated with a higher risk of mortality. Ablative radiation dose and more recent year of treatment were associated with a reduced risk of death.
  > CONCLUSION: To our knowledge, this study represents the largest multinational cohort of patients with HCC treated with EBRT. OS outcomes with EBRT for very early- and early-stage HCC appear to be comparable with resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies. These data support the inclusion of EBRT in the BCLC HCC clinical decision-making process.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

Phase II RCT-level evidence for MDT now spans 53-month median f/u across six histologic baskets.

### EXTEND Trial

> EXTEND phase II: MDT+SOC vs SOC in oligometastatic solid tumors, PFS HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001 at 53-month follow-up.

- 📊 1° EP (PFS, per-protocol, all baskets): HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001
- 📊 PFS excluding prostate baskets: HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.40-0.89) — benefit maintained across histologies
- 🔍 Phase II multicenter RCT; N=334 analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC n=166, SOC n=168); 1-5 mets; 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, 2 prostate, 'Other'); median f/u 53 mo; RT used as MDT for 98% of mets
- 📊 Basket-level PFS: superiority in pancreas, prostate, and 'Other'; breast and kidney inconclusive — no effect sizes reported in source tweets
- 🔗 Translational: baseline detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS/OS; ctDNA clearance at 3 mo post-enrollment correlated with improved survival; MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation most pronounced in PFS-superior baskets
- ⚠️ Phase II, basket design — individual baskets underpowered; breast and kidney signals null/inconclusive; heterogeneous histology mix limits effect-size interpretability across-basket; no OS benefit reported in source tweets
- ❓ Which histologies warrant phase III? ctDNA as prospective eligibility/stratification tool? Immune activation mechanistically causal or epiphenomenal?

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Sherry AD, Haymaker C, Wang S, Liu S, Bathala TK, Medina-Rosales MN, Seo A, Hara K, Reddy J, Chun SG, Mayo LL, Walker G, Pant S, Zhao D, Kovitz CA, Ramirez D, Ha CS, Smith BD, Gomez D, Cohen L, Koong AC, Reuben A, Tannir N, Corn PG, Tran PT, Siddiqui BA, Subudhi SK, Msaouel P, Ludmir EB, Tang C. ## 🫘 Kidney

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 36/60/84mo with SABR for inoperable primary RCC, median f/u 62mo, N=70.

- 📊 1° EP (freedom from local progression): 100% LC at 36, 60, and 84 months — no local recurrences observed
- 🔍 phase 2 non-randomised, N=70 (71 enrolled, 1 withdrew pre-treatment), 8 sites AU/NL; median age 77 yrs, 70% male; median tumour 46mm; 56% T1b, 34% T1a, 9% T2a, 1% T3a; 1% N1
- 💊 dose: single 26 Gy for tumours ≤4cm; 42 Gy/3fx (48h apart) for >4cm
- ⚠️ G3 AEs within 9mo (possibly/probably/definitely related), N=7 (10%)
  - Nausea/vomiting: 3 events (4%)
  - Abdominal/flank/tumour pain: 4 events (6%)
  - Colonic obstruction: 2 events (3%)
  - Diarrhoea: 1 event (1%)
  - No G4 events, no treatment-related deaths, no new long-term safety signals
- ⚠️ single-arm phase 2, no comparator arm — LC rates vs partial nephrectomy or RFA not directly tested; selection bias toward medically inoperable/high surgical risk pts skews toxicity profile favorably
- 🔗 vs SAFIR (single-arm SBRT series) and STAR-TRK registry data: consistent LC signal, FASTRACK II adds longest median f/u (62mo) in prospective phase 2 setting for primary RCC SABR
- ❓ OS and CSS not yet mature enough to displace surgery in operable candidates; randomised data (vs partial nephrectomy) still absent
