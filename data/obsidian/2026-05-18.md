# 2026-05-18

**NRG/RTOG 1005: concurrent boost noninferior to sequential (HR 1.31, 90% CI 0.84-2.04); breast WBI+boost consolidates to 3 weeks.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Fractionation consolidation dominated breast and prostate: NRG/RTOG 1005 sealed the 3-week concurrent boost, HEAT confirmed AHRT non-inferiority. Oligometastatic disease got the meeting's strongest prospective MDT signal (EXTEND HR 0.54, p<0.001), while PEACE 2's null pelvic RT result complicates the VHR prostate field.

### OPERA Trial (5-year rectal preservation)

> OPERA post-hoc: W14 DRE+rectoscopy reliably flags CR; 5yr organ preservation 75% (Arm A) vs 83% (Arm B), p=0.24.

![OPERA Trial (5-year rectal preservation)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIm49InWMAAbrtG.jpg)

| Endpoint | Arm A | Arm B | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| W14 good response (cCR+nCR) | 65% | 88% | 0.004 |
| 5yr organ preservation | 75% | 83% | 0.24 |

- 🔍 Post-hoc of OPERA RCT; tested W14 clinical tumor response evaluation (DRE+rectoscopy) as earlier decision point vs prespecified W24 3-modality assessment
- 📊 122/141 pts (87%) had CTRE at W14: 76% good response (cCR+nCR), 24% PR
- 📊 W14 good response: Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65%, p=0.004

- ⚠️ nCR reflects radiation effect per authors; similar 5yr OP to cCR argues against reflexive radical surgery for nCR

## 👄 Head & Neck

ENI omission (pooled INRT, N=117) showed 0% solitary elective nodal recurrence at 5yr; TORPEdO found no QoL advantage for proton over photon in OPSCC.

### ENI Omission in HNSCC (INRT-AIR & DARTBOARD pooled)

> Pooled INRT analysis (N=117): 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%, 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74% with preserved swallowing function.

![5-yr solitary ENI recurrence 0%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%; MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl3v12W4AAZgD4.jpg)
*5-yr solitary ENI recurrence 0%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%; MDADI 84.9 at 12 mo*

- 📊 5-yr risk of solitary elective nodal recurrence: 0%
- 📊 Outcomes at specified timepoints
  - 3-yr local recurrence: 9.5%
  - 3-yr regional recurrence: 4.3%
  - 3-yr distant metastasis: 11%
  - 5-yr OS: 87%
  - 5-yr PFS: 74%
- 💊 Mean composite MDADI score at 12 months: 84.9, no significant decline post-treatment
- 🔍 Patient-level pooled analysis of 2 prospective trials (INRT-AIR + DARTBOARD); N=117, median f/u 3.4 yrs; oropharynx/larynx/hypopharynx, stage I-IVB (excl T1-2N0 larynx); required PET/CT + neck CT
- 🔍 INRT approach: AI-assisted identification of suspicious lymph nodes; ENI omitted entirely

- ❓ No direct comparison to ENI-inclusive arms; regional recurrence rate of 4.3% requires contextualisation vs ENI-standard historical controls

### TORPEdO (CRUK/18/010)

> TORPEdO: no difference in UW-QoL physical composite IMPT vs IMRT at 12mo in OPSCC (phase 3 RCT, N=205).

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=205, OPSCC requiring bilateral neck CRT; 2:1 IMPT:IMRT randomisation
- 🔍 Both arms: 70/56 Gy in 33 fr over 6.5 weeks, cisplatin 100mg/m² D1+D22
- 📊 Co-primary pt-reported EP: UW-QoL physical composite at 12mo post CRT
  - Domains: saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech
  - No differences in mean scores between arms at 3, 12, or 24 months post RT
- 📊 HR-QoL trajectory similar both arms: decline at CRT end, recovery, stabilise by 12mo
- ⚠️ Some pts: meaningful HR-QoL deterioration persisting up to 2 years post CRT (both arms)
- ⚠️ Clinician co-primary (G3 weight loss ≥20% or gastrostomy dependence at 12mo) not reported in this presentation
- ⚠️ Novice UK proton centers + identical planning constraints may have limited IMPT advantage; experienced centers report ongoing clinical benefit
- 🔗 McBride et al. Lancet 2026 ([[PMID 42134353]]): concurrent proton vs photon analysis in OPC published simultaneously

## 🌰 Prostate

A split day: HEAT confirmed AHRT non-inferiority at interim; PEACE 2 found no clinical benefit for pelvic RT in VHR prostate, conflicting with POP-RT.

### PEACE 2

> PEACE 2: pelvic RT failed to improve cPFS (HR 0.81, p=0.088) or any secondary clinical endpoint vs prostate-only RT in VHR localised prostate cancer.

![PEACE 2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl8Pa0XQAAYLXz.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6–72.2] | 0.81 (0.63–1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4–68.1] | ref | — |

- 🔍 Phase III 4-arm 2×2 factorial (±pelvic RT × ±cabazitaxel); ADT x 3yr + high-dose prostate RT in all arms
- 🔍 VHR eligibility: ≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20 ng/mL; N0M0 on conventional imaging or Choline PET/CT
- 📊 No benefit on MFS, PCSS, or OS — all secondary clinical endpoints null for pelvic RT
- 🔗 Conflicts with POP-RT (2021): pelvic RT improved bFFS in VHR prostate; PEACE 2 null across all clinical endpoints

- ⚠️ Side effects minimal with modern RT techniques, per investigators

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT interim: AHRT non-inferior to EHRT for biochemical control (7% vs 7.4%, P-noninf=0.007) in localized low-intermediate risk PCa.

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)

| Arm | BF rate (Phoenix, 4.25y) | P-noninf |
|---|---|---|
| AHRT | 7% | 0.007 |
| EHRT | 7.4% | ref |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, non-inferiority margin 12%; interim analysis
- 🔍 AHRT: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx), GTV SIB to 40 Gy
- 🔍 EHRT (control): 70.2 Gy/26 fx IMRT
- 🔍 n=156 randomized, n=142 analyzed; 82.4% intermediate-risk; 28% received ADT; median FU 59.7 months
- 💊 Toxicity (clinician-reported)
  - Acute G2+ GI: lower with AHRT

- 🔗 First phase III trial comparing AHRT vs EHRT 1:1 with modern IMRT + ADT
- 🔗 Prior AHRT trials (HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005): heterogeneous controls, limited IMRT, no ADT
- ⚠️ Interim analysis; accrual goal n=456, n=420 evaluable; definitive primary analysis pending
- ⚠️ Biochemical (Phoenix) endpoint only; OS/MFS not reported in source

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

Largest EBRT IPD cohort for BCLC-0/A HCC to date; OS approaches resection and ablation benchmarks without a randomised comparator.

### HCC EBRT International IPD Cohort

> BCLC-0/A HCC EBRT IPD cohort (n=4,913): median OS 6.8 yr (BCLC-0) and 4.6 yr (BCLC-A), comparable to ablative locoregional therapies.

- 📊 Median OS by BCLC stage and treatment status

  | Stage | All pts (mOS, 95% CI) | Treatment-naïve (mOS, 95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | BCLC-0 | 6.8 yr (5.7–8.7) | NR (8.6–NR) |
  | BCLC-A | 4.6 yr (4.1–5.1) | 5.4 yr (4.5–6.7) |

- 🔍 Systematic review + IPD meta-analysis; n=4,913; median f/u 5.0 yr; multinational, multi-institution
- 📐 Multivariable: advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse PS, Child-Pugh B/C → higher mortality risk
- 📐 Ablative radiation dose and more recent year of treatment → reduced risk of death
- 🔗 Authors conclude OS comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies for BCLC-0/A
- ⚠️ No randomised comparator; resection/ablation comparison is external and historical, not within-cohort
- ⚠️ Retrospective IPD; era effect likely (more recent year of treatment independently predicts better OS, suggesting practice drift confounds)

## 🫘 Kidney

FASTRACK II 84-mo data: mature local control signal for SABR in inoperable primary RCC, single-arm design limits SOC comparison.

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II SABR for inoperable RCC: 100% local control at 84 months, no treatment-related deaths (N=70, median f/u 62mo).

- 📊 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months (primary endpoint: RECIST-based freedom from local progression)
- 🔍 Non-randomized phase 2, N=70, 8 centers (Australia + Netherlands); median f/u 62mo (IQR 60-72)
- 🔍 Regimen: 26 Gy ×1 for tumors ≤4cm; 42 Gy ×3fr 48h apart for tumors >4cm
- 🔍 Population: medically inoperable, high-risk, or declined surgery; ECOG ≤2; T1b 56%, T1a 34%; median tumor 46mm (37-55)
- 📊 G3 AEs possibly/probably/definitely related: 7 (10%) pts within 9mo
  - abdominal/flank/tumor pain: 4 (6%)
  - nausea/vomiting: 3 (4%)
  - colonic obstruction: 2 (3%)
  - diarrhea: 1 (1%)
- 📊 No G4 events, no new long-term safety signals, no treatment-related deaths
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomized comparator vs partial nephrectomy, RFA, or cryoablation
- ⚠️ 56% T1b disease; 'declined surgery' subgroup may select for lower-risk physiology than 'medically inoperable'

## 🎀 Breast

NRG/RTOG 1005 closes the sequential-vs-concurrent boost debate with a large phase III enrolling predominantly high-risk histology.

### NRG/RTOG 1005 · [[NCT01349322]]

> NRG/RTOG 1005: concurrent boost noninferior to sequential for IBR, HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04); treatment shortened to 3 weeks.

- 📊 7-year IBR 2.2% sequential vs 2.6% concurrent; noninferiority met
- 📐 HR 1.31 (90% CI 0.84-2.04), p=0.037; upper bound 2.04 < predefined 2.12 margin
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2,255 eligible; 278 sites; median f/u 7.3 years
- 🔍 High-risk enrollment: 52.7% grade 3, 29.6% ER-neg, 16.7% LVI, 16.7% close/positive margins
- 🔍 Treatment regimens
  - Sequential: WBI 50Gy/25F or 42.7Gy/16F + boost 12Gy/6F or 14Gy/7F after WBI
  - Concurrent: WBI 40Gy/15F with 0.53Gy/day concurrent boost (3 weeks total)
- 📊 DFS, OS, regional nodal recurrence: no significant arm differences
- 📊 Cosmetic outcomes (BCTOS + physician rating) noninferior at 3 years
- ⚠️ G3-4 AEs comparable between arms (p=0.81); dermatitis, fatigue, breast pain most prevalent
- ⚠️ Superiority test not significant; noninferiority criterion used one-sided 90% CI

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42114027]] **Concurrent Versus Sequential Radiation Dose Escalation to the Surgical Cavity for Conservative Treatment of High-Risk Early Breast Cancer: NRG/RTOG 1005 Phase III Trial.** — Vicini; Winter; Freedman et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02465](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02465)
  > PURPOSE: For patients with breast cancer undergoing breast conservation, escalating the dose (boost) of radiation to the lumpectomy cavity after whole-breast irradiation (WBI) reduces ipsilateral breast recurrence (IBR) but extends treatment duration. This phase III trial investigated whether boost delivery during WBI versus after WBI provides noninferior IBR and preserves cosmetic appearance.
  > METHODS: NRG/RTOG 1005 randomly assigned patients at higher risk for IBR after lumpectomy and axillary surgery to either a sequential boost of 12 Gy in six fractions(F) or 14 Gy in 7F after WBI of 50 Gy in 25F or 42.7 Gy in 16F (sequential arm) or a concurrent boost of 8 Gy in 15F of 0.53 Gy per day with WBI of 40 Gy in 15F (concurrent arm) using 3-dimensional conformal radiation therapy (RT) or intensity-modulated RT. Based on 1.59% 5-year IBR for the sequential arm, defining the noninferiority margin as a hazard ratio upper limit on the 90% CI of 2.12, 2,312 patients provide 80% power for noninferiority of IBR as first recurrence for the concurrent arm. Secondary end points included disease-free survival and overall survival, adverse events (AEs), and cosmetic outcomes.
  > RESULTS: Between May 24, 2011, and June 20, 2014, 2,354 patients were randomly assigned, with 2,255 eligible for analysis (sequential arm, n = 1,118; concurrent arm, n = 1,137). With median follow-up of 7.3 years, there were 56 IBR events; 5- and 7-year IBR were 2.1% and 2.2% on the sequential arm and 1.9% and 2.6% on the concurrent arm, respectively. The noninferiority criterion was met: HR (90% CI): 1.31 (0.84 to 2.04), P = .037. No differences were observed in AEs, cosmetic outcomes, or survival between arms.
  > CONCLUSION: Concurrent boost during WBI results in noninferior IBR compared with sequential boost without worsening toxicity or cosmetic outcomes and reduces overall treatment time.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND is the strongest randomized MDT signal to date in mixed oligometastatic solid tumors; histology-specific phase III still needed before practice change.

### EXTEND Trial

> EXTEND: MDT+SOC improved PFS vs SOC alone in oligometastatic solid tumors (HR 0.54, p<0.001) across 6 histology baskets.

- 📊 PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001 favoring MDT+SOC; median f/u 53 months
- 📊 PFS excluding prostate baskets: HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.40-0.89)
- 📊 Basket-specific PFS results
  - Pancreas: superiority for MDT+SOC
  - Prostate (both baskets): superiority for MDT+SOC
  - Other basket: superiority for MDT+SOC
  - Breast: inconclusive
  - Kidney: inconclusive
- 🔍 Phase II randomized, N=334 per protocol (MDT+SOC n=166, SOC n=168), 1-5 mets, 6 histology baskets
- 💊 RT used as MDT in 98% of metastases (370/379)
- 📐 ctDNA detectable at enrollment → shorter PFS and survival; clearance at 3 months → improved survival
- 📐 MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation most pronounced in baskets with PFS superiority
- 🔗 vs SABR-COMET (Lancet 2019): directionally consistent; SBRT across mixed oligometastatic histologies showed OS benefit in similar design
- ⚠️ Phase II; breast and kidney baskets inconclusive; authors recommend histology-specific phase III before changing practice
- ⚠️ PFS primary endpoint; OS not reported in primary analysis

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
