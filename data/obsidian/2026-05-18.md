# 2026-05-18

**EXTEND: MDT+SOC vs SOC, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001 · PFS benefit confirmed across oligomets baskets at 53mo.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> RT across disease sites dominates: EXTEND's mature phase II (HR 0.54, 53mo) is the day's sharpest signal for oligomets. PEACE 2's null on pelvic irradiation in high-risk prostate and TORPEdO's null on protons continue a theme of escalation trials not delivering on dosimetric promise.

## 🌀 Lower GI

W14 clinical assessment emerging as an actionable W&W decision point in rectal cancer, 10 weeks earlier than standard W24.

### OPERA Trial (5-year rectal preservation)

> OPERA post-hoc: W14 clinical exam (DRE+rectoscopy) reliably identifies cCR, enabling safe W&W with 5-yr OP rates 75-83%.

![OPERA Trial (5-year rectal preservation)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIm49InWMAAbrtG.jpg)

| Outcome | Arm A | Arm B | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| W14 good response (cCR+nCR) | 65% | 88% | 0.004 |
| 5-yr organ preservation | 75% | 83% | 0.24 |

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of OPERA trial; W14 CTRE (DRE+rectoscopy±MRI) evaluated in 122/141 pts (87%); response categories: cCR, nCR, PR
- 📊 W14 good response (cCR+nCR): Arm B 88% vs Arm A 65% (p=0.004); MRI TRG1-2 concordance in 98% (80/82) of CR pts
- 📊 5-yr organ preservation: Arm A 75% vs Arm B 83% (p=0.24); cCR vs nCR similar (81% vs 77%)
- 🔗 Validates W14 as feasible decision point, 10 weeks earlier than standard W24 assessment used in original OPERA protocol
- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis; W14 CTRE available in only 87% of pts; nCR partly reflects radiation side-effects, not true residual tumor
- ❓ Whether W14 W&W selection changes long-term OP rates vs W24-only selection remains to be confirmed in prospective design

## 👄 Head & Neck

Two competing questions today: proton vs photon QoL (null at 24mo), and ENI omission safety (reassuring but single-arm).

### INRT-AIR & DARTBOARD pooled (ENI omission in HNSCC)

> Pooled prospective INRT: 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%, OS 87%, PFS 74% in 117 HNSCC pts.

![5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr solitary ENI recurrence 0%; median f/u 3.4 yrs; N=117](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIl3v12W4AAZgD4.jpg)
*5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%; 3-yr LR 9.5%, RR 4.3%, DM 11%; 5-yr solitary ENI recurrence 0%; median f/u 3.4 yrs; N=117*

- 📊 5-yr solitary elective nodal recurrence 0%; 5-yr OS 87%, PFS 74%; median f/u 3.4 yrs
- 📊 3-yr cumulative incidence
  - Local recurrence: 9.5%
  - Regional recurrence: 4.3%
  - Distant metastasis: 11%
- 🔍 Patient-level pooled analysis of 2 prospective trials (INRT-AIR + DARTBOARD); N=117; oropharynx/larynx/hypopharynx stage I-IVB (excl T1-2N0 larynx); AI-assisted suspicious LN identification; required PET/CT + neck CT
- 💊 INRT omits ENI; AI model guides involved-node target delineation; definitive CRT backbone
- 📊 Mean composite MDADI score 84.9 at 12 months, no significant decline post-treatment
- ⚠️ N=117, pooled not randomised, no ENI control arm; cannot formally attribute toxicity/QoL benefit to ENI omission without comparator; authors explicitly state randomised evidence required before non-trial implementation
- 🔗 vs standard ENI-inclusive CRT: elective nodal recurrence rates with ENI historically ~2-5%; 0% here is reassuring but single-arm, short f/u for definitive conclusions

### TORPEdO

> TORPEdO (N=205): IMPT vs IMRT, no UW-QoL composite difference at 3/12/24 months in oropharyngeal SCC.

![TORPEdO](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HImrQwvWMAA-Laf.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=205, 2:1 IMPT:IMRT; oropharyngeal SCC + bilateral neck RT; cisplatin 100mg/m² D1+D22; 70/56 Gy in 33 fractions over 6.5 wks
- 📊 Patient-reported co-primary (UW-QoL physical composite: saliva, taste, chewing, swallowing, appearance, speech): no differences between arms at 3, 12, 24 months post-RT; similar trajectories throughout
- 📊 Clinician-reported co-primary (CTCAE G3 weight loss ≥20% or gastrostomy dependence at 12 months) not reported in source tweets
- ⚠️ Identical planning constraints across centers regardless of IMPT institutional experience; peer commentary notes that dosimetric advantage of protons is center-volume-dependent and UK novice-center mix may have blunted separation
- 🔗 Consistent with prior multi-institutional null signals (e.g., MD Anderson proton vs photon cohort, Blanchard series): dosimetric gain does not reliably translate to patient-reported QoL benefit across populations
- ❓ Clinician co-primary results not presented here; 5-yr HRQoL f/u ongoing; center-experience subgroup analysis warranted

## 🟡 Hepatobiliary

### HCC EBRT multinational IPD cohort

> EBRT for early-stage HCC (N=4,913): mOS 5.4yr for treatment-naive BCLC-A, comparable to resection and ablation.

- 📊 mOS by BCLC stage: BCLC-0 6.8yr (95% CI 5.7-8.7), BCLC-A 4.6yr (95% CI 4.1-5.1); treatment-naive BCLC-0 mOS not reached (95% CI 8.6-NR), naive BCLC-A 5.4yr (95% CI 4.5-6.7)
- 🔍 IPD systematic review, N=4,913, 5.0yr median f/u, random-effects Cox modeling; search date Dec 2022, invited authors submitted individual pt data
- 📊 Multivariable: advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse PS, Child-Pugh B/C all associated with higher mortality; ablative dose and more recent tx year associated with reduced death risk
- 🔗 Authors conclude OS comparable to resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies for BCLC-0/A — no head-to-head randomized data presented; comparison is cross-cohort
- ⚠️ Non-randomized IPD meta-analysis; no comparator arm within the study. Cross-study comparisons to resection/ablation carry selection bias (who gets EBRT vs surgery differs by era, comorbidity, tumor location). Era effects explicitly noted as a covariate.
- ❓ Direct RCT vs resection or thermal ablation still absent; optimal candidacy criteria (size, location, Child-Pugh, portal hypertension) within BCLC-0/A not defined by this dataset

[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02399](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02399)
  > PURPOSE: External beam radiation therapy (EBRT) has gained delayed acceptance as a recommended first-line treatment modality for patients with hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), given limited evidence that it improves overall survival (OS). We analyzed individual patient data (IPD) from an international cohort to assess OS among patients with HCC treated with EBRT.
  > METHODS: We performed a systematic review of publications that assessed EBRT, met prespecified technical standards for HCC, and reported OS (search date December 15, 2022). Corresponding authors were invited to submit IPD for the study. We performed Kaplan-Meier survival analyses to determine OS and restricted mean survival time (RMST) stratified by Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer (BCLC) stage and treatment status (ie, treatment-na&#xef;ve and experienced). We performed random effects Cox proportional hazards modeling to assess clinical characteristics associated with OS.
  > RESULTS: Data were provided on 4,913 patients treated with EBRT with a median follow-up time of 5.0 years. The median OS was 6.8 years (95% CI, 5.7 to 8.7) for BCLC-0 and 4.6 years (95% CI, 4.1 to 5.1) for BCLC-A. Among treatment-na&#xef;ve patients, the median OS was not reached (95% CI, 8.6 to not reached) for BCLC-0 and was 5.4 years (95% CI, 4.5 to 6.7) for BCLC-A. In multivariable models, more advanced BCLC stage, higher tumor burden, worse performance status, and Child-Pugh class B or C were associated with a higher risk of mortality. Ablative radiation dose and more recent year of treatment were associated with a reduced risk of death.
  > CONCLUSION: To our knowledge, this study represents the largest multinational cohort of patients with HCC treated with EBRT. OS outcomes with EBRT for very early- and early-stage HCC appear to be comparable with resection, thermal ablation, and other ablative locoregional therapies. These data support the inclusion of EBRT in the BCLC HCC clinical decision-making process.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND is the largest and most mature basket-trial evidence yet for MDT across solid tumor histologies.

### EXTEND Trial

> EXTEND phase II: MDT+SOC vs SOC in oligomets, HR 0.54 (0.41-0.72), p<0.001 for PFS across all baskets.

- 📊 1° EP PFS: HR 0.54 (95% CI 0.41-0.72), p<0.001 (per-protocol, all baskets); excluding prostate baskets HR 0.60 (0.40-0.89)
- 🔍 Phase II randomized, N=334 per protocol; 1-5 mets, 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, 2 prostate, Other); MDT was RT in 98% of mets; median f/u 53mo
- 📊 Basket-level PFS signals
  - Superiority: pancreas, prostate (both baskets), Other
  - Inconclusive: breast, kidney
- 🔗 vs ORIOLE (JCO 2020) and STOMP (Lancet Oncol 2018): EXTEND is the largest basket trial and first to show consistent PFS benefit across histologies with mature 53mo f/u; prior trials were prostate-only or smaller
- ⚠️ Phase II, not powered for OS; basket-level N small, PFS superiority in only 3/6 baskets; prostate-dominant enrollment may inflate overall HR; no OS data reported in abstract
- ❓ ctDNA clearance at 3mo correlated with survival: can it select pts most likely to benefit from MDT? Phase III basket designs needed for breast and kidney

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42142259]] **Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial.** — Sherry; Haymaker; Wang et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856](https://doi.org/10.1200/JCO-25-02856)
  > PURPOSE: We tested the hypothesis that adding metastasis-directed therapy (MDT) to standard of care (SOC) systemic therapy improves progression-free survival (PFS) among patients with oligometastatic disease.
  > METHODS: EXTEND was a multicenter randomized phase II trial. Patients with 1-5 metastases were randomized to MDT+SOC vs SOC in 1 of 6 baskets (breast, pancreas, kidney, two prostate baskets, and an "Other" basket) with basket-specific stratification and powering. PFS, the primary endpoint, was pre-specified in the per-protocol set within each basket, across all baskets, and across all baskets excluding the prostate baskets. Exploratory endpoints included circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) and immune profiling.
  > RESULTS: From 2018 through 2023, 521 patients were screened, 350 were randomized, and 334 were analyzed per protocol (MDT+SOC, n=166; SOC, n=168). Radiotherapy was used as MDT for 98% of metastases (370/379). Overall, after median follow-up of 53 months, PFS was improved with MDT+SOC (HR 0.54, 95% CI 0.41 to 0.72, p < 0.001). Similarly, PFS was improved when excluding the prostate baskets (HR, 0.60; 95% CI: 0.40 to 0.89). Within each basket, PFS superiority was identified for the pancreas, prostate, and "Other" baskets, whereas the breast and kidney baskets were inconclusive. At enrollment, detectable ctDNA correlated with shorter PFS and survival; by contrast, ctDNA clearance 3-months post-enrollment correlated with improved survival. MDT+SOC-induced systemic immune activation was most pronounced among baskets demonstrating PFS superiority.
  > CONCLUSION: The phase II EXTEND trial supports the addition of MDT to SOC for oligometastatic disease. Histology-specific efficacy signals were identified for phase III testing. Translational insights suggest the potential for optimizing the definition of oligometastasis using ctDNA, and point to systemic immune responses as a possible mechanism of benefit from MDT.
  - 📝 *Curator note:* Sherry AD, Haymaker C, Wang S, Liu S, Bathala TK, Medina-Rosales MN, Seo A, Hara K, Reddy J, Chun SG, Mayo LL, Walker G, Pant S, Zhao D, Kovitz CA, Ramirez D, Ha CS, Smith BD, Gomez D, Cohen L, Koong AC, Reuben A, Tannir N, Corn PG, Tran PT, Siddiqui BA, Subudhi SK, Msaouel P, Ludmir EB, Tang C. Addition of Metastasis-Directed Therapy to Standard of Care for Oligometastatic Solid Tumors: Primary Analysis of All Tumor-Histology Baskets of the Phase II Randomized EXTEND Trial. J Clin Oncol. 2026 May 16:101200JCO2502856.

> [!question] Open questions
> - Can ctDNA clearance at 3mo reliably select pts most likely to benefit from MDT?
> - Phase III basket designs needed for breast and kidney, where EXTEND PFS superiority was inconclusive.

## 🌰 Prostate

Fractionation (HEAT interim) and pelvic field (PEACE 2 phase III) examined in the same day, with provisional and null results respectively.

### HEAT Trial · [[NCT01794403]]

> HEAT trial: AHRT non-inferior to EHRT for biochemical failure at 4.25y (7% vs 7.4%, p=0.007) in localized PCa.

![HEAT Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInfJedWwAAReWC.jpg)

| Endpoint | AHRT | EHRT | p (non-inf) |
|---|---|---|---|
| BF at 4.25y | 7% | 7.4% | 0.007 |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT; n=156 randomized, 142 analyzed (interim; accrual goal 456); 82.4% intermediate-risk; median FU 59.7 months; 28% ADT
- 💊 AHRT: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (GTV SIB 40 Gy) vs EHRT: 70.2 Gy/26 fx IMRT; ADT ≤6 months permitted both arms
- 📊 Acute G2+ GI toxicity lower with AHRT; late G2+ GI and acute/late G2+ GU comparable between arms
- 🔗 Prior SBRT trials (HYPO-RT-PC, PACE-B, NRG-GU005) excluded ADT and used heterogeneous EHRT controls; HEAT first 1:1 with modern IMRT + ADT in both arms
- ⚠️ Interim analysis (156 of 456 planned); 12% NI margin is wide; full-cohort results needed before adoption
- ❓ Whether ADT interacts with fractionation schedule on long-term BF or late toxicity remains open

## 🫘 Kidney

### FASTRACK II (TROG 15.03) · [[NCT02613819]]

> FASTRACK II: 100% local control at 36, 60, and 84 months with SABR for primary RCC in non-surgical pts (N=70, median f/u 62mo).

- 📊 1° EP (freedom from local progression): 100% LC at 36mo, 60mo, and 84mo; no local recurrences observed
- 🔍 Phase 2 non-randomised, N=70 (1 withdrew pre-treatment), 8 centres Australia/Netherlands; median f/u 62mo (IQR 60-72)
- 🔍 Dose: 26 Gy single fraction for tumours ≤4cm; 42 Gy/3fr (48h apart) for >4cm; ITT analysis
- 📊 G3 AEs within 9mo: 10% pts; nausea/vomiting 4%, pain 6%, colonic obstruction 3%, diarrhoea 1%; no G4 events, no treatment-related deaths
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomised comparator vs partial nephrectomy or thermal ablation; predominately T1b (56%) and medically inoperable/high-risk pts, median age 77 — limits generalisability to operable pts
- 🔗 Consistent with STAR-TRK and prior SABR series; 100% LC durable to 7yr is the longest follow-up reported for SABR in primary RCC to date
- ❓ No OS benefit or QoL data vs surgery/ablation; optimal candidate selection (T2a, younger operable pts) and sequencing with systemic IO therapy remain open

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42144018]] **Long-term outcomes of stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy for primary kidney cancer (TROG 15.03 FASTRACK II): a multicentre, non-randomised, phase 2 study.** — Siva; Pryor; Martin et al.. *The Lancet. Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S1470-2045(26)00091-4)
  > BACKGROUND: Stereotactic ablative body radiotherapy (SABR) is an emerging, non-invasive alternative for primary renal cell carcinoma. We aimed to provide the final long-term trial outcomes of TransTasman Radiation Oncology Group (TROG) 15.03 FASTRACK II, the first phase 2 trial investigating SABR for primary renal cell carcinoma to our knowledge.
  > METHODS: FASTRACK II was a non-randomised, phase 2 study conducted in eight hospitals in Australia and the Netherlands by TROG and the Australian and New Zealand Urogenital and Prostate (ANZUP) Cancer Trials Group. Here, we report the final pre-planned follow-up results. Adult patients (aged &#x2265;18 years) with histologically confirmed primary renal cell carcinoma, who were medically inoperable, high risk, or declined surgery, had an Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group performance status of 2 or less, had tumours 10 cm or less in size, and had N0-N1 disease were included. Patients underwent either a single fraction SABR of 26 Gy for tumours 4 cm or less in maximum diameter, or 42 Gy in three fractions delivered 48 h apart for tumours more than 4 cm in maximum diameter. The primary outcome was freedom from local progression to assess local control after SABR evaluated with the Response Evaluation Criteria in Solid Tumours. The primary endpoint and safety were evaluated in the intention-to-treat population. A patient representative was involved in the study design and conduct. The trial was registered with ClinicalTrials.gov ([[NCT02613819]]) and is closed to enrolment.
  > FINDINGS: Between July 28, 2016, and Feb 27, 2020, 71 patients were enrolled and one withdrew consent before treatment. Median follow-up was 62 months (IQR 60-72), median age was 77 years (70-82). 49 (70%) of 70 patients were male and 21 (30%) were female. Race and ethnicity data were not collected. The median tumour size was 46 mm (37-55), with 24 (34%) patients with T1a disease, 39 (56%) with T1b disease, six (9%) with T2a disease, and one (1%) with T3a disease. One patient (1%) had nodal involvement (N1). SABR resulted in 100% local control at 36 months, 60 months, and 84 months. Seven (10%) patients had at least one grade 3 adverse event within 9 months of SABR that was designated possibly, probably, or definitely related to treatment: nausea and vomiting (three [4%] events); abdominal, flank, or tumour pain (four [6%]); colonic obstruction (two [3%]); and diarrhoea (one [1%]). No new long-term safety signals, grade 4 events, or treatment-related deaths were noted.
  > INTERPRETATION: Long-term follow-up supports the safety and local control of SABR for non-surgical patients with renal cell carcinoma, with no observed local recurrences or cancer-related deaths in this cohort, which had predominantly T1b disease or higher.
