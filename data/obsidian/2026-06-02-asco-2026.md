# 2026-06-02 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**LBA8005: concurrent thoracic RT adds nothing to chemoIO in ES-SCLC (OS 10.0 vs 11.8mo, HR 1.14, p=0.40). First randomized TRT test in the IO era, and it's negative.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> The day's thread is RT plus immunotherapy integration, running in opposite directions. Thoracic: adding concurrent thoracic RT to chemoIO in ES-SCLC was flatly negative (LBA8005). Lower-GI: node-sparing short-course RT + tislelizumab doubled pCR in pMMR/MSS rectal (mRCAT-III, 61 vs 29%), though confounded. Breast adds a practical RT/systemic concurrency guide.

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

First randomized read on adding consolidative thoracic RT to modern chemoIO, and it lands null.

### Concurrent Thoracic RT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC (LBA8005)

> Adding concurrent thoracic RT to chemoimmunotherapy did not improve OS (10.0 vs 11.8mo, HR 1.14, p=0.40) in extensive-stage SCLC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> First randomized test of thoracic RT in the chemoIO era, and it is negative: identical 30 Gy/10 fx to CREST but no OS benefit (HR 1.14) and null in every subgroup shown, including chemo completers. Stops the CREST extrapolation to durvalumab-treated ES-SCLC; concurrent thoracic RT is not supported here.

- 🔍 Randomized phase 3, N=228 (115 chemoIO+TRT vs 113 chemoIO); stratified by liver and brain mets
- 🔍 Thoracic RT 30 Gy/10 fx, concurrent, starting day 21-28 (cycle 2)
- 💊 Backbone: 4× durvalumab 1500mg + carboplatin AUC5 + etoposide, then durvalumab Q4W maintenance
- 🔗 vs CREST (Slotman, Lancet 2015): identical 30 Gy/10 fx improved 2yr OS in the pre-IO era
- 🔗 First randomized test of thoracic RT in the chemoIO era, and it is negative
- ⚠️ No rescue in the 'best' subgroups: chemo completers and pts without brain/liver mets both null
- ⚠️ TRT arm trended numerically worse for OS, not merely null; no directional hint of benefit
- 🔍 PCI and WBRT permitted in both arms, so thoracic RT is the only randomized variable

## 🌀 GI Lower

IO enters pMMR/MSS rectal, the setting where it usually does nothing, paired with a node-sparing RT change.

### mRCAT-III · [[NCT06507371]]

> pCR 61.0 vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) with node-sparing SCRT + tislelizumab vs conventional SCRT, both + CAPOX, in pMMR/MSS LARC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read: node-sparing (tumor-bed-only 5Gy×5) and added tislelizumab move together in the experimental arm, so the doubled pCR (61.0 vs 28.6%) can't be attributed to nodal omission alone. It doesn't yet move the elective-nodal-coverage decision, a factorial or IO-matched design is needed before dropping nodal RT in pMMR LARC.

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13rE-XwAERv45.jpg)

| Endpoint (ITT, BICR) | Experimental (n=77) | Control (n=77) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| pCR | 61.0% (47/77) | 28.6% (22/77) | <0.0001 |
| MPR (TRG0+1) | 77.9% (60/77) | 50.6% (39/77) | <0.0001 |

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13qZ3W8AII7TS.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 3 open-label RCT, 17 China sites, N=154 (77/arm), stratified by cN (cN0 vs cN+)
- 📐 Both arms 5Gy×5 short-course RT; experimental spares tumor-draining nodes (tumor bed only)
- 💊 Both arms CAPOX; experimental adds tislelizumab 200mg IV D1 (anti-PD1)
- ⚠️ Confounded: experimental changes TWO things at once (node-sparing RT + tislelizumab); pCR gain not attributable to nodal omission alone
- ⚠️ pMMR/MSS setting where IO is usually inactive; the immune-sparing mechanism is hypothesis, not proven by this design
- ⚠️ 1° EP pCR is a surrogate; organ preservation, EFS, OS all secondary and immature
- 📊 Reported fewer severe GI AEs in experimental arm; no toxicity numbers given in source
- 🔍 Eligibility: cT3-4N0/+M0, tumor ≤10cm from anal verge, no positive lateral node, age 18-75, ECOG 0-1

## 🎀 Breast

Not a trial, a practical map of which systemic agents run concurrent with locoregional RT.

### RT + Systemic Therapy Sequencing (Speers ASCO26 talk)

> Which breast systemic agents run concurrent with locoregional RT vs hold: endocrine + HER2 mAbs continue, cytotoxics/PARPi sequence, T-DXd + CDK4/6i case-by-case.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT-actionable read is agent-specific concurrency, not a single number: continue endocrine therapy, trastuzumab ± pertuzumab, and T-DM1 through breast/CW + nodal RT; hold cytotoxics and veliparib/talazoparib. For T-DXd the ILD lever is dosimetric, not temporal (RT timing 10.7% seq vs 9.6% concurrent, no effect), so sequence/hold hinges on lung dose and baseline pulmonary disease.

- 🔍 Traffic-light guide: concurrent systemic therapy with breast / CW + RNI RT

  | Agent | Concurrent with breast/CW + RNI RT |
  |---|---|
  | Endocrine therapy | Continue (minimal radiosensitization) |
  | Trastuzumab ± pertuzumab | Continue (modern heart-sparing) |
  | T-DM1 | Continue during locoregional RT; CNS-SRS caution |
  | T-DXd | Caution; sequence/hold for high lung-dose or active pulmonary disease |
  | Pembrolizumab | Generally tolerable; pneumonitis + immune-tox vigilance |
  | Olaparib | Reasonable with limited fields; concurrent only in protocol |
  | CDK4/6 inhibitors | Usually hold large fields; concurrent only in selected/protocol |
  | Cytotoxic chemo | Hold/sequence (anthracyclines, taxanes, platinum) |
  | Capecitabine | Avoid concurrent outside protocol |
  | Veliparib / talazoparib | Avoid concurrent (severe acute/late tox) |

- 🔍 Speers ASCO26 synthesis (adapted from ASCO Educational Book 2026, Table 5): what to continue, hold, or sequence during breast/CW + nodal RT
- ⚠️ T-DXd dominant toxicity is ILD; PI (5.4 mg/kg) ILD ~12%, fatal ~0.9%
- 📊 DESTINY-Breast05: ILD 9.6% T-DXd vs 1.6% T-DM1
- 📊 T-DXd RT timing showed no ILD effect: 10.7% sequential vs 9.6% concurrent
- 🔗 COMBART: 40 pts concurrent RT/SRT with T-DXd, acute tox 20%
- 🔗 KEYNOTE-522 post-hoc (1,174 pts, 715 got RT): concurrent pembro + RT well tolerated, numerically fewer G3-5 and immune AEs vs sequential
- 📊 P-RAD (TBCRC-053): preop RT 0/9/24 Gy + pembro in HR+/HER2−; 24 Gy arm raised T-cell infiltration at 2 wks, induced tertiary lymphoid structures
- ⚠️ Olaparib: complete RT 2-12 wks before starting (sequential paradigm early-stage); RadioPARP hints at concurrent safety
- ⚠️ Veliparib (TBCRC 024): severe acute + late toxicity with concurrent RT, avoid concurrency
- 🔍 CDK4/6i concurrency data mostly retrospective; hold for large fields, concurrent only in protocol
