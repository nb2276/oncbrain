# 2026-06-02 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**mRCAT-III: pCR 61.0% vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) for node-sparing SCRT + CAPOX + tislelizumab in pMMR LARC, though node-sparing and added IO are confounded.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Two trials probe RT subtraction. In ES-SCLC, adding concurrent thoracic RT to 1L chemoIO missed (OS 10.0 vs 11.8mo, HR 1.14): the CREST-era consolidation signal doesn't carry into the IO era. In LARC, dropping elective nodal RT alongside added tislelizumab doubled pCR, but the two changes are confounded and locoregional control is unreported. Thoracic and lower-GI carried the day.

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

A clean negative for concurrent thoracic RT in ES-SCLC: the consolidation-RT benefit seen in CREST's chemo-only era doesn't transfer to 1L chemoimmunotherapy.

### LBA8005: Concurrent TRT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC

> OS 10.0 vs 11.8mo, HR 1.14 (0.84-1.56), p=0.40: concurrent thoracic RT added no survival benefit to 1L chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Concurrent thoracic RT (30 Gy/10 fx) adds nothing to 1L chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC, and the subgroups where CREST's late benefit would be expected (chemoIO completers, no brain/liver mets) were null too. But this tested concurrent-during-induction RT in an unselected population, not CREST's consolidative-to-responders design, so consolidative TRT stays an open question.

- 🔍 Randomized phase III, concurrent TRT n=115 vs chemoIO alone n=113, 1° endpoint OS
- 🔍 TRT 30 Gy/10 fx given concurrently (start day 21-28, cycle 2), not as post-chemo consolidation
- 💊 Backbone: durvalumab 1500mg + carboplatin AUC5 + etoposide x4, then durvalumab maintenance to PD
- 🔍 PCI 25-30 Gy optional to responders
- 📊 Even in the subgroups most likely to benefit (chemoIO completers, no brain/liver mets), TRT showed no OS advantage
- 🔗 vs CREST (Slotman, Lancet 2015): same 30Gy/10fx but consolidative in the chemo-only era; that late-OS signal does not carry into the IO era here
- ⚠️ Concurrent timing and an unselected (not response-enriched) population differ from CREST's design; not a clean replication
- ⚠️ Toxicity was a key secondary; no AE numbers reported in source

## 🎀 Breast

A practical concurrency map for RT + systemic therapy with RNI, gating which agents continue, pause, or hold during breast/chest-wall RT.

### RT + Systemic Therapy Concurrency in Breast Cancer (Speers)

> Concurrency map for breast/CW + RNI: continue endocrine, trastuzumab/pertuzumab, T-DM1; caution T-DXd and CDK4/6i; hold cytotoxics, capecitabine, veliparib/talazoparib.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The actionable read is T-DXd: DESTINY-Breast05's RT-timing data (ILD 10.7% sequential vs 9.6% concurrent, no effect) licenses concurrent T-DXd + RT with monitoring rather than a blanket hold, except for high lung-dose plans or baseline ILD. PK washout, not reflexive holding, governs the rest.

![RT + Systemic Therapy Concurrency in Breast Cancer (Speers)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwG5cuWgAAGNpJ.jpg)

![RT + Systemic Therapy Concurrency in Breast Cancer (Speers)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwG5czWcAAubrI.png)

- 🔍 Default outside protocols: PK washout (5×t½) → RT → resume; t½ gates hold duration
  - T-DM1 5t½ ~20 days; T-DXd 5t½ ~30 days
  - Talazoparib 5t½ ~19 days (long PK tail complicates brief holds)
  - Olaparib 5t½ ~3 days; pembrolizumab 5t½ ~110 days (holding by PK rarely practical)
- 📊 Traffic-light: concurrent systemic therapy with breast/CW + RNI (ASCO Educ Book 2026, Table 5)

  | Agent | Concurrency | Note |
  |---|---|---|
  | Endocrine therapy | Continue | Minimal radiosensitization |
  | Trastuzumab ± pertuzumab | Continue | Standard practice; modern heart-sparing |
  | T-DM1 | Continue | Dermatitis/pneumonitis vigilance; caution CNS SRS |
  | Immunotherapy (pembro) | Continue | Pneumonitis vigilance; immune-tox workflow |
  | T-DXd | Caution | Sequence/hold for high lung-dose or active pulmonary disease |
  | CDK4/6i | Caution/hold | Hold large fields; concurrent only in protocol |
  | Olaparib | Caution | Limited fields; concurrent only in protocol |
  | Cytotoxic chemo | Hold | Anthracyclines, taxanes, platinum: sequence |
  | Capecitabine | Hold | Adjuvant paradigm sequential; resume after RT |
  | Veliparib / talazoparib | Hold | Severe acute/late tox concurrent |
  | mTOR / PI3K | Caution | ESMO-ESTRO advises caution |

- 📊 ADC ILD signal: T-DXd vs T-DM1 (the dominant RT-relevant toxicity)

  | ILD metric | T-DXd | T-DM1 |
  |---|---|---|
  | DESTINY-Breast05 ILD | 9.6% | 1.6% |
  | Per-PI ILD (5.4 mg/kg) | ~12% | n/a |
  | Fatal ILD | ~0.9% | n/a |

- 📊 T-DXd + RT timing (DESTINY-Breast05): ILD 10.7% sequential vs 9.6% concurrent, no effect
- 📊 COMBART: 40 pts concurrent RT/SRT, acute toxicity 20%
- 💊 KEYNOTE-522 post-hoc (1,174 pts, 715 received RT): concurrent pembro + RT well tolerated, numerically fewer G3-5 AEs vs sequential
- 💊 P-RAD (TBCRC-053): preop RT 0/9/24 Gy + pembro; 24 Gy arm → significant T-cell infiltration at 2 wks (RT as biologic stress test)
- ⚠️ Veliparib: avoid concurrency, dose-limiting moist desquamation + fibrosis (TBCRC 024)
- ⚠️ Olaparib: RT must complete 2-12 wks before; concurrent only in limited fields (RadioPARP signal)

## 🌀 GI Lower

A pCR doubling with PD-1 in typically IO-refractory pMMR rectal, but node-sparing and added tislelizumab are confounded and locoregional endpoints are unreported.

### mRCAT-III · [[NCT06507371]]

> pCR 61.0% vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) for node-sparing SCRT + CAPOX + tislelizumab vs conventional SCRT + CAPOX in pMMR LARC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is target volume: the experimental arm both spared elective nodal RT (tumor bed only) and added tislelizumab, so the pCR doubling can't isolate node-sparing's contribution. pCR is also blind to nodal failure, the very risk nodal omission creates, so EFS (pending), not pCR, gates whether to drop elective nodal coverage with IO.

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13rE-XwAERv45.jpg)

| Endpoint | Experimental (n=77) | Control (n=77) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| pCR (ITT) | 61.0% (47/77) | 28.6% (22/77) | <0.0001 |
| MPR (TRG0+TRG1) | 77.9% (60/77) | 50.6% (39/77) | <0.0001 |

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13qZ3W8AII7TS.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 3 open-label RCT, N=154 (77/arm), 17 China sites; primary pCR by blinded independent central review
- 🔍 RT: short-course 5Gy×5d (25Gy/5fx) both arms; experimental spared elective nodal RT (tumor bed only, no draining nodes)
- 💊 Experimental added tislelizumab 200mg IV to the CAPOX backbone; control got CAPOX alone
- ⚠️ Confounded: experimental arm both spared nodal RT AND added tislelizumab, so pCR gain can't be attributed to node-sparing alone
- ⚠️ pCR can't detect nodal failure, the risk omitting elective nodal RT creates; locoregional control and EFS unreported
- 🔗 Control pCR 28.6% matches SCRT + consolidation-chemo TNT benchmarks (e.g. RAPIDO); increment reflects added IO plus node-sparing
- ⚠️ Fewer severe GI AEs reported in experimental arm; no effect size reported in source
- 🔍 MSS/pMMR rectal is typically IO-refractory; a pCR doubling with PD-1 here is the provocative signal, pending confirmation
