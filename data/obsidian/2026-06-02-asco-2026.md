# 2026-06-02 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**mRCAT-III: pCR 61.0% vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) — node-sparing short-course RT + tislelizumab + CAPOX doubles pCR in pMMR LARC.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Rectal and thoracic carried the RT news, both on how RT meets IO. mRCAT-III doubles pCR (61 vs 28.6%) by node-SPARING to preserve PD-1 priming; LBA8005 shows adding concurrent thoracic RT to chemoIO does nothing in ES-SCLC (OS 10.0 vs 11.8mo). Breast: a concurrency traffic-light for RNI RT.

## 🎀 Breast

No new trial: a continue/caution/hold framework for timing systemic agents against breast/CW + RNI RT.

### Concurrent Systemic Therapy + Radiation Timing (Speers)

> Traffic-light framework for what runs concurrent with breast/CW + RNI RT vs hold: continue endocrine + trastuzumab/pertuzumab, caution T-DXd/CDK4/6i, hold cytotoxics/PARPi.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The actionable RT read: in the DESTINY-Breast05 arm, T-DXd ILD was 10.7% sequential vs 9.6% concurrent, so timing around RT did not change ILD, and concurrency is reasonable with lung-dose limits. The 'do not ignore' signal is T-DM1 plus CNS SRS radionecrosis; hold CDK4/6i for large fields.

![Concurrent Systemic Therapy + Radiation Timing (Speers)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwG5cuWgAAGNpJ.jpg)

![Concurrent Systemic Therapy + Radiation Timing (Speers)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwG5czWcAAubrI.png)

- 🔍 ASCO 2026 educational review (Speers): concurrency of systemic agents with breast/chest-wall + regional nodal RT, sorted continue / caution / hold. Adapted from Wong, Speers, Schaverien, ASCO Educational Book 2026, Table 5.
- 📊 Concurrency traffic-light with breast/CW + RNI RT

  | Agent class | With RT | Notes / evidence |
  |---|---|---|
  | Endocrine therapy | Continue | minimal radiosensitization |
  | Trastuzumab ± pertuzumab | Continue | concurrent standard (HERA, NCCTG N9831, APHINITY) |
  | T-DM1 | Continue | per KATHERINE / ATEMPT; watch dermatitis, pneumonitis, CNS SRS necrosis |
  | T-DXd | Caution | ILD dominant; sequence/hold for high lung-dose or active pulmonary disease |
  | Pembrolizumab | Continue, monitor | KEYNOTE-522 concurrent tolerated; pneumonitis vigilance |
  | CDK4/6i (palbo/ribo/abema) | Hold large fields | mostly retrospective; concurrent only in protocol |
  | Olaparib | Sequence | complete RT 2-12 wks before; RadioPARP suggests concurrent safety |
  | Veliparib / talazoparib | Avoid concurrent | veliparib severe acute/late tox (TBCRC 024) |
  | Capecitabine | Hold / sequence | adjuvant paradigm sequential (CREATE-X) |
  | Cytotoxics (anthracycline/taxane/platinum) | Hold | sequence, do not give concurrently |

- 📐 T-DXd ILD signal + RT-timing data

  | Metric | Value |
  |---|---|
  | ILD, T-DXd 5.4 mg/kg (PI) | ~12%; fatal ~0.9% |
  | DESTINY-Breast05 ILD | 9.6% T-DXd vs 1.6% T-DM1 |
  | RT timing (T-DXd arm) | 10.7% seq vs 9.6% concurrent, no effect |
  | COMBART concurrent RT/SRT | 40 pts; acute tox 20% |

- 💊 KEYNOTE-522 amended to permit concurrent pembro + RT; post-hoc 1,174 pts (715 got RT) showed numerically fewer G3-5 and immune AEs concurrent vs sequential.
- 🔬 P-RAD (TBCRC-053): preop RT 0 / 9 / 24 Gy + pembro in HR+/HER2-; 24 Gy arm gave significant T-cell infiltration + tertiary lymphoid structures at 2 wks (RT as immune primer).
- ⚠️ CNS radionecrosis with SRS is the 'do not ignore' signal for T-DM1; extra caution with large lung volumes, IMN coverage, bolus, reconstruction.
- ⚠️ Default outside protocol: PK-based washout → RT → resume. Much concurrency evidence is retrospective, post-hoc, or small (COMBART n=40).

## 🌀 GI Lower

Node-sparing RT reframed as an immune-priming move in IO-resistant pMMR rectal cancer.

### mRCAT-III · [[NCT06507371]]

> pCR 61.0% vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) with node-sparing SCRT + tislelizumab + CAPOX vs conventional SCRT + CAPOX in pMMR LARC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is a confound, not a green light for nodal omission: the experimental arm changed two things at once, tumor-bed-only targeting AND added tislelizumab, so node-sparing's own contribution to the pCR doubling (61.0% vs 28.6%) can't be isolated. Node-sparing plausibly drove the lower severe-GI toxicity, but this doesn't yet license dropping elective nodal RT.

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13rE-XwAERv45.jpg)

| Endpoint | Experimental | Control | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| pCR (ITT) | 61.0% (47/77) | 28.6% (22/77) | <0.0001 |
| MPR (TRG0+1) | 77.9% (60/77) | 50.6% (39/77) | <0.0001 |

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13qZ3W8AII7TS.jpg)

- 🔍 Open-label phase 3 RCT, 17 China sites, 77 pts per arm, stratified by clinical N stage
- 🔍 Both arms short-course RT 5Gy×5; experimental targets tumor bed only (node-sparing), control covers tumor-draining nodes
- ⚠️ Experimental arm changes two variables at once (node-sparing target + added tislelizumab); RT-only contribution can't be isolated
- 💊 CAPOX both arms (oxaliplatin 130mg/m² d1, capecitabine 1000mg/m² d1-14); tislelizumab 200mg d1 experimental only
- 🔗 pMMR/MSS rectal ca is typically IO-resistant; rationale is nodal-RT omission preserves draining-node priming for PD-1 response
- ⚠️ pCR/MPR are pathologic surrogates; organ-preservation, EFS, OS not reported in source
- 📊 Experimental arm reported fewer severe GI AEs (author takeaway); no AE rates given in source
- 🔗 Control pCR 28.6% aligns with short-course RT + chemo benchmarks (RAPIDO ~28%)

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

### LBA8005: Concurrent Thoracic RT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC

> OS 10.0 vs 11.8 mo, HR 1.14 (0.84-1.56), p=0.40: concurrent thoracic RT added no benefit to durvalumab-based chemoIO in ES-SCLC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The tested RT is concurrent (30 Gy/10 fx, day 21-28 with chemoIO), not CREST's consolidative post-chemo RT in responders, so the consolidative question stays open. Even the low-burden subgroup without brain or liver metastases was null (HR 1.10). Adding concurrent thoracic RT to chemoIO carries no OS benefit.

![LBA8005: Concurrent Thoracic RT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJyHFUtXwAARqHk.jpg)

| Arm | Median OS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| ChemoIO + TRT | 10.0 mo | 1.14 (0.84-1.56) | 0.40 |
| ChemoIO | 11.8 mo | n/a | n/a |

![LBA8005: Concurrent Thoracic RT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJyHFUtXoAArOYK.jpg)

| Subgroup | ChemoIO+TRT | ChemoIO | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Completed 4 chemoIO courses | 11.9 mo | 12.1 mo | 1.02 (0.72-1.44) | 0.92 |
| No brain/liver mets | 11.9 mo | 13.2 mo | 1.10 (0.65-1.87) | 0.72 |

![LBA8005: Concurrent Thoracic RT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJyHFfxXMAAZ21N.jpg)

| Arm | Median PFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| ChemoIO + TRT | 5.1 mo | 1.10 (0.84-1.45) | 0.49 |
| ChemoIO | 5.0 mo | n/a | n/a |

- 🔍 Randomized phase III, both arms on durvalumab + platinum/etoposide (chemoIO+TRT n=115, chemoIO n=113)
- 🔍 Experimental arm adds 30 Gy/10 fx concurrent thoracic RT, starting day 21-28
- 🔍 Treatment-naïve ES-SCLC (stage IV, or III ineligible for curative chemoRT); asymptomatic/stable brain mets allowed
- 🔍 PCI 25-30 Gy offered to responders in both arms
- 🔗 vs CREST (Slotman, Lancet 2015): same 30 Gy/10 fx, but consolidative (post-chemo, in responders) and pre-immunotherapy
- ⚠️ This tests concurrent TRT with chemoIO, not consolidative timing; the consolidative question stays open
- ⚠️ OS numerically favored chemoIO alone; prespecified subgroups (completers; no brain/liver mets) also null
- ⚠️ PFS also negative (see figure); no subgroup rescued a TRT benefit
