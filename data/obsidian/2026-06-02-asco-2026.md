# 2026-06-02 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**mRCAT-III: pCR 61% vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) adding node-sparing SCRT + tislelizumab in pMMR/MSS LARC, but confounded by the added PD-1.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> RT-systemic integration is the day's thread across three sites. mRCAT-III doubled pCR in pMMR LARC (61 vs 28.6%) but bundled node-sparing RT with PD-1, so the RT-volume effect is unattributable. Concurrent TRT added nothing to chemoIO in ES-SCLC (HR 1.14). Breast offered a practical traffic-light for timing systemic agents around RT.

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

A negative randomized test of concurrent thoracic RT layered onto first-line chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC.

### LBA8005: Concurrent TRT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC

> OS 10.0 vs 11.8mo HR 1.14 (p=0.40): adding concurrent thoracic RT to chemoIO did not improve survival in ES-SCLC.

![LBA8005: Concurrent TRT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJyHFUtXwAARqHk.jpg)

| Arm | Median OS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| ChemoIO + TRT | 10.0 mo (8.3-11.7) | 1.14 (0.84-1.56) | 0.40 |
| ChemoIO | 11.8 mo (10.0-13.6) | n/a | n/a |

![LBA8005: Concurrent TRT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJyHFfxXMAAZ21N.jpg)

| Arm | Median PFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| ChemoIO + TRT | 5.1 mo (4.7-5.4) | 1.10 (0.84-1.45) | 0.49 |
| ChemoIO | 5.0 mo (4.6-5.4) | n/a | n/a |

![LBA8005: Concurrent TRT + Chemoimmunotherapy in ES-SCLC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJyHFUtXoAArOYK.jpg)

| Subgroup | +TRT median | ChemoIO median | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Completed all 4 courses | 11.9 mo (9.7-14.1) | 12.1 mo (9.4-14.8) | 1.02 (0.72-1.44) | 0.92 |
| No brain/liver mets | 11.9 mo (6.2-17.7) | 13.2 mo (10.4-16.1) | 1.10 (0.65-1.87) | 0.72 |

- 📊 Adding concurrent thoracic RT to chemoIO improved neither OS nor PFS, with both endpoints numerically favoring chemoIO alone
- ⚠️ Null even in subgroups predicted to favor TRT: no brain/liver mets, and completers of all 4 chemoIO courses
- 🔍 Randomized phase III, 1° endpoint OS; treatment-naive ES-SCLC, stage IV or III unfit for curative CRT
- 📐 N=115 (+TRT) vs 113 (chemoIO alone); stratified by liver and brain metastases
- 🔍 Regimen and RT design
  - Durvalumab 1500mg + carboplatin (AUC5)/etoposide ×4, then durvalumab maintenance until PD
  - TRT 30 Gy/10 fractions concurrent, starting day 21-28 (~cycle 2), not consolidative
  - PCI 25-30 Gy to responders; optional WBRT 20-30 Gy for brain mets
- 🔗 vs CREST (Slotman, Lancet 2015): same 30 Gy/10 fx but consolidative post-chemo, and suggested a thoracic-RT survival benefit
- ⚠️ Key difference: TRT here is concurrent (day 21-28), not CREST-style consolidation, so doesn't directly refute consolidative TRT
- ⚠️ Toxicity (a key 2° endpoint) not reported in source; can't weigh added RT harm vs any benefit

## 🎀 Breast

An educational round-up on continuing, holding, or sequencing systemic agents around breast/CW + nodal RT.

### RT + Systemic Therapy Combinations in Breast Cancer (Speers)

> Educational round-up on continuing, holding, or sequencing systemic agents around breast/chest-wall + nodal RT; PK-based washout the default outside protocols.

![RT + Systemic Therapy Combinations in Breast Cancer (Speers)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwG5cuWgAAGNpJ.jpg)

![RT + Systemic Therapy Combinations in Breast Cancer (Speers)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJwG5czWcAAubrI.png)

- 🔍 Traffic-light guidance for concurrent systemic therapy with breast/CW + nodal RT (ASCO Educ Book 2026, Table 5)
- 💊 Concurrency with breast/CW + nodal RT, by agent

  | Agent | With concurrent RT |
  |---|---|
  | Endocrine therapy | Continue (minimal radiosensitization) |
  | Trastuzumab ± pertuzumab | Continue (modern heart-sparing) |
  | T-DM1 | Continue; dermatitis/pneumonitis vigilance, caution CNS SRS |
  | T-DXd | Caution; sequence/hold for high lung-dose or active pulmonary disease |
  | CDK4/6 inhibitors | Usually hold large fields; concurrent only in protocol |
  | Olaparib | Limited fields reasonable; concurrent only in protocol |
  | Pembrolizumab | Generally tolerable; pneumonitis vigilance |
  | Cytotoxic chemo | Hold/sequence (anthracycline, taxane, platinum) |
  | Capecitabine / veliparib / talazoparib | Hold; severe acute/late tox concurrent |

- 📊 T-DXd ILD signal (the highest-stakes thoracic concurrency)

  | Source | ILD / tox signal |
  |---|---|
  | T-DXd PI (5.4 mg/kg) | ILD ~12%, fatal ~0.9% |
  | DESTINY-Breast05 | ILD 9.6% T-DXd vs 1.6% T-DM1 |
  | RT timing (T-DXd arm) | 10.7% seq vs 9.6% concurrent, no effect |
  | COMBART (n=40, concurrent RT/SRT) | acute tox 20% |

- 💊 KEYNOTE-522 post-hoc (1,174 pts, 715 got RT): concurrent pembro+RT well tolerated, numerically fewer G3-5 and immune AEs vs sequential
- 📊 P-RAD (TBCRC-053): preop RT 0/9/24 Gy + pembro; 24 Gy arm raised T-cell infiltration + TLS at 2 wks (HR+/HER2-)
- ⚠️ PARPi + RT: sequence, don't run concurrent outside trials
  - Veliparib (TBCRC 024): severe acute/late tox concurrent — avoid (moist desquamation, fibrosis)
  - Olaparib (OlympiA): RT must finish 2-12 wks before; RadioPARP hints concurrent safety
  - Talazoparib: long t½ (90 hr) complicates brief holds; favors sequential
- ⚠️ CDK4/6i (abemaciclib/ribociclib/palbociclib): hold for large fields; concurrent only in protocol; evidence mostly retrospective
- ⚠️ Educational synthesis of largely retrospective + PK data, not new RCT evidence; PK washout → RT → resume is the default

## 🌀 GI Lower

A node-sparing short-course RT + PD-1 strategy nearly doubled pCR in MSS rectal cancer, though RT volume and IO are confounded.

### mRCAT-III · [[NCT06507371]]

> pCR 61.0% vs 28.6% (P<0.0001) adding node-sparing SCRT + tislelizumab vs conventional SCRT+CAPOX in pMMR LARC.

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13rE-XwAERv45.jpg)

| Endpoint | Experimental (N=77) | Control (N=77) | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| pCR (ITT) | 61.0% (47/77) | 28.6% (22/77) | <0.0001 |
| MPR (TRG0+TRG1) | 77.9% (60/77) | 50.6% (39/77) | <0.0001 |

![mRCAT-III](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJ13qZ3W8AII7TS.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 3 open-label RCT, 17 sites in China, N=154 (77/arm), stratified by clinical N stage
- 🔍 Population: pMMR/MSS LARC, cT3-4N0/+M0, tumor ≤10cm from anal verge, no positive lateral nodes
- 📐 Identical RT dose both arms (short-course 5Gy×5); sole difference is target volume
- 🔍 Experimental RT spares tumor-draining nodes (tumor bed only) vs conventional elective-nodal coverage
- ⚠️ Confounded: experimental adds tislelizumab (PD-1) plus node-sparing, control has neither; pCR gain not attributable to RT volume alone
- 🔗 pMMR/MSS rectal ca: PD-1 usually inactive (unlike dMMR); an IO signal in MSS is the surprising element if it holds
- ⚠️ pCR is a surrogate; organ preservation, EFS, OS (secondary) unreported, so durability and clinical benefit unknown
- 🔍 Open-label trial, but pCR/MPR adjudicated by blinded independent central review
- Authors report fewer severe GI AEs in the experimental arm; no AE numbers in source
