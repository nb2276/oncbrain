# 2026-05-29 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**Wait or Treat?: upfront cranial RT cut 2y intracranial PD (21.7% vs 50%, sub-HR 0.35) but no OS gain in asymptomatic EGFR/ALK NSCLC brain mets.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> CNS carried the day. The first phase III on cranial-RT timing for asymptomatic BM in TKI-treated EGFR/ALK NSCLC shows upfront RT controls the brain (sub-HR 0.35) yet doesn't extend survival, which numerically favored deferral (HR 1.45). Argues for CNS-active-TKI-first: hold RT until intracranial PD.

## 🧠 CNS

The question is timing, not addition, of cranial RT when a CNS-active TKI is already holding the brain.

### Wait or Treat? · [[NCT05236946]]

> Upfront cranial RT cut 2y intracranial progression (21.7% vs 50%, sub-HR 0.35) but no OS gain; survival numerically favored delayed (HR 1.45).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The primary endpoint actually favored upfront RT (2y intracranial progression 21.7% vs 50%, sub-HR 0.35), yet OS trended toward delayed (2y 48% vs 60%) with 6% radiation necrosis vs none. Better CNS control bought no survival and added toxicity, so upfront cranial RT can be safely deferred to intracranial progression.

| Endpoint | Upfront RT | Delayed RT |
|---|---|---|
| Events | 20 | 47 |
| 1-yr intracranial PD | 8.7% (2.9-14.5) | 25.7% (16.8-34.7) |
| 2-yr intracranial PD | 21.7% (12.6-30.8) | 50% (39.2-60.9) |
| Sub-HR (95% CI) | 0.35 (0.21-0.59), p<0.001 | ref |

- 🔍 First completed phase III RCT on timing of cranial RT for asymptomatic BM in EGFR/ALK-mutant NSCLC
- 🔍 Open-label, N=208 (105 upfront vs 103 delayed cranial RT); both arms TKI + chemo; median f/u 30.6mo
- 🔍 Delayed arm: RT held until intracranial PD or patient request; MRI brain q3mo year 1, then q6mo
- 📊 1° EP intracranial PFS met: upfront RT reduced intracranial progression (sub-HR 0.35, p<0.001)
- 📊 Survival + radiation necrosis favored the delayed arm
- 📊 No OS benefit from upfront RT; survival numerically favored the delayed arm
- ⚠️ 'Timing does not affect survival' per authors; the OS trend favoring delayed was not significant
- ⚠️ Open-label, single-institution; powered for intracranial PFS not OS, so OS/necrosis signals are secondary
- 🔗 Reinforces CNS-active TKI-first approach (osimertinib, alectinib, lorlatinib): defer RT while systemic CNS control holds
