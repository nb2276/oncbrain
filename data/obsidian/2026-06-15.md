# 2026-06-15

**NIVOPOSTOP: 3y DFS ~63% vs 53% adding adjuvant nivo to post-op cisplatin-CRT; KEYNOTE-689 EFS-positive perioperative pembro, both layering IO around an unchanged (chemo)RT backbone.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Two positive IO trials in resectable LA-HNSCC, but for the radiation oncologist neither touches RT: dose, fractionation, and target volume are unchanged, with IO layered before/after the curative (chemo)RT course. NIVOPOSTOP targets the classic high-risk adjuvant group (ENE, +margins, ≥4 nodes); both still abstract/EFS-stage with DFS/EFS HRs not in source.

## 👄 Head & Neck

IO is moving into resectable HNSCC around an unchanged curative (chemo)RT backbone, so the RT decision stays fixed while only the systemic layer changes.

### KEYNOTE-689 vs NIVOPOSTOP

> Two positive IO trials in resectable LA-HNSCC: NIVOPOSTOP 3y DFS ~63% vs 53% (adjuvant nivo), KEYNOTE-689 EFS-positive (perioperative pembro).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Neither trial changes the RT itself: adjuvant (chemo)RT stays the curative backbone and IO is layered around it. NIVOPOSTOP adds nivo to post-op cisplatin-CRT in the classic ENE/positive-margin high-risk group (3y DFS ~63% vs 53%); KEYNOTE-689 shifts IO to neoadjuvant. The move is whether to add peri-RT IO, not to alter dose or volume.

- 🔍 Educational round-up (curator infographic) contrasting two IO trials in resectable/resected LA-HNSCC, not new primary data
- 🔍 Design contrast: KEYNOTE-689 vs NIVOPOSTOP

  | Feature | KEYNOTE-689 | NIVOPOSTOP |
  |---|---|---|
  | Drug | Pembrolizumab | Nivolumab |
  | IO timing | Perioperative (neoadj + adj + maint) | Purely postoperative |
  | Population | Resectable stage III-IVA | High-risk resected: ENE, +margins, ≥4 nodes |
  | Control arm | Surgery → RT/CRT (cisplatin) | Post-op cisplatin-CRT |
  | Primary EP | EFS | DFS |

- 📊 NIVOPOSTOP: 3-yr DFS ~63% vs 53% adding nivo to post-op cisplatin-CRT vs CRT alone (ASCO 2025)
- 📊 KEYNOTE-689: significant EFS benefit, first positive perioperative IO trial in resectable HNSCC in >2 decades; no effect size reported in source tweets
- 🔍 Both keep (chemo)RT as the curative backbone; neither changes RT dose, fractionation, or target volume, IO is layered around the RT course
- 🔍 NIVOPOSTOP enrolls the classic adjuvant-CRT high-risk group (ENE, positive margins, ≥4 nodes, extensive PNI) per Bernier/Cooper indication
- 🔍 Infographic suggests greater benefit in PD-L1/CPS-high tumors; no cutoff or subgroup effect size given in source
- ⚠️ NIVOPOSTOP is ASCO 2025 abstract-stage (DFS), not yet full peer-reviewed publication; OS not reported in source
- ⚠️ KEYNOTE-689 EFS magnitude (HR/median) and NIVOPOSTOP DFS HR not in source; numbers here limited to the 3y DFS % pair
