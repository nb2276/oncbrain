## 🍽️ GI Upper

Resected pancreatic adenocarcinoma: does consolidative chemoRT after adjuvant chemo earn a role over chemo alone?

### NRG Oncology/RTOG 0848 · [[NCT01013649]]

> Adjuvant chemoRT did not improve OS overall (HR 0.96, p=0.38); node-negative subgroup gained OS (5yr 48.1% vs 28.6%).

- 📊 1° EP negative — OS, all pts (N=354): median 2.6 vs 2.3 yr, HR 0.96 (90% CI 0.79-1.18), p=0.38 (1-sided log-rank)
- 🔍 Phase III RT randomization: post-resection + completed gemcitabine-based adjuvant chemo, then randomized ± fluoropyrimidine-sensitized CXRT
- 📊 Node-negative subgroup (N=91): Chemo vs Chemo+CXRT

  | Endpoint | Chemo (n=42) | Chemo+CXRT (n=49) |
  |---|---|---|
  | Median OS | 3.0 yr (2.2-4.0) | 3.9 yr (2.5-NR) |
  | Median DFS | 1.5 yr (0.8-2.7) | 2.3 yr (1.4-NR) |
  | 5-yr OS | 28.6% (14.9-42.2) | 48.1% (33.3-62.9) |

- ⚠️ Node-negative OS benefit is a subgroup signal (interaction p=0.022); hypothesis-generating, needs confirmation
- 📊 Node-positive (N1, n=263): no signal — 5yr OS 21.2% vs 20.9%
- ⚠️ Gemcitabine-based backbone (pre-FOLFIRINOX); generalizability to current mFOLFIRINOX adjuvant standard uncertain
- ⚠️ RT dose / fractionation / target volume not reported in source text
- 🔗 Consistent with ESPAC-1's case against adjuvant chemoRT; modern adjuvant SOC is chemo alone (mFOLFIRINOX, PRODIGE-24)
