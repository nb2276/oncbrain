# 2026-07-08

**NRG-GU005: prostate SBRT no DFS edge over MH-IMRT (HR 1.38, futility) but bowel QoL co-primary met (MCID 35% vs 44%, p=0.034) with less G≥3 GU.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate SBRT was the through-line: NRG-GU005 phase III shows SBRT matches MH-IMRT oncologically (no DFS gain) while cutting bowel and GU toxicity, and mature 10-yr monotherapy data (OS 84%, late G3 GI/GU 1.4-1.5%) back its durability. Two consensus docs (ARS radiorecurrence salvage, ESTRO post-op oral-cavity CTV) fill in RT technique.

## 👄 Head & Neck

First ESTRO post-op CTV delineation consensus for resected oral-cavity SCC.

### ESTRO Post-operative CTV Delineation Guidelines (Oral Cavity SCC)

> CTV-P = GTV-P pre-op +10mm composited with surgical defect/flap +5mm; first ESTRO post-op oral-cavity SCC delineation consensus.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The delineation change is re-creating a surrogate GTV-P pre-op rather than expanding the surgical defect alone: the guideline shows defect-only contouring geographically misses (Fig 3, infratemporal fossa). Margins are GTV-P pre-op +10mm plus defect/flap +5mm, at 60 Gy EQD2. Moves post-op OCSCC target-volume practice toward a reproducible standard.

- 📊 CTV-P is a composite volume, then edited for air, bone, teeth and anatomical barriers
  - GTV-P pre-op + 10mm isotropic margin
  - surgical defect/reconstruction flap + 5mm isotropic margin
  - composite = (GTV-P pre-op + 10mm) + (defect/flap + 5mm)
- 🔍 Surrogate GTV-P/GTV-N re-created on the planning CT (image registration or anatomical landmarks) since resection leaves no residual GTV
- 🔍 CTV-N1 wraps the involved nodal level around a surrogate GTV-N; CTV-N2 covers at-risk levels at prophylactic dose
- 🔍 Includes a decision framework for unilateral vs bilateral neck treatment
- 📐 Case dose (Fig 5): PTV/CTV-P EQD2 60 Gy in 2 Gy fractions
- 🔗 Adapts the 2018 definitive-setting '5mm + 5mm' geometric GTV→CTV expansion to the post-op bed
- 🔗 DAHANCA: geometric margin more conformal than anatomical; protocol switch correlated with lower late dysphagia (post-hoc De-ESCALaTE analysis)
- ⚠️ Expert consensus only, no outcome or validation data; reduces variation, does not prove better control or lower toxicity
- ⚠️ Composite depends on re-creating GTV-P pre-op; defect-only contouring risks geographic miss (Fig 3, infratemporal fossa)

## 🌰 Prostate

SBRT-heavy day: a null phase III on DFS, mature 10-yr monotherapy outcomes, and radiorecurrence salvage consensus.

### 10-yr SBRT for Prostate Cancer (Nonrandomized Trial, Meier et al.)

> 10-yr OS 84%, RFS 90% (LR 94% / IR 86%), late G3 GI/GU 1.4-1.5% after 40Gy/5fx SBRT monotherapy, no ADT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The unfavorable-IR subgroup is the read: 10-yr RFS 77% vs 92% in favorable IR under SBRT monotherapy with no ADT allowed. Supports 40Gy/5fx alone in low- and favorable-intermediate-risk disease; unfavorable-IR durability lags, keeping ADT or intensification in play there. Late G2+ GU 14%.

- 📊 10-yr OS 84%; overall relapse-free survival 90% (median f/u 9 yr)
- 📊 10-yr RFS by risk group
  - Low-risk 94%
  - Intermediate-risk 86%
  - Favorable IR 92% vs unfavorable IR 77%
- 📊 Late toxicity (CTCAE v3, >90d), 10-yr cumulative
  - G3 GI/GU 1.4% (LR), 1.5% (IR)
  - No grade 4-5 events
  - G2+ GI 2.1%
  - G2+ GU 14%
- 🔍 Phase 2 nonrandomized, N=310 (172 LR, 138 IR), 21 centers, median age 68
- 📐 40 Gy in 5 fx (8 Gy × 5), noncoplanar robotic platform, real-time motion mgmt; ≈100 Gy EQD2 (a/b=2)
- 🔍 No androgen suppression allowed → isolates the SBRT effect, no ADT confounding cancer control
- 🔗 vs PACE-B: SBRT noninferior to conventional EBRT at 5 yr; this extends single-arm durability + late toxicity to 10 yr
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomized comparator; physician-reported (not patient-reported) toxicity likely under-captures late GU
- ⚠️ Treated 2008-2010; predates routine MRI staging and modern planning, and biochemical failure scored as nadir+2

### American Radium Society AUC: Intraprostatic Radiorecurrence Workup/Treatment

> ARS consensus: PSMA PET + mpMRI staging, mandatory pre-salvage biopsy, and salvage reirradiation in ≤6 fractions for intraprostatic radiorecurrence after definitive RT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT-actionable core: salvage reirradiation is preferred over other local salvage to limit toxicity, and most regimens run in ≤6 fractions. Radiosensitizing ADT should be short-course classic ADT, not ARSIs. Gating caveat: the evidence is all conventionally fractionated EBRT with pre-PSMA-PET-era workup, so primary-SBRT recurrences sit outside it.

- 🔍 ARS GU AUC panel; modified Delphi + RAND consensus across 4 topics: staging, biopsy, local salvage, hormonal therapy
- PSMA PET + mpMRI recommended to exclude mets and localize the recurrence before salvage
- Biopsy required before local salvage; avoids treating a radiographic treatment-effect
- Local salvage preferred over noncurative hormonal manipulation alone, shared decision-making critical
- Salvage reirradiation is the preferred local salvage approach to limit toxicity; most regimens ≤6 fractions
- 💊 Hormonal therapy for salvage radiosensitization
  - Only if radiotherapeutic salvage is pursued, and only short duration
  - Classic ADT preferred over novel hormonal agents (ARSIs)
- Salvage extent
  - Focal salvage when focal recurrence confirmed by multiple imaging + tissue modalities
  - Whole-gland salvage toxicity reported as very tolerable
- 📊 FORECAST diagnostic accuracy for radiorecurrence (systematic biopsy reference)

  | Analysis | Sensitivity | Specificity |
  |---|---|---|
  | mpMRI (n=155) | 94% (88-98%) | 18% (7-35%) |
  | MRI-targeted biopsy (n=87) | 92% (83-97%) | 75% (45-92%) |

- 🔗 RTOG 9408 post-hoc: positive post-RT biopsy → higher BCR, HR 1.7 (n=831), independent of upfront ADT
- 🔗 Liu et al: PSMA PET vs conventional imaging concordant in only 56% of 79 radiorecurrent men; 34% upstaged, 10% downstaged
- ⚠️ Evidence base limited to conventionally fractionated EBRT with pre-PSMA PET-era workup

### NRG-GU005

> Co-primary DFS not superior for SBRT (futility, HR 1.38) but bowel QoL co-primary met (2yr MCID 35% vs 44%, p=0.034) vs MH-IMRT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is QoL, not control: SBRT met the bowel co-primary (longitudinal LSM 2.68, p=0.0016) and lowered G≥3 GU (0.6% vs 2.5%) and rectal hemorrhage (10.5% vs 17.3%), but DFS superiority failed and 3-yr biochemical failure trended higher (8% vs 4%). The case for SBRT here is convenience and toxicity, not disease control.

- 📊 Oncologic outcomes, SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | Endpoint | SBRT | MH-IMRT | Effect |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | 5-yr DFS | 89% (85-92) | 92% (89-95) | HR 1.38 (0.91-2.09), p=0.13 |
  | 3-yr biochemical failure | 8% (5.2-11.0) | 4% (2.3-7.0) | p=0.037 |
  | 5-yr OS | 91% (85-95) | 94% (90-97) | p=0.66 |

- ⚠️ No oncologic advantage for SBRT: DFS superiority not met (interim futility), 3-yr biochemical failure trended toward IMRT
- 📊 Bowel QoL co-primary met: longitudinal effect LSM 2.68 (95% CI 1.02-4.34), p=0.0016 favoring SBRT
- 📊 Minimal clinically important decline in EPIC-26 domains, SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | Domain (timepoint) | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Bowel (1 yr) | 33% | 46% | 0.002 |
  | Bowel (2 yr) | 35% | 44% | 0.034 |
  | Sexual (1 yr) | 34% | 44% | 0.026 |
  | Urinary incontinence (2 yr) | 26% | 35% | 0.023 |
  | Urinary irritation/obstr (2 yr) | 35% | 34% | 0.68 |

- 📊 Investigator-reported toxicity, SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | AE | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | G≥3 GU | 0.6% | 2.5% | 0.04 |
  | Rectal hemorrhage (any grade) | 10.5% | 17.3% | 0.01 |
  | Fatigue (any grade) | 39.2% | 50.8% | 0.0025 |

- 🔍 Phase III non-blinded, N=698 (353 SBRT, 345 MH-IMRT); localized intermediate-risk, median age 68
- 🔍 SBRT 36.25 Gy/5fx (7.25 Gy/fx, 2-3×/wk) vs MH-IMRT 70/28 or 60/20; CTV prostate ±1cm proximal SV
- 🔍 Rectal spacer in ~55% both arms; associated with better bowel domain (LSM -2.81, p=0.0011)
- 🔗 vs PACE-B: both show lower urinary-incontinence QoL decline with SBRT vs MH-IMRT
