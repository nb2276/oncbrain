# 2026-07-08

**NRG-GU005: 5-fx prostate SBRT not superior to MH-IMRT for DFS (HR 1.38, futility), but bowel-QoL MCID 35% vs 44% (p=0.034) favors SBRT.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate SBRT carried the day. NRG-GU005 (phase III) missed DFS superiority over moderate hypofrac but delivered less bowel/GU/incontinence QoL decline, echoed by a 10-yr nonrandomized series (90% RFS, late G3 GI/GU ≤1.5%, no ADT). Two consensus docs round it out: ARS AUC for intraprostatic radiorecurrence salvage and ESTRO post-op oral-cavity CTV delineation.

## 👄 Head & Neck

First ESTRO consensus method for post-op oral-cavity CTV delineation, aimed at the wide institutional variation in PORT volumes.

### ESTRO Post-op Oral Cavity CTV Guidelines

> Post-op primary CTV-P = (GTV-P pre-op +10mm) composited with (surgical defect/flap +5mm); first consensus method to standardize oral-cavity PORT delineation.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The contouring change is dual-surrogate: build CTV-P from BOTH the re-created pre-op GTV-P (+10mm) AND the surgical defect/flap (+5mm), not the defect alone, which the buccal case shows misses superiorly (infratemporal fossa). Geometric margins beat anatomical for conformality and late dysphagia (DAHANCA); example dose stays EQD2 60Gy.

- 🔍 First ESTRO consensus guideline for post-op CTV delineation in oral cavity SCC (OCSCC); fills a gap driving wide individual/institutional/national variation in PORT volumes
- 🔍 CTV-P construction (composite of two surrogate expansions)
  - Re-create surrogate GTV-P pre-op on planning CT via diagnostic MRI/CT registration or anatomical landmarks
  - GTV-P pre-op + 10mm isotropic margin
  - Surgical defect/flap + 5mm isotropic margin
  - CTV-P = union of both, edited for air, bone, teeth and anatomical barriers
- 📐 Nodal: CTV-N = surrogate GTV-N + involved level (CTV-N1); separate prophylactic-dose CTV-N2 for at-risk levels, plus uni-/bilateral neck decision rules
- 💊 Prescription in worked example: PTV/CTV-P EQD2 60Gy (2Gy fractions)
- 🔗 Adapts the 2018 definitive-RT '5mm+5mm' geometric-expansion CTV concept to the post-op setting where no residual GTV remains
- 🔗 DAHANCA: geometric (vs anatomical) GTV→CTV margins yield more conformal CTVs, less open to misinterpretation
- 🔗 Anatomical→geometric margin switch correlated with lower late dysphagia (unplanned post-hoc analysis)
- ⚠️ Consensus expert opinion, not outcome-validated; geometric margins not tested against recurrence patterns or a local-control endpoint

> [!question] Open questions
> - Extension to other head/neck subsites (larynx, oropharynx, hypopharynx)
> - Validation of geometric margins against recurrence patterns
> - Impact on inter-observer variability in prospective audit

## 🌰 Prostate

Three radiation-forward reads: a phase III hypofractionation QoL readout, 10-yr SBRT durability data, and a salvage-reirradiation AUC for radiorecurrence.

### ARS AUC: Intraprostatic Recurrence After RT

> Consensus AUC: PSMA PET + mpMRI + confirmatory biopsy before salvage reirradiation; short classic ADT preferred over ARSI, most regimens ≤6 fractions.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The operational read: confirmatory biopsy is mandatory before salvage (radiographic recurrence can be pure treatment effect), classic short-course ADT beats ARSI for radiosensitization, and whole-gland salvage toxicity is called very tolerable with most regimens ≤6 fractions. Caveat: evidence is conventional-EBRT-only, pre-PSMA-PET era, so transfer to post-SBRT recurrence is untested.

- 🔍 ARS GU Appropriate Use Criteria consensus (modified Delphi, RAND); four topics across workup + local salvage of intraprostatic radiorecurrence
- 🔍 Workup: PSMA PET + mpMRI to exclude mets and map local extent; biopsy required before salvage (radiographic recurrence may be treatment effect)
- 📊 FORECAST — mpMRI vs MRI-targeted biopsy for radiorecurrence detection (systematic biopsy reference)

  | Test (n) | Sensitivity | Specificity |
  |---|---|---|
  | mpMRI (n=155) | 94% (95% CI 88-98%) | 18% (95% CI 7-35%) |
  | MRI-targeted biopsy (n=87) | 92% (95% CI 83-97%) | 75% (95% CI 45-92%) |

- 📊 PSMA PET restaging (Liu, n=79 exclusively radiorecurrent): conventional vs PET concordant 56%, 34% upstaged, 10% downstaged
- 📊 RTOG 9408 post-hoc (n=831 post-RT biopsy): positive biopsy → higher BCR, HR 1.7, independent of upfront ADT
- 💊 Short-course classic ADT preferred over novel hormonal agents (ARSI) for radiosensitization when salvage RT pursued
- 📊 Local salvage preferred over noncurative hormonal manipulation alone; most reirradiation regimens ≤6 fractions
- 🔍 Focal salvage when focal recurrence confirmed via multiple imaging + tissue modalities; whole-gland salvage toxicity called very tolerable
- ⚠️ Evidence limited to conventionally fractionated EBRT and pre-PSMA-PET-era workup; prospective data awaited

**Sources:**
- 📄 **American Radium Society Appropriate Use Criteria for the Workup and Treatment of Local Intraprostatic Recurrence of Prostate Cancer Following Definitive Radiotherapy** — Valle, Luca F.; Jiang, Tommy; Rosenbloom, Ashton et al.. *European Urology Oncology* (2025-06). [doi:10.1016/j.euo.2024.09.005](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.euo.2024.09.005)
  - 📎 [[papers/prostate/american-radium-society-appropriate-use-criteria-for-the-workup.pdf|ARS AUC: Intraprostatic Recurrence After RT (full text)]]

> [!question] Open questions
> - Optimal salvage reirradiation modality: brachytherapy vs SBRT vs re-EBRT
> - Salvage outcomes after primary SBRT or hypofractionation, not just conventional EBRT
> - Focal vs whole-gland salvage: comparative toxicity and local control

### 10-yr SBRT for Prostate Cancer (Nonrandomized Trial)

> 10-yr RFS 90% (94% LR, 86% IR), OS 84%, late G3+ GI/GU ≤1.5% after 40Gy/5fx prostate SBRT, no ADT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The unfavorable-IR read is the additive one: 10-yr RFS 77% vs 92% in favorable IR, below the 86% pooled IR figure. With no ADT and 40Gy/5fx (≈100Gy EQD2), this backs SBRT monotherapy for favorable IR but leaves unfavorable IR as the subgroup where durability without intensification stays open.

- 📊 10-yr relapse-free survival 90% overall
- 📊 10-yr overall survival 84%
- 📊 10-yr relapse-free survival by risk group

  | Cohort | 10-yr RFS |
  |---|---|
  | Low-risk | 94% |
  | Intermediate-risk | 86% |
  | Favorable IR | 92% |
  | Unfavorable IR | 77% |
  | Overall | 90% |

- 🔍 Phase 2 nonrandomized, N=310 (172 low-risk, 138 intermediate-risk) across 21 centers, no ADT
- 🔍 Median follow-up 9 yr, median age 68; central pathologic review
- 📐 40 Gy/5fx (8 Gy ×5, ≈100 Gy EQD2 at a/b=2), noncoplanar robotic platform, real-time motion tracking
- 📊 Late toxicity, 10-yr cumulative (CTCAE v3, physician-reported)

  | Endpoint | Rate |
  |---|---|
  | Grade 3 GI/GU, low-risk | 1.4% |
  | Grade 3 GI/GU, intermediate-risk | 1.5% |
  | Grade 4-5 | none |
  | Grade 2+ GI | 2.1% |
  | Grade 2+ GU | 14% |

- 🔗 PACE-B: SBRT noninferior to conventional EBRT at 5 yr; this extends durability to 10 yr
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomized comparator; risk-group RFS vs historical EBRT only

> [!question] Open questions
> - Durability in unfavorable intermediate-risk without ADT
> - 10-yr randomized SBRT vs conventional EBRT comparison
> - Whether ADT adds benefit for intermediate-risk prostate SBRT

### NRG-GU005

> Bowel QoL MCID decline 35% vs 44% (p=0.034) favoring 5-fx SBRT; but DFS superiority not met (HR 1.38, futility) vs MH-IMRT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The subtle signal under the QoL win: 3y biochemical failure ran higher with SBRT (8% vs 4%, p=0.037), though 5y DFS and OS were comparable. Net, 5-fraction SBRT holds oncologic control while cutting bowel-QoL decline and G≥3 GU toxicity (0.6% vs 2.5%), supporting it over 20-28 fraction MH-IMRT for intermediate-risk prostate.

- 🔍 Randomized non-blinded phase III, N=698 (353 SBRT, 345 MH-IMRT), localized intermediate-risk; co-primaries DFS + EPIC-26 bowel/urinary-irritation MCID at 24mo
- 🔍 RT technique
  - SBRT 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx, 2-3×/wk); MH-IMRT 70 Gy/28 fx or 60 Gy/20 fx
  - CTV = prostate ±1cm proximal seminal vesicles
  - SBRT CTV-PTV 5mm radial, 3mm posterior/anterior; MH-IMRT 8mm except 5mm posterior
  - Fiducials + MRI fusion + daily IGRT mandated
- 📊 DFS co-primary superiority NOT met: interim futility bound crossed; SBRT not superior to MH-IMRT for disease-free survival
- 📊 Oncologic outcomes, SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | Endpoint | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | 5y disease-free survival | 89% (85-92) | 92% (89-95) | n/a |
  | DFS hazard ratio | 1.38 (0.91-2.09) | ref | 0.13 |
  | 3y cumulative biochemical failure | 8% (5.2-11.0) | 4% (2.3-7.0) | 0.037 |
  | 5y overall survival | 91% (85-95) | 94% (90-97) | 0.66 |

- 📊 Longitudinal bowel domain favored SBRT: LS mean 2.68 (95% CI 1.02-4.34, p=0.0016)
- 📊 Co-primary QoL split: bowel MCID met (favors SBRT), urinary irritation/obstruction MCID not met (1 of 2 positive)
- 📊 EPIC-26 MCID decline by domain, SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | Domain (timepoint) | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Bowel, 2y (co-1°) | 35% | 44% | 0.034 |
  | Urinary irritation, 2y (co-1°) | 35% | 34% | 0.68 |
  | Urinary incontinence, 2y | 26% | 35% | 0.023 |
  | Bowel, 1y | 33% | 46% | 0.002 |
  | Sexual, 1y | 34% | 44% | 0.026 |

- 📊 Investigator-reported toxicity, SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | Toxicity | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | G≥3 GU | 0.6% | 2.5% | 0.04 |
  | Rectal hemorrhage, any grade | 10.5% | 17.3% | 0.01 |
  | Fatigue, any grade | 39.2% | 50.8% | 0.0025 |

- 🔗 vs PACE-B (other phase III of SBRT vs MH-IMRT): both trials show lower urinary-incontinence QoL decline with SBRT
- ⚠️ Superiority (not non-inferiority) design: futility means SBRT unproven-superior, not established equivalent
- ⚠️ Rectal spacer used ~55% both arms, independently improved EPIC bowel scores (LS mean -2.81, 95% CI -4.49 to -1.13, p=0.0011); partial confounder for bowel-QoL benefit
