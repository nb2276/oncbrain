# 2026-07-08

**NRG-GU005: 5y DFS superiority not met (HR 1.38, futility crossed) for prostate SBRT vs MH-IMRT, but SBRT cut 2y bowel-QoL decline (35% vs 44%, p=0.034).**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate carried the day, all through an RT lens: GU005 says 5-fx SBRT isn't more effective than moderate hypofx but is less toxic (G≥3 GU 0.6% vs 2.5%, better bowel QoL), while Meier's 10-yr single-arm adds durability (OS 84%, RFS 90%) for 40Gy/5fx, no ADT. Head-neck: first ESTRO post-op oral-cavity CTV consensus.

## 👄 Head & Neck

First ESTRO consensus for post-op oral-cavity SCC target delineation, built to cut inter-institutional CTV variation and enable trial RTQA.

### ESTRO Oral Cavity CTV Delineation Guidelines

> New consensus recipe for post-op CTV: recreate pre-op GTV-P +10mm composited with surgical defect/flap +5mm; standardizes OCSCC delineation at EQD2 60 Gy.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The recipe is asymmetric: 10mm around the recreated pre-op GTV-P but only 5mm around the surgical defect/flap, composited. Recreating the GTV-P pre-op (not relying on the defect alone) is the load-bearing step; one buccal case would have missed the infratemporal fossa otherwise. Standardizes OCSCC post-op contouring at EQD2 60 Gy.

- 🔍 First ESTRO consensus for post-op primary + nodal CTV delineation in oral cavity SCC (OCSCC); no prior systematic guidance existed
- 🔍 Method: ESTRO multidisciplinary expert panel, then second-tier international review (Japan, HK, Australia, Brazil, others) for global applicability
- 📐 Post-op target-volume recipe (composite geometric expansions)
  - CTV-P = (GTV-P pre-op + 10mm) + (surgical defect/flap + 5mm), edited for air/bone/teeth
  - CTV-N1 = surrogate GTV-N + involved nodal level (post-op dose)
  - CTV-N2 = prophylactic at-risk nodal levels; plus uni- vs bilateral neck decision
- 🔍 Surrogate GTV-P recreated on planning CT via MRI/CT registration or anatomical landmarks, since radical surgery leaves no residual GTV
- 📐 Example dose: CTV-P/PTV EQD2 60 Gy (2 Gy fractions)
- 🔗 Extends the 2018 primary-setting '5mm + 5mm' geometric-expansion CTV guidelines into the post-op setting
- ⚠️ Case examples: recreating GTV-P pre-op (not surgical defect alone) avoided geographical miss into the infratemporal fossa
- ⚠️ Expert consensus, not outcome-validated; goal is reduced inter-institutional variation + enabling RTQA in trials

[doi:10.1016/j.radonc.2025.111135](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.radonc.2025.111135)

## 🌰 Prostate

An all-radiation slate: NRG-GU005's randomized SBRT-vs-hypofx efficacy null (with a toxicity/QoL win), 10-yr single-arm SBRT durability, and ARS salvage-reirradiation criteria for radiorecurrence.

### 10-yr SBRT for Prostate Cancer (Meier Nonrandomized Trial)

> 10-yr OS 84%, RFS 90% (94% LR, 86% IR); late G3 GU/GI ≤1.5%, no G4-5; 40Gy/5fx SBRT, no ADT, 21 centers

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The unfavorable-IR subgroup is the read: 10-yr RFS 77% vs 92% favorable-IR, so SBRT monotherapy without ADT looks adequate for LR/favorable-IR but leaves unfavorable-IR pts short. 40Gy/5fx transfers directly to practice. Late GU G2+ 14% (vs GI 2.1%) is the toxicity to counsel.

- 🔍 Phase 2 nonrandomized single-arm, N=310 evaluable across 21 community/regional/academic centers; median f/u 9 yr
- 🔍 172 low-risk (T1b-T2a, GS6, PSA≤10), 138 intermediate-risk (GS7 PSA≤10, or GS6 PSA 10-20); median age 68
- 📐 40 Gy/5fx (8Gy×5, ~100 Gy EQD2 at α/β=2) on noncoplanar robotic platform with real-time motion tracking
- 🔍 ADT not allowed; prostate volumes up to 100 cc eligible; treated 2008-2010
- 📊 10-yr OS 84%
- 📊 10-yr relapse-free survival by risk group

  | Subgroup | 10-yr RFS |
  |---|---|
  | Overall | 90% |
  | Low-risk | 94% |
  | Intermediate-risk | 86% |
  | Favorable IR | 92% |
  | Unfavorable IR | 77% |

- 📊 10-yr late toxicity (physician-reported CTCAE v3)
  - G3 GI/GU: 1.4% LR, 1.5% IR
  - No grade 4-5 events
  - G2+ GI 2.1%
  - G2+ GU 14%
- 🔗 PACE-B (5-yr) showed SBRT noninferior to conventional EBRT for relapse + late toxicity; this adds 10-yr durability, single-arm
- ⚠️ Single-arm nonrandomized; no comparator arm (PACE-B carries the randomized evidence)
- ⚠️ RFS folds any salvage/antiandrogen/systemic therapy into relapse; biochemical failure scored nadir+2

[doi:10.1016/j.eururo.2026.04.029](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.eururo.2026.04.029)
  - 📎 [[papers/prostate/10-yr-survival-and-toxicity-outcomes-of-stereotactic-body-radiot.pdf|10-yr SBRT for Prostate Cancer (Meier Nonrandomized Trial) (full text)]]

### American Radium Society AUC: Intraprostatic Recurrence After RT

> PSMA PET + mpMRI plus mandatory pre-salvage biopsy, then reirradiation (≤6 fx) over ADT alone, for radiorecurrent intraprostatic disease.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT-actionable read is workup discipline: mandatory biopsy before salvage (radiographic recurrence is often just treatment effect) and PSMA PET + mpMRI to exclude nodal/distant disease first. Preferred salvage is reirradiation in ≤6 fx over ADT alone, with short-course classic ADT (not ARSIs) for radiosensitization.

- 🔍 ARS GU Appropriate Use Criteria; modified Delphi consensus across 4 topics: staging, biopsy, salvage modality, hormone therapy
- 🔍 Workup: PSMA PET + mpMRI to exclude metastatic disease and map local extent before salvage
- 🔍 Biopsy mandatory before local salvage; avoids treating radiographic change that is only treatment effect
- 📊 Salvage reirradiation preferred over noncurative ADT alone; most regimens ≤6 fractions
- 💊 Hormone therapy for radiosensitization only, short duration; classic ADT preferred over ARSIs
- 📊 Focal salvage when focal recurrence confirmed by imaging + tissue; whole-gland salvage toxicity called very tolerable
- 📊 FORECAST diagnostic performance (radiorecurrence detection)

  | Modality | Sensitivity (95% CI) | Specificity (95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | mpMRI (n=155) | 94% (88-98%) | 18% (7-35%) |
  | MRI-targeted biopsy (n=87) | 92% (83-97%) | 75% (45-92%) |

- 🔗 RTOG 9408 post hoc (n=831): positive post-RT biopsy → higher BCR, HR 1.7, independent of upfront ADT
- 🔗 Liu et al (n=79 radiorecurrent): PET vs conventional imaging concordant 56%; 34% upstaged, 10% downstaged
- ⚠️ Evidence base: conventionally fractionated EBRT only, workup pre-PSMA PET era; no randomized salvage trials

[doi:10.1016/j.euo.2024.09.005](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.euo.2024.09.005)
  - 📎 [[papers/prostate/american-radium-society-appropriate-use-criteria-for-the-workup.pdf|American Radium Society AUC: Intraprostatic Recurrence After RT (full text)]]

### NRG-GU005

> DFS superiority not met (HR 1.38, futility crossed); SBRT improved 2y bowel QoL (35% vs 44% MCID decline, p=0.034) vs MH-IMRT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The urinary-irritation co-primary SBRT was powered for came back flat (35% vs 34%, p=0.68); the QoL edge is bowel and incontinence, not irritation. DFS superiority failed and 3y biochemical failure ran numerically higher on SBRT (8% vs 4%), so the case for 5-fx SBRT is convenience and lower GU/bowel toxicity, not better control.

- 🔍 Phase III randomized non-blinded, N=698 (SBRT 353 / MH-IMRT 345), localized intermediate-risk prostate; co-primary DFS + EPIC-26 QoL
- 📐 SBRT 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx, 2-3×/wk) vs MH-IMRT 70 Gy/28 fx or 60 Gy/20 fx
- 🔍 CTV = prostate ±1 cm proximal SV; SBRT PTV 5 mm radial / 3 mm AP; fiducials + MRI fusion + daily IGRT mandated
- 📊 Oncologic outcomes, SBRT vs MH-IMRT (superiority not met)

  | Endpoint | SBRT | MH-IMRT | Effect / p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | 5y DFS | 89% (85-92%) | 92% (89-95%) | HR 1.38 (0.91-2.09), p=0.13 |
  | 3y cum. biochemical failure | 8% (5.2-11.0) | 4% (2.3-7.0) | p=0.037 |
  | 5y OS | 91% (85-95%) | 94% (90-97%) | p=0.66 |

- 📊 EPIC-26 MCID decline by domain (fewer = better QoL), SBRT vs MH-IMRT

  | Domain · MCID decline | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Bowel, 2y | 35% | 44% | 0.034 |
  | Urinary irritation/obstruction, 2y | 35% | 34% | 0.68 |
  | Bowel, 1y | 33% | 46% | 0.002 |
  | Sexual, 1y | 34% | 44% | 0.026 |
  | Urinary incontinence, 2y | 26% | 35% | 0.023 |

- 📊 Investigator-reported toxicity, any grade (all favor SBRT)

  | AE (any grade unless noted) | SBRT | MH-IMRT | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | G≥3 GU | 0.6% | 2.5% | 0.04 |
  | Rectal hemorrhage | 10.5% | 17.3% | 0.01 |
  | Fatigue | 39.2% | 50.8% | 0.0025 |

- ⚠️ Efficacy superiority NOT met: DFS futility bound crossed; SBRT numerically favored IMRT on DFS and 3y biochemical control
- 🔗 vs PACE-B: both trials show lower urinary-incontinence QoL decline with SBRT vs MH-IMRT
- Rectal spacer used in 56% SBRT / 55% IMRT; spacer → better EPIC bowel scores (LS mean -2.81, -4.49 to -1.13, p=0.0011)
