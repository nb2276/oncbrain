# 2026-06-09

**FIRESTORM: dose-escalated postop RT (BED ≥79.2 Gy) lifts 5yr PFS 65.8% vs 38.8% (HR 0.40) in high-risk meningioma, but radionecrosis doubles (33.9% vs 13.2%).**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Meningioma RT carries the day from both ends of the dose question: FIRESTORM shows escalation (BED ≥79.2 Gy) nearly doubles 5yr PFS in high-risk disease at a radionecrosis cost, while RTOG 0539's mature 10yr data backs observation for low-risk grade 1 yet flags high-risk PFS still poor (42.5%) at 60 Gy. In breast, RAPCHEM affirms response-adapted nodal RT after NAC (2.9% 10yr LRR).

## 🧠 CNS

Two meningioma RT studies frame the dose question from both ends: escalate for high-risk residual disease, de-escalate to observation for resected grade 1.

### FIRESTORM

> Dose-escalated postop RT improved 5yr PFS 65.8% vs 38.8% (MVA HR 0.40, 0.24-0.69) in high-risk meningioma; no OS benefit.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Dose escalation to BED ≥79.2 Gy (66 Gy/33fx equivalent) roughly halves progression (MVA HR 0.40), largest in Simpson 4-5 resections (5yr PFS 63.3% vs 38.4%) and HR 0.37 once prior-RT pts are excluded. The toxicity cost is any-grade radionecrosis (33.9% vs 13.2%), not severe RN (G3+ 5.1% vs 3.2%); the escalation decision turns on RN surveillance, since OS stayed flat.

- 🔍 Design + cohort
  - IPD-level meta-analysis, 7 institutions, N=248 (59 DE-RT vs 189 SD-RT), non-randomized
  - 75.8% WHO grade 2 (rest grade 3), 41.5% recurrent
  - 75.2% subtotal resection
- 📐 DE-RT defined as BED ≥79.2 Gy (66 Gy/33fx equivalent) vs standard-dose postop RT
- 📊 PFS rates: DE-RT vs SD-RT

  | PFS | DE-RT | SD-RT |
  |---|---|---|
  | 3-year | 86.4% | 55.6% |
  | 5-year | 65.8% | 38.8% |

- 📐 PFS treatment effect (overall log-rank P=.0022)

  | Analysis | HR (95% CI) | P |
  |---|---|---|
  | MVA | 0.40 (0.24-0.69) | .001 |
  | IPTW | 0.45 (0.24-0.83) | .01 |

- 📊 OS: DE-RT not significantly better — comparison values omitted (cell value ".42" not verified in source)
- ⚠️ Radionecrosis cost of escalation

  | Radionecrosis | DE-RT | SD-RT | P |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Any grade | 33.9% (20/59) | 13.2% (25/189) | .001 |
  | Grade ≥3 | 5.1% | 3.2% | ns |

- 📊 DE-RT PFS benefit by subgroup
  - Simpson 4-5 resections (largest gain): 5yr 63.3% vs 38.4%, HR 0.55 (0.36-0.84), P=.006
  - Conventional-fx SD-RT, no prior RT: HR 0.37 (0.19-0.73), P=.004
  - Mixed carbon/photon vs photon-alone DE-RT: HR 0.36 (0.11-1.08), P=.067 (ns)
- 🔗 SD-RT comparator was conventional 59.4 Gy/33fx or 60 Gy/30fx (per source); benefit came from pushing BED ≥79.2 Gy above standard adjuvant dose
- ⚠️ Non-randomized: DE-RT allocation by indication/institution, residual confounding despite IPTW
- ⚠️ PFS gain without OS signal (UVA P=.056, ns adjusted); could reflect salvage at progression or selection

### NRG Oncology RTOG 0539 · [[NCT00895622]]

> Risk-adapted RT: 10yr PFS 85.2% low-risk (observed), 72.2% intermediate (54Gy), 42.5% high-risk (60Gy); supports observation for low-risk grade 1.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The dose/risk split is the actionable read: 54Gy/30fx held intermediate-risk to 21.2% 10yr progression, but 60Gy left high-risk at 39.3% (42.5% PFS), inadequate local control for grade 3 / recurrent disease. Low-risk observation (85.2% PFS) validates RT omission after GTR/STR. Sets the benchmark ROAM and NRG-BN003 will test.

- 🔍 Prospective phase 2 (NRG/RTOG), risk-adapted RT for WHO grade 1-3 meningioma; central path review; 165 treated of 244 consented
- 🔍 Risk-adapted assignment (grade + extent of resection + recurrence)
  - Low (n=60): grade 1 post-GTR/STR → observation
  - Intermediate (n=52): recurrent grade 1 or new grade 2 post-GTR → 54Gy/30fx
  - High (n=53): new grade 2 post-STR, grade 3, or recurrent grade 2/3 → 60Gy/30fx
- 📊 10yr outcomes by risk cohort (KM PFS/OS; competing-risk progression)

  | Cohort | 10yr PFS | 10yr OS | 10yr cum. incidence prog. |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Low-risk (observed) | 85.2% | 94.1% | 8.9% (3.2-18.2) |
  | Intermediate (54Gy) | 72.2% | 84.7% | 21.2% (10.8-33.9) |
  | High-risk (60Gy) | 42.5% | 51.1% | 39.3% (25.8-52.5) |

- 📐 Mature update: median f/u 12.1/12.0/11.1 yrs; long-term extension of the original 3yr PFS primary endpoint
- ⚠️ Late grade 3+ toxicity attributed to RT (low-risk untreated)
  - Intermediate (54Gy): 9.6% (5/52)
  - High-risk (60Gy): 15.1% (8/53)
- ⚠️ Non-randomized: each cohort single-arm, no RT-vs-observation comparator for grade 2, so RT's causal effect isn't isolated
- ⚠️ High-risk PFS poor despite 60Gy/30fx; durable local control unmet, supports dose escalation or systemic intensification trials
- 🔗 Sets the benchmark for randomized grade-2 trials (ROAM/EORTC-1308, NRG-BN003: observation vs adjuvant RT post-GTR)

**Sources:**
- 📄 **Long-Term Analysis of NRG Oncology RTOG 0539: A Phase II Trial of Observation for Low-Risk Meningioma and Radiotherapy for Intermediate- and High-Risk Meningioma** — Kotecha, Rupesh; Polley, Mei-Yin; Vogelbaum, Michael A. et al.. *Journal of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/jco-25-01441](https://doi.org/10.1200/jco-25-01441)
  > NRG Oncology RTOG 0539 was a prospective phase II trial of risk-adapted radiotherapy for patients with WHO grade 1-3 meningioma. Low-risk (group 1, n = 60) was defined as a grade 1 tumor after gross total resection or subtotal resection (GTR/STR) and prospectively monitored. Intermediate-risk (group 2, n = 52) was defined as recurrent grade 1 or newly diagnosed grade 2 tumor after GTR and treated with radiotherapy (54 Gy). High-risk (group 3, n = 53) included a newly diagnosed grade 2 tumor after STR, newly diagnosed grade 3 tumor, or recurrent grade 2 or 3 tumor and treated with radiotherapy (60 Gy). Progression-free survival (PFS) and overall survival (OS) were estimated using the Kaplan-Meier method. The median follow-up times for the low-, intermediate-, and high-risk cohorts were 12.1, 12.0, and 11.1 years, respectively. The 10-year PFS and OS rates for the low-, intermediate-, and high-risk cohorts were 85.2% and 94.1%, 72.2% and 84.7%, and 42.5% and 51.1%, respectively. Five patients (9.6%) and eight patients (15.1%) had a grade 3+ toxicity attributed to radiotherapy in the intermediate- and high-risk cohorts, respectively. The long-term outcomes using this risk-adapted approach support observation for low-risk patients, inform radiotherapy patient selection and practice standards for intermediate- and high-risk patients, and provide comparative benchmarks for future trials.

## 🎀 Breast

Response-adapted locoregional RT after NAC keeps 10yr LRR low, but with no within-study no-RT comparator the de-escalation safety verdict awaits a pending RCT.

### RAPCHEM (BOOG 2010-03)

> 10-yr isolated locoregional recurrence 2.9% (24/838) with RT extent tailored to nodal response after NAC; ypN0 got breast-only or no RT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Actionable RT read: regional nodal irradiation was omitted in intermediate-risk (1-3 ypN+) pts and post-mastectomy RT omitted entirely in node-negative responders, yet 10-yr isolated locoregional recurrence stayed ≤3.2% across all groups. Supports response-adapted nodal de-escalation after NAC, pending the US NSABP RCT.

- 📊 Overall 10-yr isolated locoregional recurrence 2.9% (24/838 completing f/u)
- 📊 RT field and 10-yr locoregional recurrence by post-NAC risk group

  | Risk group | RT field | n | 10-yr LRR |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Low | Breast only (BCS); none if mastectomy | 291 | 2.4% (7) |
  | Intermediate | Breast/chest wall, regional nodes spared | 370 | 3.2% (12) |
  | High | Breast/CW + regional nodes | 177 | 2.8% (5) |

- 🔍 Risk by post-NAC nodal status: low = node-negative, intermediate = 1-3 positive, high = ≥4 positive nodes
- 🔍 Prospective response-adapted cohort, N=848, 17 Dutch centers, 2011-2015, 10-yr follow-up
- 🔍 Eligible: breast tumor <5cm, cN1 (1-3 positive nodes), all received NAC then surgery
- ⚠️ No within-study RT vs no-RT comparison, so low LRR can't be attributed to de-escalation over favourable biology
- ⚠️ Most pts underwent ALND, now largely replaced by SLNB; generalizability to current axillary practice limited
- 🔗 Authors defer the final safety verdict to a pending US NSABP RCT ([[NCT01872975]]) comparing RT vs no RT, due ~3yr
