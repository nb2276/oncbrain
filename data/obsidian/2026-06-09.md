# 2026-06-09

**FIRESTORM: dose-escalated RT (BED ≥79.2 Gy) 5yr PFS 65.8% vs 38.8%, HR 0.40, in high-risk meningioma; no OS gain.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Meningioma RT carried the day: FIRESTORM's dose escalation (HR 0.40) and RTOG 0539's mature 12yr risk-adapted PFS both argue for titrating RT intensity to risk, though neither moves OS. In breast, RAPCHEM backs response-adapted RNI omission after NAC (10yr LRR 2.9%).

## 🧠 CNS

Two meningioma cohorts converge on risk- and dose-stratified RT: RTOG 0539 (prospective, 12yr f/u) and FIRESTORM (dose escalation in high-risk, non-randomized IPD).

### RTOG 0539 · [[NCT00895622]]

> 10yr PFS 85.2%/72.2%/42.5% for low (observed)/intermediate (54Gy)/high (60Gy)-risk meningioma; mature 12yr f/u supports risk-adapted RT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The actionable read is intermediate-risk: GTR grade 2 or recurrent grade 1 to 54Gy/30fx reached 72.2% 10yr PFS, but with no observation control RT's absolute benefit stays unquantified. High-risk 42.5% PFS at 60Gy marks the current ceiling, a benchmark for dose-escalation trials.

- 📊 10-year outcomes by risk cohort

  | Cohort | Mgmt | 10y PFS | 10y OS |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Low (n=60) | Observation | 85.2% | 94.1% |
  | Intermediate (n=52) | RT 54 Gy | 72.2% | 84.7% |
  | High (n=53) | RT 60 Gy | 42.5% | 51.1% |

- 📊 10yr cumulative incidence of progression (competing-risk, death censored)
  - Low (observed): 8.9% (3.2-18.2)
  - Intermediate (54Gy): 21.2% (10.8-33.9)
  - High (60Gy): 39.3% (25.8-52.5)
- 🔍 Prospective phase 2 (NRG/RTOG cooperative group), risk-adapted; 244 consented, 165 protocol-treated
- 🔍 Risk group set by WHO grade, resection extent (GTR/STR), recurrence status; central path review
- 📐 30 fractions in both RT cohorts (54 Gy intermediate, 60 Gy high)
- ⚠️ Grade 3+ RT-attributed toxicity
  - Intermediate (54Gy): 9.6% (5/52)
  - High (60Gy): 15.1% (8/53)
- 🔍 Median f/u 12.1 / 12.0 / 11.1 yrs (low/intermediate/high); median PFS not reached low + intermediate
- ⚠️ No randomized observation control in the RT cohorts, so RT's absolute benefit over surgery alone isn't isolable

Low-risk (group 1, n = 60) was defined as a grade 1 tumor after gross total resection or subtotal resection (GTR/STR) and prospectively monitored. ### FIRESTORM

> DE-RT (BED ≥79.2 Gy) improved 5yr PFS 65.8% vs 38.8%, HR 0.40 (0.24-0.69), in high-risk meningioma; no OS benefit.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Dose is the transferable read: DE-RT meant BED ≥79.2 Gy (≈66 Gy/33fx) vs standard 59.4-60 Gy, and the largest PFS gain sat in the STR/Simpson 4-5 subgroup (HR 0.55, 0.36-0.84), the ~75% a radonc actually treats. Any-grade radionecrosis rose (33.9% vs 13.2%) while G3+ stayed low (5.1% vs 3.2%). Moves the postop dose-escalation question, not the OS case.

- 📊 1° endpoint PFS favored DE-RT (BED ≥79.2 Gy, ≈66 Gy/33fx) over SD-RT, log-rank P=.0022
- 📊 PFS rates, DE-RT vs SD-RT

  | Timepoint | DE-RT | SD-RT |
  |---|---|---|
  | 3-yr PFS | 86.4% | 55.6% |
  | 5-yr PFS | 65.8% | 38.8% |

- 📐 Adjusted effect: MVA HR 0.40 (0.24-0.69), P=.001; IPTW HR 0.45 (0.24-0.83), P=.01
- 🔍 Individual patient-level meta-analysis, 7 institutions, N=248 (59 DE-RT, 189 SD-RT); non-randomized, propensity/IPTW-adjusted
- 🔍 Population: 75.8% WHO grade 2, 41.5% recurrent, 75.2% subtotal resection
- 🔗 Largest gain in STR/Simpson 4-5: HR 0.55 (0.36-0.84), P=.006; 5yr PFS 63.3% vs 38.4%
- 🔍 Held on sensitivity (SD-RT limited to 59.4-60 Gy, prior-RT excluded): HR 0.37 (0.19-0.73), P=.004
- ⚠️ CNS radionecrosis by grade (any-grade P=.001)

  | Radionecrosis | DE-RT | SD-RT |
  |---|---|---|
  | Any grade | 33.9% (20/59) | 13.2% (25/189) |
  | Grade ≥3 | 5.1% | 3.2% |

- ⚠️ No OS benefit: DE-RT not significantly associated with OS (MVA HR 0.66, 0.33-1.34; IPTW HR 0.77, 0.41-1.45), both NS

- 📄 [[PMID 41956162]] **Improved Progression-Free Survival Following Dose-Escalated Versus Standard-Dose Postoperative Radiation Therapy for High-Risk Meningiomas: An International Multicenter Individual Patient–Level Meta-Analysis (FIRESTORM)** — Singh, Raj; Koempel, Andrew; French, Beck et al.. *International Journal of Radiation Oncology*Biology*Physics* (2026-07). [doi:10.1016/j.ijrobp.2026.03.036](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijrobp.2026.03.036)
  > PURPOSE: We performed an individual patient-level meta-analysis of high-risk meningiomas to compare the outcomes of dose-escalated radiation therapy (DE-RT) versus standard-dose postoperative radiation therapy (SD-RT).
  > METHODS AND MATERIALS: A total of 7 institutions participated. DE-RT was defined as treatment with a biologically effective dose of &#x2265;79.2 Gy (equivalent of 66 Gy in 33 fractions). We compared progression-free survival (PFS) with DE-RT versus SD-RT via Kaplan-Meier analysis and log-rank t tests, a Cox proportional hazards multivariable model, and propensity score analyses with inverse probability of treatment weighting (IPTW). We also compared incidences of central nervous system radionecrosis (RN) with DE-RT versus SD-RT.
  > RESULTS: The analysis included 248 patients with high-risk meningioma (59 received DE-RT and 189 received SD-RT). One hundred and eighty-eight cases (75.8%) were World Health Organization grade 2, and 103 cases (41.5%) were recurrent meningiomas. Extent of resection was subtotal resection in 182 of 248 (75.2%). Three- and 5-year PFS rates were 62.8% (95% CI, 55.8%-69.0%) and 45.0% (95% CI, 37.3%-52.3%), respectively. DE-RT was associated with superior PFS rates (P = .0022), with 3-year (86.4% vs 55.6%) and 5-year (65.8% vs 38.8%) PFS rates favoring DE-RT. On multivariable analysis, DE-RT was associated with superior PFS (hazard ratio, 0.40; 95% CI, 0.24-0.69; P = .001). On IPTW, DE-RT continued to be associated with superior PFS (hazard ratio, 0.45; 95% CI, 0.24-0.83; P = .01). A greater incidence of any grade RN was observed following DE-RT (20 of 59; 33.9%) versus SD-RT (25 of 189; 13.2%) (P = .001) but with similar grade 3 or greater RN events (DE-RT 5.1% vs SD-RT 3.2%).
  > CONCLUSIONS: DE-RT resulted in superior PFS for patients with high-risk meningiomas over SD-RT without an increase in severe toxicities.

## 🎀 Breast

RAPCHEM tests RT de-escalation by post-NAC nodal response, including RNI omission; descriptive cohort, no randomized comparator yet.

### RAPCHEM (BOOG 2010-03)

> 10yr LRR 2.9% (24/838) with RT de-escalated by nodal response to NAC; ≤3.2% across all risk groups, including RT omission in ypN0.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read: nodal-response-guided de-escalation held up at 10yr. Intermediate-risk pts (ypN 1-3) had regional nodal irradiation omitted yet LRR was 3.2%; low-risk ypN0 got breast-only RT or none post-mastectomy at 2.4%. Moves the RNI-omission decision in good NAC responders (most had ALND).

- 📊 10yr locoregional recurrence by post-NAC nodal risk group (overall 2.9%, 24/838)

  | Risk group | RT approach | 10yr LRR |
  |---|---|---|
  | Low (node-neg post-NAC) | Breast RT if BCS, none if mastectomy | 2.4% (7/291) |
  | Intermediate | Breast/chest wall, RNI omitted | 3.2% (12/370) |
  | High | Breast/chest wall + RNI | 2.8% (5/177) |

- 🔍 Prospective cohort, N=848, 17 Dutch centers (2011-2015); cT<5cm cN1 breast, neoadjuvant chemo then surgery
- 🔍 Risk set by residual nodal disease after NAC: node-negative (low), 1-3 positive (intermediate), ≥4 positive (high)
- ⚠️ No RT-vs-no-RT comparator; single descriptive cohort, can't establish de-escalation non-inferiority
- ⚠️ Low LRR partly reflects favorable biology of good chemo responders, not proof RT was safely cut
- ⚠️ Most pts had ALND; less used now (SLNB era), limits transfer to current axillary staging
- 🔗 Awaits NSABP randomized trial ([[NCT01872975]]), results ~3yr, the confirmatory de-escalation RCT
