# 2026-06-09

**FIRESTORM: dose-escalated postop RT (BED ≥79.2Gy) lifts 3-yr PFS to 86.4% vs 55.6% in high-risk meningioma (MVA HR 0.40), no OS gain.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Three risk-calibrated RT studies, split between escalation and omission. FIRESTORM escalates high-risk meningioma dose (3-yr PFS 86.4 vs 55.6%, HR 0.40) at 2.5x radionecrosis and no OS gain; RTOG 0539 sets mature 10-yr risk-adapted benchmarks. RAPCHEM de-escalates regional nodal RT after NAC (2.9% 10-yr LRR), uncontrolled.

## 🎀 Breast

Lone breast entry is a de-escalation signal: regional nodal RT omitted by nodal response after NAC, but uncontrolled and pending the randomized NSABP test (~3yr out).

### RAPCHEM

> 10-yr locoregional recurrence 2.9% (24/838) with risk-adapted RT after neoadjuvant chemo; regional nodes omitted in pts with 1-3 residual nodes.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Risk-adapted by post-chemo nodal response, not baseline stage: pts with 1-3 residual nodes (ypN1) got breast/chest-wall RT with regional nodes OMITTED and reached 3.2% 10-yr LRR; ypN0 post-mastectomy pts received no RT (within the 2.4% low-risk stratum). Moves the RNI-omission decision, though most had ALND.

- 🔍 Prospective cohort (BOOG 2010-03), N=848, 17 Dutch centers 2011-2015; RT tailored to nodal response after neoadjuvant chemo + surgery
- 🔍 Eligibility: breast tumor <5cm with 1-3 clinically positive nodes (cN1); most underwent axillary lymph node dissection
- 📊 10-yr locoregional recurrence by risk stratum (RT received)

  | Risk stratum | RT received | 10-yr LRR |
  |---|---|---|
  | Low: no residual nodal disease | Breast RT (BCS) / no RT (mastectomy) | 7/291 (2.4%) |
  | Intermediate: one to three nodes | Breast/chest wall RT, no regional nodes | 12/370 (3.2%) |
  | High: four or more nodes | Breast/chest wall + regional nodal RT | 5/177 (2.8%) |

- 📊 Overall 10-yr locoregional recurrence 24/838 (2.9%), all without distant disease
- ⚠️ No comparator: study did not compare with vs without RT, so de-escalation non-inferiority is unproven
- ⚠️ Most had ALND (now less common); limits transfer to SLNB-era axillary de-escalation
- 🔗 NSABP randomized trial ([[NCT01872975]]) is the pending definitive test of regional nodal RT after NAC; results expected ~3yr per authors

## 🧠 CNS

Both entries are postop meningioma RT: FIRESTORM tests dose escalation above the ~60Gy standard that RTOG 0539 benchmarks; neither is randomized.

### RTOG 0539 · [[NCT00895622]]

> 10-yr PFS/OS 85/94% low-risk (obs), 72/85% intermediate (54Gy), 43/51% high-risk (60Gy); mature risk-adapted RT benchmarks.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The intermediate cohort (grade 2 post-GTR, 54 Gy) reaching 72.2% 10-yr PFS is the exact benchmark ROAM/EORTC-1308 tests against observation, so this validates the dose but not the indication. The 42.5% high-risk PFS at 60 Gy marks conventional-dose RT's ceiling and motivates escalation for grade 3 / recurrent disease.

- 🔍 Phase 2 prospective risk-adapted RT strategy (NRG/RTOG); 165 treated of 244 consented; centrally-confirmed pathology; [[NCT00895622]]
- 🔍 Risk groups (WHO grade × resection × recurrence)
  - Low (n=60): grade 1 post-GTR/STR → observed, no RT
  - Intermediate (n=52): recurrent grade 1 or new grade 2 post-GTR → 54 Gy
  - High (n=53): new grade 2 post-STR, grade 3, or recurrent grade 2/3 → 60 Gy
- 🔍 Adjuvant EBRT, both cohorts 30 fractions conventional (54 Gy intermediate, 60 Gy high-risk)
- 📊 10-yr outcomes by risk cohort

  | Cohort · RT | 10y PFS | 10y OS | 10y CI-prog |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Low · obs | 85.2% | 94.1% | 8.9% |
  | Interm · 54Gy | 72.2% | 84.7% | 21.2% |
  | High · 60Gy | 42.5% | 51.1% | 39.3% |

- 🔍 Median f/u 12.1/12.0/11.1 yrs; median PFS not reached for low- and intermediate-risk cohorts
- ⚠️ Grade 3+ toxicity attributed to RT
  - Intermediate (54 Gy): 9.6% (5/52)
  - High-risk (60 Gy): 15.1% (8/53)
- 🔗 Intermediate indication (grade 2 post-GTR → 54 Gy) is exactly what ROAM/EORTC-1308 randomizes vs observation
- ⚠️ Non-randomized, one cohort per risk tier; no internal observation control for irradiated groups, so RT benefit vs natural history is unproven
- ⚠️ Long-term analysis of a previously-reported 3-yr PFS primary; descriptive benchmarks, not a fresh hypothesis test

  > NRG Oncology RTOG 0539 was a prospective phase II trial of risk-adapted radiotherapy for patients with WHO grade 1-3 meningioma. Low-risk (group 1, n = 60) was defined as a grade 1 tumor after gross total resection or subtotal resection (GTR/STR) and prospectively monitored. Intermediate-risk (group 2, n = 52) was defined as recurrent grade 1 or newly diagnosed grade 2 tumor after GTR and treated with radiotherapy (54 Gy). High-risk (group 3, n = 53) included a newly diagnosed grade 2 tumor after STR, newly diagnosed grade 3 tumor, or recurrent grade 2 or 3 tumor and treated with radiotherapy (60 Gy). ### FIRESTORM

> 3-yr PFS 86.4% vs 55.6% with dose-escalated (BED ≥79.2Gy) vs standard-dose postop RT for high-risk meningioma; MVA HR 0.40, no OS benefit.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The PFS benefit is largest where residual disease is greatest: Simpson grade 4-5 resections showed 5-yr PFS 63.3% vs 38.4% (HR 0.55), and DE-RT held after excluding hypofractionated and prior-RT cases (HR 0.37). It moves the dose-escalation-above-60Gy decision, but OS was unchanged (HR 0.66, ns).

- 📊 PFS by dose group (DE-RT ≥79.2 Gy BED vs SD-RT)

  | Endpoint | DE-RT | SD-RT |
  |---|---|---|
  | 3-yr PFS | 86.4% | 55.6% |
  | 5-yr PFS | 65.8% | 38.8% |
  | MVA HR | 0.40 (0.24-0.69), p=.001 | ref |
  | IPTW HR | 0.45 (0.24-0.83), p=.01 | ref |

- 🔍 IPD meta-analysis, 7 institutions, N=248 (59 DE-RT, 189 SD-RT); non-randomized, propensity IPTW-adjusted
- 🔍 DE-RT = BED ≥79.2 Gy (66 Gy/33 fx eq); SD-RT = 59.4 Gy/33 fx or 60 Gy/30 fx
- 📊 Largest benefit in Simpson grade 4-5: 5-yr PFS 63.3% vs 38.4%, MVA HR 0.55 (0.36-0.84), p=.006
- ⚠️ Radionecrosis by dose group

  | Radionecrosis | DE-RT | SD-RT |
  |---|---|---|
  | Any grade | 33.9% (20/59) | 13.2% (25/189) |
  | Grade 3+ (severe) | 5.1% | 3.2% |

- ⚠️ No OS benefit: 5-yr OS 83.8% vs 68.4% (UVA p=.056); MVA HR 0.66 (0.33-1.34), p=.27
- ⚠️ Non-randomized dose comparison: DE-RT allocation prone to confounding by indication despite IPTW
- 🔗 Escalates beyond the ~60 Gy postop standard (RTOG 0539 high-risk arm, EORTC 22042); PFS-only, no randomized dose-escalation data yet

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 41956162]] **Improved Progression-Free Survival Following Dose-Escalated Versus Standard-Dose Postoperative Radiation Therapy for High-Risk Meningiomas: An International Multicenter Individual Patient–Level Meta-Analysis (FIRESTORM)** — Singh, Raj; Koempel, Andrew; French, Beck et al.. *International Journal of Radiation Oncology*Biology*Physics* (2026-07). [doi:10.1016/j.ijrobp.2026.03.036](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijrobp.2026.03.036)
  > PURPOSE: We performed an individual patient-level meta-analysis of high-risk meningiomas to compare the outcomes of dose-escalated radiation therapy (DE-RT) versus standard-dose postoperative radiation therapy (SD-RT).
  > METHODS AND MATERIALS: A total of 7 institutions participated. DE-RT was defined as treatment with a biologically effective dose of &#x2265;79.2 Gy (equivalent of 66 Gy in 33 fractions). We compared progression-free survival (PFS) with DE-RT versus SD-RT via Kaplan-Meier analysis and log-rank t tests, a Cox proportional hazards multivariable model, and propensity score analyses with inverse probability of treatment weighting (IPTW). We also compared incidences of central nervous system radionecrosis (RN) with DE-RT versus SD-RT.
  > RESULTS: The analysis included 248 patients with high-risk meningioma (59 received DE-RT and 189 received SD-RT). One hundred and eighty-eight cases (75.8%) were World Health Organization grade 2, and 103 cases (41.5%) were recurrent meningiomas. Extent of resection was subtotal resection in 182 of 248 (75.2%). Three- and 5-year PFS rates were 62.8% (95% CI, 55.8%-69.0%) and 45.0% (95% CI, 37.3%-52.3%), respectively. DE-RT was associated with superior PFS rates (P = .0022), with 3-year (86.4% vs 55.6%) and 5-year (65.8% vs 38.8%) PFS rates favoring DE-RT. On multivariable analysis, DE-RT was associated with superior PFS (hazard ratio, 0.40; 95% CI, 0.24-0.69; P = .001). On IPTW, DE-RT continued to be associated with superior PFS (hazard ratio, 0.45; 95% CI, 0.24-0.83; P = .01). A greater incidence of any grade RN was observed following DE-RT (20 of 59; 33.9%) versus SD-RT (25 of 189; 13.2%) (P = .001) but with similar grade 3 or greater RN events (DE-RT 5.1% vs SD-RT 3.2%).
  > CONCLUSIONS: DE-RT resulted in superior PFS for patients with high-risk meningiomas over SD-RT without an increase in severe toxicities.
