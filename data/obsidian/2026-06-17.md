### INDIBLADE

> 2y bladder-intact EFS 78% (0.67-0.9) and 2y OS 96% after induction ipi/nivo then chemoRT in cT2-4a N0-2 MIBC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Node-positive disease was eligible (cN0-2), uncommon for a bladder-preservation cohort, yet 2y OS reached 96% (0.91-1). RT dose, fractionation, target volume and the concurrent chemo agent aren't in the source, so whether this transfers to your trimodality protocol is unconfirmed.

- 📊 2y bladder-intact EFS 78% (95% CI 0.67-0.9)
- 📊 2y OS 96% (95% CI 0.91-1)
- 🔍 Single-arm: induction ipilimumab + nivolumab → chemoradiation, bladder-preservation intent
- 🔍 Stage II/III MIBC, cT2-4a; nodes cN0-2 eligible (node-positive allowed)
- 💊 Dual checkpoint induction (ipi + nivo) layered onto trimodality bladder preservation
- ⚠️ RT dose, fractionation, target volume and concurrent chemo agent not reported in source
- ⚠️ Single-arm, 2y f/u; no randomised comparator vs chemoRT alone or radical cystectomy
- ⚠️ Bladder-intact EFS is a composite endpoint; IO's added contribution over CRT alone unquantified
