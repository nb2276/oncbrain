**INDIBLADE: induction ipi+nivo before chemoRT, 2yr bladder-intact EFS 78% and OS 96% in cT2-4a MIBC (single-arm, hypothesis-generating).**

> [!summary] TL;DR

## 💧 Bladder

Induction dual-IO layered ahead of bladder-preserving chemoRT, tested for bladder-intact survival in cT2-4a MIBC.

### INDIBLADE

> 2yr bladder-intact EFS 78% (67-90%) with induction ipi+nivo before chemoRT; 2yr OS 96% in cT2-4aN0-2 MIBC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Cohort spans cT2-4a and clinically node-positive (N1-2), pushing bladder preservation into nodal disease that usually routes to cystectomy. The novel lever is induction ipi+nivo before chemoradiation, not the RT itself; 78% 2yr bladder-intact EFS tests whether induction dual-IO belongs in a trimodality pathway. RT dose and fractionation absent from source, so transferability stays unconfirmed.

- 📊 2yr bladder-intact EFS 78% (95% CI 67-90%)
- 📊 2yr OS 96% (95% CI 91-100%)
- 🔍 Induction ipilimumab + nivolumab → chemoradiation, bladder-preservation intent
- 🔍 Stage II/III MIBC, cT2-4aN0-2; includes clinically node-positive (N1-2)
- 🔗 Adds induction dual-IO ahead of standard trimodality-style bladder-preserving chemoRT
- ⚠️ Single-arm; no randomized comparator vs chemoRT alone or radical cystectomy
- ⚠️ CRT dose, fractionation, and radiosensitizer not specified in source
- ⚠️ Short 2yr follow-up; bladder-intact EFS is a composite surrogate, not OS-mature
