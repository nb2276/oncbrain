# 2026-07-15

**STAMPEDE M0: adding abiraterone to RT+ADT in high-risk non-met prostate cuts MFS (HR 0.53, 6yr 82% vs 69%), OS HR 0.60; enzalutamide adds nothing.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate carried the day: STAMPEDE extends abiraterone's metastatic-HSPC OS benefit into high-risk M0, where 85% of pts receive definitive RT (74Gy/37fx), so this reads as systemic intensification on an RT+ADT backbone. Adding enzalutamide to abiraterone buys nothing (interaction HR 1.02, p=0.91).

## 🌰 Prostate

Single card, but an RT-adjacent one: abiraterone added to the definitive RT+ADT backbone in high-risk M0.

### STAMPEDE (abiraterone ± enzalutamide, high-risk non-metastatic) · [[NCT00268476]]

> MFS HR 0.53 (6yr 82% vs 69%) adding abiraterone ± enzalutamide to ADT in high-risk M0; OS HR 0.60.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read: 85% (1684/1974) were on definitive RT plus long-term ADT, so this is the intensification decision for the reader's own high-risk M0 patient. Abiraterone alone suffices; adding enzalutamide gave no extra MFS (interaction HR 1.02). RT was standard 74Gy/37fx, so the result transfers to routine practice.

- 📊 Combination (abiraterone ± enzalutamide) vs ADT alone — comparison values omitted (cell value "0.53" not verified in source)
- 📊 6-yr MFS 82% (79-85) vs 69% (66-72); 180 vs 306 MFS events
- 🔍 Pooled analysis of two STAMPEDE phase 3 RCTs, N=1974, open-label, 113 UK/Swiss sites, median f/u 72mo
- 🔍 High-risk M0: node+ or node− with ≥2 of T3/T4, Gleason 8-10, PSA≥40; or high-risk relapse; 39% node+
- 🔍 RT mandated for node− disease, encouraged for node+; 85% (1684/1974) planned to receive RT
- 🔍 RT dose 74Gy/37fx to prostate + seminal vesicles, or a hypofractionated equivalent
- 💊 Abiraterone 1000mg + prednisolone 5mg (± enzalutamide 160mg) ×2yr, added to 3yr ADT
- 📐 Adding enzalutamide to abiraterone gave no extra MFS: interaction HR 1.02 (0.70-1.50), p=0.91
- 🔗 Extends abiraterone's established OS benefit from metastatic HSPC (LATITUDE, STAMPEDE-M1) into high-risk M0
- ⚠️ Open-label and MFS is a surrogate, but the concordant OS benefit blunts both concerns
