# 2026-07-15

**STAMPEDE (M0 high-risk): 2yr abiraterone on ADT+RT cuts MFS HR 0.53 (6yr 82% vs 69%), OS HR 0.60; adding enzalutamide gains nothing.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate carried the day: 2yr abiraterone added to definitive ADT+RT in high-risk M0 disease (85% RT'd, 74Gy/37fx) drove MFS HR 0.53 and OS HR 0.60. Enzalutamide on top added tox for no extra MFS (HR 1.02). RT-attributable subgroup HR not broken out in source.

## 🌰 Prostate

The intensification question moves from metastatic into high-risk non-metastatic definitive ADT+RT, where abiraterone (not enzalutamide) carries the benefit.

### STAMPEDE (abiraterone ± enzalutamide, high-risk non-metastatic) · [[NCT00268476]]

> MFS HR 0.53 (6yr 82% vs 69%) and OS HR 0.60 adding 2yr abiraterone to ADT in high-risk M0 prostate (85% also had RT).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read: abiraterone sits on top of definitive ADT+RT (74Gy/37fx to prostate+SV in the 85% who got RT), not RT vs no-RT. It moves systemic intensification for the high-risk M0 pt you're already irradiating; enzalutamide adds nothing over abiraterone (interaction HR 1.02, p=0.91).

- 📊 Efficacy: ADT + abiraterone (±enzalutamide) vs ADT alone
- 🔍 Pooled meta-analysis of 2 open-label STAMPEDE phase 3 RCTs, N=1974, 113 UK/Swiss sites; median f/u 72mo
- 🔍 High-risk M0: node+ or (node-neg with ≥2 of T3/T4, Gleason 8-10, PSA≥40); 39% node+, median PSA 34
- 🔍 RT backbone: 85% (1684/1974) planned RT, 74Gy/37fx to prostate+SV or hypofractionated equivalent
- 🔍 RT mandated if node-negative, encouraged if node-positive
- 💊 Abiraterone 1000mg + prednisolone 5mg daily × 2yr on 3yr ADT; control = ADT alone
- ⚠️ Adding enzalutamide to abiraterone gave no extra MFS benefit (interaction HR 1.02, 0.70-1.50, p=0.91); more tox for no gain
- 🔗 Extends abiraterone from the metastatic setting into high-risk non-metastatic definitive ADT+RT
- ⚠️ No RT-treated subgroup HR in source excerpt; benefit presumed across the 85% RT majority, not separately reported
