# 2026-06-25

**ENZARAD: enza added to definitive RT+ADT missed 1° MFS (8yr 74 vs 72%, HR 0.88, p=0.34); benefit isolated to cN1/pelvic-RT subgroup (MFS HR 0.47).**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate carried the day. Adding enzalutamide to a definitive RT+ADT backbone was overall null (MFS HR 0.88), but the cN1/pelvic-RT subgroup saw MFS HR 0.47 (0.29-0.76). Lower baseline risk vs STAMPEDE (11% vs 39% cN1) explains the muted overall effect; hypothesis-generating for node-positive pelvic-RT candidates.

## 🌰 Prostate

### ENZARAD (ANZUP 1303)

> MFS 8yr 74% vs 72%, HR 0.88 (0.67-1.15) p=0.34; enza on an RT+ADT backbone missed 1° endpoint, benefit isolated to cN1/pelvic-RT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The intensification benefit tracks the pelvic-RT-planned subgroup (MFS HR 0.47, OS HR 0.53), but that arm was enriched for cN1 (28% vs 0%), so nodal burden, not the RT field itself, likely drives it. Planning whole-pelvis RT for cN1 disease is where adding enzalutamide over 6mo NSAA earns its place; cN0 pts gained nothing.

- 📊 Efficacy (8-yr rates, enza vs conventional NSAA)

  | Endpoint | Enza | Control | HR (95% CI), p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | MFS (1°) | 74% | 72% | 0.88 (0.67-1.15), p=0.34 |
  | PFS | 67% | 62% | 0.78 (0.61-0.99), p=0.044 |
  | OS | 83% | 80% | 0.87 (0.63-1.20), p=0.40 |

- 📊 Enzalutamide benefit by prespecified subgroup (MFS / OS HRs) — the RT-actionable read

  | Subgroup | MFS HR (95% CI) | OS HR (95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | cN1 nodal | 0.43 (0.20-0.92) | 0.46 (0.17-1.26) |
  | Pelvic RT planned | 0.47 (0.29-0.76) | 0.53 (0.30-0.95) |
  | Very high-risk | 0.85 (0.64-1.13) | 0.81 (0.57-1.13) |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=802, 8 countries (2014-2018), median f/u 8yr; enza 160mg×24mo vs active NSAA×6mo on identical 2yr-LHRH + EBRT backbone
- 🔍 RT technique + QA (identical both arms)
  - Prostate 78Gy, or 46Gy + brachytherapy boost (8% got brachy)
  - Pelvic nodes 46Gy elective + boost to gross nodes
  - Pelvic RT required for cN1; optional cN0, declared pre-randomization (40% planned)
  - QA: credentialing, real-time review first 5 plans/site, 20% sampled after
- 🔍 Baseline: 90% Gleason 8-10, 36% PSA >20, 12% cN1 by conventional imaging
- 🔗 vs STAMPEDE (abiraterone): lower baseline risk explains ENZARAD's weaker overall effect

  |  | STAMPEDE | ENZARAD |
  |---|---|---|
  | cN1 | 39% | 11% |
  | Median PSA | 35 ng/ml | 14 ng/ml |
  | cT3-4 | 92% | 47% |
  | Overall MFS HR | 0.53 | 0.88 |

- ⚠️ Control arm was active NSAA (6mo), not placebo; enza's edge measured vs a real antiandrogen
- ⚠️ Subgroup benefit prespecified but hypothesis-generating; pelvic-RT arm enriched for N1 (28% vs 0%), Gleason 9-10 (62% vs 49%)
- ⚠️ OS was original 1° endpoint, switched to MFS mid-trial for fewer-than-expected deaths
