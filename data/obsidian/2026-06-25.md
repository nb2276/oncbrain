## 🌰 Prostate

One trial: ENZARAD tests ARSI intensification on top of definitive EBRT + long-term ADT in high-risk localized disease, with the actionable signal confined to node-positive/pelvic-RT pts.

### ENZARAD (ANZUP 1303)

> 1° MFS not met (8y 74% vs 72%, HR 0.88, p=0.34), but pelvic-RT/cN1 subgroups showed enzalutamide benefit (HR 0.47/0.43).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The pelvic-RT subgroup, not the negative primary, is the RT read: enzalutamide cut MFS events only where pelvic nodal RT was planned (HR 0.47, 0.29-0.76) or cN1 (HR 0.43, 0.20-0.92). But that group was higher-risk (28% vs 0% N1, 62% vs 49% G9-10), so selection confounds the field signal. Moves intensification toward whole-pelvis/cN1 pts.

- 📊 1° EP MFS not met: 8y 74% vs 72%, HR 0.88 (0.67-1.15), p=0.34
- 📊 OS ns: 8y 83% vs 80%, HR 0.87 (0.63-1.20), p=0.40
- 📊 PFS favored enza: 8y 67% vs 62%, HR 0.78 (0.61-0.99), p=0.044 (nominal)
- 📊 Enzalutamide effect by prespecified subgroup (MFS + OS HRs)

  | Subgroup | MFS HR (95% CI) | OS HR (95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | cN1 (node+) | 0.43 (0.20-0.92) | 0.46 (0.17-1.26) |
  | Pelvic RT planned | 0.47 (0.29-0.76) | 0.53 (0.30-0.95) |
  | Very high-risk | 0.85 (0.64-1.13) | 0.81 (0.57-1.13) |

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=802, 8 countries, recruited 2014-2018; median f/u 8y. Enza 160mg ×24mo vs NSAA ×6mo, both + 24mo LHRH + EBRT
- 🔍 RT: prostate 78Gy or 46Gy + brachy boost; cN1 required pelvic nodes (46Gy elective + boost to gross nodes), optional/pre-declared for cN0
- 🔗 vs STAMPEDE abiraterone: weaker overall HR reflects a lower-risk ENZARAD cohort

  | Feature | STAMPEDE | ENZARAD |
  |---|---|---|
  | MFS HR (all pts) | 0.53 | 0.88 |
  | cN1 | 39% | 11% |
  | Median PSA | 35 ng/ml | 14 ng/ml |
  | cT3-4 | 92% | 47% |

- ⚠️ Subgroup benefits nominal, unadjusted for multiplicity; 5 prespecified subgroups tested by interaction p, hypothesis-generating
- ⚠️ Pelvic-RT group higher-risk than no-pelvic-RT (28% vs 0% N1, 62% vs 49% G9-10); benefit may track risk selection, not RT field
