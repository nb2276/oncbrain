### ProtecT (secondary analysis: cribriform morphology)

> Cribriform-negative pts (87%): radical Rx gave no significant 15yr metastasis reduction vs active monitoring; cribriform-negative GG2 matched GG1 risk.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Selection, not technique, is the RT read: cribriform-negative GG2 matched GG1 on 15yr metastasis risk and gained no significant benefit from radical treatment, so definitive RT is deferrable there. Cribriform-positive (~13%) is where local treatment changed metastasis outcomes, moving the surveillance-vs-treat decision at biopsy.

- 🔍 Secondary analysis of ProtecT; cribriform status was NOT a randomization characteristic
- 🔍 Biopsy slides from 712 of 1,643 randomized pts centrally reviewed (centralization ongoing)
- 🔍 Arms: active monitoring vs surgery vs EBRT + 3-6mo neoadjuvant ADT
- 📊 ~13% cribriform-positive on biopsy; 87% cribriform-negative
- 📊 Cribriform-negative (87%): early radical Rx gave no significant 15yr metastasis reduction vs active monitoring (ITT + per-protocol)
- 📊 Cribriform-negative GG2 pts had the same 15yr metastasis risk as Gleason 6 / GG1 (multivariable Cox)
- 🔍 Gleason re-graded to 2019 ISUP from original 2005 criteria; result unchanged
- 🔗 First trial-level backing for EAU advice against active surveillance in cribriform disease (prior guidance had no prospective basis)
- ⚠️ Cribriform not randomized; RT+ADT arm carried higher cribriform prevalence by chance
