# 2026-06-16

**DOREMY: 5yr LRFS 97.4% with de-escalated 36Gy/18fx preop RT in myxoid liposarcoma, supports dropping below the standard 50Gy.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Sarcoma carries the day's actionable RT signal: DOREMY holds 97.4% 5yr LRFS after cutting preop dose to 36Gy/18fx in myxoid liposarcoma (single-arm, 66mo f/u). In prostate, REVELUTION supplies a coronary-plaque substrate favoring relugolix over leuprolide in men on pelvic RT + ADT (surrogate endpoint, N=94).

## 🦴 Sarcoma

MLS radiosensitivity underwrites the sub-50Gy preop dose question DOREMY tests.

### DOREMY · [[NCT02106312]]

> 5yr LRFS 97.4% after de-escalated 36Gy/18fx preop RT (vs standard 50Gy) in myxoid liposarcoma; wound complications 21%.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is de-escalation: 36Gy/18fx (vs standard 50Gy/25fx) held 5yr LRFS at 97.4% with 21% wound complications in translocation-confirmed trunk/extremity MLS. It moves the preop-dose decision toward de-escalation for this radiosensitive histology, though without a randomised 50Gy comparator this rests on shared decision-making, not proven non-inferiority.

- 📊 Long-term outcomes at 5 years (single arm, N=90)

  | 5yr endpoint | Rate | 95% CI |
  |---|---|---|
  | LRFS | 97.4% | 93.9-100% |
  | PFS | 81.0% | 72.6-89.4% |
  | DSS | 89.5% | 82.6-96.4% |
  | OS | 88.5% | 81.2-95.8% |

- 🔍 Prospective single-group phase 2, 9 tertiary sarcoma centers (EU + US), N=90, median f/u 66.4mo
- 🔍 Preop RT 36Gy/18fx (2Gy daily), de-escalated from the standard 50Gy/25fx, then resection
- 📊 Wound complications 21% (18/90); 16% (14) required intervention
- 📊 Late toxicity low: G2 15% (13 pts), G3 3% (3 pts)
- 🔗 MLS is exquisitely radiosensitive; this is the rationale for testing a lower dose vs standard 50Gy
- ⚠️ Single-arm, nonrandomized: no direct comparator, non-inferiority vs 50Gy not formally tested
- 🔍 Surgery omitted in 3 pts (3%) due to intercurrent metastatic disease

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42141895]] **Dose Reduction of Preoperative Radiotherapy in Myxoid Liposarcoma: The Phase 2 DOREMY Nonrandomized Clinical Trial.** — Lansu; Bov&#xe9;e; Braam et al.. *JAMA oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1577](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamaoncol.2026.1577)
  > IMPORTANCE: Prospective data from 2 phase 2 trials showed favorable wound complication rates and promising local control after a reduced preoperative radiotherapy dose for myxoid liposarcoma (MLS). However, long-term follow-up data are currently lacking.
  > OBJECTIVE: To determine the efficacy and toxicity profile of a reduced preoperative radiotherapy dose in patients with MLS with long-term follow-up.
  > DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS: The Dose Reduction of Preoperative Radiotherapy in Myxoid Liposarcoma (DOREMY) trial is a prospective, single-group, phase 2 nonrandomized clinical trial conducted in 9 tertiary sarcoma centers in Europe and the US. Eligible patients were adults with biopsy-proven and translocation-confirmed localized MLS of the trunk or extremity who were enrolled from November 24, 2010, to May 14, 2020. Data were analyzed from January to December 2025.
  > INTERVENTION: Preoperative radiotherapy to a reduced dose of 36 Gy in once-daily 2-Gy fractions followed by resection.
  > MAIN OUTCOMES AND MEASURES: Long-term local recurrence-free survival, progression-free survival, disease-specific survival, overall survival, and late toxic effects.
  > RESULTS: Ninety patients (mean [SD] age, 47 [13.1] years; 50 [56%] male) were included and followed up for a median (IQR) of 66.4 (48.8-87.5) months. Preoperative radiotherapy was delivered according to protocol in all patients. Surgery was not performed in 3 patients (3%) due to intercurrent metastatic disease. Local recurrence-free survival, progression-free survival, disease-specific survival, and overall survival rates at 5 years were 97.4% (95% CI, 93.9%-100%), 81.0% (95% CI, 72.6%-89.4%), 89.5% (95% CI, 82.6%-96.4%), and 88.5% (95% CI, 81.2%-95.8%), respectively. In total, 18 patients (21%) experienced a wound complication, and 14 (16%) required intervention. Any grade 2 or grade 3 late toxic effects were seen among 13 patients (15%) and 3 patients (3%), respectively.
  > CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE: This long-term analysis of the DOREMY nonrandomized clinical trial demonstrated excellent local control and a favorable toxicity profile following dose reduction of preoperative radiotherapy in patients with MLS. These compelling phase 2 findings support adoption of this regimen as an appropriate treatment option through shared decision-making with the patient, given the impracticality of conducting a phase 3 trial for a rare cancer.
  > TRIAL REGISTRATION: ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: [[NCT02106312]].

## 🌰 Prostate

Not an efficacy signal: the read here is cardiovascular safety of the ADT agent chosen alongside pelvic RT.

### REVELUTION

> Leuprolide added 68.9 mm³ more total coronary plaque than relugolix at 12mo (adjusted), in men on pelvic RT + ADT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Non-calcified plaque, the MACE-predictive subtype, drove the 68.9 mm³ adjusted total-plaque difference; calcified and low-attenuation plaque didn't differ. For the radonc prescribing ADT alongside pelvic RT in intermediate/high-risk disease, this is a mechanistic reason to favor relugolix over leuprolide in cardiovascularly at-risk men.

- 🔍 Single-institution open-label RCT, 4 Emory centers, N=94 non-metastatic prostate CA, all on pelvic RT ± pelvic nodes
- 🔍 ADT-eligible pts randomized 1:1 relugolix vs leuprolide; parallel prospective RT-alone cohort (no ADT) as control
- 🔍 1° EP: 12-mo change in total coronary plaque volume on serial CCTA (HeartFlow automated quant, reads blinded to arm)
- 💊 Relugolix 360mg load → 120mg/day; leuprolide 3-mo depots; ADT ≥6mo, randomization stratified by ASCVD 10-yr risk
- 📊 12-mo coronary plaque change, leuprolide vs relugolix

  | Plaque subtype | Crude 12-mo diff (leuprolide vs relugolix) | Adjusted mean diff |
  |---|---|---|
  | Total plaque volume | 56 vs 25 mm³ | 68.9 mm³ more with leuprolide |
  | Non-calcified plaque | higher with leuprolide (main driver) | not reported in source |
  | Calcified plaque | no significant difference | not reported in source |
  | Low-attenuation plaque | no significant difference | not reported in source |

- 🔗 vs HERO (2020): HERO showed lower MACE with relugolix but mechanism unclear (similar T-suppression); REVELUTION supplies a coronary-plaque substrate
- ⚠️ Surrogate imaging endpoint (plaque volume), not clinical MACE; N=94, single institution, 12-mo f/u
- ⚠️ Open-label; per-arm randomized counts not reported in source
