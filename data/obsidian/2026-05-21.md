## 🎀 Breast

RS-guided chemo omission may extend to RT: RNI appears to add little in biologically favorable N1 disease.

### SWOG S1007 RNI Analysis (Jagsi et al.) · [[NCT01272037]]

> SWOG S1007 secondary analysis: RNI vs no RNI not associated with IDFS in RS≤25 N1 HR+/HER2-; 5-yr LRR <1% after BCS.

- 📐 IDFS by RNI receipt (landmark analysis from 1 yr)

  | Menopausal status | HR (95% CI) | p |
  |---|---|---|
  | Premenopausal | 1.03 (0.74-1.43) | 0.87 |
  | Postmenopausal | 0.85 (0.68-1.07) | 0.16 |

- 📊 5-yr cumulative LRR by locoregional therapy

  | Treatment | 5-yr LRR |
  |---|---|
  | BCS + RT + RNI | 0.85% |
  | BCS + RT without RNI | 0.55% |
  | Mastectomy + PMRT | 0.11% |
  | Mastectomy without RT | 1.7% |

- 🔍 Secondary analysis of SWOG S1007 RCT (randomized: chemo vs endocrine alone); RT receipt observational, not randomized
- 🔍 N=4871 with RT forms; 81% received RT; of those, 59% received RNI targeting ≥ supraclavicular region
- 🔍 Population: HR+/HER2-, Oncotype DX RS ≤25, 1-3 nodes, median age 57; median f/u 6.1 yrs
- ⚠️ RNI vs no RNI not randomized; confounding by indication unresolved across diverse practice settings
- ⚠️ Post-hoc secondary analysis of a trial designed to answer a chemotherapy question, not an RT question
- 🔗 vs MA.20 (NEJM 2015) and EORTC 22922: both showed DFS benefit from RNI but enrolled higher-risk N+ pts in pre-RS era; this RS ≤25 favorable subset is a different biologic context
- 📊 Chemo omission per S1007 assignment not an independent indication for RNI; LRR equally low in endocrine-only arm

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 37410451]] **Radiotherapy Use and Incidence of Locoregional Recurrence in Patients With Favorable-Risk, Node-Positive Breast Cancer Enrolled in the SWOG S1007 Trial.** — Jagsi; Barlow; Woodward et al.. *JAMA oncology* (2023-08). [doi:10.1001/jamaoncol.2023.1984](https://doi.org/10.1001/jamaoncol.2023.1984)
  > IMPORTANCE: Little is known about regional nodal irradiation (RNI) practice patterns or rates of locoregional recurrence (LRR) with and without RNI in patients with limited nodal disease and favorable biology treated with modern surgical and systemic therapy, including approaches that de-escalate those latter treatments.
  > OBJECTIVE: To investigate how often patients with low-recurrence score breast cancer with 1 to 3 nodes involved receive RNI, incidence and predictors of LRR, and associations between locoregional therapy and disease-free survival.
  > DESIGN, SETTING, AND PARTICIPANTS: In this secondary analysis of the SWOG S1007 trial, patients with hormone receptor-positive, ERBB2-negative breast cancer, and a Oncotype DX 21-gene Breast Recurrence Score assay result of no more than 25, were randomized to endocrine therapy alone vs chemotherapy then endocrine therapy. Prospectively collected radiotherapy information was collected from 4871 patients treated in diverse settings. Data were analyzed June 2022 to April 2023.
  > EXPOSURE: Receipt of RNI (targeting at least the supraclavicular region).
  > MAIN OUTCOME(S) AND MEASURE(S): Cumulative incidence of LRR was calculated by locoregional treatment received. Analyses were assessed for associations between invasive disease-free survival (IDFS) and locoregional therapy, adjusted for menopausal status, treatment group, recurrence score, tumor size, nodes involved, and axillary surgery. Radiotherapy information was recorded in the first year after randomization, so survival analyses were landmarked as starting at 1 year among those still at risk.
  > RESULTS: Of 4871 female patients (median [range] age, 57 [18-87] years) with radiotherapy forms, 3947 (81.0%) reported radiotherapy receipt. Of 3852 patients who received radiotherapy and had complete information on targets, 2274 (59.0%) received RNI. With a median follow-up of 6.1 years, the cumulative incidence of LRR by 5 years was 0.85% among patients who received breast-conserving surgery and radiotherapy with RNI; 0.55% after breast-conserving surgery with radiotherapy without RNI; 0.11% after mastectomy with postmastectomy radiotherapy; and 1.7% after mastectomy without radiotherapy. Similarly low LRR was observed within the group assigned to endocrine therapy without chemotherapy. The rate of IDFS did not differ by RNI receipt (premenopausal: hazard ratio [HR], 1.03; 95% CI, 0.74-1.43; P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.87; postmenopausal: HR, 0.85; 95% CI, 0.68-1.07; P&#x2009;=&#x2009;.16).
  > CONCLUSIONS AND RELEVANCE: In this secondary analysis of a clinical trial, RNI use was divided in the setting of biologically favorable N1 disease, and rates of LRR were low even in patients who did not receive RNI. Disease-free survival was not associated with RNI receipt; omission of chemotherapy among patients similar to those enrolled in the S1007 trial is not an independent indication for use of RNI.

> [!question] Open questions
> - Would a dedicated RCT randomizing RNI vs omission in RS ≤25 N1 confirm safety of de-escalation?
> - Optimal locoregional strategy in this population receiving adjuvant CDK4/6 inhibitors?
> - LRR trajectory beyond 10 years in this favorable-biology cohort?

## 💧 Bladder

First phase III data for adj RT in high-risk MIBC; RT + adjuvant IO sequencing now the open question.

### Bladder Adjuvant Radiotherapy Phase III (Murthy et al.)

> Adj pelvic RT post-cystectomy improves 2-yr LRFS vs observation (87.1% vs 76.0%, HR 0.43, p=.04) in high-risk MIBC.

- 📊 1° EP 2-yr LRFS: 87.1% vs 76.0%, HR 0.43 (95% CI 0.20-0.96), p=.04
- 📊 Secondary endpoints (RT vs Obs)

  | Endpoint | RT | Obs | HR (95% CI) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | DFS | 71.6% | 58.7% | 0.62 (0.36-1.05) |
  | BCSS | 79.6% | 65.0% | 0.59 (0.33-1.10) |
  | OS | 70.4% | 57.4% | 0.78 (0.49-1.26) |

- 🔍 Phase III multicenter, N=153 (RT=77, Obs=76); median f/u 47 months
- 🔍 RT: stoma-sparing IG-IMRT 50.4Gy/28fx to cystectomy bed + pelvic nodes
- 🔍 Eligibility: any of pT3-4, pN1-3, margin+, ≤10 nodes dissected; 62% pT3-4, 41% pN+
- 🔍 >90% periop chemo (71% neoadjuvant, 20% adjuvant); zero immunotherapy
- ⚠️ N=153 underpowered for survival; all secondary HRs trending but CI crosses 1
- ⚠️ No immunotherapy era pts; CheckMate-274 nivolumab adjuvant now approved in this setting
- 🔗 CheckMate-274 (NEJM 2021): nivolumab adjuvant vs placebo improved DFS in high-risk MIBC post-RC; RT + IO sequencing untested

**Sources:**
- 📄 [[PMID 42166701]] **Bladder Adjuvant Radiotherapy: Phase III Multicenter Randomized Controlled Trial of Adjuvant Radiotherapy or Observation for Postcystectomy Muscle-Invasive Bladder Cancer.** — Murthy; Maitre; Pal et al.. *Journal of clinical oncology : official journal of the American Society of Clinical Oncology* (2026-05). [doi:10.1200/jco-25-02093](https://doi.org/10.1200/jco-25-02093)
  > PURPOSE: To report the primary analysis of a multicenter, phase III randomized trial of adjuvant radiotherapy (RT) after chemotherapy and radical cystectomy (RC) in patients with high-risk muscle-invasive bladder cancer (MIBC).
  > METHODS: Patients with nonmetastatic urothelial MIBC at high risk after RC (any one of: T3-4, N1-3, margin positive, &#x2264;10 nodes dissected) were randomly assigned 1:1 to adjuvant RT or observation (Obs), stratified by nodal involvement (yes/no) and chemotherapy (neoadjuvant/adjuvant/none). Stoma-sparing IG-IMRT 50.4Gy in 28 fractions was prescribed to the cystectomy bed and pelvic nodes. The primary end point was 2-year locoregional recurrence-free survival (LRFS), and the secondary end points were disease-free survival (DFS), bladder cancer-specific survival (BCSS), and overall survival (OS).
  > RESULTS: From June 2016 to May 2024, 153 patients were randomly assigned (Obs = 76, RT = 77), with 62% and 41% of patients having pT3-T4 and pN+ stages, respectively. Over 90% of the patients received systemic chemotherapy (71% neoadjuvant and 20% adjuvant), and none received immunotherapy. After a median follow-up of 47 months, the 2-year LRFS was significantly higher with adjuvant RT versus observation (87.1% v 76.0%, hazard ratio [HR], 0.43 [95% CI, 0.20 to 0.96], P = .04). The DFS was 71.6% versus 58.7% (HR, 0.62 [95% CI, 0.36 to 1.05]), BCSS was 79.6% versus 65.0% (HR, 0.59 [95% CI, 0.33 to 1.10]), and OS was 70.4% versus 57.4% (HR, 0.78 [95% CI, 0.49 to 1.26]) for RT and Obs, respectively.
  > CONCLUSION: Adjuvant pelvic IMRT after radical cystectomy and perioperative chemotherapy suggests an improvement in locoregional control in patients with high risk urothelial MIBC with no additional severe toxicity.

> [!question] Open questions
> - LRFS benefit: will it translate to OS in adequately powered trial?
> - Optimal sequencing of adj RT with nivolumab adjuvant (CheckMate-274 SOC)?
> - Which high-risk subgroup (pN+ vs pT3-4 vs margin+) drives LRFS benefit?
