# 2026-06-03

**AREST: adjuvant RT improved 3-yr LRFS 89.2% vs 80.9% (HR 0.52, p=0.02) in pT1-2N0 intermediate-risk OSCC — no OS signal at 47mo.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Head-neck dominated today with AREST, the first RCT to address adjuvant RT in intermediate-risk N0 OSCC post-surgery. LRFS benefit is real; OS follow-up too short to read, especially in early-stage disease where events accrue slowly.

## 👄 Head & Neck

Adjuvant management of intermediate-risk N0 OSCC has had no RCT evidence since RTOG 9501/EORTC 22931 defined the high-risk (ENE/margin-positive) standard; AREST fills that gap.

### AREST

> 3-yr LRFS 89.2% vs 80.9% (HR 0.52, p=0.02) favoring adjuvant RT in pT1-2N0 intermediate-risk OSCC; no OS benefit at 47.2mo.

![AREST](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJVgwera0AAs6s2.jpg)

| Arm | 3-yr LRFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Adjuvant RT | 89.2% (84.3–93.3%) | 0.52 (0.30–0.91) | 0.02 |
| Observation | 80.9% (74.6–86.1%) | ref | — |

- 🔍 multicenter phase III RCT, India, N=392 (191 RT vs 201 observation), median f/u 47.2mo
- 🔍 population: pT1-2N0 OSCC post-curative surgery with intermediate-risk features
- ⚠️ no DFS or OS benefit at median 47.2mo follow-up
- 📊 exploratory: LRFS benefit signal stronger in oral tongue subgroup
- 🔗 RTOG 9501/EORTC 22931 established adjuvant CRT for high-risk H&N (positive margins, ENE); AREST addresses the intermediate-risk N0 gap with no prior RCT
- ⚠️ OS follow-up likely insufficient: early-stage OSCC events accrue slowly; 47.2mo median too short to detect OS separation
- ⚠️ single-country trial (India); surgical quality and pathologic staging practice may limit direct generalizability
