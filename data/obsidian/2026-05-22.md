# 2026-05-22

**DBCG IMN2: 15yr OS 65.0% vs 60.8% with IMNI (HR 0.85, p=0.0016); internal mammary nodal RT benefit holds under modern systemic therapy.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Regional nodal RT carried the day. Breast IMN2 (nationwide cohort, N=4541) keeps internal-mammary irradiation's OS edge alive under modern systemic Rx, while prostate PEACE V-STORM is the first randomised ENRT-vs-MDT read (4-yr MFS 76% vs 63%, p=0·063, phase 2). Skin adds EORTC low-dose RT dosing for indolent cutaneous lymphomas.

## 🌞 Skin / Melanoma

EORTC consensus on RT dose per cutaneous lymphoma entity, though no RCT fixes a standard.

### EORTC Cutaneous Lymphoma RT Recommendations

> Low-dose RT (8-12 Gy) recommended for indolent cutaneous lymphomas; MF 1-yr local control ≥92%, but no RCT defines a standard dose per entity.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The actionable read is the dose floor: 4 Gy (1-2 fx) underperformed vs 8-12 Gy, so 8-12 Gy stays the low-dose local standard for MF, 24 Gy reserved for large/refractory lesions or pre-autoSCT remission. Low-dose TSEBT trades durability for repeatability and lower toxicity, moving the de-escalation decision in indolent CTCL.

- 🔍 RT dose recommendations by cutaneous lymphoma entity

  | Entity / setting | RT dose |
  |---|---|
  | MF plaque/tumour (local) | 8-12 Gy / 2-3 fx |
  | Low-dose TSEBT (palliative) | 8-12 Gy |
  | TSEBT pre-autoSCT | up to 24 Gy |
  | Cutaneous DLBCL, leg type | 16-45 Gy (4 Gy/wk to ≤40 Gy) |

- 📊 MF low-dose RT: 1-yr local control ≥92%
- ⚠️ 4 Gy (1-2 fx) gave inferior local control vs 8-12 Gy
- 🔗 Folliculotropic MF: RT more effective than other modalities (Dutch registry, 203 cases, retrospective)
- 🔍 Sézary syndrome: TSEBT controversial, rarely achieves long-term remission
- 💊 TSEBT in SS also lowers circulating Sézary cell counts (systemic effect)
- 💊 12 Gy TSEBT + mechlorethamine gel maintenance reported with encouraging results
- ⚠️ No completed RCTs defining standard dose per entity; prospective trial urgently needed

**Sources:**
- 📄 **Radiotherapy in cutaneous lymphomas: Recommendations from the EORTC cutaneous lymphoma tumour group** — Khaled Elsayad; Emmanuella Guenova; Chalid Assaf et al.. *European Journal of Cancer* (2024). [doi:10.1016/j.ejca.2024.115064](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ejca.2024.115064)
  - 📎 [[papers/lymphoma/radiotherapy-in-cutaneous-lymphomas-recommendations-from-the-eor.pdf|EORTC Cutaneous Lymphoma RT Recommendations (full text)]]

> [!question] Open questions
> - Optimal radiation dose per cutaneous lymphoma entity
> - Durability of low-dose TSEBT vs higher-dose regimens
> - TSEBT combined with immunotherapy in Sézary syndrome

## 🎀 Breast

DBCG IMN2 revives the internal-mammary nodal RT question for the modern-therapy era.

### DBCG IMN2 · [[NCT06549920]]

> 15yr OS 65.0% vs 60.8% with IMNI, adjusted HR 0.85 (0.76-0.94); benefit persists under modern systemic therapy and 3D RT.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The 1-3 positive-node subgroup is the RT read: IMNI benefit held at the lowest nodal burden, no subgroup found for omission, moving elective IMN coverage where guidelines diverge. Reassuring on toxicity too: 15yr ischemic/valvular cardiac death 0.2% (right/IMNI) vs 0.7% (left) under 3D planning.

- 🔍 Prospective nationwide population-based cohort, N=4541, 6 RT centres; right-sided tumours → IMNI, left-sided → no IMNI. Median f/u 13.7yr.
- 📊 IMNI vs no IMNI, adjusted HRs (Cox)

  | Endpoint | Adjusted HR (95% CI) | p |
  |---|---|---|
  | Overall survival | 0.85 (0.76-0.94) | 0.0016 |
  | Breast cancer mortality | 0.84 (0.74-0.95) | 0.0077 |
  | Distant metastasis | 0.87 (0.78-0.98) | 0.026 |

- 📊 15yr OS 65.0% (IMNI) vs 60.8% (no IMNI); absolute gain 4.2%
- 📊 IMNI benefit maintained in 1-3 positive-node pts; no subgroup identified for IMNI omission
- 💊 Modern systemic backbone (taxanes, trastuzumab, aromatase inhibitors), the era thought to erase residual IMNI gain
- 📊 15yr cardiac death (ischemic/valvular) 0.2% right/IMNI vs 0.7% left; no excess cardiac mortality from IMNI
- 🔗 IMN2 vs prior IMNI/regional-node evidence

  | Study | Design | Signal |
  |---|---|---|
  | DBCG IMN1 (2003-07) | prospective cohort, n=3089 | +4.7% abs OS, f/u 14.8yr |
  | EBCTCG meta-analysis | n=12,167 | +3% abs 15yr survival, regional node RT |
  | Korean KROG 06-08 | 3D-RT, modern systemic | negative for IMNI |

- ⚠️ Non-randomized: IMNI assigned by tumour laterality, not randomization; residual confounding possible
- ⚠️ Excludes neoadjuvant-treated pts; result doesn't transfer to the growing NAC population

**Sources:**
- 📄 **Internal mammary node irradiation in 4541 node-positive breast cancer patients treated with newer systemic therapies and 3D-based radiotherapy (DBCG IMN2): a prospective, nationwide, population-based cohort study** — Anders W. Mølby Nielsen; Lise B. J. Thorsen; Demet Özcan et al.. *The Lancet Regional Health - Europe* (2025-02). [doi:10.1016/j.lanepe.2024.101160](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.lanepe.2024.101160)
  - 📎 [[papers/breast/internal-mammary-node-irradiation-in-4541-node-positive-breast-c.pdf|DBCG IMN2 (full text)]]

> [!question] Open questions
> - Randomized confirmation of IMNI benefit in 1-3 node patients
> - IMNI value in neoadjuvant-treated patients (excluded here)
> - IMNI cardiac safety with modern breath-hold or proton planning

## 🌰 Prostate

PEACE V-STORM pits elective nodal RT against metastasis-directed therapy for pelvic nodal oligorecurrence.

### PEACE V-STORM · [[NCT03569241]]

> 4-yr MFS 76% vs 63% favoring ENRT over MDT for pelvic nodal oligorecurrence, HR 0·62 (80% CI 0·44-0·86, p=0·063).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read: whole-pelvis ENRT (45 Gy/25fx + SIB 65 Gy) beat node-only MDT on 4-yr MFS (76% vs 63%, HR 0·62), consistent with occult pelvic nodal disease driving the failures MDT leaves untreated. Moves the elective-nodal-coverage decision in ≤5-node pelvic recurrence.

- 📊 1° EP metastasis-free survival by arm (mITT, 4-yr)

  | Endpoint | ENRT (80% CI) | MDT (80% CI) | HR (80% CI) |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | 4-yr MFS | 76% (69-81) | 63% (56-69) | 0·62 (0·44-0·86), p=0·063 |

- 🔍 Phase 2 open-label RCT, 196 randomised (MDT 99, ENRT 97) across 21 hospitals, 6 countries
- 🔍 Population: PET-detected pelvic nodal oligorecurrence (≤5 nodes), post radical local Rx, WHO PS 0-1
- 🔍 ENRT: 45 Gy/25fx whole pelvis + SIB 65 Gy to PET-positive nodes (or salvage LND), 6mo ADT
- 🔍 MDT: SBRT 30 Gy/3fx every other day (or salvage LND), 6mo ADT
- ⚠️ Grade 3 AEs by arm (numerically higher with ENRT)

  | Grade 3 AE | ENRT | MDT |
  |---|---|---|
  | Urinary incontinence | 10% | 6% |
  | Diarrhoea | 2% | 1% |

- 🔗 First randomised ENRT-vs-MDT head-to-head; prior oligomet RCTs (STOMP, ORIOLE) tested MDT vs observation, not elective nodal RT
- ⚠️ 1° EP p=0·063 on an 80% CI phase 2 screening design; authors frame ENRT as potential SOC awaiting phase 3

**Sources:**
- 📄 **Salvage metastasis-directed therapy versus elective nodal radiotherapy for oligorecurrent nodal prostate cancer metastases (PEACE V–STORM): a phase 2, open-label, randomised controlled trial** — Piet Ost; Shankar Siva; Sigmund Brabrand et al.. *The Lancet Oncology* (2025-05). [doi:10.1016/s1470-2045(25)00197-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/s1470-2045(25%2900197-4)
  - 📎 [[papers/prostate/salvage-metastasis-directed-therapy-versus-elective-nodal-radiot.pdf|PEACE V-STORM (full text)]]

> [!question] Open questions
> - Phase 3 confirmation of ENRT superiority over MDT
> - Whether the MFS benefit translates to overall survival
> - Long-term GU and GI toxicity of whole-pelvis ENRT with SIB
