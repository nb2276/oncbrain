## 🎀 Breast

De-escalating axillary surgery in a setting where the nodal basin is still being irradiated.

### SENOMAC · [[NCT02240472]]

> 5-yr RFS 89.7% vs 88.7%, HR 0.89 (0.66-1.19): omitting completion ALND noninferior, with nodal RT given in ~90% of both arms.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Nodal RT with nodal target volumes reached ~90% in both arms (89.9% vs 88.4%), so SENOMAC validates dropping completion ALND within regional nodal irradiation, not omitting axillary treatment. For the RT reader it positions nodal RT as the axillary treatment when SNB shows 1-2 macromets; it does not test omitting both surgery and RT.

- 📊 1° EP is overall survival (not yet reported); this presents the prespecified secondary recurrence-free survival
- 📊 Recurrence-free survival by arm

  | Endpoint | SNB only | cALND |
  |---|---|---|
  | 5-yr RFS | 89.7% (87.5-91.9) | 88.7% (86.3-91.1) |
  | HR recurrence/death | 0.89 (0.66-1.19) | ref |

- 🔍 Phase 3 noninferiority, 1:1, N=2766 enrolled (2540 per-protocol), 67 sites in 5 countries, median f/u 46.8 mo
- 🔍 cN0 T1-T3 with 1-2 sentinel-node macrometastases (>2mm); extends beyond Z0011 to mastectomy, T3, extracapsular extension, and men
- 🔍 Nodal RT (incl. nodal target volumes) reached 89.9% (SNB-only) and 88.4% (cALND): axillary basin irradiated in ~90% of both arms
- 🔗 vs Z0011 and AMAROS: both showed no ALND benefit at 10 yr; SENOMAC confirms in a larger macromet-only cohort with new subgroups
- ⚠️ RT confounds attribution: this validates dropping ALND WITH regional nodal irradiation, not omitting axillary treatment entirely
- ⚠️ Secondary RFS at 46.8-mo median f/u is short for ER+ biology
- ⚠️ Noninferiority margin (HR 1.44) is generous

**Sources:**
