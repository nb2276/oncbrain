# 2026-05-17

**OLIGOMA: MDT + systemic therapy mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48, p=0.021) -- first RCT PFS benefit in oligoMBC.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast carried the day: OLIGOMA (first positive MDT RCT, HR 0.48) and EORTC IM-MS 20yr (BCM benefit erased by cardiac mortality) reset oligoMBC and IM field practice. In prostate, POP-RT/PEACE-2 divergence implicates PSMA staging as the key variable eliminating the WPRT gain established in 2yr-ADT-era trials.

## 🌸 Breast

Five breast datasets spanning fractionation, partial breast, IM nodal fields, and oligometastatic MDT.

### OLIGOMA (NCT04495309) · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT + systemic therapy improved mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48, p=0.021) in oligometastatic breast cancer.

![OLIGOMA (NCT04495309)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 Co-primary EP (PFS): mPFS 35.8mo (24.1-NR) vs 20.4mo (12.4-28.4), HR 0.48 (95% CI 0.25-0.91), p=0.021
- 📊 Co-primary EP (QoL): EORTC QLQ-C30 at 12wk — 72.2 vs 74.3 (diff -2.1, 95% CI -9.2-5.1); non-inferiority margin -10pts met
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=87 (44 ctrl / 43 exp); oligoMBC any line, max 5 lesions; SBRT/ablative RT to all lesions in experimental arm
- 🔍 Population: majority ER/PR+ HER2- 1L; >80% had 1-3 mets; ~2/3 lesions were bone
- ⚠️ Stopped early — enrolled <20% of original target, <40% of amended target; underpowered, wide CI (0.25-0.91) limits precision
- 🔗 First RCT to show PFS benefit from MDT in oligoMBC; adds to SABR-COMET (mixed primaries) and ongoing TAORMINA/STEREO-SEIN/OLIGAMI
- ❓ OS data immature; which subgroup (bone-only vs visceral, 1L vs later lines) drives the signal remains undefined

### IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)

> IMPORT HIGH 10yr: SIB 48Gy/15F IBTR 3.7% vs sequential 40+16Gy 3.5%, non-inferior with similar/reduced late AEs.

![10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0); 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3); 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3).](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)
*10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0); 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3); 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3).*

- 📊 10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0), 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3), 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2617, 1:1:1, pT1-3 pN0-N3a M0 BCS requiring tumour bed boost, 76 UK centres 2009-2015
- 📐 OS absolute diff at 10yr vs 40Gy/15F: -0.5 (-3.0, 2.8) for 48Gy; +1.5 (-1.4, 5.1) for 53Gy — no OS detriment
- 💊 Late AEs at 10yr: breast distortion/shrinkage <18%, induration <10%, telangiectasia <2%, oedema <2% across all arms
- 🔗 5yr IBTR (Lancet 2023): 40+16Gy 1.9%, 48Gy 2.0%, 53Gy 3.2% — 10yr data directionally consistent; 53Gy arm underperforms at both timepoints
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm IBTR numerically higher at both 5 and 10yr; authors conclude further dose escalation beyond 48Gy not advantageous
- ❓ Whether SIB benefit translates to pts receiving regional nodal irradiation or post-mastectomy RT remains unaddressed

### APBI-IMRT Florence (NCT02104895) · [[NCT02104895]]

> At 15-year f/u, PBI IMRT vs WBI: IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17) -- no difference in LRR, DM, or OS.

![IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)
*IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%.*

- 📊 15-yr IBTR: APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%; HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 -- not statistically significant
- 🔍 IBTR signal driven by new ipsilateral primaries (APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09), not true local relapse (2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75)
- 🔍 Phase III, N=520, 1:1 randomization 2005-2013; PBI 30Gy/5fx IMRT vs WBI 50Gy/25fx + 10Gy TBB; selected pts: BCS, pT <25mm, FSM ≥5mm, age >40
- 📊 No differences in locoregional recurrence (7.2% vs 5.0%, p=0.28), distant metastasis (2.7% vs 4.6%, p=0.35), or overall mortality (21.5% vs 19.6%, p=0.66) at 15 yrs
- 🔗 Consistent with IMPORT LOW and GEC-ESTRO APBI data showing non-inferiority of partial-breast approaches in selected low-risk early BC
- ⚠️ Numerical IBTR doubling (7.7% vs 4.2%) didn't reach significance but trial powered for 5-yr equivalence margin of 5% -- underpowered to detect late divergence at 15 yrs
- ❓ Whether the new-primary signal reflects inadequate field coverage vs independent second tumors requires genomic/pathologic analysis not reported here

### EORTC IM-MS trial (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS 20-yr OS: 61.0% vs 61.8% (HR 1.00, p=0.967) — no OS benefit from IM-MS RT in ITT.

![BCM rate: 18.6% vs 22.4%, HR=0.82 (0.72-0.95), p=0.006. Non-BCM rate: 20.4% vs 15.8%, HR=1.26 (1.09-1.46), p=0.002. 15-year timepoint shown.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh9WMAAK6Lh.jpg)
*BCM rate: 18.6% vs 22.4%, HR=0.82 (0.72-0.95), p=0.006. Non-BCM rate: 20.4% vs 15.8%, HR=1.26 (1.09-1.46), p=0.002. 15-year timepoint shown.*

- 📊 1° EP OS at 20 yrs: 61.0% (+IM-MS) vs 61.8% (−IM-MS); HR=1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967
- 📊 BCM rate 18.6% vs 22.4% (HR=0.82, 95% CI 0.72-0.95, p=0.006) — breast cancer mortality benefit persists at 20 yrs
- ⚠️ non-BCM mortality 20.4% vs 15.8% (HR=1.26, 95% CI 1.09-1.46, p=0.002) — competing cardiovascular/non-cancer deaths offset BCM gain, erasing OS signal
- 🔍 pN0 subgroup at 20 yrs: DFS 53.9% vs 53.6% (HR=0.93, p=0.318); DMFS 67.2% vs 67.4% (HR=0.93, p=0.397) — no benefit in node-negative pts
- 🔗 DBCG IMN2 (Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): modern IM-RT with MHD 1.2-2.3 Gy (4-9× lower than EORTC) showed OS + BCM benefit in node-positive BC — technique gap likely explains divergence
- ⚠️ EORTC trial era RT: high cardiac dose exposure; non-BCM mortality signal almost certainly cardiac toxicity — results not generalisable to modern deep-inspiration breath-hold / IMRT techniques
- ❓ Whether contemporary low-cardiac-dose IM-MS RT changes the OS calculus in node-positive pts remains the operative question post-2020

### DBCG RT Natural

> DBCG RT Natural: PBI halts trial early -- 5-yr LR 9.8% (no PBI) vs 2.5% (PBI arm) in low-risk breast conservation.

![LR by PBI & ET: +RT+ET 0.0% (0/105); +RT-ET 3.0% (2/132); -RT+ET 3.7% (7/213); -RT-ET 12.2% (32/352, 95% CI 5.2-17.1%). 4% threshold line shown.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhIv1kXYAAR4xZ.jpg)
*LR by PBI & ET: +RT+ET 0.0% (0/105); +RT-ET 3.0% (2/132); -RT+ET 3.7% (7/213); -RT-ET 12.2% (32/352, 95% CI 5.2-17.1%). 4% threshold line shown.*

- 📊 Median f/u 4 yrs: 39/41 LR events occurred in no-PBI arm; PBI arm CIF 2.5% (1/103) vs no-PBI 9.8% (19/272, 95% CI 5.9-14.9%)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=926 planned (pT1N0, ER+, HER2 normal, grade 1-2, BCT); primary EP 5-yr invasive LR, threshold 4%; trial stopped early
- 💊 Subgroup by ET: +RT+ET CIF 0.0% (0/105); +RT-ET 3.0% (2/132); -RT+ET 3.7% (7/213); -RT-ET 12.2% (32/352)
- 🔗 Consistent with PRIME II and CALGB 9343 showing omission feasible in selected pts -- but those trials required ET compliance; here ET non-adherence in no-RT arm drove excess LR
- ⚠️ Self-selection bias: no-PBI arm included pts who opted out of RT, not purely randomized -- may overstate LR risk of protocol-randomized omission
- ⚠️ 4-yr median f/u short for breast cancer LR endpoint; late events likely undercounted in both arms
- ❓ Whether strict ET adherence (>4.5 yr) would have closed the gap in -RT+ET subgroup (CIF 3.7%) remains unanswered

## 🍇 Prostate

Two WPRT trials in high-risk PCa, opposite results -- staging era explains more than treatment era.

### PRIME Trial (NCT03561961) · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME: 5-fraction SBRT + whole-pelvis RT + ADT non-inferior to moderate hypofractionation in high-risk/node-positive PCa at interim; mature data pending.

- 🔍 Phase III open-label non-inferiority RCT, N=434, high-risk/very high-risk/node-positive non-metastatic PCa; 1:1 SBRT 36.25 Gy/5fx vs moderate hypo ~68 Gy/25fx; both arms whole-pelvis RT + 2yr ADT
- 📊 1° EP BFFS (Phoenix definition): interim early data show no signal of inferiority for SBRT arm; mature 4-5yr results awaited
- 📐 No HR/CI reported for BFFS at interim; efficacy data immature — MFS and OS also show no early difference
- 💊 Acute GU/GI grade 2+ toxicity: SBRT ~5.4%/~2.2% vs moderate hypo ~4.0%/~3.7%; grade 3+ GU/GI <1% both arms
- 💊 Late grade 2+ GU toxicity: SBRT ~10-12% vs moderate hypo ~9-11% (NS); late GI ~5-7% vs ~4-6% (NS, p=0.20/0.59)
- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (Lancet Oncol 2019/2024 10-yr): HYPO-RT-PC confirmed non-inferiority of 7fx (42.7 Gy) vs conventional in node-negative, no-ADT pts — PRIME extends SBRT into node-positive disease with mandatory whole-pelvis RT and ADT, a meaningfully higher-risk population
- ⚠️ Interim analysis only with 1-2yr follow-up; BFFS in high-risk PCa typically assessed at 5yr; no efficacy numbers beyond 'no signal of inferiority' — cannot yet conclude non-inferiority formally

### POP-RT vs PEACE-2

> POP-RT (2yr ADT): WPRT improved bFFS HR 0.50, MFS HR 0.72; PEACE-2 (3yr ADT, modern staging): no significant gain in any endpoint.

![bFFS/bPFS: POP-RT HR 0.50 (0.42-0.61) p<0.001; PEACE-2 HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16) p=0.73. cFFS/cPFS: POP-RT HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90) p=0.002; PEACE-2 HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03) p=0.09. MFS: POP-RT HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89) p=0.002; PEACE-2 HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17) p=0.54.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkfsYnaYAAfa1-.jpg)
*bFFS/bPFS: POP-RT HR 0.50 (0.42-0.61) p<0.001; PEACE-2 HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16) p=0.73. cFFS/cPFS: POP-RT HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90) p=0.002; PEACE-2 HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03) p=0.09. MFS: POP-RT HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89) p=0.002; PEACE-2 HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17) p=0.54.*

- 📊 POP-RT bFFS: HR 0.50 (95% CI 0.42-0.61), p<0.001 favoring WPRT; cFFS HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002; MFS HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002
- 📊 PEACE-2 bPFS: HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73; cPFS HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09; MFS HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 — no endpoint reached significance
- 🔍 POP-RT: Ph III, high/very high-risk N0M0, conventional staging, 2yr ADT, prostate dose 74-76 Gy EQD2, f/u 6.3-7.2yr; PEACE-2: Ph III, very high-risk N0M0, PSMA PET/CT staging, 3yr ADT, 78 Gy EQD2, median f/u ~5.5yr (interim, ESTRO 2026)
- ⚠️ Era confounding is the dominant issue: PEACE-2 used PSMA PET/CT, which upstages ~25-30% of conventional high-risk pts to M1 — those men never enrolled, leaving a cleaner (lower-risk) cohort where pelvic RT adds less
- ⚠️ PEACE-2 also used higher dose escalation and longer ADT, both of which may independently reduce residual pelvic nodal burden — shrinking the incremental benefit of WPRT
- 🔗 Consistent with RTOG 0924 signal: elective nodal RT benefit may be diluted by intensified systemic + local therapy in contemporary cohorts
- ❓ Key open question: does PSMA PET/CT-staged, node-negative high-risk PCa still benefit from WPRT in the era of ARSI + dose-escalated RT?

### RCC SBRT local control

> RCC SBRT: 100% local control at 5 years reported — no effect size beyond LC rate in source tweets.

- 📊 1° EP: 100% local control at 5 years per source tweet — no additional efficacy data reported
- 🔍 setting: RCC treated with SBRT; no trial name, N, phase, or design details in source tweets
- ⚠️ single tweet with no linked data, no NCT, no institutional series detail — cannot verify endpoint definition or f/u methodology
- 🔗 consistent with published single-institution SBRT RCC series (e.g., Siva et al., Correa et al.) showing LC rates >90-95% at 2-3 yrs; 5-yr 100% LC claim exceeds most published benchmarks

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

OligoCare (N=2447, 57 institutions) is the largest prospective SBRT oligometastases registry reported to date.

### OligoCare (EORTC prospective registry)

> OligoCare: SBRT for oligomets achieves 88.6% local control at 3 yrs across 2447 pts, 57 institutions.

![Local in-field progression by primary: 1yr/3yr — Prostate 2.7%/8.1%, NSCLC 6.0%/9.8%, Breast 4.1%/11.3%, CRC 9.3%/19.6%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg6aU7WAAEf5lR.jpg)
*Local in-field progression by primary: 1yr/3yr — Prostate 2.7%/8.1%, NSCLC 6.0%/9.8%, Breast 4.1%/11.3%, CRC 9.3%/19.6%.*

- 📊 Local in-field progression: 5.0% at 1yr, 11.4% at 3yrs (inverse: 88.6% LC at 3yrs)
- 🔍 Prospective registry, N=2447 pts / 3533 lesions, 57 institutions, median f/u 31mo; sites: bone 25%, lung 23%, spine 22%, liver 9%, brain 7%
- 📊 LC by primary: prostate best (2.7% / 8.1% at 1/3yr), CRC worst (9.3% / 19.6%)
- 🔗 CRC local failure 19.6% at 3yrs despite highest median dose/fraction — consistent with known CRC radioresistance; suggests dose escalation or combination strategies needed
- 🔍 Minimum PTV dose identified as most critical technical factor for local control
- ⚠️ Registry design: no randomization, no systemic therapy data reported, heterogeneous histologies pooled; LC rates reflect real-world practice variation, not optimized protocol
- ❓ OS and PFS outcomes not reported in this interim; local control surrogacy for survival in OMD remains debated
