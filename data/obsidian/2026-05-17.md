# 2026-05-17

**OLIGOMA: oligometastatic breast MDT vs systemic alone, mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48), first breast oligo RCT PFS signal despite N=87 early stop.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast and oligo-mets dominated today. OLIGOMA provides the first RCT PFS benefit for MDT in oligometastatic breast (HR 0.48), though severely underpowered. EORTC IM-MS 20y confirms IM-MS BCM gain (HR 0.82) is real but fully offset by excess cardiac mortality under older techniques, contextualizing why modern low-dose cardiac technique is now standard.

## 🌸 Breast

Five RCTs spanning 10-20yr follow-up converge on RT field precision over omission and settle the cardiac cost of older IM-MS technique.

### IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)

> IMPORT HIGH 10y: SIB 48Gy/15F IBTR 3.7% vs sequential 40+16Gy 3.5%, non-inferior with similar late AEs.

![IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)

| Arm | 10-yr IBTR (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| 40Gy/15F + 16Gy/8F | 3.5% (2.4, 5.0) |
| 48Gy/15F (3.2Gy/F) | 3.7% (2.6, 5.3) |
| 53Gy/15F (3.5Gy/F) | 5.5% (4.1, 7.3) |

- 📊 10-year IBTR rates (95% CI) by arm

  | Arm | 10-yr IBTR (95% CI) |
  |---|---|
  | 40Gy/15F + 16Gy/8F (sequential) | 3.5% (2.4, 5.0) |
  | 48Gy/15F SIB (3.2Gy/F) | 3.7% (2.6, 5.3) |
  | 53Gy/15F SIB (3.5Gy/F) | 5.5% (4.1, 7.3) |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2617, 76 UK hospitals (2009-2015), 1:1:1, pT1-3 pN0-N3a M0 requiring tumour bed boost after BCS
- 📐 OS at 10y vs 40Gy/15F: abs diff -0.5% (-3.0, 2.8) for 48Gy; +1.5% (-1.4, 5.1) for 53Gy — no OS signal either arm
- 💊 Late mod/marked AEs at 10y: breast distortion/shrinkage <18%, induration <10%, telangiectasia <2%, breast oedema <2% across all groups
- 🔗 5-yr IBTR from Lancet 2023 publication: 1.9% (sequential), 2.0% (48Gy SIB), 3.2% (53Gy SIB) — 10-yr data confirm non-inferiority of 48Gy SIB holds
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm shows numerically higher IBTR (5.5% vs 3.5-3.7%) without OS benefit — further dose escalation not advantageous per investigators
- ❓ Whether SIB benefit extends to pts with nodal irradiation or post-mastectomy RT remains unaddressed by this dataset

### OLIGOMA (NCT04495309) · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT + systemic therapy vs systemic alone in oligometastatic breast cancer; mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48 favoring MDT.

![OLIGOMA (NCT04495309)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 Co-primary endpoint PFS — comparison values omitted (cell number "0.91" not verified in source)
- 📊 Co-primary endpoint QoL at 12 wks: EORTC QLQ-C30 mean 72.2 vs 74.3; between-group difference -2.1 (-9.2 to 5.1) — non-inferiority margin -10pts met
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=87 (44 exp, 43 ctrl); oligometastatic breast any line, ≤5 lesions; SBRT/SABR to all metastatic sites; systemic regimen per MDT; stratified by systemic type + line
- ⚠️ Stopped early (<20% of original target, <40% of amended target) — severely underpowered; HR CI wide (0.25-0.91) and crosses near 1 at upper bound; OS data not reported
- 🔍 Majority ER/PR+ HER2- first-line; >80% had 1-3 metastases; 2/3 of lesions were bone — limits generalizability to HER2+/TNBC or visceral-dominant disease
- 🔗 First RCT showing PFS benefit from MDT in oligometastatic breast; prior data from SABR-COMET (mixed histologies) and OligoCare (registry) suggested benefit but lacked breast-specific RCT evidence
- ❓ Patient selection for MDT benefit unclear; multiple ongoing trials (TAORMINA, STEREO-SEIN, LARA, CLEAR, COSMO, ARCHER, ISTMET, OLIGAMI) may inform subgroup definition

### APBI-IMRT Florence (NCT02104895) · [[NCT02104895]]

> 15-year phase III APBI vs WBI in early breast cancer: IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2%, HR 1.57 (0.82-3.04), p=0.17.

![APBI-IMRT Florence (NCT02104895)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJaWEAAdjH2.jpg)

| Endpoint | APBI 15yr N (%) | WBI 15yr N (%) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| IBTR | 20 (7.7) | 11 (4.2) | 0.14 |
| Local relapse | 5 (2.1) | 4 (1.6) | 0.75 |
| New ipsilateral primary | 15 (5.9) | 7 (2.7) | 0.09 |
| Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
| Distant metastasis | 7 (2.7) | 12 (4.6) | 0.35 |
| Deaths | 56 (21.5) | 51 (19.6) | 0.66 |

- 📊 1° EP IBTR at 15yr: APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%, HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 — no significant difference
- 🔍 Phase III, N=520, 1:1 randomization 2005-2013; BCS pts, pT <25mm, FSM ≥5mm, age >40; APBI 30Gy/5# IMRT vs WBI 50Gy/25# + 10Gy TBB
- ⚠️ Numerical IBTR excess in APBI driven by new ipsilateral primaries (15 vs 7), not true local recurrences (5 vs 4) — questions whether this reflects field effect or biology, not RT failure
- 📊 Key outcomes at 15 years (ITT), APBI vs WBI

  | Endpoint | APBI N (%) | WBI N (%) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | IBTR | 20 (7.7) | 11 (4.2) | 0.14 |
  | Local relapse | 5 (2.1) | 4 (1.6) | 0.75 |
  | New ipsilateral primary | 15 (5.9) | 7 (2.7) | 0.09 |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
  | Distant metastasis | 7 (2.7) | 12 (4.6) | 0.35 |
  | Deaths | 56 (21.5) | 51 (19.6) | 0.66 |
  | Breast cancer deaths | 6 (2.3) | 8 (3.1) | 0.79 |

- 🔗 vs IMPORT/RAPID (10-yr data): similar pattern of IBTR numerical excess with APBI, no OS difference; Florence adds 15-yr maturity confirming no late divergence in BC-specific survival
- ⚠️ Trial powered for 5-yr IBTR equivalence (delta 5%); 15-yr analysis is secondary/descriptive — not powered to exclude a clinically meaningful late difference
- ❓ Whether new ipsilateral primaries in APBI field reflect inadequate subclinical disease coverage or independent second primaries remains unresolved; implications for patient selection differ substantially

### EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS 20y: OS identical (61.0% vs 61.8%, HR 1.00) despite BCM benefit; excess non-BCM mortality offsets gain.

![EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh9WMAAK6Lh.jpg)

| Endpoint | +IM-MS RT | -IM-MS RT | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BCM rate | 18.6% | 22.4% | 0.82 (0.72-0.95) | 0.006 |
| Non-BCM mortality rate | 20.4% | 15.8% | 1.26 (1.09-1.46) | 0.002 |

- 📊 1° EP OS at 20y: 61.0% (+IM-MS) vs 61.8% (-IM-MS), HR 1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967 — no OS benefit
- 📊 Competing mortality at 20y (ITT)

  | Endpoint | +IM-MS RT | -IM-MS RT | HR (95% CI) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | BCM rate | 18.6% | 22.4% | 0.82 (0.72-0.95) | 0.006 |
  | Non-BCM mortality rate | 20.4% | 15.8% | 1.26 (1.09-1.46) | 0.002 |

- ⚠️ BCM reduction fully offset by excess non-BCM mortality — likely cardiac toxicity from older RT techniques with high mean heart doses
- 🔍 Randomized phase III; stage I-III breast cancer; ITT analysis; 20-year follow-up
- 📊 pN0 subgroup at 20y: no benefit

  | Endpoint | +IM-MS RT | -IM-MS RT | HR (95% CI) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | DFS rate | 53.9% | 53.6% | 0.93 (0.81-1.07) | 0.318 |
  | DMFS rate | 67.2% | 67.4% | 0.93 (0.78-1.10) | 0.397 |

- 🔗 DBCG IMN2 (Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): MHD 1.2 Gy (right) / 2.3 Gy (left) — 4-9x lower than EORTC; showed IM-MS benefit at 15y in node-positive pts, suggesting modern technique resolves the cardiac offset seen here
- ❓ Whether contemporary low-dose cardiac IM-MS RT yields net OS benefit in node-negative (pN0) pts remains unanswered

### DBCG RT Natural

> DBCG RT Natural: omitting PBI in low-risk early BC (pT1N0 grade 1-2) yielded 8.2% 4-yr LR vs 1.5% with PBI; -RT -ET arm exceeded pre-specified 4% threshold.

![DBCG RT Natural](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhIv1kXYAAR4xZ.jpg)

- 📊 4-yr CIF LR: randomized +RT ~1.5% vs randomized -RT 8.2% (4.5-13.3%) vs self-selecting -RT 9.8% (5.9-14.9%); 39/41 LRs in no-PBI group
- 🔍 phase III RCT; pop: ≥60 yr, pT1N0, breast conservation, HER2 normal, grade 1-2, unifocal, margin ≥2mm; PBI 40Gy/15fr vs no RT; primary EP 5-yr invasive LR; pre-specified max-acceptable threshold 4%; N=926 planned
- 📊 4-yr LR CIF by RT and ET status — comparison values omitted (cell number "1.3" not verified in source)
- ⚠️ -RT -ET arm drove threshold breach (12.2%); curator notes ET adherence was suboptimal — this may reflect real-world non-adherence rather than a clean RT-omission biologic effect; -RT +ET at 3.7% stayed just below 4%
- 🔗 vs PRIME II (Kunkler, Lancet Oncol 2015): similar ≥65yr ER+ pT1-2 pop omitting RT with ET — consistent signal that RT omission carries meaningful LR risk even in low-risk selected pts; DBCG confirms in ≥60yr cohort
- ❓ 5-fraction PBI regimens may improve patient acceptance vs 15fr used here; whether improved ET adherence alone can substitute for RT in -RT +ET subgroup (3.7% CIF) needs longer f/u

> [!question] Open questions
> - Does improved ET adherence alone approach RT-equivalent LR control in the DBCG -RT +ET subgroup (4-yr CIF 3.7%), or will longer follow-up reveal late divergence?
> - Are the excess new ipsilateral primaries after APBI-IMRT Florence at 15yr (15 vs 7) a field-effect failure or independent second primaries, and should the distinction change patient selection criteria?
> - Does modern IM-MS RT (MHD <2Gy, per DBCG IMN2) yield net OS benefit in pN0 pts where EORTC 20y showed no DFS or DMFS signal at any time point?

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

OligoCare confirms SBRT achieves durable multi-histology local control but exposes CRC as a persistent outlier with 19.6% 3-yr local failure despite high dose-per-fraction.

### OligoCare (EORTC)

> OligoCare registry: SBRT for oligomets achieves 88.6% local control at 3 yrs across 2447 pts, 57 institutions.

![OligoCare (EORTC)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg6aU7WAAEf5lR.jpg)

| Primary | 1-year LF | 3-year LF |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate | 2.7% | 8.1% |
| NSCLC | 6.0% | 9.8% |
| Breast | 4.1% | 11.3% |
| CRC | 9.3% | 19.6% |

- 📊 Local in-field progression: 5.0% at 1 yr, 11.4% at 3 yrs (median f/u 31 months, N=2447 pts, 3533 lesions)
- 📊 Local in-field progression by primary tumor type at 1 yr / 3 yrs

  | Primary | 1-year LF | 3-year LF |
  |---|---|---|
  | Prostate | 2.7% | 8.1% |
  | NSCLC | 6.0% | 9.8% |
  | Breast | 4.1% | 11.3% |
  | CRC | 9.3% | 19.6% |

- 🔍 Prospective registry (EORTC OligoCare), 57 institutions, July 2019-July 2025; median age 69 yrs, 69% male; met sites: bone 25%, lung 23%, lymph node 16%, liver 9%, brain 7%
- ⚠️ Registry design — no randomized control arm, heterogeneous primary histologies and met burden; local control comparisons across primaries are observational
- 🔗 CRC highest local failure despite highest median dose per fraction — consistent with known CRC radioresistance seen in SBRT series; suggests dose escalation or combination strategies needed
- 📐 Minimum PTV dose identified as most critical technical factor for local control; de novo OMD outperforms repeat OMD (higher delivered dose in de novo cohort)
- ❓ Optimal SBRT dose fractionation for CRC oligo-mets and role of systemic combination (e.g., IO) remain open

## 🍇 Prostate

### PRIME Trial (NCT03561961) · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim (ESTRO 2026): 5-fx SBRT non-inferior trend vs 25-fx hypo for BFFS in high-risk/node+ PCa; grade 3+ toxicity <1% both arms.

![PRIME Trial (NCT03561961)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)

| Domain | SBRT 5-fx | Moderate Hypo 25-fx |
|---|---|---|
| Acute GU ≥2 | ~5.4% | ~4.0% |
| Acute GI ≥2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% |
| Late GU ≥2 | ~10-12% | ~9-11% |
| Late GI ≥2 | ~5-7% | ~4-6% |
| Grade 3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% |

- 🔍 Phase III randomized non-inferiority; N~434; high-risk/VHR and/or node-positive non-metastatic PCa; both arms whole-pelvis RT + ~2yr ADT; 1° EP BFFS (Phoenix nadir+2)
- 💊 A: 36.25 Gy/5 fx SBRT EOD; B: ~68 Gy/25 fx; both + pelvic 25 Gy/5 fx; SIB to positive nodes allowed in SBRT arm
- 📊 Interim BFFS/MFS/OS (1-2 yr f/u): no signal of inferiority for SBRT; no formal HR/CI/p reported; 4-5 yr mature results awaited
- 📊 Toxicity by arm (interim, up to 1-2 yr)

  | Domain | SBRT 5-fx | Moderate Hypo 25-fx |
  |---|---|---|
  | Acute GU ≥2 | ~5.4% | ~4.0% |
  | Acute GI ≥2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% |
  | Late GU ≥2 | ~10-12% | ~9-11% |
  | Late GI ≥2 | ~5-7% | ~4-6% |
  | Grade 3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% |

- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC 10-yr: adjusted HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.69-1.03), non-inferior in intermediate/high-risk node-negative PCa without ADT — PRIME extends to node-positive + WPRT + ADT
- 🔗 WPRT benefit context: POP-RT (2yr ADT, conventional imaging) vs PEACE-2 (3yr ADT, PSMA PET/CT)

  | Endpoint | POP-RT | PEACE-2 |
  |---|---|---|
  | bFFS/bPFS | HR 0.50 (0.42-0.61), p<0.001 | HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73 |
  | cFFS/cPFS | HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09 |
  | MFS | HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- ⚠️ All efficacy conclusions are early trends only; non-inferiority formally undetermined until 4-5 yr primary analysis; OS data immature
