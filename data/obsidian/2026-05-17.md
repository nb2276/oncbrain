# 2026-05-17

**OLIGOMA: first breast oligomets MDT RCT; mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4 mo, HR 0.48 (stopped early, N=87).**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast and prostate carried the meeting. OLIGOMA (first breast MDT RCT, HR 0.48) and DBCG RT Natural (PBI 4-yr LR 1.5% vs 9.8% no-RT) frame opposite intensity directions in breast. PEACE-2 null for pelvic RT in VHR prostate (HR 0.81, p=0.088) challenges POP-RT extrapolation in the PSMA-staged era.

## 🌰 Prostate

Prostate RT today: one efficacy null (PEACE-2 pelvic RT) and three phase 3 trials reporting toxicity-first readouts with primary endpoints still pending.

### PEACE-2

> PEACE-2: pelvic RT no significant cPFS benefit vs prostate-only in VHR localized disease (HR 0.81, p=0.088).

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6;72.2] | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4;68.1] | reference | - |

- 📊 All secondary endpoints (bPFS, MFS, CSS, OS) also null; no significant benefit on any efficacy measure
- 🔍 Phase III, 4-arm: ADT 3yr ± cabazitaxel x4 × pelvic or prostate-only RT; VHR localized N0M0
- 🔍 VHR eligibility: ≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20; PSMA PET/CT staging widely used
- 🔗 WPRT vs prostate-only RT: POP-RT vs PEACE-2 cross-trial comparison

  | Endpoint | POP-RT HR (95%CI) | PEACE-2 HR (95%CI) |
  |---|---|---|
  | bFFS/bPFS | 0.50 (0.42-0.61), p<0.001 | 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73 |
  | cFFS/cPFS | 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09 |
  | MFS | 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- ⚠️ Interim analysis (median f/u ~5.5yr); prespecified final analysis pending
- ⚠️ PSMA PET staging filters out occult pelvic mets, shrinking baseline nodal risk for pelvic RT to address
- ⚠️ 3yr ADT + 78Gy EQD2 (vs 2yr + 74-76Gy in POP-RT) may sterilize subclinical pelvic disease before RT field matters

### PACE-NODES

> PACE-NODES: PPN-SBRT raised CTCAE ≥2 GI toxicity (28% vs 21%); resolved by wk 12; no acute GU difference; efficacy data pending.

![PACE-NODES](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLTUXoAAbiFs.jpg)

| Arm | CTCAE ≥2 GI (over 12wk) |
|---|---|
| PPN-SBRT | 28% |
| P-SBRT | 21% |

- 📊 No difference in acute GU toxicity (clinician or patient-reported)
- 📊 GI differences at 4wk reflected in EPIC-26 bowel domain (PRO); resolved with no difference at 12wk
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1166 randomised 1:1; high-risk localised prostate (T3a-T4 and/or Gleason 8-10 and/or PSA>20) + 12-36mo ADT
- 💊 PPN-SBRT: 36.25Gy/5f prostate + 25Gy/5f pelvic nodes on alternate days; P-SBRT: 36.25Gy/5f prostate only
- ⚠️ 11% PPN-SBRT vs 4% P-SBRT did not receive allocated treatment, mostly due to planning constraints
- ⚠️ Primary efficacy endpoint (time to BCF) not mature; this analysis is acute toxicity only
- 🔗 Prior moderate-hypofractionation nodal RT trials (e.g. POP-RT) showed BCF benefit in high-risk prostate; PACE-NODES extends the approach to ultrahypofractionated 5f SBRT

### PIVOTALboost

> PIVOTALboost phase 3 (N=1465): focal boost + pelvic node RT (20 fx) safe; no late G2+ bowel/bladder increase vs prostate-only at 2 yrs.

![PIVOTALboost](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiVWznXoAAqNwC.jpg)

| Arm | G2+ Bowel (2yr) | G2+ Bladder (2yr) |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate (n=281) | 8.2% (5.7-11.7) | 19.5% (15.5-24.4) |
| Prostate+Boost (n=345) | 8.7% (6.3-12.1) | 24.1% (20.2-28.7) |
| Prostate+Nodes+Boost (n=347) | 6.5% (4.5-9.5) | 16.5% (13.1-20.6) |

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1465 randomised, 39 UK centres; 20-fraction IMRT ± focal boost ± pelvic node irradiation; high-risk localised prostate
- 🔍 Primary endpoint: biochemical/clinical failure (not reported here); acute and 2-yr late toxicity are secondary endpoints
- ⚠️ Pelvic node RT increased early bowel toxicity (up to 18 wks post-RT) but resolved fully by 18 weeks
- 🔗 vs FLAME (JCO 2021): focal boost improved bDFS without added toxicity in conventional fractionation; PIVOTALboost extends the safety finding to a 20-fraction regimen
- ⚠️ Efficacy data pending; 2-yr f/u is preliminary for BCF endpoint in high-risk pts

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim: G3+ toxicity <1% both arms; no BFFS inferiority signal for 5-fx SBRT vs 25-fx moderate hypofractionation; 4-5yr data pending.

- 📊 BFFS (primary endpoint): early data show no signal of inferiority for SBRT arm; no HR reported in source
- 🔍 Treatment regimens (both arms: prostate + whole-pelvis RT + long-course ADT ~2yr)
  - Arm A (SBRT): 36.25 Gy/5 fx every other day + pelvic 25 Gy/5 fx
  - Arm B (Mod Hypo): ~68 Gy/25 fx × 5 weeks + pelvic 25 Gy/5 fx
- 🔍 Phase III open-label RCT, N~434, 1:1; high-risk/very high-risk/node+ non-metastatic PCa; Tata Memorial, India; PSMA PET/CT staging allowed
- 📊 Acute toxicity (≤90 days)

  | Toxicity | SBRT (5 fx) | Mod Hypo (25 fx) |
  |---|---|---|
  | GU G≥2 | ~5.4% | ~4.0% |
  | GI G≥2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% |
  | G3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% |

- 📊 Late toxicity (up to 1-2 years)

  | Toxicity | SBRT (5 fx) | Mod Hypo (25 fx) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | GU G≥2 | ~10-12% | ~9-11% | 0.59 |
  | GI G≥2 | ~5-7% | ~4-6% | 0.20 |
  | G3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% | — |

- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (10-yr data): adj HR 0.84 (0.69-1.03) non-inferior; different population (N-neg, no ADT, 7 fx, no pelvic RT)
- ⚠️ Interim analysis; 4-5yr follow-up required for primary BFFS read; OS/MFS immature; no BFFS HR reported
- ⚠️ Single-institution Indian cohort (Tata Memorial); generalizability to Western pts uncertain

## 🎀 Breast

Dense breast day: de-escalation signals in low-risk disease (boost omission, PBI vs no-RT), 10-yr fractionation confirmations, and the first MDT RCT specific to breast oligomets.

### Dutch breast boost (DCBRG analysis)

> Dutch registry (N>30k): 10-yr IBTR 1.2% with or without boost in 0-2 RF pts; boost may be omitted in modern systemic era.

![Dutch breast boost (DCBRG analysis)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdFyGwWoAAD-Ft.jpg)

| RF group | N (no boost / boost) | 5yr IBTR (no boost / boost) | 10yr IBTR (no boost / boost) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-2 | 15,085 / 13,845 | 0.6% / 0.7% | 1.2% / 1.2% |
| ≥3 | 149 / 733 | 1.3% / 2.9% | 2.7% / 3.3% |
| Uncertain | 592 / 944 | 0.8% / 3.3% | 1.4% / 3.6% |

- 🔍 Retrospective DBRT registry, Dutch BCT pts 2012-2016; IBTR identified via pathology report algorithm
- 🔍 Five risk factors defined
  - Age ≤40 years
  - Grade 3 tumour
  - Triple-negative tumour
  - Guideline-indicated systemic therapy not received
  - No pCR post-NAC (TNBC or HER2+)
- ⚠️ Boost allocation not randomized; ≥3 RF no-boost arm N=149 vs 733 boosted — high-RF pts preferentially boosted
- ⚠️ 'Uncertain' RF group: 5-yr IBTR 3.3% with boost vs 0.8% without — likely reflects selection of higher-risk pts into the boosted arm, not a boost-harm signal
- 🔗 EORTC 22881/10882 (Lancet Oncol 2015, pre-modern systemic era): boost reduced IBTR ~50%; present data questions residual benefit with contemporary systemic therapy
- 🔗 Assisi thresholds (2025): boost omission acceptable if no-boost 10-yr IBTR <6% and boosted <3%; both met in 0-2 RF group

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT (LAR + systemic) vs systemic alone in oligometastatic breast; mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4 mo, HR 0.48, first breast RCT.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 QoL co-primary EP met (non-inferiority): EORTC QLQ-C30 72.2 vs 74.3
- 📐 QoL group difference -2.1 (-9.2-5.1); non-inferiority margin -10 pts
- 🔍 Randomized, N=87; LAR to all mets + systemic vs systemic alone; stratified by systemic type and line
- 🔍 ≤5 oligometastatic lesions, any treatment line; >80% had 1-3 mets
- 🔍 2/3 of metastatic lesions were bone
- 🔍 Majority ER/PR+, HER2-; ~74% first-line endocrine or chemotherapy
- ⚠️ Stopped early: <20% of initial planned enrollment, <40% of amended target; early stopping inflates PFS estimate
- ⚠️ No OS data reported
- 🔗 First breast cancer-specific MDT RCT; prior MDT trials (SABR-COMET, ORIOLE) enrolled mixed histologies

### DBCG RT Natural

> DBCG RT Natural: 4-yr LR 1.5% (PBI) vs 9.8% (no PBI) in pT1N0 early BC; 0 recurrences with PBI+ET.

![DBCG RT Natural](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg_rzdWIAAlnil.jpg)

| Arm | Events/N | 4-yr LR CIF (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|
| +RT | 2/236 | 1.5% (0.3-5.1%) |
| -RT (randomized) | 19/272 | 9.8% (5.9-14.9%) |
| S-RT (self-selecting) | 18/278 | 8.2% (4.5-13.3%) |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT; ≥60 yr, pT1N0, ER+≥10%, HER2-normal, grade 1-2, breast conservation, margin ≥2mm; PBI 40Gy/15fr vs no RT
- 🔍 N≈508 randomized (planned 926); 278 additional self-selecting no-RT (non-randomized); median FU 4 yr
- 📊 4-yr LR by RT + ET status — comparison values omitted (cell number "1.3" not verified in source)
- ⚠️ Interim analysis at partial accrual; prespecified primary endpoint is 5-yr LR at full 926 pts — final analysis not yet reached

- ⚠️ Self-selecting no-RT arm (S-RT, N=278) not randomized; similar LR rate to randomized -RT arm but selection bias limits interpretation
- 🔗 EUROPA trial: RT associated with superior QoL vs endocrine-only in low-risk BC; direction consistent with this signal

### DBCG HYPO

> DBCG HYPO 10yr: 40 Gy/15fr reduced breast induration vs 50 Gy (19.5% vs 24.7%, HR 0.76) with no oncologic compromise.

![DBCG HYPO](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhgdACW0AAaAeV.jpg)

| Endpoint (10yr) | 50 Gy/25fr | 40 Gy/15fr | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Breast induration G2-3 | 24.7% | 19.5% | 0.76 (0.62-0.92) | 0.005 |
| Overall survival | 92.1% | 93.0% | 0.81 (0.63-1.04) | 0.10 |

- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority RCT 1:1; N=1,882 node-negative BC or DCIS; 2009-2014 (Denmark, Norway, Germany)
- 🔍 1° EP: 3-yr G≥2 breast induration at consecutive visits; 10-yr recurrence/OS data are prespecified secondary analyses
- 📊 No significant difference in locoregional recurrence, distant failure, or BC mortality at 10 yrs
- 🔗 Consistent with START-B (Lancet Oncol 2013): largest Scandinavian dataset confirming long-term toxicity advantage for 40 Gy/15fr
- ⚠️ Trial underpowered for OS; oncologic endpoint non-significant but directionally favors hypofractionation
- ⚠️ 2009-2014 enrollment; generalizability to contemporary IMRT/prone technique uncertain

### EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS: 20-yr OS null (HR=1.00, p=0.967) despite BCM reduction; old-era cardiac doses negated the benefit in stage I-III BC.

![EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh9WMAAK6Lh.jpg)

| Endpoint (15yr) | IM-MS+ | IM-MS- | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BCM rate | 18.6% | 22.4% | 0.82 (0.72-0.95) | 0.006 |
| non-BCM rate | 20.4% | 15.8% | 1.26 (1.09-1.46) | 0.002 |

- 📊 1° EP OS at 20yr (ITT): 61.0% vs 61.8% (IM-MS+ vs IM-MS-); HR=1.00 (0.90-1.10), p=0.967
- 📊 DFS and DMFS at 20yr (ITT): both null
  - DFS 48.2% vs 49.0%; HR=0.97 (0.89-1.06), p=0.515
  - DMFS 58.9% vs 59.8%; HR=0.97 (0.88-1.08), p=0.578
- 📊 pN0 subgroup at 20yr: DFS and DMFS both null
  - DFS 53.9% vs 53.6%; HR=0.93 (0.81-1.07), p=0.318
  - DMFS 67.2% vs 67.4%; HR=0.93 (0.78-1.10), p=0.397
- 🔍 Stage I-III BC, multicenter RCT, N=4004 (2002 per arm); EORTC era, pre-IMRT cardiac-sparing techniques
- ⚠️ RT-related late AEs (IM-MS+ vs IM-MS-)
  - Lung fibrosis: 6.3% vs 3.2%
  - Cardiac fibrosis: 2.7% vs 1.7%
  - Cardiac diseases: 15.2% vs 11.7%
- ⚠️ BCM reduction (HR=0.82 at 15yr) offset by excess non-BCM mortality (HR=1.26); net OS null at 20yr — cardiac late toxicity from old-era MHD the operative mechanism
- ⚠️ No statistical difference in secondary cancers or contralateral BC between arms

### APBI-IMRT Florence · [[NCT02104895]]

> APBI-IMRT Florence 15yr: IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17); no OS, BCSS, or DM difference.

![APBI-IMRT Florence](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)

|  | APBI | WBI | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 15-yr IBTR | 7.7% | 4.2% | 1.57 (0.82-3.04) | 0.17 |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=520, 1:1; APBI 30Gy/5# vs WBI 50Gy/25# + 10Gy TBB; enrollment 2005-2013
- 🔍 Eligibility: BCS, pT<25mm, FSM≥5mm, age>40
- 📊 15-yr IBTR excess driven by new ipsilateral primaries, not local relapse
  - Local relapse: APBI 2.1% vs WBI 1.6%, p=0.75
  - New ipsilateral BC: APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09
- 📊 No locoregional or DM difference at 15yr
  - Locoregional recurrence: APBI 7.2% vs WBI 5.0%, p=0.28
  - Distant metastasis: APBI 2.7% vs WBI 4.6%, p=0.35
- 📊 No survival difference at 15yr
  - Deaths: APBI 21.5% vs WBI 19.6%, p=0.66
  - Breast cancer deaths: APBI 2.3% vs WBI 3.1%, p=0.79
- 🔗 15-yr update of Livi EJC 2015 (5yr) + Meattini JCO 2020 (10yr); IBTR trend tracks upward but remains non-significant
- ⚠️ CI wide (HR 0.82-3.04); trial powered for 5-yr equivalence (delta 5%), not designed for 15-yr IBTR comparison
- ⚠️ Single-center, tightly selected (pT<25mm, margins≥5mm); applicability to broader APBI eligibility criteria uncertain
