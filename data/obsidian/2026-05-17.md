# 2026-05-17

**OLIGOMA: mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48, p=0.021); first positive phase III for MDT in oligometastatic breast cancer.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast and prostate carried ESTRO 2026. OLIGOMA delivers the first positive phase III for MDT in oligometastatic breast (HR 0.48); multiple 10-yr fractionation trials consolidate hypofractionation safety across the board. Prostate: PEACE-2 pelvic RT neutral in PSMA-staged era while RADIOSA adds the only randomized ADT + SBRT data in oligorecurrence (HR 0.39).

## 🌰 Prostate

Five trials, two toxicity readouts and three efficacy signals, with pelvic nodal RT benefit now in question in the PSMA-staged long-ADT era.

### PACE-NODES

> PACE-NODES: pelvic nodal SBRT adds acute GI toxicity (28% vs 21% G≥2) over prostate-only SBRT; GU toxicity no different; efficacy data pending.

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1166, high-risk localised PCa (cT3a-T4 and/or Gleason 8-10 and/or PSA>20); 12-36mo ADT; P-SBRT 36.25Gy/5f vs PPN-SBRT 36.25Gy/5f prostate + 25Gy/5f nodes on alternate days
- 📊 Primary acute toxicity EPs: CTCAE G≥2 GI and GU up to 12wk; primary efficacy EP (time to biochemical/clinical failure) not yet mature
- 📊 G≥2 GI toxicity: 28% PPN-SBRT vs 21% P-SBRT over 12wk; no difference at 12wk endpoint (symptoms resolved quickly)
- 📊 G≥2 GU toxicity: no difference between arms (clinician or patient-reported)
- 📊 Patient-reported EPIC-26 bowel domain worse in PPN-SBRT at 4wk; GU domain similar
- ⚠️ 11% PPN-SBRT and 4% P-SBRT did not receive allocated treatment, mostly constraint violations -- higher non-delivery rate in nodal arm suggests technical complexity in multicentre rollout
- 🔗 vs POP-RT and PEACE-2 (conventional fractionation pelvic nodal RT): those trials showed bcFFS benefit; PACE-NODES will test whether SBRT fractionation replicates that -- cancer control data awaited

### RADIOSA

> RADIOSA post-hoc: SBRT + short-term ADT vs SBRT alone, median MFS 16.6mo vs not reached, HR 0.39 favoring combination.

![RADIOSA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdDCQkW0AAvnIw.jpg)

| Endpoint | Arm A (SBRT) | Arm B (SBRT + ADT) |
|---|---|---|
| Metastatic progression | 32/51 (62.7%) | 19/51 (37.3%) |
| Median MFS | 16.6 mo (95% CI 12.83-NA) | Not reached |
| HR (95% CI) | 0.3894 (0.2201-0.6888) | — |
| p-value | 0.00119 (log-rank 0.00079) | — |

- 📊 Metastatic progression: 32/51 (62.7%) Arm A (SBRT) vs 19/51 (37.3%) Arm B (SBRT + ADT); median MFS 16.6mo (95% CI 12.83-NA) vs not reached
- 📐 HR 0.3894 (95% CI 0.2201-0.6888), p=0.00119; log-rank p=0.00079 — 61% risk reduction for metastatic progression
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=102, 1:1 randomization, oligorecurrent prostate cancer; median f/u 49.23mo (95% CI 42.47-54.8); post-hoc MFS/eugonadal MFS analysis
- 📊 Eugonadal MFS (after testosterone recovery) also significantly longer in Arm B (p<0.05); all but 2 Arm B pts recovered testosterone within f/u
- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis of phase II trial — not powered for MFS; primary endpoint was biochemical PFS; eugonadal MFS is a novel non-validated endpoint
- 🔗 Aligns with ORIOLE/STOMP showing metastasis-directed therapy benefit in oligorecurrence, but RADIOSA is the only randomized data adding short-term ADT to SBRT in this setting
- ❓ Whether eugonadal MFS advantage translates to OS benefit and optimal ADT duration (6mo used here) remain open

### PIVOTALboost

> PIVOTALboost: focal boost + pelvic nodal RT (20 fx) safe at 2 yrs; early bowel toxicity with nodal RT resolves by 18 weeks.

![PIVOTALboost](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiVWznXoAAqNwC.jpg)

| Arm | Late G2+ Bowel (95% CI) | Late G2+ Bladder (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate (n=281) | 8.2% (5.7-11.7) | 19.5% (15.5-24.4) |
| Prostate+Boost (n=345) | 8.7% (6.3-12.1) | 24.1% (20.2-28.7) |
| Prostate+Nodes+Boost (n=347) | 6.5% (4.5-9.5) | 16.5% (13.1-20.6) |

- 🔍 phase 3 RCT, N=1465, 39 UK centres; 3 arms: prostate IMRT alone (P), P+focal boost (P+B), P+pelvic nodes+boost (PPN+B); 20-fraction moderately hypofractionated; primary EP biochemical/clinical failure (not reported here)
- 📊 Late G3+ bowel side effects at 2 yrs: 8.2% (P), 8.7% (P+B), 6.5% (PPN+B) — no significant differences across arms
- 📊 Cumulative late G2+ side effects at 2 years (973 pts, 74% with ≥2yr f/u)
- 📊 Early bowel toxicity (≤18 wks): increased with pelvic node RT (PPN+B arm) vs prostate-only; no effect size reported in source tweets beyond visual curve separation
- ⚠️ Only secondary toxicity endpoints reported here; primary EP (biochemical/clinical failure) not yet presented — efficacy inference premature
- 🔗 Consistent with FLAME trial showing intraprostatic focal boost improves biochemical control without added late toxicity; PIVOTALboost extends this to 20-fx hypofractionation context
- ❓ Efficacy data (bFFS) pending; whether PPN+B arm translates early nodal RT toxicity spike into biochemical benefit vs P+B alone is the key unanswered question

### PEACE-2

> PEACE-2: pelvic RT did not significantly improve cPFS vs prostate-only RT (HR 0.81 [0.63-1.03], p=0.088) in very high-risk localized PCa.

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4; 68.1] | — | — |
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6; 72.2] | 0.81 [0.63; 1.03] | 0.088 |

- 🔍 Phase III 4-arm open-label RCT; very high-risk localized PCa (≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20, N0M0); ADT 3 yrs; RT 78 Gy EQD2 prostate boost; primary EP cPFS
- 🔗 WPRT vs prostate-only RT efficacy: POP-RT vs PEACE-2

  | Endpoint | POP-RT HR (95% CI), p | PEACE-2 HR (95% CI), p |
  |---|---|---|
  | bFFS/bPFS | 0.50 (0.42-0.61), <0.001 | 0.97 (0.81-1.16), 0.73 |
  | cFFS/cPFS | 0.74 (0.61-0.90), 0.002 | 0.81 (0.63-1.03), 0.09 |
  | MFS | 0.72 (0.58-0.89), 0.002 | 0.93 (0.74-1.17), 0.54 |

- ⚠️ Divergent results from POP-RT likely reflect modern PSMA PET/CT staging (filters occult nodal mets), longer ADT (3 vs 2 yrs), and higher RT dose — all attenuating incremental WPRT benefit
- ⚠️ <1/10 men died of PCa in first decade — challenges 'very high-risk' designation in PSMA-staged cohorts where occult mets are already excluded
- ❓ Whether WPRT benefits a biomarker-selected subset (high genomic risk, PSMA-occult nodal disease) remains unanswered

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim (ESTRO 2026): no inferiority signal for 5-fx SBRT vs 25-fx moderate hypofractionation in high-risk/node-positive PCa; efficacy HR pending at 4-5yr.

![PRIME](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)

| Toxicity | SBRT 5fx | Mod hypo 25fx |
|---|---|---|
| Acute GU ≥G2 | ~5.4% | ~4.0% |
| Acute GI ≥G2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% |
| Acute G3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% |
| Late GU ≥G2 | ~10-12% | ~9-11% |
| Late GI ≥G2 | ~5-7% | ~4-6% |
| Late G3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% |

- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority, N~434; high-risk/node-positive non-metastatic PCa; both arms: prostate + whole-pelvis RT + long-course ADT (~2yr); PSMA PET staging allowed
- 💊 Arm A: 36.25 Gy/5fx SBRT (every other day); Arm B: ~68 Gy/25fx moderate hypo (5fx/wk); pelvic nodes 25 Gy/5fx in both arms
- 📊 Primary BFFS (Phoenix nadir+2): early data show no inferiority signal for SBRT arm; no HR/CI/p reported yet; 4-5yr mature data awaited
- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (10-yr data): FFS HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.69-1.03), non-inferior; distinct population: node-negative only, no ADT, no pelvic RT
- ⚠️ Interim only: median f/u 1-2yr; no efficacy HR/CI; MFS and OS immature; non-inferiority not formally declared
- ❓ Whether SBRT non-inferiority holds at 4-5yr in node-positive disease with whole-pelvis RT is the pivotal unresolved question

## 🎀 Breast

Heavy fractionation and de-escalation day; OLIGOMA the standout with the first positive MDT RCT in the histology.

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT + systemic therapy vs systemic therapy alone in oligometastatic breast cancer; mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48 (0.25-0.91), p=0.021.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase III RCT (ARO-2021-09, [[NCT04495309]]); N=87 (43 exp, 44 control); oligometastatic breast cancer any line, ≤5 metastatic lesions; randomized to systemic therapy ± local ablative RT to all lesions
- 📐 HR 0.48 (95% CI 0.25-0.91), p=0.021; median PFS 35.8mo (24.1-NR) vs 20.4mo (12.4-28.4)
- 📊 QoL co-primary endpoint met non-inferiority: EORTC QLQ-C30 summary score 72.2 vs 74.3 at 12 weeks; between-group difference -2.1 (95% CI -9.2 to 5.1); non-inferiority margin -10 pts
- 🔍 Cohort: majority ER/PR+ HER2- in 1L; >80% had 1-3 metastases; ~2/3 of lesions were bone; ~72% receiving 1L endocrine or chemo at enrollment
- ⚠️ Stopped early at <20% of original and <40% of amended target N; HR CI wide (0.25-0.91), reflecting small sample; OS data not reported; underpowered for subgroup analyses
- 🔗 First positive RCT for MDT in oligometastatic breast cancer specifically; adds to SABR-COMET, ORIOLE, and STOMP data in mixed/prostate oligomet settings; breast-specific evidence was largely retrospective until now
- ❓ Which subgroup benefits most (1L vs later lines, bone-dominant vs visceral, HR+ vs TNBC) remains open; mature OS and comprehensive subgroup analyses pending

### EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS at 20 years: IM-MS RT improves BCM but OS neutral (HR 1.00), counterbalanced by late cardiac and pulmonary toxicity.

![EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg98ZFXsAAQDr2.jpg)

| Late AE | IM-MS+ | IM-MS- |
|---|---|---|
| Lung fibrosis | 6.3% | 3.2% |
| Cardiac fibrosis | 2.7% | 1.7% |
| Cardiac diseases | 15.2% | 11.7% |

- 📊 1° EP OS at 20y: 61.0% (IM-MS+) vs 61.8% (IM-MS-); HR=1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967 — no difference
- 📊 Competing mortality at 20y (ITT): BCM reduced, non-BCM deaths increased
- 📊 DFS and DMFS at 20y (ITT): no significant difference
- 📊 pN0 subgroup at 20y: DFS 53.9% vs 53.6% (HR=0.93, p=0.318); DMFS 67.2% vs 67.4% (HR=0.93, p=0.397) — no benefit in node-negative pts
- ⚠️ Late RT-related toxicity (absolute rates, IM-MS+ vs IM-MS-)
  - Lung fibrosis: 6.3% vs 3.2%
  - Cardiac fibrosis: 2.7% vs 1.7%
  - Cardiac diseases: 15.2% vs 11.7%
- 🔗 DBCG IMN2 (Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): modern IM-MS RT with mean heart doses 4-9x lower (MHD 1.2 Gy right-sided, 2.3 Gy left-sided) showed OS benefit at 15y in node-positive pts — era effect likely explains divergence from EORTC
- ❓ whether modern cardiac-sparing IM-MS RT (as in DBCG IMN2) avoids the non-BCM mortality offset seen here remains the key unanswered question

### IMPORT HIGH

> IMPORT HIGH 10-yr: 48Gy/15F SIB non-inferior to standard sequential boost (IBTR 3.7% vs 3.5%) with similar AEs; 53Gy SIB no added benefit.

![IMPORT HIGH](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)

| Arm | 10-yr IBTR (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| 40Gy/15F + 16Gy/8F | 3.5% (2.4, 5.0) |
| 48Gy/15F (3.2Gy/F) | 3.7% (2.6, 5.3) |
| 53Gy/15F (3.5Gy/F) | 5.5% (4.1, 7.3) |

- 📊 10-yr IBTR rates (95% CI)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2617, 76 UK hospitals (2009-2015), pT1-3 pN0-N3a M0 BCS pts requiring tumour bed boost; 1:1:1 randomisation
- 📊 OS at 10 yrs: absolute difference vs 40Gy/15F = -0.5% (-3.0, 2.8) for 48Gy/15F and +1.5% (-1.4, 5.1) for 53Gy/15F — no OS detriment or benefit
- 💊 Late G3+ AEs at 10 yrs: breast distortion/shrinkage <18%, induration <10%, telangiectasia <2%, breast oedema <2% across all arms
- 🔗 5-yr IBTR from Lancet 2023 publication: 40+16Gy 1.9%, 48Gy 2.0%, 53Gy 3.2% — 10-yr data consistent with non-inferiority of 48Gy SIB holding over time
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm IBTR numerically higher at both 5 and 10 yrs — further dose escalation not beneficial and may be detrimental; no subgroup rescued this arm
- ❓ Whether SIB approach extends to nodal RT or post-mastectomy settings remains untested; long-term cardiac / second malignancy data not reported here

### DBCG HYPO

> DBCG HYPO 10-yr: 40Gy/15fr reduces grade 2-3 breast induration vs 50Gy/25fr (19.5% vs 24.7%, HR 0.76, p=0.005) with no difference in recurrence or survival.

![DBCG HYPO](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhgdACW0AAaAeV.jpg)

| Endpoint | 50 Gy/25 fr | 40 Gy/15 fr | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10-yr G2-3 breast induration | 24.7% | 19.5% | 0.76 (0.62-0.92) | 0.005 |
| 10-yr overall survival | 92.1% | 93.0% | 0.81 (0.63-1.04) | 0.10 |

- 📊 1° EP (3-yr G≥2 breast induration): 10-yr update shows 19.5% (40Gy) vs 24.7% (50Gy); HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.62-0.92), p=0.005
- 🔍 Phase III RCT (1:1), N=1,882 women with node-negative breast cancer or DCIS; enrolled Denmark/Norway/Germany 2009-2014; median f/u 12.8 yrs
- 📊 10-yr OS: 93.0% (40Gy) vs 92.1% (50Gy); HR 0.81 (95% CI 0.63-1.04), p=0.10 — no significant difference
- No significant differences in locoregional recurrence, distant failure, or breast cancer mortality between arms
- 🔗 Consistent with START-A/B (Lancet Oncol 2013) and FAST-Forward (Lancet 2020) showing non-inferior tumor control with hypofractionation; DBCG HYPO adds 10-yr induration data in a prospectively randomized Nordic/German cohort
- ⚠️ Primary EP was 3-yr induration (morbidity), not oncologic outcome — long-term recurrence/survival comparisons are secondary analyses; trial not powered for OS differences
- ❓ Ultra-hypofractionation (FAST-Forward 26Gy/5fr) now increasingly adopted — unclear where 40Gy/15fr fits vs 5-fraction schedules in current practice

### APBI-IMRT Florence · [[NCT02104895]]

> APBI-IMRT Florence: 15-yr IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17) — no difference in LRC, DM, or OS.

![IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)
*IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%.*

- 🔍 Phase III, N=520, 1:1 APBI (30Gy/5#) vs WBI (50Gy/25# + 10Gy TBB); eligibility: BCS, pT<25mm, FSM ≥5mm, age >40; accrual 2005-2013; [[NCT02104895]]
- 📊 15-yr IBTR: APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%, HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 — numerical excess not statistically significant
- ⚠️ IBTR excess driven by new ipsilateral primaries (APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09), not true local relapses (2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75) — raises question of field effect or at-risk parenchyma outside PBI field
- 📊 15-yr outcomes across endpoints (ITT)

  | Endpoint | APBI N (%) | WBI N (%) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | IBTR | 20 (7.7) | 11 (4.2) | 0.14 |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
  | Contralateral breast | 10 (3.8) | 13 (5.0) | 0.67 |
  | Distant metastasis | 7 (2.7) | 12 (4.6) | 0.35 |
  | Deaths (all-cause) | 56 (21.5) | 51 (19.6) | 0.66 |

- 🔗 Consistent with IMPORT/RAPID/GEC-ESTRO: no OS or LRC detriment at long f/u for APBI in selected low-risk pts; IBTR signal magnitude similar to RAPID (8.8% vs 3.0% at 8yr) though p not significant here

- ❓ Whether IBTR excess (new primaries specifically) translates to meaningful survival impact at 20yr remains open; contralateral breast event rate numerically higher in WBI arm (unexplained)
