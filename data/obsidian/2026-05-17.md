# 2026-05-17

> [!summary] TL;DR
> ESTRO26 Day 3 featured landmark long-term breast cancer radiotherapy data, with 10-, 15-, and 20-year results from IMPORT HIGH, the Florence APBI-IMRT Florence phase III trial, and EORTC 22922/10925 collectively reinforcing dose-escalated SIB, partial breast irradiation, and regional nodal field design. The EORTC OLIGOCARE prospective registry provided the largest SABR outcomes dataset to date (~2,500 patients, ~3,500 metastases), reporting 5% local failure at 1 year and 11% at 3 years across oligometastatic histologies. ## Breast Cancer Radiotherapy — Mature Randomized Trial Outcomes

Three pivotal trials reported mature long-term data at ESTRO26. The 10-year IMPORT HIGH trial (ISRCTN47437448) results, presented by Charlotte Coles, evaluated dose-escalated simultaneous integrated boost (SIB) radiotherapy in early breast cancer. The 15-year data from the APBI-IMRT Florence phase III trial compared accelerated partial breast irradiation (APBI) versus whole-breast irradiation (WBI) in early-stage disease, with durable outcomes supporting APBI as a standard option. The EORTC trial 22922/10925 presented 20-year results of internal mammary chain and medial supraclavicular (IMN/MS) irradiation in stage I–III breast cancer, including pN0 patients, providing the most mature randomized evidence to date for regional nodal irradiation benefit.

A complementary observation noted that even in intermediate-risk early breast cancer, optimal locoregional control depends on combined RT and endocrine therapy (ET) adherence — and that the availability of 5-fraction regimens now substantially simplifies the decision-making framework for this approach.

## Oligometastatic Disease — SABR/SBRT Efficacy and Local Control

The OLIGOMA trial (ARO-2021-09, [[NCT04495309]]) presented results of metastases-directed therapy with SBRT in oligometastatic breast cancer, adding prospective evidence to the growing body of literature supporting ablative local treatment in this setting. The EORTC prospective OLIGOCARE registry — currently the largest dedicated SABR oligometastasis dataset — reported outcomes across approximately 2,500 patients and 3,500 metastases: local failure rates were 5% at 1 year and 11% at 3 years. Colorectal cancer histology was associated with higher risk of disease progression, and minimum PTV dose correlated with local control, reinforcing the importance of dosimetric adequacy.

For renal cell carcinoma specifically, SBRT demonstrated 100% local control at 5 years, a result characterized as exceptional by attendees and supporting SBRT as a definitive local treatment option for RCC oligometastases.

## Localized Prostate Cancer — Hypofractionation, SBRT, and Pelvic Nodal Irradiation with ADT

The PRIME trial evaluated the feasibility and safety of ultra-short SBRT (36.25 Gy in 5 fractions over 1–2 weeks) inclusive of pelvic nodal irradiation versus moderate hypofractionation (~68 Gy in 25 fractions over 5 weeks), both delivered concurrently with ADT in biologically aggressive prostate cancer — benchmarked against the HYPO RT-PC approach. This addresses a key clinical question of whether extreme hypofractionation can safely encompass the pelvis without excess toxicity.

A separate head-to-head comparison framed the ongoing clinical tension between POP RT (2 years ADT plus whole-pelvis RT) and the PEACE II regimen (3 years ADT plus prostate-only RT), highlighting unresolved uncertainty about whether intensifying systemic therapy duration or expanding the radiation field confers greater benefit in high-risk localized disease.
