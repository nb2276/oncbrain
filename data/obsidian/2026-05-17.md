# 2026-05-17

**PEACE-2: pelvic RT null in very high-risk localized prostate with PSMA staging + 3yr ADT (HR 0.81, p=0.09), challenging POP-RT in the modern systemic era.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> PEACE-2 null pelvic RT (HR 0.81, p=0.09) conflicts with POP-RT, raising whether PSMA PET + 3yr ADT erases nodal RT benefit in very high-risk disease. Breast dominated by volume: de-escalation signals from boost (Dutch registry, IMPORT HIGH 10yr), PBI (DBCG RT Natural), and hypofrax (DBCG HYPO). OLIGOMA, first breast-specific phase III MDT RCT, shows PFS HR 0.48 in oligoMBC.

## 🌰 Prostate

Pelvic RT intensification is this meeting's central prostate question: PEACE-2 null, PACE-NODES toxicity acceptable, PIVOTALboost safe — efficacy endpoints across all three still pending.

### RADIOSA

> RADIOSA post-hoc: SBRT+ADT vs SBRT alone, median MFS not reached vs 16.6mo, HR 0.39 in oligorecurrent prostate.

![RADIOSA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdDCQkW0AAvnIw.jpg)

| Endpoint | Arm A (SBRT) | Arm B (SBRT+ADT) | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Metastatic progression | 32/51 (62.7%) | 19/51 (37.3%) | 0.3894 (0.2201-0.6888) | 0.00119 |
| Median MFS | 16.6mo (CI 12.83-NA) | Not reached | — | 0.00079 (log-rank) |

- 📊 Metastatic progression: 19/51 (37.3%) Arm B vs 32/51 (62.7%) Arm A; median MFS not reached vs 16.6mo (95% CI 12.83-NA)
- 📐 HR 0.3894 (95% CI 0.2201-0.6888), p=0.00119; log-rank p=0.00079
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=102, 1:1 randomization; SBRT alone (Arm A) vs SBRT + 6mo ADT (Arm B); median f/u 49.23mo (95% CI 42.47-54.8)
- 📊 Eugonadal MFS (post-testosterone recovery) also favored Arm B, p<0.05; all but 2 Arm B pts recovered testosterone within f/u
- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis of a phase II trial; MFS is a surrogate endpoint, not OS; N=102 limits power; no biomarker stratification
- 🔗 Eugonadal MFS benefit suggests durable synergy beyond castration period, extending prior RADIOSA primary endpoint findings
- ❓ Whether short-term ADT duration (6mo) is optimal vs longer course in oligorecurrent setting remains open

### PEACE-2

> PEACE-2: pelvic RT added to ADT + dose-escalated EBRT shows no significant cPFS benefit in very high-risk localized prostate cancer (HR 0.81, p=0.088).

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% (61.6–72.2) | 0.81 (0.63–1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% (57.4–68.1) | ref | — |

- 📊 Primary endpoint cPFS and key secondaries — WPRT vs prostate-only RT

  | Endpoint | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
  |---|---|---|
  | cPFS (1° EP) | 0.81 (0.63–1.03) | 0.088 |
  | 7-yr cPFS: pelvic RT 67.1% vs prostate-only 62.9% | — | — |

- 🔗 Cross-trial comparison: WPRT efficacy vs prostate-only RT (HR <1 favors WPRT)

  | Endpoint | POP-RT HR (95% CI) | POP-RT p | PEACE-2 HR (95% CI) | PEACE-2 p |
  |---|---|---|---|---|
  | bFFS / bPFS | 0.50 (0.42–0.61) | <0.001 | 0.97 (0.81–1.16) | 0.73 |
  | cFFS / cPFS | 0.74 (0.61–0.90) | 0.002 | 0.81 (0.63–1.03) | 0.09 |
  | MFS | 0.72 (0.58–0.89) | 0.002 | 0.93 (0.74–1.17) | 0.54 |

- 🔍 Phase III, 4-arm RCT (arms A–D: prostate vs pelvic RT ± cabazitaxel x4); very high-risk localized CaP ≥2 risk factors (Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20), N0M0 on conventional or choline PET/CT; 3yr ADT; prostate dose 78 Gy EQD2; median f/u ~5.5yr (interim at ESTRO 2026)
- ⚠️ Key design differences vs POP-RT: longer ADT (3 vs 2yr), modern PSMA PET/CT staging, higher dose escalation, and contemporary systemic intensification — each of these may reduce residual benefit attributable to elective pelvic RT alone
- ⚠️ Interim analysis with ~5.5yr median f/u; OS and CSS data not mature. PSMA PET/CT staging likely excluded occult metastatic pts who might have benefited most from pelvic nodal coverage
- ❓ With <10% prostate cancer mortality at 10yr, does this population truly represent 'very high-risk'? Biomarker/genomic stratification needed to identify pts where pelvic RT intensification still adds value

### PACE-NODES

> PACE-NODES: pelvic nodal SBRT adds short-term GI toxicity (28% vs 21% CTCAE ≥2) vs prostate-only SBRT; resolved by 12wk, no GU difference.

![CTCAE ≥2 GI: 28% PPN-SBRT vs 21% P-SBRT over 12-week period. Resolved by 12wk. No difference in GU toxicity.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLTUXoAAbiFs.jpg)
*CTCAE ≥2 GI: 28% PPN-SBRT vs 21% P-SBRT over 12-week period. Resolved by 12wk. No difference in GU toxicity.*

- 📊 CTCAE ≥2 GI symptoms: 28% PPN-SBRT vs 21% P-SBRT over 12-week period; difference resolved with no gap at 12wk
- 📊 No difference in acute GU toxicity clinician- or patient-reported; EPIC-26 bowel domain diverged at 4wk only
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1166, high-risk PCa (T3a-T4 and/or GS 8-10 and/or PSA >20 ng/ml), 12-36mo ADT; 36.25Gy/5f prostate ± 25Gy/5f pelvic nodes on alternate days
- ⚠️ 11% PPN-SBRT vs 4% P-SBRT did not receive allocated treatment, mostly planning constraints; primary EP (time to BCF) not yet mature, this is interim toxicity data only
- 🔗 POP-RT and PEACE-2 established pelvic nodal RT benefit with moderate hypofractionation; PACE-NODES extends the question to 5-fraction SBRT fractionation
- ❓ Primary efficacy EP (BCF-FS) pending; late GI/GU toxicity and cancer control outcomes unknown

| Prostate+Nodes+Boost (n=347) | 6.5% (4.5-9.5) | 16.5% (13.1-20.6) |

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME (interim): 5 fx SBRT vs 25 fx moderate hypofrax + pelvic RT in high-risk/node+ prostate; BFFS not yet mature, G3+ toxicity <1% both arms.

![Late G2+ GU p=0.59; G2+ GI p=0.20; G3+ GU/GI <1% both arms (1-2yr follow-up).](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)
*Late G2+ GU p=0.59; G2+ GI p=0.20; G3+ GU/GI <1% both arms (1-2yr follow-up).*

- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority, N=434, high-risk/very high-risk or node-positive non-metastatic prostate; both arms: whole-pelvis RT + 2yr ADT; PSMA PET/CT staging allowed
- 💊 Arm A (SBRT): 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx) every other day; Arm B: ~68 Gy/25 fx (2.7 Gy/fx); both arms pelvic nodes 25 Gy/5 fx
- 📊 1° EP (BFFS, Phoenix): no HR reported at interim; early 1-2yr data show no signal of inferiority for SBRT arm; 4-5yr mature data awaited
- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC: node-negative, no ADT, no pelvic RT; 10yr FFS HR 0.84 (0.69-1.03), non-inferiority confirmed. PRIME extends to node-positive with mandatory ADT + whole-pelvis RT
- ⚠️ Interim analysis only; 1-2yr median f/u insufficient for BFFS in pts on 2yr ADT; no efficacy HR yet reported; non-inferiority formally unevaluable
- ❓ Will BFFS non-inferiority hold at 4-5yr? Optimal SIB dose to PET-positive pelvic nodes?

## 🎀 Breast

De-escalation dominated: boost omission in low-risk pts, PBI for elderly BCS, 10-yr hypofrax durability confirmed, and locoregional omission post-NAT still gated on B-51.

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT + systemic therapy vs systemic alone in oligometastatic breast, mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 Co-primary endpoint PFS

  |  | Experimental (MDT) | Control |
  |---|---|---|
  | Median PFS (95% CI) | 35.8 mo (24.1-NR) | 20.4 mo (12.4-28.4) |
  | HR (95% CI) | 0.48 (0.25-0.91) | ref |
  | p-value | 0.021 |  |

- 📊 Co-primary QoL (EORTC QLQ-C30) at 12 wks: 72.2 vs 74.3, diff -2.1 (95% CI -9.2 to 5.1); non-inferiority margin -10 pts, met
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=87 (43 exp / 44 ctrl); oligoMBC any line, ≤5 mets; SABR/SBRT to all lesions + ongoing systemic; [[NCT04495309]]
- 🔍 >80% had 1-3 mets; ~2/3 lesions were bone; ~75% first-line endocrine or chemo; majority ER/PR+ HER2-neg
- ⚠️ Recruitment stopped early at <20% of initial and <40% of amended target N; underpowered, wide CI on HR (0.25-0.91); OS data not reported in source
- 🔗 Adds to SABR-COMET, ORIOLE, STOMP in MDT for oligomet disease; breast-specific first RCT showing PFS benefit
- ❓ Which pts benefit most unclear; comprehensive biomarker analysis pending; OS and longer f/u needed

### EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS at 20yr: OS null (HR 1.00); BCM benefit (HR 0.82) offset by non-BCM excess (HR 1.26) from late RT cardiotoxicity.

![EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh9WMAAK6Lh.jpg)

| Cause-specific mortality | IM-MS+ | IM-MS- | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BCM | 18.6% | 22.4% | 0.82 (0.72-0.95) | 0.006 |
| non-BCM | 20.4% | 15.8% | 1.26 (1.09-1.46) | 0.002 |

- 📊 1° EP OS at 20yr: 61.0% vs 61.8%, HR 1.00 (0.90-1.10), p=0.967
- 📊 ITT DFS 48.2% vs 49.0% HR 0.97 (0.89-1.06) p=0.515; DMFS 58.9% vs 59.8% HR 0.97 (0.88-1.08) p=0.578
- 🔍 pN0 subgroup: DFS 53.9% vs 53.6% HR 0.93 (p=0.318), DMFS 67.2% vs 67.4% HR 0.93 (p=0.397) — no benefit
- ⚠️ RT-related late toxicity (IM-MS+ vs -)
  - Lung fibrosis: 6.3% vs 3.2%
  - Cardiac fibrosis: 2.7% vs 1.7%
  - Cardiac diseases: 15.2% vs 11.7%
- 🔗 DBCG IMN2 (Nielsen AWM, Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): modern RT MHD 1.2/2.3 Gy right/left (4-9x lower than this trial) showed IM-MS RT improved BCM and OS in node+ at 15yr
- ⚠️ Era caveat: EORTC heart doses far exceed modern planning; non-BCM mortality excess likely not reproducible with current technique

### Dutch Breast Boost Era Analysis

> IBTR <3% at 10yr for 0-2 risk factor pts post-BCT in modern systemic therapy era; boost may be omitted in this group.

![Dutch Breast Boost Era Analysis](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdFyGwWoAAD-Ft.jpg)

| Risk group | N (no boost / boost) | 5yr no boost | 5yr boost | 10yr no boost | 10yr boost |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0-2 RF | 15,085 / 13,845 | 0.6% | 0.7% | 1.2% | 1.2% |
| ≥3 RF | 149 / 733 | 1.3% | 2.9% | 2.7% | 3.3% |
| Uncertain | 592 / 944 | 0.8% | 3.3% | 1.4% | 3.6% |

- 📊 Cumulative incidence IBTR by risk factor group (no boost / boost)
- 🔍 Retrospective Dutch registry cohort, BCT pts 2012-2016; IBTR identified via pathology report algorithm; Assisi 2024 thresholds used (<3% at 10yr with boost, <6% without boost)
- 📐 Only ≥3 risk factor subgroup with boost exceeded Assisi threshold at 10yr (3.3%); all other subgroups remained below thresholds
- 🔗 Boost benefit established by EORTC 22881/10882 (Bartelink, Lancet Oncol 2015): ~50% relative IBTR reduction; current data suggests modern systemic therapy may attenuate boost necessity for lower-risk pts
- ⚠️ Retrospective registry design; no randomization; adequacy of systemic therapy not uniformly verified; 'uncertain' risk category poorly defined; absolute N in high-risk subgroups small (149-149 no-boost ≥3RF pts)
- ❓ Which specific pts within ≥3 RF group benefit from boost? Prospective de-escalation trial needed to validate omission strategy

### DBCG HYPO

> DBCG HYPO at 10yr: 40Gy/15fr reduces grade 2-3 breast induration (HR 0.76, p=0.005) vs 50Gy/25fr; no difference in recurrence or survival.

![DBCG HYPO](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhgdACW0AAaAeV.jpg)

| Arm | 10-yr G2-3 induration | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50 Gy/25 fr | 24.7% | ref |  |
| 40 Gy/15 fr | 19.5% | 0.76 (0.62-0.92) | 0.005 |

- 📐 10-yr OS: 93.0% (40Gy) vs 92.1% (50Gy), HR 0.81 (0.63-1.04), p=0.10; no significant difference in LRR or distant failure
- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority RCT, 1,882 node-negative breast/DCIS pts, 2009-2014 (Denmark/Norway/Germany); median f/u 12.8 yrs; morbidity assessed at yrs 0,1,2,3,4,5,10
- 🔗 Consistent with START-A/B (2008/2013) and FAST-Forward (2020) at longer follow-up; adds 10-yr morbidity durability to the hypofractionation evidence base

- ❓ Trial restricted to node-negative pts; applicability to node-positive pts receiving regional nodal RT or boost not addressed

### IMPORT HIGH

> IMPORT HIGH 10yr: IBTR 3.5% (40+16Gy) vs 3.7% (48Gy SIB); 53Gy higher at 5.5%; SIB reaffirmed as SOC.

![IMPORT HIGH](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)

| Arm | 10-yr IBTR (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| 40Gy/15F + 16Gy/8F | 3.5% (2.4, 5.0) |
| 48Gy/15F (3.2Gy/F) | 3.7% (2.6, 5.3) |
| 53Gy/15F (3.5Gy/F) | 5.5% (4.1, 7.3) |

- 🔍 Phase III 1:1:1 RCT, N=2617, 76 UK hospitals 2009-2015; pT1-3 pN0-3a M0 post-BCS requiring boost; annual f/u to 10yr
- 📊 OS abs diff vs 40+16Gy at 10yr: 48Gy -0.5% (95% CI -3.0, 2.8); 53Gy +1.5% (-1.4, 5.1) -- no OS signal either arm
- 💊 Late AEs at 10yr (moderate/marked): low and similar across all arms
  - Breast distortion/shrinkage: <18% all groups
  - Induration: <10% all groups
  - Telangiectasia: <2% all groups
  - Breast oedema: <2% all groups
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm IBTR numerically higher (5.5% vs 3.5-3.7%); no OS benefit; further dose escalation beyond 48Gy not supported
- 🔗 5yr data (Lancet 2023): 40+16Gy 1.9%, 48Gy 2.0%, 53Gy 3.2%; 10yr confirms -- all arms remain below original 5% control estimate
- ❓ Whether subgroups (high-grade, LVI+) within this higher-risk cohort might benefit from escalation beyond 48Gy not explored

### APBI-IMRT Florence · [[NCT02104895]]

> APBI-IMRT Florence: 15-yr IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17); no difference in locoregional control, DM, OS.

![APBI-IMRT Florence](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJaWEAAdjH2.jpg)

| Endpoint | APBI 5yr | WBI 5yr | APBI 10yr | WBI 10yr | APBI 15yr | WBI 15yr |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| IBTR N(%) | 6(2.3) | 3(1.2) | 11(4.2) | 7(2.7) | 20(7.7) | 11(4.2) |
| Locoregional recurrence N(%) | 6(2.3) | 5(1.9) | 11(4.2) | 9(3.5) | 20(7.2) | 13(5.0) |
| Distant metastasis N(%) | 4(1.5) | 4(1.5) | 7(2.7) | 6(2.3) | 7(2.7) | 12(4.6) |
| Deaths N(%) | 4(1.5) | 9(3.5) | 28(10.8) | 25(9.6) | 56(21.5) | 51(19.6) |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=520, 1:1 APBI (30Gy/5#) vs WBI (50Gy/25# + 10Gy boost); BCS pts, pT<25mm, margins ≥5mm, age >40; enrolled 2005-2013
- 📊 15-year event rates, APBI vs WBI (ITT)

  | Endpoint | APBI N(%) | WBI N(%) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | IBTR | 20 (7.7) | 11 (4.2) | 0.14 |
  | Local relapse only | 5 (2.1) | 4 (1.6) | 0.75 |
  | New ipsilateral primary | 15 (5.9) | 7 (2.7) | 0.09 |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
  | Distant metastasis | 7 (2.7) | 12 (4.6) | 0.35 |
  | Deaths | 56 (21.5) | 51 (19.6) | 0.66 |

- 📐 HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 for IBTR; equivalence margin was delta 5%, 80% power
- ⚠️ IBTR excess in APBI arm driven by new ipsilateral primaries (5.9% vs 2.7%), not true local recurrence (2.1% vs 1.6%); biologically distinct events collapsed into same endpoint
- ⚠️ Equivalence design powered for 5-yr IBTR 3%; 15-yr follow-up is exploratory, not the pre-specified analysis window. Trial not powered to detect the observed numeric differences.
- 🔗 vs FAST-Forward and other APBI trials: longer follow-up here than most; IBTR signal at 15yr consistent with Florence 5yr (EJC 2015) and 10yr (JCO 2020) prior publications showing trend without significance
- ❓ Whether new ipsilateral primaries after APBI represent inadequate field coverage or independent biology remains unresolved; impacts how IBTR endpoint should be weighted in APBI decisions
