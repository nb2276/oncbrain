# 2026-05-17

**EORTC IM-MS 20y: OS HR 1.00 as late non-BCM excess (HR 1.26) fully offsets BCM benefit (HR 0.82); IM nodal RT no longer improves survival.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast and prostate dominated: EORTC IM-MS 20y OS now null as late cardiac mortality erodes BCM gains; PEACE-2 null pelvic RT result diverges from POP-RT in the PSMA staging era. DBCG RT Natural challenges RT omission in favorable-biology breast (1.5% vs 9.8% LR at 4yr). OLIGOMA first RCT to show MDT PFS benefit in oligo-BC.

## 🌰 Prostate

Three phase 3 RCTs presented toxicity data at 2-5yr; PEACE-2's null pelvic RT result in VHR disease diverges from POP-RT, likely shaped by PSMA staging selecting out occult nodal disease.

### PACE-NODES

> PACE-NODES: pelvic nodal SBRT adds short-term GI toxicity (28% vs 21% G≥2) vs prostate-only SBRT; cancer control endpoint not yet mature.

![PACE-NODES](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLTUXoAAbiFs.jpg)

|  | PPN-SBRT | P-SBRT |
|---|---|---|
| CTCAE G≥2 GI (12wk) | 28% | 21% |
| Did not receive allocation | 11% | 4% |

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1166, 1:1; high-risk localised prostate (T3a-T4 and/or Gleason 8-10 and/or PSA >20 ng/ml), 12-36mo ADT planned
- 🔍 PPN-SBRT: 36.25 Gy/5f prostate + 25 Gy/5f pelvic nodes; P-SBRT: 36.25 Gy/5f prostate-only; both on alternate days
- ⚠️ 11% PPN-SBRT vs 4% P-SBRT did not receive allocated treatment, mostly due to planning constraints not met
- 📊 CTCAE G≥2 GI toxicity over 12 weeks: 28% PPN-SBRT vs 21% P-SBRT
- 📊 GI differences reflected in EPIC-26 bowel domain at 4 weeks; resolved by 12 weeks with no difference at that point
- 📊 No differences in acute GU toxicity (CTCAE or patient-reported) between arms
- ⚠️ Primary cancer control endpoint (time to biochemical or clinical failure) not yet mature; this presentation is acute toxicity data only
- 🔗 Adds 5f nodal SBRT feasibility data to the prior moderate hypofractionation nodal RT evidence base (POP-RT, PEACE-2); first phase 3 of this fractionation for pelvic nodes

### RADIOSA

> RADIOSA post-hoc: median MFS 16.6mo vs NR, HR 0.39 favoring SBRT+ADT in oligorecurrent prostate cancer.

![RADIOSA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdDCQkW0AAvnIw.jpg)

| Endpoint | Arm A (SBRT) | Arm B (SBRT+ADT) |
|---|---|---|
| Events | 32/51 (62.7%) | 19/51 (37.3%) |
| Median MFS | 16.6 mo (95% CI 12.83-NR) | Not reached |
| HR (95% CI) | 0.3894 (0.2201-0.6888) | ref |
| p (Cox/log-rank) | 0.00119 / 0.00079 | — |

- 📊 Metastatic progression: 32/51 (62.7%) Arm A vs 19/51 (37.3%) Arm B
- 📊 Median MFS 16.6mo (95% CI 12.83-NR) Arm A vs not reached Arm B
- 📐 HR 0.3894 (95% CI 0.2201-0.6888), Cox p=0.00119; log-rank p=0.00079
- 📊 Eugonadal MFS (post-testosterone recovery) also favored Arm B, p<0.05
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=102, 1:1 SBRT alone vs SBRT + 6-month ADT; oligorecurrent prostate cancer

- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis: MFS was not the prespecified primary endpoint of RADIOSA
- ⚠️ Phase II, N=102; underpowered for definitive conclusions

> PIVOTALboost phase 3: focal boost + pelvic node RT (20 fractions) safe at 2 years; G2+ late bowel/bladder similar across all arms.

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim: 5-fraction SBRT + whole-pelvis RT comparable to moderate hypofractionation at 1-2yr; primary BFFS endpoint immature, 4-5yr data awaited.

![PRIME](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)

| Toxicity | SBRT 5fx | Mod Hypo 25fx | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acute GU Gr≥2 | ~5.4% | ~4.0% | 0.59 |
| Acute GI Gr≥2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% | 0.20 |
| Acute Gr3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% | NS |
| Late GU Gr≥2 (1-2yr) | ~10-12% | ~9-11% | NS |
| Late GI Gr≥2 (1-2yr) | ~5-7% | ~4-6% | NS |
| Late Gr3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% | NS |

- 🔍 Phase III open-label non-inferiority RCT; N=434
  - High-risk/very-high-risk/node-positive non-metastatic PCa; ECOG 0-2
  - Tata Memorial Centre + India collaborators; accrual 2018-2023 (completed)
  - PSMA PET/CT staging allowed; modern IMRT/VMAT + daily IGRT
- 💊 Both arms: whole-pelvis RT (25 Gy/5 fx) + long-course ADT ~2yr
  - Arm A SBRT: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx), every other day
  - Arm B mod hypo: ~68 Gy/25 fx (2.7 Gy/fx), 5 fx/wk ~5wks
- 📊 1° EP BFFS (Phoenix nadir+2): early trend shows no inferiority signal for SBRT; no effect size reportable (primary analysis immature)
- 📊 MFS and OS: no significant difference at interim; both immature
- 🔗 HYPO-RT-PC (10-yr mature, HR 0.84 [0.69-1.03], non-inferior): node-negative only, no ADT, no pelvic RT — lower-risk, different population
- ⚠️ Interim analysis (f/u 1-2yr only); prespecified primary BFFS analysis requires 4-5yr follow-up
- ⚠️ Single-institution lead (Tata Memorial Centre, India); external validity to Western centers uncertain

### PEACE-2

> PEACE-2: pelvic RT did not improve cPFS vs prostate-only RT (HR 0.81, p=0.088) in VHR prostate cancer with 3yr ADT.

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% [61.6-72.2] | 0.81 [0.63-1.03] | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% [57.4-68.1] | ref | ref |

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, 4-arm factorial (pelvic vs prostate-only RT ± cabazitaxel x4), VHR CaP ≥2 factors, N0M0, 3yr ADT
  - Eligibility: ≥2 of GS ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20 ng/mL
  - Staging: conventional imaging or Choline PET/CT (PSMA PET/CT widely used during accrual)
  - Accrual 2018-2023; median f/u ~5.5 yrs; interim analysis presented ESTRO 2026
- 🔗 POP-RT vs PEACE-2: WPRT benefit by endpoint (HR vs prostate-only RT)
- ⚠️ Key design differences vs POP-RT may explain divergent results
  - POP-RT: 2yr ADT, conventional imaging, prostate dose 74-76 Gy EQD2
  - PEACE-2: 3yr ADT, modern staging (PSMA PET/CT widely used), prostate dose 78 Gy EQD2
- ⚠️ PSMA PET/CT likely staged out pts with occult nodal disease, diluting incremental pelvic RT signal
- ⚠️ Interim analysis: OS not yet mature
- ⚠️ <10% CaP-specific mortality at 10yr raises questions about the current 'VHR' definition
- 💊 No additional toxicity with pelvic RT in either trial (comparable G≥2 GU rates)

## 🎀 Breast

Eight studies spanning fractionation, boost, and nodal RT; EORTC IM-MS 20y is the headline, showing IM nodal RT no longer improves OS as late non-BCM deaths accumulate.

### Dutch Breast Boost Analysis

> Dutch DBRT registry: 10yr IBTR 1.2% (boost vs no boost) for 0-2 RF pts post-BCS, supporting omission in modern systemic therapy era.

![Dutch Breast Boost Analysis](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdFyGwWoAAD-Ft.jpg)

| RF group | 10yr IBTR (no boost) | 10yr IBTR (boost) |
|---|---|---|
| 0-2 factors | 1.2% | 1.2% |
| ≥3 factors | 2.7% | 3.3% |
| Uncertain | 1.4% | 3.6% |

- 🔍 Retrospective Dutch DBRT registry; BCS pts treated 2012-2016, N≈31,350 with or without RT boost
- 🔍 5 risk factors: age ≤40, grade 3, TNBC, inadequate systemic therapy per Dutch guideline, no pCR after neoadjuvant chemo (TNBC/HER2+)
- 🔍 Assisi thresholds as benchmark: <3% 10yr IBTR (boost arm) and <6% (no-boost arm) to identify candidates for safe omission
- 📊 Only subgroup exceeding the Assisi threshold: ≥3 RF + boost at 10yr (3.3% vs 3% cutoff); boost not bringing IBTR below benchmark in highest-risk pts
- ⚠️ Non-randomized registry; confounding by indication expected — higher-risk pts preferentially selected for boost, inflating boost-arm IBTR rates
- ⚠️ ≥3 RF no-boost arm N=149; too small to draw conclusions about omission safety in the highest-risk subgroup
- 🔗 EORTC 22881/10882 (Bartelink, Lancet Oncol 2015): boost reduced IBTR ~50% in pre-modern-systemic-therapy era; current absolute rates far lower across all subgroups

> DBCG HYPO 10-yr: 40Gy/15fr reduced grade 2-3 induration vs 50Gy/25fr (19.5% vs 24.7%, HR 0.76, p=0.005) with no difference in recurrence or survival.

- 📊 10-yr grade 2-3 breast induration: 19.5% (40Gy) vs 24.7% (50Gy), HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.62-0.92), p=0.005
- 📊 10-yr OS: 93.0% (40Gy) vs 92.1% (50Gy), HR 0.81 (95% CI 0.63-1.04), p=0.10

- 🔗 Consistent with START-A/B (UK) and FAST-Forward long-term data supporting hypofractionation as standard for early node-negative disease

### APBI-IMRT Florence · [[NCT02104895]]

> APBI-IMRT Florence: 15-yr IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17), no difference in locoregional control, DM, or OS.

![APBI-IMRT Florence](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJaWEAAdjH2.jpg)

- 📊 15-yr IBTR: APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%, HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17
- 📊 IBTR difference driven by new ipsilateral primaries (APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09), not true local relapse (2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75)
- 📊 No difference in locoregional recurrence at 15 yrs: APBI 7.2% vs WBI 5.0%, p=0.28
- 📊 Distant metastasis at 15 yrs: APBI 2.7% vs WBI 4.6%, p=0.35
- 📊 Deaths at 15 yrs: APBI 21.5% vs WBI 19.6%, p=0.66
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=520, 1:1, enrolled 2005-2013; APBI 30 Gy/5# IMRT vs WBI 50 Gy/25# + 10 Gy boost; ITT for survival, per-protocol for toxicity/cosmesis
- 🔍 Eligibility: BCS, pT <25 mm, FSM ≥5 mm, age >40 yrs
- ⚠️ Trial powered for equivalence with 5% delta on 5-yr IBTR; 15-yr IBTR signal numerically higher in APBI arm, though not significant (underpowered for late events)
- ⚠️ Excess IBTR attributable to new primaries, not recurrence; may reflect surveillance artifact or field effect rather than APBI inadequacy
- 🔗 Consistent with IMPORT LOW (Lancet 2017) and RAPID (NEJM 2019): no significant local control detriment with APBI at 5-10 yr; Florence adds mature 15-yr data
- 🔗 Prior JCO 2020 (Meattini, 10-yr) and EJC 2015 (Livi, 5-yr) showed equivalent outcomes; 15-yr update reinforces durability

> DBCG RT Natural: PBI 40Gy/15fr cut 4-yr LR to 1.5% vs 9.8% without RT in older ER+ pT1N0 breast cancer.

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48, p=0.021) with MDT + systemic vs systemic alone in oligometastatic breast cancer.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 🔍 ~77% ER+, ~7% HER2+; >80% had 1-3 mets, 2/3 of lesions bone mets; ~72% 1st-line systemic (endocrine or chemo)

- ⚠️ Bone-dominant cohort (2/3 of lesions bone) — generalizability to visceral-dominant oligomet uncertain
