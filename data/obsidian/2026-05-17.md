# 2026-05-17

**PEACE-2: elective pelvic RT misses cPFS (HR 0.81, p=0.088), splitting from POP-RT (bPFS HR 0.50); whole-pelvis nodal benefit now contested.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate led with the elective pelvic-RT question splitting: PEACE-2 null on cPFS against POP-RT's positive biochemical signal. Breast brought long-horizon de-escalation reads (APBI, hypofx, boost/IM-MS omission). OLIGOMA delivered the first positive breast-oligomet MDT RCT (mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4, HR 0.48).

## 🌰 Prostate

Elective pelvic nodal RT is the day's fault line: PEACE-2 null on cPFS while POP-RT stayed positive, and PACE-NODES/PIVOTALboost price the added nodal-RT bowel toxicity.

### POP-RT vs PEACE-2 Comparison

> WPRT cut biochemical failure in POP-RT (HR 0.50) but not PEACE-2 (HR 0.97); longer ADT and modern staging may blunt elective pelvic-RT benefit.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is elective nodal coverage: PEACE-2's null WPRT signal (bPFS HR 0.97) carries two intensifications POP-RT (HR 0.50) lacked, 3yr vs 2yr ADT and 78 vs 74-76 Gy EQD2. Either could absorb the pelvic-RT benefit, so the divergence is confounded, not a clean refutation.

![POP-RT vs PEACE-2 Comparison](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkfsYnaYAAfa1-.jpg)

- 🔍 Both phase III open-label RCTs: whole-pelvis + boost vs prostate-only IMRT in N0M0 high/very-high-risk prostate ca
- 📊 WPRT vs prostate-only RT · HRs by trial (HR<1 favors WPRT)

  | Endpoint | POP-RT (2yr ADT) | PEACE-2 (3yr ADT) |
  |---|---|---|
  | bPFS/bFFS | HR 0.50 (0.42-0.61), p<0.001 | HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73 |
  | cPFS/cFFS | HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09 |
  | MFS | HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- 📐 Prostate RT dose: 74-76 Gy EQD2 (POP-RT) vs dose-escalated 78 Gy EQD2 (PEACE-2)
- 🔍 ADT duration: 2yr (POP-RT) vs 3yr (PEACE-2)
- 📊 Toxicity: comparable Grade ≥2 GU between arms in both; no increase with WPRT
- ⚠️ PEACE-2 is interim (median f/u ~5.5yr, ESTRO 2026); POP-RT updated at 6.3-7.2yr
- ⚠️ Cross-trial comparison, not head-to-head; era, staging, ADT, and dose all differ
- 🔗 Author's read: longer ADT + modern staging + higher dose may reduce elective pelvic RT's incremental benefit

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> 5-fx SBRT (36.25Gy) shows no early inferiority vs 25-fx (68Gy) moderate hypofx, both + whole-pelvis nodal RT and 2y ADT, high-risk/N+ prostate.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The new ground is nodal RT in 5 fractions: PRIME gives whole-pelvis elective coverage at 25Gy/5fx (SIB to positive nodes) with 36.25Gy/5fx to prostate, pushing extreme hypofx into node-positive high-risk disease HYPO-RT-PC (node-negative, prostate-only, no ADT) never tested. Interim Grade 3+ GU/GI <1% both arms; BFFS non-inferiority waits on 4-5y.

- 📊 1° EP biochemical FFS (Phoenix nadir+2): no early signal of inferiority for 5-fx SBRT at interim; mature 4-5y data pending
- 🔍 Phase III open-label non-inferiority RCT, N≈434, Tata Memorial + Indian centers; accrual 2018-2023; stratified by risk group + nodal status
- 🔍 RT regimen by arm (both arms + whole-pelvis nodal RT + ~2y ADT)

  | Parameter | Arm A (SBRT) | Arm B (moderate hypo) |
  |---|---|---|
  | Prostate dose | 36.25 Gy / 5 fx | 68 Gy / 25 fx |
  | Dose per fraction | 7.25 Gy | 2.7 Gy |
  | Schedule | Every other day | 5 fx/wk, ~5 wk |
  | Pelvic nodal RT | 25 Gy / 5 fx | 25 Gy / 5 fx |

- 🔍 Both arms: whole-pelvis elective nodal RT + long-course ADT (~2y); modern IMRT/VMAT with daily IGRT
- 🔍 SIB to positive nodes allowed in the SBRT arm
- 📊 Grade 3+ GU/GI <1% in both arms (acute and late); no significant difference in grade ≥2 toxicity
- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (42.7Gy/7fx, 10y non-inferior): node-negative, prostate-only, no ADT, mostly 3D-CRT. PRIME extends 5-fx to N+/high-risk with whole-pelvis RT + ADT
- ⚠️ Interim only, 1-2y f/u; 'no signal of inferiority' is not established non-inferiority. Primary BFFS reads out at 4-5y
- ⚠️ BFFS is a biochemical surrogate; OS and MFS immature, no significant early difference

### PACE-NODES

> Grade ≥2 GI toxicity 28% vs 21% adding pelvic nodal to prostate SBRT (5f); GU unchanged, primary efficacy endpoint immature.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is deliverability, not just the 28% vs 21% grade ≥2 GI: 11% of PPN-SBRT pts missed allocation on unmet planning constraints vs 4% prostate-only, a workflow signal for 5-fraction whole-pelvis. GI was transient (no difference by 12wk) and GU unaffected, so the tolerability cost of elective nodal coverage reads modest and short-lived.

![CTCAE grade ≥2 GI 28% (PPN-SBRT) vs 21% (P-SBRT) over 12 weeks.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLTUXoAAbiFs.jpg)
*CTCAE grade ≥2 GI 28% (PPN-SBRT) vs 21% (P-SBRT) over 12 weeks.*

![P-SBRT 36.25Gy in 5f to prostate; PPN-SBRT adds 25Gy in 5f to nodes.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLcDXAAAcNgO.jpg)
*P-SBRT 36.25Gy in 5f to prostate; PPN-SBRT adds 25Gy in 5f to nodes.*

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, 1166 randomised, high-risk localised prostate; primary efficacy endpoint (biochemical/clinical failure) not yet mature
- 🔍 Both arms 36.25Gy/5f to prostate on alternate days; PPN adds 25Gy/5f elective pelvic nodal
- 📊 CTCAE grade ≥2 GI 28% (PPN-SBRT) vs 21% (P-SBRT) over 12 weeks
- 📊 No difference in acute GU toxicity, clinician- or patient-reported
- 📊 GI difference mirrored in EPIC-26 bowel domain at 4 weeks, resolved by 12wk
- ⚠️ 11% PPN-SBRT vs 4% P-SBRT did not receive allocation, mostly unmet dose constraints
- ⚠️ Acute-toxicity readout only (≤12wk); late effects and cancer control pending
- 🔗 Extends 5f prostate SBRT (established in low/intermediate-risk) to high-risk with elective nodal coverage

### PIVOTALboost

> Late 2yr G2+ bowel/bladder no higher with added pelvic-node RT + focal boost (20fx) than prostate-only; nodal RT's early bowel toxicity resolves by 18wk.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Late tolerability, not efficacy, is the read: pelvic-node RT + boost at 20fx left 2yr G2+ bowel 6.5%, bladder 16.5%, no worse than prostate-only (8.2%/19.5%), with the early bowel bump resolving by 18wk. Weakens the late-toxicity case against elective nodal coverage in a hypofractionated schedule.

![PIVOTALboost](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiVWznXoAAqNwC.jpg)

| Arm (n) | Late G2+ bowel | Late G2+ bladder |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate (n=281) | 8.2% (5.7-11.7) | 19.5% (15.5-24.4) |
| Prostate+Boost (n=345) | 8.7% (6.3-12.1) | 24.1% (20.2-28.7) |
| Prostate+Nodes+Boost (n=347) | 6.5% (4.5-9.5) | 16.5% (13.1-20.6) |

- 📊 Late 2yr G2+ bowel and bladder similar across all three arms; adding pelvic nodes + focal boost did not increase durable toxicity (see table)
- 📊 Nodal RT raised early (<18wk) bowel toxicity vs prostate-only; the difference resolved by 18wk
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1465 randomised at 39 UK centres; 1° endpoint biochemical/clinical failure (not reported in this analysis)
- 🔍 Three randomised arms, all moderately hypofractionated 20-fraction IMRT
  - Prostate IMRT alone: 388 received
  - Prostate IMRT + focal boost (P+B): 464 received
  - Prostate + pelvic node IMRT + focal boost (PPN+B): 462 received
- 🔍 Focal boost dose and boost/nodal dose levels not reported in source
- ⚠️ Secondary toxicity readout only; the primary efficacy endpoint (biochemical/clinical failure) is not yet reported
- ⚠️ 'Preliminary late' data: only 973/1314 (74%) had ≥2yr follow-up
- 🔗 vs FLAME (phase 3): focal boost improved biochemical control without added late toxicity; PIVOTALboost extends the boost to a 20fx schedule and adds elective nodal RT

### PEACE-2

> Pelvic RT gave no significant cPFS benefit vs prostate-only RT: HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.088; 7yr cPFS 67.1% vs 62.9%.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The elective-nodal decision: whole-pelvic RT gave a non-significant 7yr cPFS edge (67.1% vs 62.9% prostate-only, HR 0.81, p=0.088), opposite POP-RT's positive read. In PET-staged very-high-risk N0M0 pts, routine pelvic nodal irradiation is not clearly earning its toxicity. Dose/fractionation not in source.

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7yr cPFS (95% CI) | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% (61.6-72.2) | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% (57.4-68.1) | ref | n/a |

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7XXMAADF2L.jpg)

- 🔍 2×2 factorial RCT (GETUG/Unicancer, international multicenter): pelvic vs prostate-only RT, crossed with ± cabazitaxel ×4; ADT ×3yr backbone
- 🔍 Very high-risk localized N0M0: ≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20 ng/mL; staged by conventional imaging or choline PET/CT
- 📊 Pelvic nodal RT did not significantly improve cPFS vs prostate-only; 1° endpoint not met (p=0.088)
- 🔗 Diverges from POP-RT (JCO 2021), where whole-pelvic RT improved biochemical failure-free survival in high-risk N0 pts
- 🔗 vs RTOG 9413: whole-pelvic RT benefit long inconsistent; PEACE-2 adds a modern null in the PET-eligible staging era
- ⚠️ cPFS is a surrogate, not OS; CI 0.63-1.03 can't exclude a modest pelvic-RT benefit, and the numeric trend favored pelvis
- ⚠️ <1 in 10 men died of prostate cancer by 10yr, questioning the 'very high-risk' label, esp if PSMA PET N0
- 🔍 Cabazitaxel arms (C/D) results not shown in this presentation

## 🎀 Breast

Long-horizon de-escalation dominates: 15-20yr APBI, moderate hypofx, boost and IM-MS omission reads, plus OLIGOMA's first positive breast-oligomet MDT RCT.

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4 mo, HR 0.48 (0.25-0.91), p=0.021 favoring add-on ablative RT to all lesions in oligometastatic breast.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The QoL co-primary is the quiet win: ablating ALL lesions (not just symptomatic) left 12-wk QLQ-C30 non-inferior (Δ -2.1, NI margin -10), so the PFS gain (HR 0.48) doesn't cost short-term QoL. Moves the decision from palliative-RT-for-symptoms toward comprehensive ablative MDT in ER/PR+ first-line breast oligomets. Dose/fractionation unreported, so technique transfer is unresolved.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiTEAqWkAAsym0.jpg)

| Arm | Median PFS (95% CI) | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Experimental (MDT) | 35.8 mo (24.1-NR) | 0.48 (0.25-0.91) | 0.021 |
| Control | 20.4 mo (12.4-28.4) | — | — |

![12-wk QLQ-C30: 72.2 (67.2-77.2) exp vs 74.3 (69.3-79.3) control; between-group Δ -2.1 (-9.2-5.1), NI margin -10.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrAwXYAA_Wd6.jpg)
*12-wk QLQ-C30: 72.2 (67.2-77.2) exp vs 74.3 (69.3-79.3) control; between-group Δ -2.1 (-9.2-5.1), NI margin -10.*

- 🔍 First RCT adding metastasis-directed RT (ablative to all lesions) to systemic therapy in oligometastatic breast cancer (≤5 mets, any line)
- 🔍 Co-primary EPs: PFS and QLQ-C30 QoL at 12wk; stratified by systemic-therapy type and treatment line
- 🩻 >80% had 1-3 mets, 2/3 of lesions bone; mostly ER/PR+ HER2- in the first-line setting
- 📊 QoL co-primary non-inferior at 12wk: ablative RT to all mets didn't degrade short-term QoL
- ⚠️ Stopped early: <20% of initial, <40% of amended accrual target (43 exp / 44 control). Small trial, wide CI (HR upper bound 0.91)
- ⚠️ PFS is a surrogate; OS not reported in source. Open-label (MDT vs no-MDT can't be blinded)
- 🔗 SABR-COMET (mixed histology) first signaled an MDT survival benefit; OLIGOMA is the first positive RCT specific to breast oligomets
- ⚠️ RT dose, fractionation, and modality of the 'local ablative RT' not specified in source
- 🔍 Confirmatory breast-MDT trials still accruing: TAORMINA, STEREO-SEIN, LARA, CLEAR, COSMO, ARCHER, ISTMET, OLIGAMI

### APBI-IMRT Florence · [[NCT02104895]]

> 15yr IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (APBI 30Gy/5fx vs WBI+boost), HR 1.57 (0.82-3.04) p=0.17; excess is new primaries, not true relapse.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is local control: true in-field relapse stayed flat (2.1% vs 1.6% at 15yr), and the numerical IBTR excess is new ipsilateral primaries the partial-breast volume never covered. Supports 30Gy/5fx APBI as durable de-escalation in pT<25mm, margin-negative, age>40 breast, though powered only at 5yr (15yr CI 0.82-3.04 stays wide).

![IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)

- 📊 1° EP IBTR at 15yr: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 — numerically higher with APBI, not significant
- 📊 15yr recurrence by arm (ITT)

  | Endpoint (15yr) | APBI | WBI | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | IBTR | 20 (7.7%) | 11 (4.2%) | 0.14 |
  | True local relapse | 5 (2.1%) | 4 (1.6%) | 0.75 |
  | New ipsilateral primary | 15 (5.9%) | 7 (2.7%) | 0.09 |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2%) | 13 (5.0%) | 0.28 |

- 🔍 Phase III equivalence RCT, N=520, 1:1; enrolled 2005-2013; median f/u now 15yr (Livi EJC 2015, Meattini JCO 2020)
- 📐 APBI 30Gy/5fx IMRT to partial breast vs WBI 50Gy/25fx + 10Gy/5fx tumour-bed boost
- 📊 Authors: the IBTR excess is new ipsilateral primaries, not true local relapse
- ⚠️ New-primary vs true-relapse is a soft distinction at 15yr; the absolute IBTR gap is real to the pt regardless
- ⚠️ Powered for 5yr IBTR equivalence (Δ5%, 80% power); wide 15yr HR CI (0.82-3.04) can't exclude a real local-control decrement
- 🔗 No difference in locoregional control, distant mets, breast-cancer-specific or overall survival at 15yr

### DBCG RT Natural

> Invasive LR 1.5% with PBI vs 9.8% without at median 4y in low-risk elderly breast; omitting both RT+ET → 12.2%.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is the 2×2: PBI alone (3.0%) and ET alone (3.7%) give near-identical local control, both far below the omit-everything arm (12.2%). Because real-world ET adherence is often poor, PBI (40Gy/15fr) offers adherence-independent control. Moves the omit-PBI decision toward keeping RT when endocrine therapy can't be assured.

![DBCG RT Natural](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg_rzdWIAAlnil.jpg)

| Arm | Events/Total | Invasive LR, CIF % (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|
| +RT (PBI) | 2/236 | 1.5 (0.3-5.1) |
| -RT (randomized) | 19/272 | 9.8 (5.9-14.9) |
| S-RT (self-selected no PBI) | 18/278 | 8.2 (4.5-13.3) |

![DBCG RT Natural](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhIv1kXYAAR4xZ.jpg)

| RT × ET | Events/Total | Invasive LR CIF % |
|---|---|---|
| +RT +ET | 0/105 | 0.0 |
| +RT -ET | 2/132 | 3.0 |
| -RT +ET | 7/213 | 3.7 |
| -RT -ET | 32/352 | 12.2 |

- 🔍 Phase III randomized: PBI 40Gy/15fr vs no PBI, stratified by institution and ET use
- 🔍 Eligible: ≥60y, pT1N0, unifocal non-lobular, ER≥10%, HER2-normal, grade 1-2, margin ≥2mm, post-BCS
- 🔍 1° EP: 5-yr invasive LR (assumed 2%, max acceptable 4%); planned N=926
- ⚠️ First/interim results at median 4y FU (5-yr primary immature); stopped on planned rules, so magnitude may be inflated
- 📊 41 invasive LR total (36 isolated), all invasive; 39 of 41 occurred in no-PBI pts
- 📊 Only 4 distant failures (2 +RT, 2 no PBI); no survival signal reported in source
- ⚠️ Real-world ET adherence often suboptimal; PBI delivers local control independent of adherence
- 🔗 vs PRIME II / LUMINA (both support RT omission in low-risk elderly on ET): DBCG counters that omitting both RT+ET is unsafe
- 🔗 vs EUROPA (PBI vs endocrine in elderly low-risk): RT arm had better QoL (per curator note)

### IMPORT HIGH

> 10yr IBTR 3.7% (48Gy SIB) vs 3.5% (sequential 40+16Gy) vs 5.5% (53Gy); SIB non-inferior, further escalation not advantageous.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The durability is the read: 48Gy/15F SIB stays non-inferior to the sequential 40Gy+16Gy boost at 10yr (IBTR 3.7% vs 3.5%), while escalating to 53Gy costs local control (5.5%) with no OS or late-toxicity offset. Backs 48Gy SIB in 15 fractions over 3 weeks as the boost standard, no further escalation.

![IMPORT HIGH](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)

| Arm | 10yr IBTR % (95% CI) |
|---|---|
| 40Gy/15F + 16Gy/8F | 3.5 (2.4-5.0) |
| 48Gy/15F (3.2Gy/F) | 3.7 (2.6-5.3) |
| 53Gy/15F (3.5Gy/F) | 5.5 (4.1-7.3) |

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, 1:1:1, N=2617, 76 UK hospitals (2009-2015); higher-than-average local-relapse risk group
- 🔍 Three RT arms, all delivered over 3 weeks
  - Control: 40Gy/15F whole breast + sequential 16Gy/8F boost
