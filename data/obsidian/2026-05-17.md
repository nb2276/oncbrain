# 2026-05-17

**PEACE-2: elective pelvic RT misses cPFS (HR 0.81, p=0.088), splitting from POP-RT (bPFS HR 0.50); whole-pelvis nodal benefit now contested.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Prostate led with the elective pelvic-RT question splitting: PEACE-2 null on cPFS against POP-RT's positive biochemical signal. Breast brought long-horizon de-escalation reads (APBI, hypofx, boost/IM-MS omission). OLIGOMA delivered the first positive breast-oligomet MDT RCT (mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4, HR 0.48).

## 🌰 Prostate

Pelvic nodal RT is on trial from both ends: PEACE-2 negative on efficacy, PACE-NODES quantifying what the nodal volume costs in a 5-fraction regimen.

### POP-RT vs PEACE-2

> WPRT bFFS HR 0.50 in POP-RT vs bPFS HR 0.97 in PEACE-2; pelvic nodal RT benefit vanishes with 3yr ADT and higher prostate dose.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The two trials differ on multiple axes at once, so the null in PEACE-2 cannot be attributed to WPRT alone: 36 vs 24 months ADT and 78 vs 74-76 Gy EQD2 to prostate. That confounding is the whole elective-nodal decision.

![POP-RT vs PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkfsYnaYAAfa1-.jpg)

| Endpoint | POP-RT HR (95% CI), p | PEACE-2 HR (95% CI), p |
|---|---|---|
| bFFS / bPFS | 0.50 (0.42-0.61), p<0.001 | 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73 |
| cFFS / cPFS | 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09 |
| MFS | 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- 📊 Whole-pelvis RT efficacy by trial (HR <1 favors WPRT)

  | Endpoint | POP-RT | PEACE-2 |
  |---|---|---|

  | cFFS / cPFS | HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09 |
  | MFS | HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- 🔍 POP-RT: 24mo ADT, prostate 74-76 Gy EQD2, conventional CT/bone-scan staging
- 🔍 PEACE-2: 36mo ADT, prostate 78 Gy EQD2, conventional/choline PET/CT staging
- 🔍 Both phase III, open-label, randomized, WPRT+prostate boost vs prostate-only IMRT, N0M0
- 🔍 POP-RT accrued 2011-2016 (median f/u 6.3-7.2 yr); PEACE-2 accrued 2018-2023
- ⚠️ PEACE-2 read is an interim analysis at ESTRO 2026, median f/u ~5.5 yr
- ⚠️ Populations differ: POP-RT high/very-high risk vs PEACE-2 very-high risk only
- ⚠️ Cross-trial comparison, not a randomized head-to-head; era and systemic intensification confound the RT question
- 📊 Both trials: comparable grade ≥2 late GU toxicity between arms, no toxicity penalty for WPRT

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> Interim data: 36.25 Gy/5 fx SBRT with whole-pelvis RT and 2y ADT shows no inferiority signal vs 68 Gy/25 fx; BFFS immature.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The transferable point is the pelvic dose: 25 Gy / 5 fx elective nodal with SIB permitted to involved nodes, grade 3+ under 1%, which is the constraint question that has kept 5-fraction prescribing confined to node-negative prostate-only. HYPO-RT-PC never tested nodes or ADT, so this extends the ultrahypofractionation toxicity envelope, not its efficacy.

![PRIME](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)

| Toxicity (Grade ≥2) | SBRT (5 fx) | Moderate Hypo (25 fx) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acute GU | ~5.4% | ~4.0% | 0.59 |
| Acute GI | ~2.2% | ~3.7% | 0.20 |
| Late GU | ~10-12% | ~9-11% | NS |
| Late GI | ~5-7% | ~4-6% | NS |
| Grade 3+ GU/GI | <1% | <1% | n/a |

![PRIME](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmcyeaQAAEf2f.jpg)

|  | HYPO-RT-PC | PRIME |
|---|---|---|
| Experimental arm | 42.7 Gy / 7 fx | 36.25 Gy / 5 fx |
| Control arm | 78 Gy / 39 fx | ~68 Gy / 25 fx |
| Accrual | 2010 - 2015 | 2018 - 2023 |
| Primary endpoint result | 10y FFS 72% vs 65%, HR 0.84 (0.69-1.03) | Not yet mature |

- 🔍 Phase III open-label randomized non-inferiority, Tata Memorial Centre + collaborating Indian centers; accrual 2018-2023, N~434, 1:1 stratified by risk group and nodal status
- 🔍 Eligibility: high-risk, very high-risk and/or node-positive non-metastatic prostate; ECOG 0-2; life expectancy 10 years; PSMA PET/CT staging allowed
- 📊 1° EP: BFFS (Phoenix, nadir + 2 ng/mL). Interim only, median f/u 1-2y; mature 4-5y efficacy pending
- 📐 RT prescription, both arms include whole-pelvis elective nodal RT
  - Arm A: 36.25 Gy / 5 fx (7.25 Gy per fx), every other day
  - Arm B: ~68 Gy / 25 fx (2.7 Gy per fx), 5 fx/week, ~5 weeks
  - Pelvic nodes: 25 Gy / 5 fx (or equivalent) in both arms
  - SIB to positive nodes allowed in the SBRT arm
  - Long-course ADT ~2 years in both arms
- 📊 Acute grade ≥2 GU ~5.4% SBRT vs ~4.0% moderate hypo, p=0.59; GI ~2.2% vs ~3.7%, p=0.20
- 📊 Grade 3+ acute and late toxicity <1% in both arms; late grade ≥2 GU ~10-12% vs ~9-11% (NS), GI ~5-7% vs ~4-6% (NS)
- 📊 QoL: EORTC QLQ-C30 / QLQ-PR25 / IPSS urinary and bowel domains stable and comparable; transient on-treatment declines with recovery
- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (ISRCTN45905321): 42.7 Gy/7 fx vs 78 Gy/39 fx, node-negative only, NO pelvic RT and NO ADT, mostly 3D-CRT; 10y FFS 72% vs 65%, adjusted HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.69-1.03), non-inferiority confirmed
- ⚠️ Efficacy read is interim at 1-2y for a BFFS endpoint under 2y of ADT; testosterone suppression masks biochemical failure, so early curve overlap carries little information
- ⚠️ 'No signal of inferiority' is a trend statement, not a met non-inferiority margin; no HR, CI, or margin reported in source
- ⚠️ Toxicity numbers in source are approximate ranges (~5.4%, ~10-12%), not point estimates with CIs; abstract-level reporting

### PIVOTALboost

> Late 2yr G2+ bowel 8.2% / 8.7% / 6.5% and bladder 19.5% / 24.1% / 16.5% across prostate, +boost, +pelvic nodes arms.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The late bladder G2+ signal sits with the boost, not the nodes: 24.1% in P+B vs 19.5% prostate-only and 16.5% in PPN+B, so adding elective nodal volume did not track with worse 2yr bladder or bowel toxicity. That makes the 20-fraction focal boost plus pelvic node volume a tolerable technique choice while the biochemical/clinical failure endpoint matures.

|---|---|---|
| Prostate (n=281) | 8.2% (5.7-11.7) | 19.5% (15.5-24.4) |
| Prostate+Boost (n=345) | 8.7% (6.3-12.1) | 24.1% (20.2-28.7) |
| Prostate+Nodes+Boost (n=347) | 6.5% (4.5-9.5) | 16.5% (13.1-20.6) |

![PIVOTALboost](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiVWumXYAAAIXc.jpg)

| Arm | Received |
|---|---|

| Prostate + Pelvic IMRT + Boost (PPN+B) | 462 |

![PIVOTALboost](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiVW0cXoAATPVi.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1465 randomised, 39 UK centres; 1° EP is biochemical/clinical failure (not reported here)
- 🔍 Three arms received: prostate IMRT 388, prostate IMRT + prostate boost (P+B) 464, prostate + pelvic IMRT + boost (PPN+B) 462
- 📐 20-fraction moderate hypofractionation; focal intraprostatic boost; elective pelvic nodal volume in the PPN+B arm
- 📊 Late toxicity cohort = 973 (74%) pts with ≥2yr follow-up; cumulative G2+ rates at 6-24 months
- 📊 Nodal RT raised early bowel side effects but resolved by 18 weeks post-RT; no significant 2yr differences
- ⚠️ Toxicity is a SECONDARY endpoint; efficacy (biochemical/clinical failure) not reported in source, so the boost/nodal benefit question stays open
- ⚠️ Late analysis restricted to the 74% with 2yr follow-up; no p-values or between-arm comparative statistics reported in source
- ⚠️ Denominators shift between randomisation (388/464/462) and the late-toxicity cohort (281/345/347); reasons not reported in source
- 🔗 Complements PIVOTAL (pelvic nodal IMRT feasibility) and the 20-fraction backbone from CHHiP; extends focal-boost safety beyond FLAME's 35-fraction schedule

### PACE-NODES

> Grade ≥2 GI toxicity 28% vs 21% with added pelvic nodal SBRT over 12wk; GU unchanged, symptoms resolved by 12wk.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The deliverability number is the buried read: 11% of PPN-SBRT pts never received the allocated treatment vs 4% prostate-only, mostly unmet constraints. Nodal dose was 25Gy/5f alongside 36.25Gy/5f prostate on alternate days. That gates whether 5f elective nodal coverage is exportable outside high-volume centres, before any efficacy signal arrives.

![PACE-NODES](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLVQWgAAvWBw.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 3 randomised, 1:1, N=1166 randomised (target n=1128), multicentre UK
- 🔍 Eligibility: high-risk localised (T3a-T4 and/or Gleason 8-10 and/or PSA>20), planned 12-36mo ADT
- 📊 Acute toxicity co-primaries: CTCAE grade ≥2 GI and GU up to 12wk

### PEACE-2

> 7yr cPFS 67.1% pelvic vs 62.9% prostate-only RT, HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.088: primary endpoint not met.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The elective pelvic nodal decision in very high-risk N0 disease loses its randomized support: 4.2 absolute points of 7yr cPFS, HR 0.81, CI crossing 1. Staging was conventional or choline PET, so pts PSMA PET would now upstage sat in both arms, diluting any true nodal benefit. Toxicity by arm not reported in source.

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% (61.6-72.2) | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% (57.4-68.1) | n/a | n/a |

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7XXMAADF2L.jpg)

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7ZWkAAoHej.jpg)

- 🔍 International multicenter randomized trial, 2×2 design: prostate-only vs pelvic RT, ± cabazitaxel ×4, all with ADT ×3yr
- 🔍 Eligibility: very high-risk localized CaP with ≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20 ng/mL
- 🔍 N0M0 staging by conventional imaging or choline PET/CT, i.e. pre-PSMA-PET era enrollment
- 📐 Pelvic RT arm n=381, prostate-only n=380 at risk at randomization
- ⚠️ HR from multivariable analysis, not the unadjusted stratified estimate; p=0.088 crosses no prespecified threshold
- ⚠️ Absolute 7yr cPFS difference is 4.2 points, with 95% CIs that overlap substantially (57.4-68.1 vs 61.6-72.2)
- ⚠️ Presenter's own framing: <1 in 10 men die of prostate cancer in the first decade, which questions whether this "very high-risk" definition selects a population with enough events for whole-pelvis RT to matter
- 🔗 Directionally opposite to POP-RT (Murthy, JCO 2021), which showed a bFFS benefit for whole-pelvis RT in high-risk N0 disease; PEACE-2 pelvic HR does not reproduce that magnitude

### RADIOSA (MFS post-hoc)

> Post-hoc MFS 16.6mo vs not reached, HR 0.39 favoring SBRT + 6mo ADT over SBRT alone in oligorecurrent prostate.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The additive read is the eugonadal MFS: benefit persisted after testosterone recovery (p<0.05), so the ADT effect is not just on-treatment suppression of imaging progression. That argues against reading RADIOSA's MFS split as a testosterone artifact, and moves the SBRT-alone vs SBRT + short-course ADT decision in oligorecurrence.

## 🎀 Breast

Long-horizon data (15-20yr Florence, EORTC IM-MS, DBCG HYPO at 12.8yr median) says de-escalation holds and dose escalation above 48Gy is a dead end.

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48 (0.25-0.91), p=0.021 for MDT to all lesions in oligometastatic breast, QoL non-inferior.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is who was actually irradiated: 2/3 of lesions were bone and >80% of pts had 1-3 mets, so this is largely bone-directed ablative RT in first-line ER+ disease, not a visceral-oligomet population. Dose and fractionation are not reported in source, which gates transfer to practice. QoL non-inferiority at 12wk (-2.1, margin -10) removes the main argument against adding local therapy to a working systemic backbone.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrAwXYAA_Wd6.jpg)

| Arm | QLQ-C30 mean at 12wk (95% CI) | Between-group change (ANCOVA) |
|---|---|---|
| Experimental | 72.2 (67.2-77.2) | -2.1 (-9.2-5.1) |
| Control | 74.3 (69.3-79.3) | n/a |

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiIcqBWAAEXppV.jpg)

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEdWoAADclc.jpg)

- 🔍 Randomised phase 2 (ARO-2021-09), N=87 (43 exp / 44 control), any treatment line, max 5 metastatic lesions
- 🔍 Randomisation stratified by type of systemic therapy and treatment line; systemic regimen set by MDT board
- 📐 Co-primary endpoints: PFS and EORTC QLQ-C30 summary score at 12 weeks post-randomisation
- 📊 QoL at 12wk (n=64): 72.2 (67.2-77.2) exp vs 74.3 (69.3-79.3) control; ANCOVA change -2.1 (-9.2-5.1), non-inferiority margin -10
- 🔍 Population (per curator slide)
  - Median age 58 exp / 59 control; ECOG 0 in 76.7% exp
  - ER+ 76.7% exp / 81.8% control; HER2+ 7.5% exp / 15.0% control
  - TNBC 12.5% exp / 11.4% control
  - First-line endocrine 55.8% exp / 54.5% control
  - >80% had 1-3 metastases, 2/3 of lesions were bone
- ⚠️ Recruitment stopped early: <20% of the initial target, <40% of the amended target. HR CI 0.25-0.91 is wide
- ⚠️ RT dose, fractionation, and technique for the local ablative arm not reported in source; transferability to practice unverifiable
- ⚠️ Open-label by design; PFS assessment blinding not stated in source. QoL co-primary is patient-reported in an unblinded trial
- ⚠️ No OS signal reported in source (listed as secondary); PFS in a systemic-therapy-driven disease is a soft endpoint for a local intervention
- 🔗 First RCT to show a PFS benefit from MDT specifically in oligometastatic breast, per the presenter's own discussion slide
- 🔗 Eight further RCTs still accruing per the discussion slide (TAORMINA, STEREO-SEIN, LARA, CLEAR, COSMO, ARCHER, ISTMET, OLIGAMI)

### APBI-IMRT Florence · [[NCT02104895]]

> 15yr IBTR 7.7% APBI vs 4.2% WBI, HR 1.57 (0.82-3.04) p=0.17; excess is new primaries, not true local relapse.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The 15yr excess sits in new ipsilateral primaries (5.9% vs 2.7%, p=0.09), not true local relapse (2.1% vs 1.6%). That splits the decision: 30Gy in 5# to the tumour bed holds the bed, but leaves untreated breast tissue at risk, which is a surveillance and endocrine-adherence question rather than an argument for whole-breast coverage.

| Local relapse | 5 (2.1) | 4 (1.6) | 0.75 |
| New ipsilateral breast cancer | 15 (5.9) | 7 (2.7) | 0.09 |
| Locoregional tumour recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
| Contralateral breast tumour | 10 (3.8) | 13 (5.0) | 0.67 |
