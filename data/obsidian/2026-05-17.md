# 2026-05-17

**OLIGOMA: mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48) — first breast-specific RCT PFS signal for MDT in oligoMBC.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast dominated today: locoregional RT questions resolved (IMPORT HIGH SIB non-inferior at 10yr, EORTC IM-MS OS null at 20yr) while OLIGOMA opens oligoMBC MDT as a phase III target. OligoCare's 2447-pt registry flags CRC as the outlier with 19.6% 3-yr local failure despite highest doses.

## 🌸 Breast

Five breast trials spanning RT technique, fractionation, IM-MS, RT omission, and oligometastatic MDT — a broad locoregional portfolio day.

### IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)

> IMPORT HIGH 10yr: SIB 48Gy/15F IBTR 3.7% vs sequential 40+16Gy 3.5% -- non-inferior, low late toxicity.

![10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0); 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3); 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3).](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)
*10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0); 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3); 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3).*

- 📊 10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0), 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3), 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=2617, 76 UK hospitals (2009-2015); 1:1:1; pT1-3 pN0-N3a M0 requiring boost after BCS
- 🔗 5-yr data (Lancet 2023): IBTR 1.9%, 2.0%, 3.2% for same three arms -- 10-yr rates roughly double but tracking same rank order
- 📊 OS difference vs 40Gy at 10yr: -0.5% (-3.0, 2.8) for 48Gy; +1.5% (-1.4, 5.1) for 53Gy -- no significant OS difference
- 📊 Late mod/marked AEs at 10yr: breast distortion/shrinkage <18%, induration <10%, telangiectasia <2%, breast oedema <2% across all arms
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm shows numerically higher IBTR (5.5% vs 3.5-3.7%) without OS benefit -- further dose escalation not supported
- ❓ Whether SIB benefit persists in pts receiving contemporary systemic therapy (CDK4/6i, neoadjuvant pembrolizumab) remains unaddressed

### APBI-IMRT Florence (NCT02104895) · [[NCT02104895]]

> APBI-IMRT Florence 15yr: IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17) — no difference in locoregional control, DM, or OS.

![IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. APBI 20 (7.7%) vs WBI 11 (4.2%) at 15 years.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)
*IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. APBI 20 (7.7%) vs WBI 11 (4.2%) at 15 years.*

- 📊 15-yr IBTR: APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%, HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 — not statistically significant
- 🔍 excess IBTR in APBI arm driven by new ipsilateral primaries (5.9% vs 2.7%, p=0.09), not true local relapse (2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75)
- 📊 Key outcomes at 15 years (ITT), APBI vs WBI

  | Endpoint | APBI N (%) | WBI N (%) | p-value |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | IBTR | 20 (7.7) | 11 (4.2) | 0.14 |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
  | Distant metastasis | 7 (2.7) | 12 (4.6) | 0.35 |
  | Deaths (all cause) | 56 (21.5) | 51 (19.6) | 0.66 |
  | Breast cancer deaths | 6 (2.3) | 8 (3.1) | 0.79 |

- 🔍 phase III RCT, N=520, 1:1 APBI (30Gy/5#) vs WBI (50Gy/25# + 10Gy boost); selected early BC: BCS, pT <25mm, FSM ≥5mm, age >40; accrual 2005-2013
- 🔗 consistent with IMPORT LOW and GEC-ESTRO APBI trials showing non-inferiority in selected low-risk pts at 5-10yr; 15yr data now longest mature APBI-IMRT f/u
- ⚠️ numerically higher IBTR rate (7.7% vs 4.2%) remains; trial powered for equivalence margin Δ5% at 5yr — 15yr absolute gap of 3.5% approaches that margin even if p=0.14
- ❓ whether new ipsilateral primaries reflect true RT field-miss or second primary biology — implications for patient selection and surveillance remain open

### OLIGOMA (NCT04495309) · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4 mo (HR 0.48, p=0.021) favoring MDT + systemic over systemic alone in oligometastatic breast cancer.

![OLIGOMA (NCT04495309)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 Co-primary PFS: mPFS 35.8 mo (95% CI 24.1-NR) vs 20.4 mo (12.4-28.4), HR 0.48 (0.25-0.91), p=0.021
- 📊 Co-primary QoL at 12 wks: EORTC QLQ-C30 72.2 vs 74.3; delta -2.1 (95% CI -9.2 to 5.1) -- non-inferiority margin -10 pts met
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=87 (43 MDT arm, 44 control); oligoMBC any line, max 5 lesions; majority ER/PR+ HER2- in 1L; >80% had 1-3 mets, 2/3 lesions bone
- ⚠️ Recruitment stopped early (<20% of initial planned N, <40% of amended target); wide CI on HR (0.25-0.91) reflects severe underpowering -- positive p-value must be interpreted with caution
- 🔗 vs SABR-COMET (Lancet 2019): cross-histology MDT signal included breast; OLIGOMA is first breast-specific RCT with a PFS benefit, not just OS signal in mixed cohort
- ❓ OS immature; multiple larger ongoing trials (TAORMINA, STEREO-SEIN, LARA, OLIGAMI) needed to define patient selection and confirm OS benefit

### EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS 20yr: OS identical (61.0% vs 61.8%, HR 1.00) with IM-MS RT in stage I-III breast cancer.

![OS (ITT): HR=1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967. 20yr OS 61.0% vs 61.8%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh-XcAAFDgr.jpg)
*OS (ITT): HR=1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967. 20yr OS 61.0% vs 61.8%.*

- 📊 1° EP OS at 20yr (ITT): 61.0% (+IM-MS) vs 61.8% (-IM-MS), HR=1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967 — null result
- 📊 BCM at 20yr: 18.6% vs 22.4%, HR=0.82 (0.72-0.95), p=0.006 — significant BCM reduction persists
- 📊 Non-BCM mortality at 20yr: 20.4% vs 15.8%, HR=1.26 (1.09-1.46), p=0.002 — competing-cause deaths higher in irradiated arm, offsetting BCM benefit
- 📊 pN0 subgroup at 20yr: DFS 53.9% vs 53.6% HR=0.93 (0.81-1.07), p=0.318; DMFS 67.2% vs 67.4% HR=0.93 (0.78-1.10), p=0.397 — no signal in node-negative pts
- 🔗 DBCG IMN2 (Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): mean heart doses 4-9x lower than EORTC IM-MS; modern IM-MS RT reduced distant metastasis, BCM, and improved OS in node-positive pts at 15yr — suggests cardiac toxicity from older technique explains non-BCM excess mortality here
- ⚠️ Higher non-BCM death rate in irradiated arm likely reflects excess cardiac toxicity from 1990s-era technique (MHD ~3-8x higher than modern IM-RT); limits OS interpretation for current practice
- ❓ Whether modern low-dose IM-MS RT (MHD 1.2-2.3 Gy per DBCG IMN2) can achieve BCM benefit without the non-BCM mortality penalty remains the key question for node-positive pts

### DBCG RT Natural

> DBCG RT Natural: PBI omission yielded 9.8% vs 1.5% 5-yr LR at 4yr median f/u in low-risk breast conservation pts.

![LR by PBI + ET: +RT+ET 0/105 (0.0%); +RT-ET 2/132 (3.0%); -RT+ET 7/213 (3.7%); -RT-ET 32/352 (12.2%, 95% CI 5.2-17.1%). 4% threshold shown.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhIv1kXYAAR4xZ.jpg)
*LR by PBI + ET: +RT+ET 0/105 (0.0%); +RT-ET 2/132 (3.0%); -RT+ET 7/213 (3.7%); -RT-ET 32/352 (12.2%, 95% CI 5.2-17.1%). 4% threshold shown.*

- 📊 1° EP (5-yr invasive LR): 9.8% (no PBI arm, CIF 5.9-14.9%) vs 1.5% (PBI arm) at median 4yr f/u; 39 of 41 LR events in no-PBI arm
- 📊 LR by PBI + ET status (4yr CIF) — comparison values omitted (cell number "1.3" not verified in source)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT; N=926 planned; eligibility: ≥60yr, pT1N0, breast conservation, HER2-normal, grade 1-2, limited DCIS, margin ≥2mm; PBI arm 40Gy/15fr; trial stopped early per interim stopping rules
- 🔗 Validates PRIME II / CALGB 9343 omission signal but with harder LR endpoint -- omission group here hitting ~10% at 4yr, above the pre-specified 4% acceptable threshold
- ⚠️ No-PBI arm was partially self-selecting (pts opting out of RT), introducing selection bias; ET adherence suboptimal in -ET/-RT group driving the 12.2% tail
- ❓ Whether -RT +ET (3.7% CIF) is acceptable in shared decision-making for truly low-risk pts who decline RT remains open -- curves still separating at 4yr

> [!question] Open questions

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

### OligoCare (EORTC)

> OligoCare registry: SBRT for oligomets achieves 88.6% local control at 3 yrs across 2447 pts, 57 institutions.

![Local in-field progression by primary tumor type. 1-yr: CRC 4.1%, Breast 9.3%, NSCLC 6.0%, Prostate 2.7%. 3-yr: CRC 19.6%, Breast 11.3%, NSCLC 9.8%, Prostate 8.1%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg6aU7WAAEf5lR.jpg)
*Local in-field progression by primary tumor type. 1-yr: CRC 4.1%, Breast 9.3%, NSCLC 6.0%, Prostate 2.7%. 3-yr: CRC 19.6%, Breast 11.3%, NSCLC 9.8%, Prostate 8.1%.*

- 📊 Local in-field progression: 5.0% at 1 yr, 11.4% at 3 yrs (local control 88.6% at 3 yrs); median f/u 31 months
- 🔍 Prospective registry, N=2447 pts / 3533 lesions, July 2019-July 2025, 57 institutions; de novo OMD outperformed repeat OMD (higher delivered dose)
- 📊 Local in-field progression at 1 and 3 yrs by primary tumor type

  | Primary | 1-yr LF | 3-yr LF |
  |---|---|---|
  | Prostate | 2.7% | 8.1% |
  | NSCLC | 6.0% | 9.8% |
  | Breast | 9.3% | 11.3% |
  | Colorectal | 4.1% | 19.6% |

- ⚠️ CRC had highest local failure at 3 yrs (19.6%) despite receiving highest median dose per fraction — highlights relative radioresistance; authors flag need for combination tx or dose escalation
- 🔍 Minimum PTV dose identified as most critical technical factor for local control; dose optimization emphasized in conclusions
- 🔗 Real-world registry data complement randomized evidence (SABR-COMET, STOMP, ORIOLE) showing OS/PFS benefit of SBRT for oligomets; this is the largest prospective OMD-SBRT dataset to date
- ❓ No OS or PFS data reported in this interim; systemic therapy heterogeneity across 57 institutions limits dose-response interpretation

## 🍇 Prostate

### PRIME Trial (NCT03561961) · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim: 5-fx SBRT non-inferior to 25-fx modHypo with pelvic RT + ADT; Grade 3+ toxicity <1% both arms; mature efficacy pending.

- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority RCT; N~434; high-risk/node-positive non-metastatic PCa; both arms: whole-pelvis RT + 2yr ADT; accrual 2018-2023 (Tata Memorial, India)
- 💊 Arm A: 36.25 Gy/5 fx (7.25 Gy/fx) every other day; Arm B: ~68 Gy/25 fx (2.7 Gy/fx); pelvic nodal 25 Gy/5 fx both arms
- 📊 1° EP BFFS (Phoenix): no inferiority signal at interim (1-2yr f/u); no effect size reported in source tweets -- mature 4-5yr data awaited
- 📊 Toxicity by timing and arm

  | Endpoint | SBRT (5 fx) | Moderate Hypo (25 fx) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | Acute G2+ GU | ~5.4% | ~4.0% | NS |
  | Acute G2+ GI | ~2.2% | ~3.7% | NS |
  | Acute G3+ | <1% | <1% | NS |
  | Late G2+ GU | ~10-12% | ~9-11% | NS |
  | Late G2+ GI | ~5-7% | ~4-6% | NS |
  | Late G3+ | <1% | <1% | NS |

- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (10-yr): FFS 72% vs 65%, adj HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.69-1.03) non-inferior; but node-negative, no ADT, no pelvic RT -- PRIME tests a meaningfully higher-risk population
- ⚠️ 1-2yr f/u insufficient to adjudicate non-inferiority for PCa BFFS; no HR or CI reportable from interim -- practice change requires mature 4-5yr data
- ❓ PEACE-2 (3yr ADT, PSMA staging) showed no WPRT benefit (bPFS HR 0.97); POP-RT (2yr ADT) did (HR 0.50) -- PRIME's pelvic RT gain uncertain at maturity
