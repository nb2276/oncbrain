# 2026-05-17

**RCC SBRT achieves 100% local control at 5 years, reported at ESTRO26.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> ESTRO26 Day 3 concentrated on long-term RT outcomes across breast cancer and oligometastatic disease. ## 🌸 Early Breast Cancer Radiotherapy — Long-Term RCT Updates

> [!info] 🧪 Intro
> Three phase III trials reported mature long-term outcomes at ESTRO26, spanning fractionation schedules, partial versus whole-breast field design, and nodal irradiation including internal mammary chains. These data directly inform current early-stage breast RT practice.

> [!example] 📐 Methods
> IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448): dose-escalated simultaneous integrated boost, 10-year follow-up. APBI-IMRT Florence phase III: partial vs whole-breast irradiation, 15-year follow-up. EORTC 22922/10925: IMN and medial supraclavicular irradiation vs no nodal RT in stage I-III breast cancer including pN0, 20-year follow-up.

### 📊 Results

- IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448): 10-year data presented; no effect sizes reported in source tweets.
- APBI-IMRT Florence phase III: 15-year outcomes presented; no effect sizes reported in source tweets.
- EORTC 22922/10925: 20-year results presented including pN0 subgroup; no effect sizes reported in source tweets.
- Commentary: suboptimal endocrine therapy adherence observed alongside RT; 5-fraction schedules noted to simplify clinical decision-making.

### 💭 Discussion

- 20-year IMN irradiation data in pN0 patients may reframe nodal field selection for lower-risk disease.
- ET non-adherence remains a persistent confounder in interpreting combined RT plus ET outcomes across trials.

## 🎯 SABR for Oligometastatic Disease — Registry and RCT Data

> [!info] 🧪 Intro
> The OLIGOCARE registry provides the largest prospective dataset on SABR across oligometastatic histologies, addressing local control rates, prognostic factors, and dosimetric correlates of outcome.

> [!example] 📐 Methods
> OLIGOCARE (e2irradiate, EORTC): prospective registry, ~2500 patients, ~3500 metastases treated with SABR across multiple histologies. OLIGOMA (ARO-2021-09, [[NCT04495309]]): RCT of metastases-directed treatment in oligometastatic breast cancer.

### 📊 Results

- OLIGOCARE: local failure 5% at 1 year and 11% at 3 years across ~3500 metastases.
- Colorectal cancer associated with higher risk of progression after SABR in OLIGOCARE.
- Minimum PTV dose correlated with outcome in OLIGOCARE.
- OLIGOMA ([[NCT04495309]]): results presented for oligometastatic breast cancer; no effect sizes reported in source tweets.

### 💭 Discussion

- Minimum PTV dose as an outcome correlate supports dose-escalation and adaptive planning strategies in SABR.
- Higher colorectal progression risk may warrant histology-stratified patient selection criteria for oligometastatic programs.

## 🍇 High-Risk Prostate Cancer — Hypofractionation with Pelvic Nodal Irradiation

> [!info] 🧪 Intro
> Optimal fractionation for high-risk prostate cancer requiring pelvic nodal coverage remains unsettled. PRIME directly tests whether ultra-short SBRT can safely replace moderate hypofractionation when pelvic nodes are included.

> [!example] 📐 Methods
> PRIME trial: biologically aggressive prostate cancer on ADT; moderate hypofractionation (~68 Gy/25 fractions/5 weeks) vs extreme hypofractionation/SBRT (36.25 Gy/5 fractions/1-2 weeks), both arms including pelvic nodal irradiation. POP RT vs PEACE II comparison: 2 years ADT plus whole-pelvis RT vs 3 years ADT plus prostate-only RT.

### 📊 Results

- PRIME trial design presented; no efficacy or toxicity outcome data reported in source tweets.
- POP RT vs PEACE II: design-level comparison presented; no outcome data reported in source tweets.

### 💭 Discussion

- If SBRT proves safe with pelvic nodal coverage in PRIME, 5-fraction delivery would substantially reduce treatment burden for high-risk disease.
- POP RT vs PEACE II comparison highlights ongoing uncertainty about ADT duration and field extent in pelvic nodal disease.
