# 2026-05-17

**DBCG RT Natural stopped early: PBI omission 9.8% vs 1.5% 4-yr LR; low-risk BCS still requires RT.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast cancer RT dominated ESTRO 2026 with five trials. DBCG RT Natural stopped early; RT omission in low-risk BCS produces unacceptable LR. EORTC IM-MS confirms BCM benefit at 20yr but OS null from 1990s-era cardiac doses. OLIGOMA delivers first RCT MDT PFS signal in oligomet BC, hypothesis-generating.

## 🌸 Breast

Five breast RT trials from ESTRO 2026: two with 15-20yr follow-up, one stopped early, one oligomets MDT RCT.

### OLIGOMA (NCT04495309) · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT + systemic therapy vs systemic therapy alone in oligometastatic breast cancer; mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48 favoring MDT.

![OLIGOMA (NCT04495309)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 Co-primary EP (PFS): mPFS 35.8mo (95% CI 24.1-NR) vs 20.4mo (95% CI 12.4-28.4); HR 0.48 (95% CI 0.25-0.91), p=0.021
- 📊 Co-primary EP (QoL at 12 wks): EORTC QLQ-C30 summary score 72.2 vs 74.3; between-group difference -2.1 (95% CI -9.2 to 5.1) — non-inferiority margin -10pts met
- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=87 (44 experimental, 43 control); oligometastatic BC any line, ≤5 lesions; ablative RT to all metastatic sites; systemic regimen per MDT discretion
- 🔍 Population: majority ER/PR+ HER2- 1L; >80% had 1-3 metastases; 2/3 of lesions were bone
- ⚠️ Recruited to <20% of initial target and <40% of amended target — severely underpowered; wide CI on HR (0.25-0.91) reflects this; results hypothesis-generating only
- 🔗 First RCT reporting PFS benefit from MDT in oligometastatic BC; several concurrent trials ongoing (TAORMINA, STEREO-SEIN, LARA, CLEAR, COSMO, ARCHER, ISTMET, OLIGAMI) — pooled/meta analyses needed
- ❓ Which subgroups benefit most (receptor subtype, line of therapy, lesion distribution) remains unanswered

### APBI-IMRT Florence (NCT02104895) · [[NCT02104895]]

> APBI-IMRT Florence 15yr: IBTR 7.7% vs 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17), no difference in OS, LRFS, or distant mets.

![IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%. Difference driven by new ipsilateral primaries (APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09); local relapse 2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)
*IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%. Difference driven by new ipsilateral primaries (APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09); local relapse 2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75.*

- 📊 15-yr IBTR: 7.7% (APBI) vs 4.2% (WBI), HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17
- 🔍 Numerical excess in IBTR driven by new ipsilateral primaries (5.9% vs 2.7%, p=0.09), not true local relapse (2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75)
- 📊 15-yr key outcomes (ITT, APBI vs WBI)

  | Endpoint | APBI N (%) | WBI N (%) | p |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | IBTR | 20 (7.7) | 11 (4.2) | 0.14 |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 20 (7.2) | 13 (5.0) | 0.28 |
  | Distant metastasis | 7 (2.7) | 12 (4.6) | 0.35 |
  | Deaths (all cause) | 56 (21.5) | 51 (19.6) | 0.66 |

- 🔍 Phase III, N=520, 1:1 randomization 2005-2013; APBI: 30Gy in 5#; WBI: 50Gy/25# + 10Gy/5# tumor bed boost; eligibility: BCS, pT <25mm, FSM ≥5mm, age >40
- 🔗 Consistent with IMPORT LOW and RAPID at 5-10yr: APBI non-inferior for local relapse in selected low-risk early BC, though new primaries inflate crude IBTR
- ⚠️ Equivalence margin was 5% for 5-yr IBTR; at 15yr the absolute gap widens to ~3.5% — trial was not powered for late divergence driven by new primaries
- ❓ Whether new ipsilateral primaries reflect field effect from omitted WBI or surveillance bias remains unresolved; implications for pts with higher contralateral/new-primary risk unclear

### EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS at 20 years: IM-MS RT shows no OS benefit (HR 1.00) in ITT but improves BCM in node-positive pts.

![EORTC IM-MS (22922/10925)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh9WMAAK6Lh.jpg)

| Endpoint | + IM-MS RT | - IM-MS RT | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| BCM rate (20 yr) | 18.6% | 22.4% | 0.82 (0.72-0.95) | 0.006 |
| Non-BCM rate (20 yr) | 20.4% | 15.8% | 1.26 (1.09-1.46) | 0.002 |

- 📊 Primary EP (OS, ITT): 61.0% vs 61.8% at 20 yrs, HR=1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967 — no difference
- 📊 Cause-specific mortality at 20 yrs (+ vs - IM-MS RT)
- 📊 pN0 subgroup at 20 yrs (+ vs - IM-MS RT)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, stage I-III breast cancer; ITT analysis; presented at ESTRO 2026 as special joint plenary
- ⚠️ OS null result at 20 yrs despite BCM benefit: non-BCM mortality significantly increased with IM-MS RT (HR 1.26, p=0.002) — cardiac dose likely culprit given mean heart doses in this era were 4-9x higher than modern techniques (DBCG IMN2: MHD 1.2 Gy right-sided, 2.3 Gy left-sided)
- 🔗 vs DBCG IMN2 (Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): modern IM-MS RT with lower MHD improved OS + reduced BCM in node-positive pts — era effect limits direct extrapolation of EORTC OS null result to current practice
- ❓ whether modern cardiac-sparing IM-MS RT recovers the OS signal that 1990s-era high heart doses obscured remains the operative clinical question

### DBCG RT Natural

> DBCG RT Natural: PBI omission yields 9.8% vs 1.5% 4-yr LR in low-risk breast conservation, trial stopped early.

![DBCG RT Natural](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhIv1kXYAAR4xZ.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=926 planned; pT1N0, unifocal, HER2-normal, grade 1-2, margin ≥2mm breast conservation; randomized PBI 40Gy/15fr vs no PBI; ET per guideline (recommended for pT1c and/or grade 2)
- 📊 At median 4yr f/u: 39 of 41 LR events in no-PBI arm; CIF 9.8% (5.9-14.9%) no-RT vs 1.5% PBI arm — crossed pre-specified 4% stopping threshold
- 📊 4-yr LR CIF by treatment combination — comparison values omitted (cell number "1.3" not verified in source)
- ⚠️ Self-selection bias: no-PBI arm includes pts who declined RT — not a pure randomized comparison of RT vs no-RT; ET adherence suboptimal in -ET groups confounds the RT-only signal
- 🔗 Consistent with PRIME II and CALGB 9343 omission data, but DBCG captures a broader grade 1-2 population and shows RT or ET each reduce LR substantially vs surgery alone
- ❓ Whether 5-fraction PBI (vs 15fr used here) would further simplify decision-making in this low-risk cohort remains open

### IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)

> IMPORT HIGH 10yr: 48Gy SIB IBTR 3.7% vs 3.5% sequential boost; further escalation to 53Gy (5.5%) not advantageous.

![IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)

| Arm | 10-yr IBTR (95%CI) |
|---|---|
| 40Gy/15F + 16Gy/8F | 3.5% (2.4-5.0) |
| 48Gy/15F (3.2Gy/F) | 3.7% (2.6-5.3) |
| 53Gy/15F (3.5Gy/F) | 5.5% (4.1-7.3) |

- 🔍 phase III RCT N=2617, pT1-3 pN0-3a M0 BCS requiring boost; 1:1:1, 76 UK centres 2009-2015, all arms 3-week delivery
- 📊 5-yr IBTR (Lancet 2023): 40Gy+16Gy 1.9%, 48Gy SIB 2.0%, 53Gy SIB 3.2%; 48Gy non-inferior with similar/reduced AEs at 5yr
- 📊 late moderate/marked AEs at 10 years (all arms)
  - breast distortion/shrinkage: <18% all arms
  - induration: <10% all arms
  - telangiectasia: <2% all arms
  - breast oedema: <2% all arms
- 📐 absolute difference in 10-yr OS vs 40Gy/15F: 48Gy -0.5% (-3.0, 2.8); 53Gy +1.5% (-1.4, 5.1) -- no OS signal either arm
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm IBTR 5.5% exceeds both lower-dose arms at 10yr -- dose escalation beyond 48Gy appears detrimental, not merely neutral
- ❓ SIB benefit in ultra-high-risk subgroups (pN3a, high nuclear grade) vs potential de-escalation in low-risk BCS pts unstudied

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

OligoCare provides the largest prospective SBRT LC dataset across primaries: 2447 pts, 57 institutions, 88.6% 3-yr LC overall.

### OligoCare (EORTC)

> OligoCare registry: SBRT for oligomets achieves 88.6% local control at 3 yrs across 2447 pts, 57 institutions.

![OligoCare (EORTC)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg6aU7WAAEf5lR.jpg)

| Primary | 1-yr LF | 3-yr LF |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate | 2.7% | 8.1% |
| Breast | 4.1% | 11.3% |
| NSCLC | 6.0% | 9.8% |
| Colorectal (CRC) | 9.3% | 19.6% |

- 🔍 Prospective EORTC registry; 57 institutions, 2447 pts, 3533 lesions; median f/u 31 months; July 2019-July 2025
- 📊 Local in-field progression: 5.0% at 1 yr, 11.4% at 3 yrs (88.6% LC at 3 yrs)
- 📊 Local in-field progression by primary tumor type
- 📐 Minimum PTV dose = most critical technical factor for LC; de novo OMD outperforms repeat OMD (higher delivered dose)
- ⚠️ CRC had highest median dose/fraction yet worst LC -- suggests intrinsic radioresistance, not underdosing; combination or dose-escalation strategies may be needed
- ⚠️ Registry design: no randomization, heterogeneous histologies/mets sites, confounders uncontrolled; LC rates not directly comparable across primaries
- ❓ Optimal SBRT dose/fractionation for CRC oligomets and role of concurrent systemic therapy remain open

## 🍇 Prostate

PRIME extends 5-fraction SBRT to high-risk/node+ PCa with mandatory whole-pelvis RT; interim only, non-inferiority not yet formally declared.

### PRIME Trial (NCT03561961) · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim: 5-fraction SBRT shows no BFFS inferiority signal vs 25fx at 1-2yr in high-risk/node+ PCa with whole-pelvis RT; G3+ tox <1%.

![PRIME Trial (NCT03561961)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)

| Toxicity | SBRT (5fx) | Moderate Hypo (25fx) |
|---|---|---|
| Acute GU ≥G2 | ~5.4% | ~4.0% |
| Acute GI ≥G2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% |
| Acute G3+ | <1% | <1% |
| Late GU ≥G2 (1-2yr) | ~10-12% | ~9-11% |
| Late GI ≥G2 (1-2yr) | ~5-7% | ~4-6% |
| Late G3+ | <1% | <1% |

- 🔍 Phase III open-label non-inferiority, N~434, high-risk/VHR/node+ non-metastatic PCa; PSMA PET staging allowed; Tata Memorial Centre India; accrual 2018-2023; median f/u 1-2yr (interim only)
- 💊 Both arms: prostate + whole-pelvis RT + long-course ADT ~2yr; SBRT arm 36.25 Gy/5fx every other day; mod hypo ~68 Gy/25fx (2.7 Gy/fx)
- 📊 Primary EP BFFS (Phoenix nadir+2): no inferiority signal at interim; HR + formal non-inferiority declaration pending 4-5yr mature analysis
- 🔗 HYPO-RT-PC (7fx vs 39fx, node-neg, no ADT): non-inferior at 10yr adj HR 0.84 (0.69-1.03); PRIME extends paradigm to node+ with mandatory pelvic RT
- 🔗 WPRT benefit vs prostate-only RT by trial -- background for PRIME mandating pelvic RT in both arms

  | Endpoint | POP-RT (2yr ADT) | PEACE-2 (3yr ADT, PSMA) |
  |---|---|---|
  | bFFS/bPFS | HR 0.50 (0.42-0.61), p<0.001 | HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73 |
  | cFFS/cPFS | HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.09 |
  | MFS | HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- ⚠️ Interim 1-2yr data only; non-inferiority not formally declared; single academic center India limits generalizability; longer f/u needed for late GU/GI with 5-fraction pelvic SBRT
