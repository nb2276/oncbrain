# 2026-05-17

**PEACE-2: pelvic RT missed cPFS primary (HR 0.81, p=0.088); POP-RT benefit not replicated in PSMA-PET-staged very high-risk PCa.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> PEACE-2 challenges the emerging pelvic nodal RT standard in PSMA-PET-staged high-risk PCa; prostate SBRT safety data (PIVOTALboost, PACE-NODES, PRIME) matures across risk strata. Breast carried the day's heaviest load: 10-15yr hypofractionation trials confirm durable equivalence, and EORTC IM-MS at 20yr quantifies the late non-BC mortality cost of IM field RT.

## 🌰 Prostate

ESTRO26 prostate session anchored on pelvic nodal RT (benefit unproven with modern PSMA-PET staging) alongside SBRT safety readouts across high-risk and oligorecurrent settings.

### PIVOTALboost

> PIVOTALboost phase 3: focal boost + pelvic node RT (20fr) safe at 2yr; early bowel toxicity with nodal RT resolves by 18wks.

![PIVOTALboost](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiVWznXoAAqNwC.jpg)

| Arm | Bowel G2+ | Bladder G2+ |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate (n=281) | 8.2% (5.7-11.7) | 19.5% (15.5-24.4) |
| Prostate+Boost (n=345) | 8.7% (6.3-12.1) | 24.1% (20.2-28.7) |
| Prostate+Nodes+Boost (n=347) | 6.5% (4.5-9.5) | 16.5% (13.1-20.6) |

- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1465, 39 UK centres, high-risk localised prostate; 4 arms: prostate IMRT alone, +boost, +pelvic nodes+boost; 1° EP biochemical/clinical failure (oncologic results not reported here)
- 📊 Late G2+ AEs at 2yr (973 pts with ≥2yr f/u)
- 📊 Early bowel toxicity elevated with pelvic node RT but resolved by 18wks post-RT; no G3+ signal persisting
- ⚠️ Only side-effect endpoints reported at ESTRO26; 1° EP (biochemical/clinical failure) not yet presented — efficacy read pending
- 🔗 Focal boost arm consistent with prior FLAME/DELINEATE data showing no excess toxicity with dose-escalated boost to dominant intraprostatic lesion

### RADIOSA

> RADIOSA post-hoc: SBRT+ADT vs SBRT alone, median MFS 16.6mo vs NR, HR 0.39 (p=0.001) in oligorecurrent prostate.

![RADIOSA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdDCQkW0AAvnIw.jpg)

| Arm | Median MFS | Events | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| SBRT alone | 16.6 mo (95% CI 12.83-NA) | 32/51 (62.7%) | 0.3894 (0.2201-0.6888) | 0.00119 |
| SBRT + ADT | Not reached | 19/51 (37.3%) |  |  |

- 🔍 Phase II RCT, N=102 (1:1); SBRT alone vs SBRT + 6-month ADT; oligorecurrent PCa; median f/u 49.23mo (95% CI 42.47-54.8)
- 📊 Eugonadal MFS (post-testosterone recovery) also favored Arm B, p<0.05; all but 2 Arm B pts recovered testosterone within f/u
- ⚠️ Post-hoc analysis; MFS was not the prespecified primary endpoint of RADIOSA; phase II, N=102 limits definitiveness
- ⚠️ MFS as OS surrogate in oligorecurrent PCa is less validated than in M0 non-metastatic settings; clinical extrapolation uncertain
- 🔗 Short-term ADT + SBRT benefit pattern directionally consistent with ORIOLE/STOMP oligomet-directed RT data, though those trials did not include ADT

### PACE-NODES

> PACE-NODES acute tox interim: PPN-SBRT raises CTCAE ≥2 GI events (28% vs 21% at 12wk) but resolves; no GU difference; cancer control endpoint immature.

![PACE-NODES](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhsLTUXoAAbiFs.jpg)

| Toxicity | PPN-SBRT | P-SBRT |
|---|---|---|
| CTCAE ≥2 GI (12-wk cumulative) | 28% | 21% |

- 📊 GI excess transient: EPIC-26 bowel domain worse at 4 weeks; no CTCAE ≥2 GI difference at 12-week endpoint
- 📊 CTCAE ≥2 GU: no difference, clinician or patient-reported
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=1166, high-risk PCa (T3a-T4 and/or GS 8-10 and/or PSA >20), 12-36mo ADT; 36.25Gy/5f prostate ± 25Gy/5f pelvic nodes on alternate days
- ⚠️ 11% PPN-SBRT vs 4% P-SBRT did not receive allocated treatment (constraints unmet); primary BCF endpoint not yet mature, cancer control data pending
- 🔗 Pelvic nodal RT benefit in high-risk PCa established with conventional/moderate hypofractionation (POP-RT, PEACE-2); PACE-NODES is first phase 3 testing SBRT delivery at scale

### PRIME · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME interim: 5-fx SBRT with pelvic RT and ADT comparable to moderate hypofractionation on early BFFS in high-risk/node-positive PCa; mature data pending.

![PRIME](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIkmdCjaMAAFt57.jpg)

| Toxicity | SBRT 5 fx | Mod Hypo 25 fx | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Acute GU ≥2 | 5.4% | ~4.0% | NS |
| Acute GI ≥2 | ~2.2% | ~3.7% | NS |
| Acute G3+ | <1% | <1% | NS |
| Late GU ≥2 | ~10-12% | ~9-11% | 0.59 |
| Late GI ≥2 | ~5-7% | ~4-6% | 0.20 |
| Late G3+ | <1% | <1% | NS |

- 🔍 Phase III non-inferiority RCT, N~434, high-risk/very high-risk/node-positive non-metastatic PCa; Tata Memorial Centre; accrual 2018-2023; median f/u 1-2yr (interim)
- 💊 Arm A: 36.25 Gy/5 fx every other day; Arm B: ~68 Gy/25 fx (2.7 Gy/fx); pelvic nodes 25 Gy/5 fx; long-course ADT ~2yr both arms
- 📊 1° EP (BFFS, Phoenix nadir+2): no inferiority signal for SBRT at interim; no effect size in source; 4-5yr mature results pending
- 🔗 vs HYPO-RT-PC (10-yr mature): adjusted HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.69-1.03), non-inferiority confirmed; key design differences
  - HYPO-RT-PC: intermediate-to-high-risk, node-negative only, NO ADT, no pelvic RT
  - PRIME: high-risk/node-positive included, mandatory 2yr ADT, whole-pelvis RT in both arms
  - PRIME is first RCT extending 5-fx SBRT to the nodal/higher-risk ADT context
- ⚠️ Interim data only (1-2yr); no HR/CI for BFFS primary endpoint; non-inferiority conclusion requires 4-5yr analysis; all efficacy currently trend-level

### PEACE-2

> PEACE-2: pelvic RT + 3yr ADT vs prostate-only RT missed primary cPFS endpoint (HR 0.81, p=0.088) in very high-risk localized PCa at interim.

![PEACE-2](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhXl7VWkAAwqHi.jpg)

| Arm | 7-yr cPFS | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pelvic RT | 67.1% (61.6-72.2) | 0.81 (0.63-1.03) | 0.088 |
| Prostate-only RT | 62.9% (57.4-68.1) | ref | ref |

- 🔍 Phase III randomized, pelvic vs prostate-only RT, very high-risk PCa (≥2 of Gleason ≥8, T3-T4, PSA ≥20), N0M0, 3yr ADT + 78Gy IMRT; N~761, f/u ~5.5yr
- 🔗 POP-RT vs PEACE-2: WPRT benefit diverges starkly across imaging eras and ADT duration

  | Endpoint | POP-RT (WPRT vs prostate-only) | PEACE-2 (WPRT vs prostate-only) |
  |---|---|---|
  | bPFS/bFFS | HR 0.50 (0.42-0.61), p<0.001 | HR 0.97 (0.81-1.16), p=0.73 |
  | cPFS/cFFS | HR 0.74 (0.61-0.90), p=0.002 | HR 0.81 (0.63-1.03), p=0.088 |
  | MFS | HR 0.72 (0.58-0.89), p=0.002 | HR 0.93 (0.74-1.17), p=0.54 |

- ⚠️ Interim analysis (ESTRO 2026); OS not reported. Modern PSMA PET/CT staging + longer ADT may have neutralized incremental pelvic RT benefit vs conventional-imaging POP-RT era.
- ⚠️ Presenter challenges 'very high-risk' label: <1/10 men died from PCa in first decade, especially with PSMA PET/CT filtering occult nodal disease pre-enrollment.

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

EXTEND RCT publishes in JCO; OligoCare registry quantifies SBRT local control across 2447 pts and 3533 lesions, flagging CRC as the outlier histology.

### EXTEND

> EXTEND phase II RCT: MDT added to SOC for oligometastatic solid tumors; primary analysis now published in JCO.

![EXTEND](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhv-tBXEAAHgLo.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase II randomized, multi-histology basket design; MDT (SBRT/SABR) + SOC vs SOC alone across oligometastatic solid tumor histologies
- 📄 Primary aggregated analysis published JCO May 16 2026 ([[doi:10.1200/JCO-25-02856]]); ctDNA correlatives presented synchronously at ESTRO26
- ⚠️ No effect sizes reported in source tweet; primary endpoint results not extractable from available OCR
- 🔗 Adds to MDT-in-oligomets RCT body alongside SABR-COMET, ORIOLE, STOMP; histology-basket design allows cross-tumor signal detection but dilutes site-specific conclusions
- ❓ ctDNA correlatives presented at ESTRO26 may identify biomarker-selected pts most likely to benefit from MDT

### OligoCare

> OligoCare registry: SBRT 88.6% LC at 3yr across 2447 oligomet pts; CRC worst at 19.6% local progression at 3yr.

![OligoCare](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg6aU7WAAEf5lR.jpg)

| Histology | 1-yr local progression | 3-yr local progression |
|---|---|---|
| Prostate (n=1021) | 2.7% | 8.1% |
| Breast (n=378) | 4.1% | 11.3% |
| NSCLC (n=530) | 6.0% | 9.8% |
| Colorectal (n=518) | 9.3% | 19.6% |

- 📊 Overall local in-field progression: 5.0% at 1yr, 11.4% at 3yr (88.6% LC); 2447 pts, 3533 lesions, 57 institutions, median f/u 31mo
- 🔍 EORTC prospective multi-institution registry, July 2019-July 2025; interim analysis, not a randomised trial
- 📐 Minimum PTV dose strongest technical predictor of local control across the dataset
- 🔗 De novo OMD: better LC than repeat OMD, attributed to higher delivered dose achievable at first treatment
- ⚠️ CRC highest local failure (19.6% at 3yr) despite receiving highest median dose per fraction; relative radioresistance flagged, combination or dose-escalation strategies proposed

## 🎀 Breast

Breast dominated numerically: 10-15yr RCT datasets across hypofractionation, APBI, IM-MS RT, boost indication, and oligomet SABR converge at the same meeting.

### OLIGOMA · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: SABR to all mets + systemic vs systemic alone for OMD breast, mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48 (0.25-0.91), p=0.021.

![OLIGOMA](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIiTEAqWkAAsym0.jpg)

| Arm | Median PFS (95% CI) | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| Systemic + MDT | 35.8 mo (24.1-NR) | 0.48 (0.25-0.91) | 0.021 |
| Systemic alone | 20.4 mo (12.4-28.4) | ref | ref |

- 🔍 Phase II RCT (ARO-2021-09), N=87; OMD breast any line, ≤5 mets; SABR to all lesions; co-primary PFS + QoL at 12wks
- 💊 Concomitant: endocrine 67%, CDK4/6i 49%, HER2-targeted 12%; ER+ ~77%; bone-dominant mets (65%); median f/u 26.0mo
- 📊 Patterns of progression

  | Pattern | MDT arm (n=43) | Control (n=44) |
  |---|---|---|
  | Initial met progression | 5 (11.6%) | 12 (27.3%) |
  | New mets | 11 (25.6%) | 18 (40.9%) |
  | Locoregional recurrence | 3 (7.0%) | 2 (4.6%) |
  | Death | 4 (9.3%) | 6 (13.6%) |

- ⚠️ Baseline imbalance: synchronous mets 54% (MDT) vs 39% (control); single-met pts 54% vs 41%; may inflate MDT arm benefit
- ⚠️ N=87, wide CI (0.25-0.91); QoL co-primary not reported in source; OS immature
- 🔗 vs SINDAS (Zheng, JAMA Oncol 2020): prior phase 2 RCT in OMD breast, SBRT positive; OLIGOMA adds CDK4/6i backbone (~49%)

- 🔍 Phase III RCT (1:1), N=1,882 node-negative BC/DCIS, 50 Gy/25f vs 40 Gy/15f, Denmark/Norway/Germany, 2009-2014; median f/u 12.8yr

### Dutch Breast Boost Indication Study

> 10yr IBTR 1.2% with or without boost in 0-2 risk factor pts; boost likely omittable in modern-era BCS.

![Dutch Breast Boost Indication Study](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIdFyGwWoAAD-Ft.jpg)

| Risk factors | N (no boost / boost) | 10yr IBTR (no boost) | 10yr IBTR (boost) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-2 RF | 15,085 / 13,845 | 1.2% | 1.2% |
| ≥3 RF | 149 / 733 | 2.7% | 3.3% |
| Uncertain | 592 / 944 | 1.4% | 3.6% |

- 🔍 Retrospective Dutch registry, BCT + WBRT 2012-2016; IBTR via pathology report algorithm; Assisi thresholds: omit boost if 10yr IBTR <3% with boost, <6% without
- 🔍 Risk factors: age ≤40, grade 3, TNBC, inadequate guideline systemic therapy, no pCR after NAC (TNBC/HER2+)
- 📊 Assisi threshold exceeded only in ≥3 RF boost arm at 10yr (3.3% > 3% threshold); all no-boost groups below 6%
- ⚠️ Non-randomized; ≥3 RF no-boost arm N=149 vs N=733 on boost — selection bias likely drives artificially low IBTR in pts forgoing boost
- 🔗 EORTC 22881/10882 (Bartelink, Lancet Oncol 2015): boost cut IBTR ~50% in 2000s era; absolute rates substantially lower with modern systemic therapy
