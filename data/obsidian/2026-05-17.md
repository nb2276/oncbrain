# 2026-05-17

**OLIGOMA: mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo (HR 0.48) — first breast-specific MDT RCT with PFS signal in oligometastatic disease.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Breast dominated today: APBI gets 15yr safety data (Florence), SIB boost confirmed non-inferior at 10yr (IMPORT HIGH), and RT omission halted early (DBCG Natural). OLIGOMA adds a PFS signal for MDT in oligo-metastatic breast, while EORTC IM-MS 20yr closes the book on older IM-MS technique -- competing mortality erases any BCM benefit.

## 🌸 Breast

Heavy RT-technique day: fractionation, field design, and omission all addressed with long-horizon data.

### APBI-IMRT Florence trial (NCT02104895) · [[NCT02104895]]

> Florence trial 15-yr: APBI IBTR 7.7% vs WBI 4.2% (HR 1.57, p=0.17); no difference in locoregional control, DM, or OS.

![IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhdbJgW4AAHzXl.jpg)
*IBTR: HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17. 15-yr APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%.*

- 📊 15-yr IBTR: APBI 7.7% vs WBI 4.2%; HR 1.57 (95% CI 0.82-3.04), p=0.17 — not significant
- 🔍 phase III, N=520, 1:1 APBI (30Gy/5fx) vs WBI (50Gy/25fx + 10Gy boost); early-stage BCS pts, pT<25mm, FSM≥5mm, age>40
- 📊 excess IBTR driven by new ipsilateral primaries (APBI 5.9% vs WBI 2.7%, p=0.09) not true local relapse (2.1% vs 1.6%, p=0.75)
- 📊 15-yr locoregional recurrence: APBI 7.2% vs WBI 5.0%, p=0.28; distant metastasis 2.7% vs 4.6%, p=0.35; deaths 21.5% vs 19.6%, p=0.66 — all NS
- 🔗 consistent with IMPORT LOW and RAPID trials showing non-inferior local control with APBI at 5-10yr in selected low-risk pts
- ⚠️ new ipsilateral primaries inflate IBTR rate — competing risk from second primary rather than treatment failure; doesn't reflect radiobiologic adequacy of APBI field
- ❓ whether 15-yr IBTR numerical gap (3.5% absolute) meets equivalence margin matters for guideline grading; original equivalence delta was 5%

### IMPORT HIGH (ISRCTN47437448)

> IMPORT HIGH 10yr: SIB 48Gy/15F IBTR 3.7% vs sequential 40+16Gy 3.5% — non-inferior with similar late toxicity.

![10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0); 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3); 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3).](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhlE_4WUAAYm7v.jpg)
*10-yr IBTR: 40Gy/15F+16Gy/8F 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0); 48Gy/15F 3.7% (2.6-5.3); 53Gy/15F 5.5% (4.1-7.3).*

- 📊 10-yr IBTR: 40+16Gy 3.5% (95%CI 2.4-5.0), 48Gy SIB 3.7% (2.6-5.3), 53Gy SIB 5.5% (4.1-7.3)
- 🔍 3-arm RCT, N=2617, pT1-3 pN0-N3a M0 requiring tumour bed boost, 76 UK centres (2009-2015)
- 🔗 5-yr data (Lancet 2023): 48Gy non-inferior at 5yr (2.0% vs 1.9%); 10yr results consistent with that finding
- ⚠️ 53Gy arm numerically higher IBTR at 10yr (5.5%) vs both other arms — further dose escalation not advantageous per investigators
- 💊 Late AEs at 10yr: breast distortion/shrinkage <18%, induration <10%, telangiectasia <2%, oedema <2% across all arms
- 📐 OS difference at 10yr vs 40+16Gy: -0.5% (-3.0, 2.8) for 48Gy; +1.5% (-1.4, 5.1) for 53Gy — no OS detriment
- ❓ Whether SIB benefit extends to post-mastectomy or post-NACT settings remains unaddressed by this dataset

### EORTC IM-MS trial (22922/10925)

> EORTC IM-MS 20yr: OS 61.0% vs 61.8% HR 1.00 — no OS benefit from IM-MS RT at 20 years.

![OS (ITT): 61.0% vs 61.8%; HR 1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg-Sh-XcAAFDgr.jpg)
*OS (ITT): 61.0% vs 61.8%; HR 1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967.*

- 📊 1° EP (OS, ITT): 61.0% vs 61.8% at 20yr; HR 1.00 (95% CI 0.90-1.10), p=0.967
- 📊 BCM rate 18.6% vs 22.4%; HR 0.82 (95% CI 0.72-0.95), p=0.006 — disease-specific benefit persists
- ⚠️ non-BCM mortality 20.4% vs 15.8%; HR 1.26 (95% CI 1.09-1.46), p=0.002 — competing cardiac/non-cancer deaths offset BCM gain
- 🔍 pN0 subgroup: DFS 53.9% vs 53.6% HR 0.93 p=0.318; DMFS 67.2% vs 67.4% HR 0.93 p=0.397 — no signal in node-negative pts
- 🔗 DBCG IMN2 (Lancet Reg Health Eur 2024): modern IM-RT with MHD 1.2-2.3 Gy (4-9x lower than EORTC) showed OS benefit in node+ — heart dose likely explains divergence
- ⚠️ era mismatch: EORTC enrolled pre-2004, high heart doses; contemporary IM-RT with DIBH/proton bears a different risk-benefit profile
- ❓ whether modern low-dose IM-RT (DBCG technique) rescues OS in node+ pts without the competing mortality penalty remains the key open question

### DBCG RT Natural trial

> DBCG RT Natural: PBI halted early -- 39/41 LRs in no-RT arm; 5-yr LR 9.8% vs 8.2% at 4yr median f/u.

![DBCG RT Natural trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhIv1kXYAAR4xZ.jpg)

- 📊 1° EP (5-yr invasive LR): trial stopped early; 39 of 41 LRs occurred in no-PBI arm
- 📊 4-yr CIF local recurrence by treatment arm
  - +RT +ET: 0/105, CIF 0.0%
  - +RT -ET: 2/132, CIF 3.0% (95% CI 0.5-9.7%)
  - -RT +ET: 7/213, CIF 3.7% (95% CI 1.3-8.4%)
  - -RT -ET: 32/352, CIF 12.2% (95% CI 5.2-17.1%)
- 📐 Randomised arms: +RT CIF 1.5% vs -RT CIF 9.8% (vs 8.2% -- likely +RT arm); 4 distant failures (2 each arm)
- 🔍 Phase III RCT, pT1N0 breast conservation, HER2-normal, grade 1-2; PBI 40Gy/15fr; 1° EP 5-yr invasive LR; interim stop at 200 pts triggered planned stopping rule
- 🔗 Consistent with PRIME II / CALGB 9343 showing omission feasible only in lowest-risk elderly pts -- DBCG natural selection here showed even 'low-risk' pts accumulate LR without RT
- ⚠️ Self-selection bias: no-PBI arm was non-randomised (pts opting out); 4-yr f/u short for breast cancer; ET adherence not reported but likely incomplete in -ET groups
- ❓ Whether ultra-hypo (5-fraction) APBI or ET alone suffices in true low-risk (grade 1, pT1a-b) remains open

### OLIGOMA trial (NCT04495309) · [[NCT04495309]]

> OLIGOMA: MDT added to systemic therapy improved PFS in oligometastatic breast cancer; mPFS 35.8 vs 20.4mo, HR 0.48, p=0.021.

![OLIGOMA trial (NCT04495309)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhYrEZWgAI2Ndq.jpg)

- 📊 1° EP PFS: mPFS 35.8mo (95% CI 24.1-NR) vs 20.4mo (95% CI 12.4-28.4) favoring MDT arm
- 📐 HR 0.48 (95% CI 0.25-0.91), p=0.021; N=87 (44 control, 43 experimental)
- 🔍 RCT any treatment line, ≤5 mets; >80% had 1-3 lesions, 2/3 bone-dominant, majority ER+/HER2- 1L
- 📊 Co-primary QoL at 12 wks: mean diff -2.1 pts (95% CI -9.2-5.1), non-inferiority margin -10 pts -- met
- ⚠️ Stopped early at <20% of initial and <40% of amended target enrollment; severely underpowered, wide PFS CI
- 🔗 First breast-specific MDT RCT with PFS signal; SABR-COMET (Palma, Lancet 2019) showed OS benefit in mixed solid tumors
- ❓ No OS data reported; QoL analysis n=64 only; which pts (line, histology, met pattern) benefit most undefined

## 🍇 Prostate

PRIME interim supports 5-fraction SBRT + whole-pelvis RT tolerability; efficacy conclusions await mature BFFS data.

### PRIME Trial (NCT03561961) · [[NCT03561961]]

> PRIME: 5-fraction SBRT + whole-pelvis RT non-inferior to 25-fraction moderate hypofractionation at interim; mature 4-5yr BFFS data pending.

- 🔍 Phase III RCT, N=434, high-risk/very high-risk/node-positive non-metastatic PCa; 1:1 SBRT (36.25 Gy/5fx) vs moderate hypo (~68 Gy/25fx); both arms: whole-pelvis RT + 2yr ADT
- 📊 1° EP (BFFS, Phoenix definition): interim data show no signal of inferiority for SBRT arm; no effect size reported in source tweets — mature 4-5yr results awaited
- 💊 Acute GU/GI G2+: SBRT ~5.4% GU / ~2.2% GI vs moderate hypo ~4.0% GU / ~3.7% GI; G3+ <1% both arms
- Late G2+ GU ~10-12% vs ~9-11% (NS); late GI ~5-7% vs ~4-6% (NS); G3+ late <1% both arms
- QoL (EORTC QLQ-C30/PR25, IPSS): urinary and bowel domains stable and comparable; transient declines during RT with recovery; no clinically meaningful global QoL deterioration
- 🔗 Contextual comparison — BFFS/FFS benefit of WPRT vs prostate-only RT in high-risk disease
  - POP-RT (bFFS, 2yr ADT, conventional imaging): HR 0.50 (95% CI 0.42-0.61), p<0.001
  - PEACE-2 (bPFS, 3yr ADT, PSMA PET staging): HR 0.97 (95% CI 0.81-1.16), p=0.73
- 🔗 HYPO-RT-PC (10yr mature): ultra-hypo 42.7 Gy/7fx non-inferior to 78 Gy/39fx in intermediate/high-risk node-negative PCa without ADT; adjusted HR 0.84 (95% CI 0.69-1.03) — PRIME adds pelvic RT and ADT to a higher-risk population
- ⚠️ Interim only — median f/u 1-2 yrs; BFFS events immature; non-inferiority not yet formally confirmed; conclusions about long-term efficacy premature

## 🎯 Oligometastatic / Mets

OligoCare provides the largest prospective LC dataset for SBRT across oligometastatic primaries.

### OligoCare registry (EORTC)

> OligoCare EORTC registry: 88.6% local control at 3yrs across 2447 pts, 3533 mets treated with SBRT for oligometastatic disease.

![Local in-field progression by tumor type. 1yr/3yr: prostate 2.7%/8.1%, NSCLC 6.0%/9.8%, breast 4.1%/11.3%, CRC 9.3%/19.6%.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIg6aU7WAAEf5lR.jpg)
*Local in-field progression by tumor type. 1yr/3yr: prostate 2.7%/8.1%, NSCLC 6.0%/9.8%, breast 4.1%/11.3%, CRC 9.3%/19.6%.*

- 📊 Local in-field progression: 5.0% at 1yr, 11.4% at 3yrs (88.6% LC at 3yrs); N=2447 pts, 3533 lesions, median f/u 31 months
- 📊 Local in-field progression by primary tumor type (1yr / 3yr)
  - Prostate: 2.7% / 8.1%
  - NSCLC: 6.0% / 9.8%
  - Breast: 4.1% / 11.3%
  - CRC: 9.3% / 19.6%
- 🔍 Prospective registry, 57 institutions, 2019-2025; tumor mix: prostate 42%, non-vertebral bone 25%, non-regional LN 16%, liver 9%, brain 7%
- 📐 Minimum PTV dose identified as most critical technical factor for local control outcome
- De novo OMD showed better LC than repeat OMD, attributed to higher achievable delivered dose
- ⚠️ Registry design — no randomization, heterogeneous primaries/regimens, selection bias inherent; OS not reported here
- ⚠️ CRC: worst LC despite highest median dose per fraction, consistent with known radioresistance; authors flag need for dose escalation or combination strategies
