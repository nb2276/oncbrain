# 2026-05-19

**SF-SABR (N=1687): 2yr LC 90-93%, G3+ AEs 2.9%, mOS 40mo (NSCLC) — single-fraction pooled benchmark across 3 institutions.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Thoracic and GU/GYN dominated today. SF-SABR delivers landmark LC and OS data across 1687 pts. PSMA PET/CT challenges routine ePLND with 96% NPV and 47.7% out-of-template mets. Spanish TMT cohort confirms bladder preservation viability at 63.7% CLR.

## 🌰 Prostate

PSMA PET/CT vs ePLND debate sharpens: high NPV and frequent out-of-template mets put routine extended dissection under pressure.

### PSMA PET/CT vs ePLND for prostate cancer staging (AUA 2026)

> PSMA PET/CT NPV ~96% for LNI at primary staging; 47.7% of LN mets fell outside ePLND template in 1253-pt cohort.

![1253 consecutive men, primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT. 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND boundaries. Yaxley et al, BJUI 2019.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhwFsnWkAAjOfG.jpg)
*1253 consecutive men, primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT. 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND boundaries. Yaxley et al, BJUI 2019.*

- 📊 47.7% of lymph node metastases were outside the boundaries of ePLND in 1253 consecutive men undergoing primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT (Yaxley et al, BJUI 2019)
- 🔍 Observational staging cohort (N=1253), not an RCT; no randomised comparison of PLND vs omission arms
- 📊 Conclusions per AUA 2026 slide: intermediate-risk with PSMA PET negative for LNI → PLND may safely be omitted; GG3 → nomograms add utility; high-risk → individualised decision
- ⚠️ PLND morbidity: lymphedema risk and DVT/PE data from systematic review and Tyritzis et al (J Urol 2015, N=3544)
  - Lower limb lymphedema: 0-14% after RP+PLND, 0-9% after pelvic node RT, 19-29% after PLND + salvage pelvic RT
  - DVT/PE: 6-10x increased risk with PLND vs no PLND (ORP and RARP)
- ⚠️ Level 1 RCT evidence for oncological benefit of ePLND absent; RCTs have not shown consistent BCR improvement per AUA 2026 summary
- 🔗 Roberts et al (PCAN 2024 perspective): PSMA-PET staging may guide post-op pelvic node RT instead of routine eLND, reducing lymphedema morbidity
- ❓ Whether PSMA-PET-directed targeted/radioguided LND (rather than extended template) preserves staging accuracy with less morbidity remains unanswered

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

Largest SF-SABR pooled dataset to date; sets a multi-institutional benchmark for LC and toxicity.

### Single-fraction SABR pooled analysis (NSCLC + pulmonary oligomets)

> SF-SABR, N=1687: 2yr LC 90-93%, G3+ AEs 2.9%, median OS 40mo (NSCLC) vs 51mo (oligomets) across 3 institutions.

![Single-fraction SABR pooled analysis (NSCLC + pulmonary oligomets)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInTCxxXIAAKCPI.jpg)

| Endpoint | Primary NSCLC | Pulmonary Oligomets |
|---|---|---|
| 1yr OS | 84% (95% CI 82-86) | 90% (95% CI 86-92) |
| 2yr OS | 67% (95% CI 64-69) | 75% (95% CI 71-79) |
| Median OS | 40mo (95% CI 36-43) | 51mo (95% CI 42-58) |

- 📊 2yr LC 90-93% across both cohorts; isolated local/locoregional failure uncommon
- 📊 mPFS: Primary NSCLC 30mo; Pulmonary Oligomets 11mo
- 🔍 Pooled multi-institutional (Peter MacCallum, Cleveland Clinic, Roswell Park); 1200 primary NSCLC + 487 pulm oligomets; single-fraction SABR throughout
- 📊 AEs, Primary NSCLC (n=789; Roswell Park excluded, no AE data)
  - G2+: 124/789 (15.7%)
  - G3+: 23/789 (2.9%)
  - Chest wall pain 14%, pneumonitis 7%, fatigue 4%
- ⚠️ Single-arm pooled analysis; no randomized comparator vs multi-fraction SABR; institution mix introduces heterogeneity in pt selection and technique

## 💧 Bladder

Spanish multicenter TMT series adds real-world CLR and salvage data to the bladder preservation evidence base.

### Bladder-preserving TMT for MIBC (ESTRO 2026)

> Spanish multicenter TMT cohort (N=369): CLR 63.7%, salvage cystectomy 9.7%; 5-FU-based CRT predicted higher CLR (OR 4.9).

![Bladder-preserving TMT for MIBC (ESTRO 2026)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIrl5sdWwAAEkhS.jpg)

- 📊 CLR 63.7%; disease progression 28.8% (local 10.1%, systemic 10.7%, combined 8.7%); salvage cystectomy 9.7%
- 🔍 Multicenter retrospective cohort (24 Spanish sites, 2010-2022); N=369 cT2-T4aN0M0 MIBC; TURBT + concurrent chemoradiotherapy; median age 76, 85.1% male
- 📊 Predictors of CLR on multivariable logistic regression
  - 5-FU-based CRT: OR 4.9 (95% CI 1.1-22.1), p=0.038 (higher CLR)
  - Weekly portal imaging: OR 0.35 (95% CI 0.20-0.60), p<0.001 (lower CLR — likely confounder; complex cases flagged for more imaging)
- 🔗 CLR and salvage rates broadly consistent with prior European TMT series; no RC control arm in this cohort for direct comparison
- ⚠️ Retrospective single-arm design; no contemporaneous RC arm; median age 76 implies selection bias toward non-surgical candidates; chemotherapy regimen heterogeneity across 24 centers limits generalizability of the 5-FU signal
