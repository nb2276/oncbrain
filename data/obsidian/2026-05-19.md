# 2026-05-19

**Single-fraction lung SABR (pooled n=1687): 2-yr LC 90-93%, G3+ AEs 2-3% · one-and-done SABR holds up across primary NSCLC + pulmonary oligomets.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Three large observational RT series, all pointing toward de-escalation: single-fraction lung SABR (n=1687) matches multi-fraction LC, TMT preserves bladder in MIBC (CLR 63.7%, n=369), and PSMA-PET (NPV ~96%) may spare ePLND. Thoracic carries the day; none randomised.

## 💧 Bladder

Retrospective multicenter TMT bladder-preservation cohort; no randomised TMT-vs-cystectomy comparator, RT dose/fractionation unreported.

### Multicenter TMT Bladder Preservation Analysis (n=369)

> CLR 63.7% in 369 MIBC pts on definitive TMT; 5-FU-based CRT and better image guidance predicted higher complete local response.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The two modifiable levers here are RT delivery and chemo backbone, both in the radonc's hands: 5-FU-based CRT predicted higher CLR (OR 4.9) and weekly portal imaging lower CLR (OR 0.35, p<0.001), favoring daily volumetric IGRT. Technique, not just patient selection, moved local control.

- 📊 1° endpoint: complete local response (CLR) 63.7% with definitive TMT (TURBT + concurrent chemoRT)
- 📊 Disease progression 28.8%, by pattern
  - Local: 10.1%
  - Systemic: 10.7%
  - Combined: 8.7%
- 📊 Salvage cystectomy 9.7%
- 📊 Multivariable predictors of CLR
- 🔍 Multicenter retrospective cohort, Spain 2010-2022; N=369, median age 76, 85.1% male; cT2-T4aN0M0
- 🔍 RT dose/fractionation and modality mix not reported in source
- ⚠️ Retrospective; CLR predictors exploratory, no randomised TMT-vs-cystectomy comparator
- ⚠️ Weekly-portal-imaging signal likely confounded by treatment era/center technique, not a clean IGRT effect
- 🔗 Authors claim survival comparable to international TMT series (e.g. RTOG pooled, BC2001); no comparative numbers in source

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

Largest pooled single-fraction lung SABR series to date, though retrospective with no head-to-head vs multi-fraction and Gy dose unstated.

### Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and lung oligomets (pooled, n=1687)

> 2-yr local control 90-93% and G3+ AEs 2-3% across 1687 pts (1200 primary NSCLC, 487 oligomets) treated with single-fraction SABR.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The transfer gate is the fraction size the source omits: outcomes are labeled single-fraction but no Gy dose is given, so you cannot map this 2y LC of 90-93% onto a specific regimen before offering one visit instead of a multi-fraction course. Toxicity (G3+ 2-3%) comes from 2 of 3 sites only. Moves the single- vs multi-fraction choice for lung SABR.

- 🔍 Pooled retrospective analysis, 3 institutions (Peter Mac, Cleveland Clinic, Roswell Park); N=1687 (1200 primary NSCLC, 487 pulmonary oligomets)
- 🔍 Single-fraction SABR; Gy dose/fractionation not reported in source
- 📊 Isolated local/locoregional failure very uncommon (authors' summary)
- 📊 Median PFS 30 mo (primary NSCLC) vs 11 mo (pulmonary oligomets)
- ⚠️ Retrospective pooled cohort, no randomised or matched comparator vs multi-fraction SABR
- ⚠️ AE data from 2 of 3 sites only (n=789, no Roswell Park); toxicity denominator ≠ full cohort
- ⚠️ Single-fraction Gy dose unstated in source, so the specific SF-SABR regimen behind these outcomes is unknown
- 🔗 Consistent with SAFRON II (TROG 13.01) and RTOG 0915 backing single-fraction lung SABR (both randomised)

## 🌰 Prostate

AUA staging round-up positioning a negative PSMA-PET as the gate to omit ePLND, with post-op pelvic nodal RT as the lower-lymphedema alternative.

### AUA 2026: ePLND / PSMA PET staging round-up

> PSMA PET NPV ~96% may safely omit ePLND in intermediate-risk pts; 47.7% of nodal mets fall outside the ePLND template.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> 47.7% of positive nodes fall outside the ePLND template (Yaxley, n=1253), so a negative PSMA PET (NPV ~96%) can route pts to nodal RT rather than dissection. Lymphedema is 0-9% with pelvic LN RT alone vs 19-29% when PLND precedes salvage RT, arguing for one nodal treatment rather than stacking both.

- 📊 PSMA PET/CT NPV ~96% for nodal disease; a negative scan may spare PLND
- 🩻 Intermediate-risk: omit PLND if PSMA PET negative; missed nodes small, likely missed by ePLND too
- 🔍 Yaxley BJUI 2019 (n=1253): 47.7% of nodal mets fell OUTSIDE the ePLND template
- 📊 Lymphedema by nodal treatment (systematic review, Clinckaert 2022)

  | Setting | Lower-limb LE | Genital LE |
  |---|---|---|
  | RP + PLND | 0-14% | nr |
  | Pelvic LN RT | 0-9% | nr |
  | PLND + salvage pelvic RT | 19-29% | 2-22% |

- ⚠️ Tyritzis J Urol 2015 (3544 RP, 547 PLND): 6-10x higher DVT/PE risk with lymphadenectomy
- 🩻 High-risk: decision individualized; ePLND plus adjuvant/salvage pelvic RT may increase side effects
- 🔗 PSMA-PET staging can prompt post-op pelvic nodal RT instead of ePLND, limiting lymphedema
- ⚠️ Roberts PCAN 2024 SWOT: no level 1 evidence for oncologic benefit of ePLND; earlier ARSI use lowers pN relevance
