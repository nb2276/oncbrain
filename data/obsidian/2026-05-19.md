# 2026-05-19

**SF-SABR (N=1687): 2-yr LC 90-93%, median OS 40mo primary NSCLC / 51mo oligomets — largest multi-institution single-fraction dataset to date.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Thoracic and prostate dominated today. SF-SABR pooled data (ESTRO 2026) offer the most mature efficacy and toxicity picture for single-fraction lung SABR yet. AUA 2026 consensus tips staging toward PSMA PET over ePLND in intermediate-risk prostate, with morbidity data now quantifying the tradeoff.

## 🌰 Prostate

AUA 2026 consensus formalizes PSMA PET as the preferred staging tool over ePLND in intermediate-risk disease, backed by NPV data and a growing morbidity literature.

### ePLND vs PSMA PET staging (AUA 2026)

> AUA 2026 consensus: PSMA PET NPV ~96% may safely replace ePLND for staging in intermediate-risk prostate cancer.

![ePLND vs PSMA PET staging (AUA 2026)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhwFr9WYAAt7_p.jpg)

- 🔍 Yaxley et al (BJUI 2019): 1253 men with primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT; 47.7% of LN metastases were outside standard ePLND template boundaries
- 📊 PSMA PET NPV ~96% for LNI; intermediate-risk pts with negative PSMA PET may safely omit PLND (missed LN likely below ePLND detection threshold anyway)
- ⚠️ No Level 1 evidence for ePLND oncological benefit; RCTs have not shown consistent BCR outcome improvements from PLND
- ⚠️ ePLND morbidity burden from systematic review (Clinckaert, Cancers 2022)
  - Lower limb lymphedema: 0-14% after RP + PLND
  - After PLND + salvage pelvic node RT: 19-29% lymphedema, 2-22% also genital
  - DVT/PE risk 6-10x increased with PLND vs no PLND (Tyritzis J Urol 2015, N=3544)
- 🔗 SWOT analysis (Roberts et al, PCAN 2024): ePLND strengths (histopath staging, tumor burden reduction) increasingly offset by PSMA-PET integration enabling targeted/sentinel LND and post-op pelvic RT planning
- 🔍 Risk-stratified conclusions: omit PLND in intermediate risk if PSMA PET negative; GG3 use nomograms adjunctively; high-risk individualize with shared decision-making re: adjuvant/salvage pelvic RT tradeoffs

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

Largest pooled SF-SABR dataset to date; toxicity profile favorable but AE capture incomplete across sites.

### Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligometastases (ESTRO 2026 pooled analysis)

> SF-SABR (N=1687): median OS 40mo primary NSCLC, 51mo oligomets; 2-yr LC 90-93%; G3+ AEs 2.9%.

![Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligometastases (ESTRO 2026 pooled analysis)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInTCxxXIAAKCPI.jpg)

| Cohort | 1-yr OS | 2-yr OS | Median OS (mo) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Primary NSCLC | 84% (95% CI 82, 86) | 67% (95% CI 64, 69) | 40 (95% CI 36, 43) |
| Oligomets | 90% (95% CI 86, 92) | 75% (95% CI 71, 79) | 51 (95% CI 42, 58) |

- 🔍 Pooled analysis, 3 institutions (Peter Mac, Cleveland Clinic, Roswell Park); 1200 primary NSCLC + 487 pulmonary oligomets treated with single-fraction SABR.
- 📊 Median PFS 30mo (primary NSCLC) and 11mo (oligomets); 2-yr LC 90-93% across both cohorts.
- 📊 AEs (primary NSCLC only, n=789; Roswell Park excluded): G2+ 15.7% (124/789), G3+ 2.9% (23/789).
- ⚠️ Single-arm pooled series; no randomized comparator vs multi-fraction SABR. AE capture incomplete (2/3 sites, n=789/1687).
- 🔗 CHISEL (TROG 09.02) is the only prior randomized single vs multi-fraction SABR trial for peripheral NSCLC; this is the largest multi-institution SF-SABR dataset to date.
