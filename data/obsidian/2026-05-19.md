# 2026-05-19

**SF-SABR (N=1687): LC 90-93% at 2yr, G3+ 2.9%; single-fraction lung SABR feasible at scale across institutions.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Thoracic and prostate carried the sharpest signals. SF-SABR pooled data (N=1687) backs single-fraction lung delivery with durable LC and low toxicity. At AUA 2026, PSMA PET staging challenges ePLND rationale: 47.7% of LN mets fall outside template, no Level 1 BCR benefit established.

## 🌰 Prostate

PSMA-era staging reopens the PLND omission debate: imaging NPV ~96% plus absent Level 1 evidence recasts ePLND as morbidity without proven benefit in most pts.

### PSMA PET/CT for primary staging and ePLND utility in prostate cancer (AUA 2026 session)

> PSMA PET/CT NPV ~96% at primary staging; 47.7% of LN mets outside ePLND template; RCTs show no consistent BCR benefit from ePLND.

![Primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT, N=1253 consecutive men; 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND template boundaries.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhwFsnWkAAjOfG.jpg)
*Primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT, N=1253 consecutive men; 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND template boundaries.*

- 📊 PSMA PET/CT NPV ~96% for LNI at primary staging (Yaxley et al, BJUI 2019; N=1253 consecutive men)
- 📊 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND template boundaries — missed regardless of dissection extent
- ⚠️ RCTs have not shown consistent BCR improvements from ePLND; session discussants acknowledge absent Level 1 evidence for oncologic benefit
- 🔍 AUA 2026 session: synthesizes retrospective cohort (N=1253), systematic lymphedema review, and perspective piece; no new RCT data presented
- 🔍 Risk-stratified session conclusions on PLND
  - Intermediate risk, PSMA PET negative: PLND may safely be omitted (missed LNs small, likely missed by ePLND too)
  - GG3: nomograms add value for patient selection
  - High-risk: individualized decision; prior PLND raises lymphedema risk if salvage pelvic XRT needed
  - If PLND performed: extended template required
- ⚠️ PLND morbidity by treatment context (Clinckaert et al, Cancers 2022)
  - Lower limb lymphedema: 0-14% after RP+PLND
  - Lower limb lymphedema: 19-29% after PLND+salvage pelvic XRT; genital lymphedema 2-22%
  - 0-9% lower limb lymphedema after pelvic lymph node XRT alone
- ⚠️ DVT/PE risk 6-10x higher with PLND vs no PLND (Tyritzis et al J Urol 2015, N=3544)
- 🔗 Alternative pathway: PSMA PET staging → post-op pelvic XRT may replace ePLND staging and limit lymphedema morbidity (Roberts et al, PCAN 2024)

## 💧 Bladder

Multicenter TMT benchmark: regimen quality (5-FU CRT, IGRT) predicts bladder preservation independently of pt factors.

### Bladder-preserving TMT for MIBC: multicenter prognostic factor analysis (ESTRO 2026)

> MIBC TMT: CLR 63.7%, salvage cystectomy 9.7% in 369-pt multicenter cohort; 5-FU CRT and IGRT quality independently predicted CLR.

![Bladder-preserving TMT for MIBC: multicenter prognostic factor analysis (ESTRO 2026)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIrl5sdWwAAEkhS.jpg)

- 📊 CLR 63.7%; salvage cystectomy 9.7%
- 📊 Disease progression 28.8%
  - Local: 10.1%
  - Systemic: 10.7%
  - Combined: 8.7%
- 🔍 Multicenter retrospective cohort, Spain, 2010-2022; N=369, cT2-T4aN0M0, median age 76, 85.1% male
- 🔍 Primary EP: CLR; secondary: OS, CSS, recurrence patterns, salvage cystectomy
- 🔍 Multivariable logistic regression to identify CLR predictors across 24 centers
- ⚠️ Retrospective design; no randomized comparator vs radical cystectomy
- ⚠️ 24-center heterogeneity and 12-year span invite era effects and regimen mix confounding
- 🔗 CLR 63.7% and salvage cystectomy 9.7% align with BC2001 and RTOG 0712 TMT benchmark data

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

Single-fraction SABR at scale: largest pooled lung dataset to date supports LC and toxicity profile across primary and oligometastatic settings.

### Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and lung oligometastases (pooled analysis)

> SF-SABR in 1687 pts: LC 90-93% at 2yr, G3+ AEs 2.9%, mOS 40mo (NSCLC) / 51mo (oligomets).

![Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and lung oligometastases (pooled analysis)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInTCxxXIAAKCPI.jpg)

| Endpoint | Primary NSCLC | Oligometastases |
|---|---|---|
| 1-yr OS | 84% (95% CI 82-86) | 90% (95% CI 86-92) |
| 2-yr OS | 67% (95% CI 64-69) | 75% (95% CI 71-79) |
| Median OS | 40 mo (95% CI 36-43) | 51 mo (95% CI 42-58) |

- 🔍 Pooled retrospective, 3 institutions (Peter Mac / Cleveland Clinic / Roswell Park): 1200 primary NSCLC + 487 pulmonary oligomets
- 📊 LC 90-93% at 2yr; isolated local/locoregional failure uncommon in both cohorts
- 📊 Median PFS: 30mo (primary NSCLC) vs 11mo (oligomets)
- ⚠️ AEs reported for primary NSCLC only (n=789; no Roswell Park data)
  - G3+: 23/789 (2.9%)
  - G2+: 124/789 (15.7%)
  - Most common: pleural effusion 14%, pneumonitis 7%, dyspnea 4%
- ⚠️ No randomized control arm; no prospective comparison to multi-fraction SABR regimens
- 🔗 Multi-fraction SABR (3-5fx) is established SOC for early NSCLC; single-fraction delivery at this scale is novel, consistent LC supports feasibility
