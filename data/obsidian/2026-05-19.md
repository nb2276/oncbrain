# 2026-05-19

**SF-SABR pooled (N=1687): LC 90-93% at 2yr, G3+ AEs 2.9%; single-fraction technique validated across primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligomets.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Thoracic and GU dominated today. SF-SABR confirmed at scale with durable LC and low toxicity; AUA 2026 review favors PSMA PET over ePLND for intermediate-risk staging. Real-world MIBC TMT (N=369) supports bladder preservation as RC alternative.

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

Largest single-fraction SABR pooled dataset to date; consistent LC and low-toxicity profile across primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligomets.

### Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligometastases (pooled analysis)

> SF-SABR (N=1687): LC 90-93% at 2yr, mOS 40mo (primary NSCLC) and 51mo (oligo), G3+ AEs 2.9%.

![Single-fraction SABR for primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligometastases (pooled analysis)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInTCxxXIAAKCPI.jpg)

| Endpoint | Primary NSCLC | Oligometastases |
|---|---|---|
| 1-yr OS | 84% (95% CI 82-86) | 90% (95% CI 86-92) |
| 2-yr OS | 67% (95% CI 64-69) | 75% (95% CI 71-79) |
| Median OS | 40mo (95% CI 36-43) | 51mo (95% CI 42-58) |

*LC 90-93% at 2yr; NSCLC mPFS 30mo, mOS 3.5yr; oligo mPFS 11mo, OS >4yr; G3+ AEs 2-3%*

*AEs, primary NSCLC (n=789): G3+ 23/789 (2.9%), G2+ 124/789 (15.7%); chest wall pain 114 (14%), pneumonitis 52 (7%)*

- 🔍 3-institution pooled analysis (Peter Mac / Cleveland Clinic / Roswell Park): 1200 primary NSCLC, 487 pulmonary oligomets
- ⚠️ AE data available for primary NSCLC only (n=789); no Roswell Park AE data included
- 📊 G2+ AEs: 124/789 (15.7%); G3+: 23/789 (2.9%); chest wall pain most common at 14%
- ⚠️ Single-arm pooled retrospective; no randomised comparator vs. multi-fraction SABR
- 🔗 LC 90-93% at 2yr consistent with multi-fraction SBRT institutional series for early-stage NSCLC; oligo mOS >4yr aligns with prior oligomet SABR data

## 🌰 Prostate

PSMA PET displacing ePLND in intermediate-risk staging; lymphedema morbidity data now quantified on the decision tree alongside NPV.

### ePLND vs PSMA PET staging for prostate cancer (AUA 2026 review)

> AUA 2026 review: PSMA PET NPV ~96%, no consistent RCT BCR benefit from ePLND; intermediate-risk pts may safely omit PLND if PET-negative.

![ePLND vs PSMA PET staging for prostate cancer (AUA 2026 review)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhwFr3XcAA-32r.jpg)

![Lymphedema prevalence after PCa treatment: 0-14% lower limb (RP+PLND); 19-29% after PLND+salvage pelvic RT; 6-10x DVT/PE risk with PLND (Tyritzis J Urol 2015; N=3544)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhwFsDXsAAqefc.jpg)
*Lymphedema prevalence after PCa treatment: 0-14% lower limb (RP+PLND); 19-29% after PLND+salvage pelvic RT; 6-10x DVT/PE risk with PLND (Tyritzis J Urol 2015; N=3544)*

![Yaxley BJUI 2019 (N=1253 primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CTs): 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND template boundaries](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIhwFsnWkAAjOfG.jpg)
*Yaxley BJUI 2019 (N=1253 primary staging Ga-PSMA PET/CTs): 47.7% of LN metastases outside ePLND template boundaries*

- 📊 PSMA PET/CT NPV ~96% for LNI at primary staging (tweet-reported; primary source not cited in source tweets)
- 📊 47.7% of LN metastases fall outside the standard ePLND template (Yaxley BJUI 2019; N=1253 consecutive staging PET/CTs)
- 🔍 AUA 2026 state-of-the-art review synthesizing observational data, systematic reviews, and prior RCTs; no new primary trial
- ⚠️ Absent Level 1 evidence for significant oncological benefit from ePLND; RCTs have not shown consistent BCR improvements
- ⚠️ ePLND morbidity: lymphedema by treatment context
  - 0-14% lower limb lymphedema after RP + PLND
  - 0-9% after pelvic lymph node RT alone
  - 19-29% after PLND + salvage pelvic node RT (+ 2-22% genital lymphedema)
  - 6-10x increased DVT/PE risk with PLND (Tyritzis J Urol 2015; N=3544)
- 🔍 Intermediate-risk: PLND may safely be omitted if PSMA PET negative for LNI; missed LN may also fall outside ePLND template
- 🔍 GG3: nomograms add value alongside PSMA PET for individualized PLND decision
- 🔍 High-risk: individualized; adjuvant/salvage pelvic RT discussed as alternative pathway, especially without prior ePLND
- 🔗 Roberts PCAN 2024: PSMA PET-guided post-op pelvic RT plan as ePLND alternative, limiting lymphedema morbidity

## 💧 Bladder

Spanish real-world cohort reinforces 5-FU CRT as preferred TMT regimen; image-guidance frequency as CLR predictor warrants prospective validation.

### Bladder-preserving TMT for muscle-invasive bladder cancer (ESTRO 2026 multicenter analysis)

> CLR 63.7% in N=369 MIBC TMT cohort; 5-FU CRT and image guidance independently predict CLR on MVA.

![Bladder-preserving TMT for muscle-invasive bladder cancer (ESTRO 2026 multicenter analysis)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HIrl5sdWwAAEkhS.jpg)

- 📊 CLR 63.7%; overall disease progression 28.8%
- 📊 Progression pattern
  - Local only 10.1%
  - Systemic only 10.7%
  - Combined 8.7%
- 📊 Salvage cystectomy 9.7%
- 🔍 Study design
  - Multicenter retrospective cohort (Spain, 2010-2022)
  - N=369; cT2-T4aN0M0; median age 76; 85.1% male
  - Definitive TMT: TURBT + concurrent chemoradiotherapy
- 📐 5-FU CRT → higher CLR: OR 4.9 (95% CI 1.1-22.1), p=0.038
- 📐 Weekly portal imaging → lower CLR: OR 0.35 (95% CI 0.20-0.60), p<0.001
- 🔗 CLR rate consistent with published European TMT series; real-world data supports bladder preservation as RC alternative
- ⚠️ Retrospective design; no RC comparator arm; TMT eligibility selection bias uncontrolled
- ⚠️ OS/CSS numbers not reported in source; comparable survival claim unverifiable from abstract
