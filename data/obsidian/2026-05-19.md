# 2026-05-19

**SF-SABR for NSCLC/pulmonary oligomets (N=1687): 2yr LC 90-93%, G3+ AEs 2.9% — single-fraction scales.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> Thoracic and GU dominated today. SF-SABR's pooled multi-institutional data (N=1687) consolidates single-fraction as a viable radiotherapy approach for primary NSCLC and pulmonary oligomets. In prostate, AUA 2026 evidence synthesis questions routine ePLND when PSMA PET is negative, with morbidity data reinforcing a more selective dissection strategy.

## 🌰 Prostate

PSMA PET precision is eroding the rationale for routine ePLND across risk groups.

### ePLND vs PSMA PET/CT for Prostate Cancer Staging (AUA 2026)

> PSMA PET/CT NPV ~96% for LNI; 47.7% of LN mets outside ePLND template, questioning routine extended dissection.

- 📊 Yaxley et al (BJUI 2019), N=1253 consecutive staging Ga-PSMA PET/CT: 47.7% of LN metastases fell outside ePLND anatomical template boundaries
- 🔍 AUA 2026 session summarizing RCT evidence, systematic review data, and a PCAN 2024 perspective piece (Roberts et al); not a single new RCT
- 📊 Lymphedema risk from OCR (Clinckaert systematic review): 0-14% lower limb lymphedema post-RP+PLND; 19-29% after PLND + salvage pelvic node RT (2-22% also genital)
- 📊 Tyritzis et al J Urol 2015, N=3544: PLND associated with 6-10x increased DVT/PE risk vs no PLND
- 🔍 Conclusions by risk group (AUA 2026 slide OCR)
  - Intermediate risk: PLND may safely be omitted if PSMA PET negative for LNI; missed nodes likely small, also missed by ePLND
  - GG3: nomograms add value alongside PSMA PET
  - High risk: individualized decision; adjuvant/salvage pelvic RT may increase side effects if PLND already performed
- ⚠️ No Level 1 evidence for oncological benefit of ePLND; RCTs have not shown consistent BCR improvement; earlier ARSI use makes pN status less clinically actionable
- ❓ Optimal integration of targeted/radioguided/sentinel LND with PSMA PET unresolved; higher nomogram cut-off thresholds for PLND omission not yet standardized

## 🫁 Thoracic / Lung

### Single-Fraction SABR for Primary NSCLC and Pulmonary Oligometastases (Pooled 1687 pts)

> SF-SABR for primary NSCLC/oligomets (N=1687): 2yr LC 90-93%, median OS 40 vs 51mo, G3+ AEs 2.9%.

![Single-Fraction SABR for Primary NSCLC and Pulmonary Oligometastases (Pooled 1687 pts)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HInTCxxXIAAKCPI.jpg)

| Timepoint | Primary NSCLC | Oligometastases |
|---|---|---|
| 1-yr OS | 84% (95% CI 82-86) | 90% (95% CI 86-92) |
| 2-yr OS | 67% (95% CI 64-69) | 75% (95% CI 71-79) |
| Median OS | 40 mo (95% CI 36-43) | 51 mo (95% CI 42-58) |

- 🔍 Pooled multi-institutional (Peter Mac, Cleveland Clinic, Roswell Park): 1200 primary NSCLC, 487 pulmonary oligomets
- 📊 2yr local control 90-93%; median PFS 30mo (NSCLC) vs 11mo (oligomets); isolated local/locoregional failure uncommon
- 📊 G3+ AEs 2.9% (23/789); G2+ 15.7% (124/789); AE data primary NSCLC only, n=789 (no Roswell Park AE data)
- 🔗 vs CHISEL (Lancet Oncol 2019): randomised single-fraction vs multifraction for peripheral T1-2 NSCLC; this pooled analysis scales to N=1687 and extends to oligomets setting
- ⚠️ Single-arm pooled analysis; no randomised comparator vs multifraction SABR; eligibility and dose heterogeneity across 3 institutions not reported
- ❓ Optimal SF-SABR vs multifraction selection by tumor size, location, and histology undefined; no QoL or patient-reported outcomes
