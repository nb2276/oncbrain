# 2026-05-30 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**ROADS: resected brain mets >2cm — surg-bed recurrence 1% GammaTile brachytherapy vs 12% post-op SRS (randomized, N=230), reopening cavity management.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> RT modality and sequencing carried the day: ROADS makes cavity brachytherapy a randomized contender against post-op SRS, and Neo-CRAG revives a neoadjuvant chemoRT survival benefit TOPGEAR missed (mOS 67.5 vs 37.6mo). Prostate ran deep on biomarker-gated intensification (TALAPRO-3, Decipher), but the RT-actionable signals sat in CNS and upper-GI.

## 🌰 Prostate

Biomarker-gated intensification dominated: Decipher and clinico-transcriptomic scores gate systemic add-ons on an RT+ADT backbone, but most signals are prognostic, not predictive.

### A-DREAM (ALLIANCE mAPMS)

> 41% (32/78) remained treatment-free with eugonadal testosterone at 18mo after interrupting ADT+ARPI in deep-responding mHSPC; primary EP met.

![A-DREAM (ALLIANCE mAPMS)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmNOo6WIAEKMLx.jpg)

![rPFS: 15/78 events, median NE.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmNOpKXsAIBSNg.jpg)
*rPFS: 15/78 events, median NE.*

![A-DREAM (ALLIANCE mAPMS)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmNOpSWAAI67Qq.jpg)

- 🔍 Single-arm phase 2 (ALLIANCE A-DREAM); N=78 eligible mHSPC, enrolled 07/2022-03/2024. No randomised continuous-therapy comparator.
- 🔍 Deep-responder selection: PSA<0.2 (stable/falling) after 18-24mo ADT + ≥12mo ARPI; 64.9% low-volume (CHAARTED).
- 🔍 Re-initiation triggers: PSA ≥5 ng/mL, radiographic change (PCWG3), or PrCa-related symptoms.
- 🔍 29.5% received radiation to metastatic sites pre-interruption; 51.3% had prior radiation as local therapy.
- 📊 Disposition at 26.9mo median follow-up
  - 30 (38.5%) continuing on treatment interruption
  - 29 (37.2%) resumed ADT+ARPI per protocol
  - 5 (6.4%) resumed before meeting resumption criteria
  - 1 (1.3%) died before next treatment (MI)
- ⚠️ 4 of 29 who resumed ADT+ARPI per protocol later progressed (2 rPD, 1 PDu, 1 PET) and discontinued original ARPI.
- ⚠️ Lenient success bar: primary EP read on 80% CI + one-sided p; ~59% did not achieve treatment-free + T-recovery at 18mo.
- ⚠️ Short FU (26.9mo); OS (4/78 events) and rPFS median NE — durability of the off-treatment interval still unknown.
- 🔗 Intermittent-therapy concept echoes SWOG 9346 (intermittent ADT, mHSPC), but here a deeper-response, post-ARPI-intensification population with both agents interrupted.

### ARACOG (AFT-47)

> Enzalutamide drove greater 24-wk cognitive decline than darolutamide (MCCD -36.1 vs -15.8, p=0.009) in advanced prostate cancer.

![ARACOG (AFT-47)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmQaM5XEAAV9a0.jpg)

| Arm | MCCD median % change (domain) | p |
|---|---|---|
| Darolutamide (n=48) | -15.8 (PALFAM) | 0.009 |
| Enzalutamide (n=47) | -36.1 (SWM) | 0.009 |

![ARACOG (AFT-47)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmQVOtW8AEcroz.jpg)

- 🔍 Randomized open-label phase 2 (Alliance, AFT-47), N=111 advanced prostate cancer spanning mHSPC, mCRPC, nmCRPC.
- 🔍 1° EP: Maximally Changed Cognitive Domain (MCCD), % change baseline→24wk across 5 remote CANTAB tests (executive function, visual memory, attention, working memory).
- 🔍 Stratified by age (<65 / 65-80 / >80); darolutamide supplied by study, enzalutamide via standard of care; enrolled 8/2021 to 3/2025.
- ⚠️ MCCD picks each arm's worst-hit domain, so the headline numbers reflect different domains (daro PALFAM, enz SWM), not a like-for-like comparison.
- ⚠️ Open-label design; CANTAB is computerized/objective, but expectancy effects on test effort aren't excluded.
- ⚠️ Cognitive surrogate: CANTAB modules are research tools, not for clinical diagnosis (per slide); link to functional decline unestablished.
- ⚠️ 24-wk analysis: daro n=48, enz n=47 (of 111 randomized); crossover pts scored at crossover within their randomized arm.
- 🔗 Direction aligns with darolutamide's low blood-brain-barrier penetration vs enzalutamide (known pharmacology), giving the divergence a mechanistic basis.

### NRG Clinico-Transcriptomic Risk Stratification

> ~¼ of NCCN high-risk pts reclassified when 22-gene GC added to clinical risk; combined score gates RT+ADT vs RT+ADT+AAP intensification.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The actionable question is systemic intensification on a fixed RT+ADT backbone: layering the 22-gene GC (Decipher) on NCCN clinical risk reclassifies ~¼ of high-risk pts, so clinical staging alone misassigns who gets abiraterone. GC is independently prognostic for MFS, DM, and OS (p<0.001).

![NRG Clinico-Transcriptomic Risk Stratification](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl7tXrWYAQbvJX.jpg)

| Endpoint | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---|---|---|
| MFS | 0.53 (0.44-0.64) | <0.0001 |
| OS | 0.60 (0.48-0.73) | <0.0001 |

![NRG Clinico-Transcriptomic Risk Stratification](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmIhjTXsAYCPnx.jpg)

![NRG Clinico-Transcriptomic Risk Stratification](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl8SgDXMAUnzKN.png)

- 🔍 Clinico-transcriptomic system layers a 22-gene GC (Decipher) on NCCN risk in ≥high-risk localized prostate; RT+ADT is the uniform backbone
- 📊 22-gene GC independently prognostic for MFS, DM, and OS beyond clinical variables alone (p<0.001)
- 📊 ~¼ of the ≥high-risk population has discordant clinical vs biomarker risk
- 🔍 Combined score gates systemic intensification on the RT+ADT backbone
  - CT HR, ≤2 points: RT+ADT
  - CT VHR, ≥3 points: RT+ADT+AAP (abiraterone intensification)
- 📊 STAMPEDE AAP absolute benefit grows over time: up to ~20% MFS, ~13% OS (Attard, Lancet 2022)
- ⚠️ GC shown prognostic, not predictive: per-stratum AAP benefit is inferred from STAMPEDE, not randomized within GC-defined groups
- ⚠️ Retrospective biomarker model; CT classification not prospectively validated for the intensification decision itself
- 🔗 Extends prior VHR framework (Spratt et al, JCO 2018) to the modern very-high-risk population

### GU Biomarkers in Clinical Practice

> Decipher/clinical-risk discordant in 24% of events; genomic classifiers validated pre-ARPI may not transfer to current ARPI-based SOC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The portability caveat is the read for anyone using Decipher: it disagreed with clinical risk in 24% of events and was derived in pre-ARPI, tissue-selected subsets (n=427 of 3816). Weigh that before trusting a genomic classifier to gate intensification in current ARPI-based practice.

![GU Biomarkers in Clinical Practice](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmHJahXYAMGSc4.png)

![Discordance in 24% of events; biomarker tissue n=427 vs full cohort n=3816.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmGFEnWUAEeQN4.png)
*Discordance in 24% of events; biomarker tissue n=427 vs full cohort n=3816.*

- 🔬 Theme: applying genomic biomarkers to therapy selection in advanced prostate; only as good as the validation population
- 💊 Biomarker-gated therapy fractions, advanced prostate (2026 landscape)
  - PARP inhibitors ~10-20% (HRR loss)
  - Lu-177 PSMA ~90% (PSMA-avid)
  - Pembrolizumab ~3% (MSI-H/dMMR)
- 💊 Tissue panel testing for HRR + tumor-suppressor loss is SOC; 'genomics is not genetics'
- ⚠️ Decipher vs clinical risk discordant in 24% of patient events
- ⚠️ Tissue-availability bias: biomarker cohort n=427 vs full trial n=3816
- 🔍 ENZAMET biomarker cohort attrition (specimen dropout)
  - Enrolled N=1,125
  - Consented for biomarker N=1,071
  - GEP in CLIA lab N=764
  - Final biomarker cohort N=634 (56% of enrolled)
  - ADT+enza+docetaxel subcohort N=320
- 🔍 Docetaxel not randomized; protocol amended after 88 pts to allow it per CHARTED
- 📊 Decipher ≤0.85 subgroup: ADT+enza+docetaxel had worse OS than ADT+enza
- ⚠️ Docetaxel subgroup confounded: more high-volume disease + older age → selection bias

### ENZAMET + Decipher

> Docetaxel-by-Decipher interaction p=0.04: triplet helps high-Decipher (>0.85) pts (OS HR 0.75) but not low-Decipher (≤0.85, HR 1.94) in mHSPC.

![ENZAMET + Decipher](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl_g2kWMAENJbO.png)

| Decipher (doce vs no-doce) | Unweighted HR | IPTW-weighted HR |
|---|---|---|
| ≤0.85 | 2.78 (1.49-5.21), p=0.001 | 1.94 (0.95-3.96), p=0.07 |
| >0.85 | 1.13 (0.71-1.79), p=0.60 | 0.75 (0.43-1.33), p=0.33 |
| Interaction p | 0.02 | 0.04 |

![ENZAMET + Decipher](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl-16JXUAEsdkA.png)

- 🔍 Post-hoc Decipher biomarker analysis of ENZAMET; ADT+ENZA+docetaxel cohort N=320, DPMC cutoff 0.85
- 🔍 Docetaxel investigator-allocated within ENZAMET, not randomized; compared by propensity-score (IPTW) weighted MVA
- 📊 Docetaxel benefit confined to high-Decipher (>0.85); low-Decipher pts show none, supporting docetaxel omission
- 🔗 Decipher >0.85 prognostic for worse OS on ADT+ENZA alone (HR 3.02, 1.50-5.76)
- 📐 MVA covariates: tumor volume, Gleason, synchronous vs metachronous, ECOG, age
- ⚠️ Within-group docetaxel HRs non-significant (CIs cross 1); read rests on the interaction test, not a positive arm
- ⚠️ Residual imbalance after propensity scoring per authors; observational, non-randomized docetaxel comparison
- 🔗 Framed Level 1B, consistent with CHAARTED and STAMPEDE docetaxel data

### TALAPRO-3

> 3-yr rPFS 77% vs 56%, HR 0.48 (95% CI 0.36-0.65), p<0.001, adding talazoparib to enza+ADT in HRR-deficient mHSPC.

![TALAPRO-3](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlcYEVXgAMU39-.jpg)

| Subgroup | Tala 3y rPFS | Placebo 3y rPFS | HR (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| ITT | 77% (67-85) | 56% (50-62) | 0.48 (0.36-0.65) |
| BRCA | 76% (69-82) | 49% (38-59) | 0.37 (0.22-0.61) |
| Non-BRCA | — | 60% (52-67) | 0.57 (0.39-0.82) |

- 📊 ITT median rPFS NC vs 45.8mo (37.7-NC); 67/300 vs 126/299 events
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT: talazoparib+enza vs placebo+enza, both with ADT; 1L metastatic HRR-deficient prostate cancer; N=599 ITT
- 🔍 Effect gradient by genotype: deepest in BRCA, attenuated but still positive in non-BRCA HRR (CI excludes 1)
- ⚠️ rPFS is the surrogate 1° EP; OS not reported in source, follow-up immature (tala median NC in every subgroup)
- 🔗 vs TALAPRO-2 (1L mCRPC): same talazoparib+enza doublet, now moved earlier into HRR-deficient disease
- ⚠️ Added PARP toxicity (anemia, fatigue) not reported in source; gates the risk/benefit in an earlier, less-symptomatic population

## 💧 Bladder

Bladder-preservation chemoRT (55Gy/20fx) is the throughline: durvalumab adds to trimodality (RAD-IO) while EV+pembro threatens to replace it (EV309 vs chemoRT).

### RAD-IO

> 12-mo DFS 40/50 (80%) cleared the GO threshold for durvalumab added to 5FU/MMC chemoRT in muscle-invasive bladder, bladder-preservation intent.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> RT-relevant extension is nodal coverage: node-positive pts (n=7) received 46Gy/20fx elective nodal RT plus 55Gy/20fx to bladder, pushing chemoRT bladder preservation past its usual node-negative limit. Dose is the hypofractionated BC2001 UK schedule (55Gy/20fx), not conventional fractionation, which gates transferability to US practice. Moves whether to add adjuvant IO and extend preservation to node+ MIBC.

![Primary outcome 12-mo DFS 40/50 (80%)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlAU46X0AA57l1.jpg)
*Primary outcome 12-mo DFS 40/50 (80%)*

![Completed planned treatment 33/54 (61%); discontinued early 21 (39%)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlAPgbWwAEOlGG.jpg)
*Completed planned treatment 33/54 (61%); discontinued early 21 (39%)*

- 📊 1° outcome: 12-mo DFS 40/50 (80%), 95% CI 0.67-0.89, cleared pre-set GO threshold (≥75%) for further evaluation
- 🔍 Single-arm multi-stage feasibility/safety trial, N=55 (54 treated), MIBC, definitive bladder-preservation intent (Abstract 4504, ND James)
- 🔍 RT: 55Gy/20fx to bladder; node-positive pts add 46Gy/20fx elective nodal RT (Stage 1 expansion, first 6 node+ pts)
- 💊 ChemoRT backbone: concurrent 5-FU + mitomycin C (BC2001 regimen)
- 💊 Durvalumab neoadjuvant, synchronous with chemoRT, then adjuvant for 12 months
- 🔍 Population (n=55)
  - cT2 44/55 (80%), cT3 11/55 (20%)
  - Node+ 7 (13%): N1 4, N2 3
  - Prior neoadjuvant chemo 41 (75%)
  - Age 69 (IQR 62-76), male 45/55 (82%)
- ⚠️ Single-arm, benchmarked vs historical BC2001; no contemporaneous chemoRT-alone or durvalumab-omission arm, so the IO contribution can't be isolated
- ⚠️ 12-mo DFS is an early surrogate; bladder-intact survival, OS, and late GU/GI toxicity not reported in source
- 🔗 Contextual benchmark BC2001 (JCO 2016), DFS by chemo randomisation: HR 0.78 (0.60-1.02), p=0.07

### Perioperative MIBC: Management Post-pCR

> 85% 5-yr OS with pT0 (SWOG 8710) makes pCR strongly prognostic, yet sandwich IO regimens continue planned adjuvant therapy regardless of response.

![Periop EVP (KEYNOTE-905) vs RC alone: OS HR 0.50, median OS 41.7 mo](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlnzOUWAAA9384.png)
*Periop EVP (KEYNOTE-905) vs RC alone: OS HR 0.50, median OS 41.7 mo*

![Perioperative MIBC: Management Post-pCR](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlnV1xXIAIlHNl.png)

- 📊 pCR (pT0N0) after neoadjuvant chemo is strongly prognostic: SWOG 8710 shows 85% 5-yr OS if pT0
- 📐 Meta-analysis: pooled RR 0.19 for RFS favoring pCR
- 🔍 pCR is registrational only as a phase 3 co-primary; standalone use limited to phase 2 signal-seeking
- 📊 Perioperative regimens by cisplatin-eligibility

  | Trial | Setting | Key result |
  |---|---|---|
  | KEYNOTE-905 | Cis-ineligible, periop EVP vs RC alone | OS HR 0.50; now standard (FDA approved) |
  | VOLGA | Cis-ineligible, preop EV + periop durva/treme | Improved EFS (press release) |
  | NIAGARA | Cis-eligible, preop Gem/Cis + periop durva | 24-mo OS 82.2% vs 75.2% |
  | VESPER | Cis-eligible, ddMVAC vs Gem/Cis | 5-yr OS 66% vs 57%; more toxicity |
  | KEYNOTE-B15 | Cis-eligible, periop EVP vs Gem/Cis | Improved OS; under FDA review |

- 🔍 KEYNOTE-905 schedule: 3 cycles EVP, cystectomy, then 6 cycles EV + 14 cycles pembro
- 💊 For sandwich IO regimens, planned adjuvant therapy continues regardless of pathologic response
- 💊 Post-pCR adjuvant continuation by regimen
  - NIAGARA: resume durvalumab for 8 months post-cystectomy
  - Periop EVP: resume EVP post-cystectomy
  - Cisplatin chemo alone: surveillance after pCR is standard
- 🔗 High-risk staging (≥ypT2/N1) after neoadjuvant chemo is an adjuvant indication (CHECKMATE-274)
- ⚠️ Not all pts in perioperative trials completed adjuvant therapy, sometimes due to toxicity

### GU Curative Intent: Living Longer, Living Better

> Discussant roundup balancing cure vs function: durvalumab+chemoRT kept full bladder RT delivery; EV+pembro bladder-sparing (EV309 vs chemoRT) and ctDNA RCC selection maturing.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The radonc read is bladder preservation under competition: durvalumab added to 55Gy/20fx MMC/5-FU chemoRT kept full RT delivery in 54/54 pts (87% without delay), but EV309 now randomizes EV+pembro against chemoradiotherapy itself. If systemic bladder-sparing matures, trimodality's role narrows.

- 🔍 ASCO 2026 GU discussant summary (Brian Rini): curative-intent bladder + RCC, framed as improving cure while preserving function/QoL
- 📊 Bladder preservation arm: durvalumab added to 55 Gy/20 fx chemoRT (mitomycin C + 5-FU backbone)
- 📊 Per Rini: durvalumab didn't compromise RT delivery, and chemo delivery not significantly compromised
- ⚠️ Deliverability-only readout (N=54); long-term bladder preservation, function, symptom burden, safety and QoL still require follow-up (Rini)
- 🔍 EV+pembrolizumab bladder-sparing trials in MIBC (EVP)

  | Population | Cystectomy-eligible | Cystectomy-ineligible/refusing |
  | Stage | cT2-4a MIBC | cT2-4a MIBC |

- ⚠️ EV309 randomizes EV+pembrolizumab against chemoradiotherapy, directly pitting systemic bladder-sparing against trimodality

## 🧠 CNS

First randomized data pitting cavity brachytherapy against post-op SRS for resected large brain mets.

### ROADS Trial

> Surg bed recurrence 1% GammaTile brachytherapy vs 12% post-op SRS for resected brain mets >2cm; 2yr OS 62% vs 36%.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Both arms are RT, so the actionable choice is GammaTile intraop brachytherapy vs post-op SRS for a resected brain met >2cm. The surg-bed-control win is large, but leptomeningeal failure ran 10% with GammaTile vs 3% with SRS, a brachytherapy pattern-of-failure cost that should temper adoption despite the headline OS edge.

![ROADS Trial](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJkZuPMWIAMSbqO.jpg)

| Endpoint | GammaTile | SRS |
|---|---|---|
| Time to surg bed recur | NR | 17 mo |
| Surg bed recur-free survival | NR | 11 mo |
| 2-yr OS | 62% | 36% |

- 📊 Surg bed recurrence 1% GammaTile vs 12% SRS
- 🔍 Randomized, N=230, resected brain mets >2 cm: intraoperative GammaTile brachytherapy vs post-op SRS (ASCO 2026 final, Weinberg)
- 📊 Intracranial failure / toxicity by arm
  - Radiation necrosis: 8% GammaTile vs 7% SRS

## 🤝 Supportive / QoL

Celiac plexus SRS for cancer pain, with a SPIN score (severe baseline pain + intact nerves) stratifying response 32→89%.

### SPIN Score / Celiac Plexus SRS · [[NCT03323489]]

> Pain response 32→53→89% across SPIN score 0/1/2 after celiac SRS; severe baseline pain + no prior neurotoxic chemo predict response.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The actionable read is timing: pts with severe baseline pain and no prior neurotoxic chemo (the score's 'intact nerves' arm) reached 89% pain response vs 32% with neither, arguing for celiac SRS referral before chemo neuropathy sets in. Neurotoxic exposure was the strongest predictor (multivariate OR 5.1, p=0.009).

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of 90 evaluable pts from the phase 2 trial ([[NCT03323489]], Lancet Oncol 2024)
- 📊 Parent-trial pain response 53% (95% CI 42-64%); ≥2-pt BPI-SF drop at 3 weeks
- 🔍 SPIN (Severe Pain + Intact Nerves): baseline pain >6 (+1), no prior neurotoxic chemo (+1)
- 📊 Pain response by SPIN score

  | SPIN score | Pain response | n |
  |---|---|---|
  | 0 | 32% | 31 |
  | 1 | 53% | 40 |
  | 2 | 89% | 19 |

- 📐 Independent predictors on multivariate logistic regression

  | Predictor | OR | p |
  |---|---|---|
  | Neurotoxic chemo exposure | 5.1 | 0.009 |
  | Baseline pain intensity | 1.8 | 0.003 |

- 📊 Response climbs with baseline pain severity; >6 cutoff chosen for optimal discrimination
- ⚠️ Age and therapy line significant on univariate but lost to collinearity in multivariate

## 🍽️ GI Upper

Neoadjuvant chemoRT revives a gastric survival benefit TOPGEAR couldn't show, though on a non-FLOT XELOX backbone.

### Neo-CRAG

> Adding neoadj chemoRT (45Gy/25fx) to periop XELOX improved mDFS 52.7 vs 24.4mo (HR 0.75) and mOS 67.5 vs 37.6mo (HR 0.78).

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT-attributable read is locoregional control: LRR halved (9.4% vs 18.3% in R0 pts) alongside higher pCR and ypN0, a mechanistic chain (downstaging → fewer locoregional relapses → OS) that TOPGEAR's null never produced. 45Gy/25fx on a perioperative backbone, so it revives neoadjuvant RT for high-risk D2-resected LAGC, if you accept XELOX rather than FLOT.

![Neo-CRAG](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlIWxCWAAMF5px.jpg)

- 📊 1° EP DFS: mDFS 52.7 vs 24.4 mo, HR 0.750 (0.607-0.928), p=0.008; 3yr DFS 55.6% vs 42.4% (CRT vs CT)
- 📊 OS (secondary): mOS 67.5 vs 37.6 mo, HR 0.781 (0.628-0.970), p=0.025; 5yr 50.1% vs 44.2%
- 🔍 Phase 3, 13 China centers, N=620 (310/arm), 2013-2022; high-risk cT3N2-3/cT4 gastric/EGJ adenoca, all D2-resected, 36.3% EGJ
- 🔍 RT: 45 Gy/25 fx concurrent, started after cycle 1, with reduced-dose chemo (oxaliplatin 100, cape 825) during RT
- 📊 Pathologic + locoregional gains, CRT vs CT (surgical cohort) — comparison values omitted (cell value "0-2" not verified in source)
- ⚠️ Non-FLOT backbone (XELOX): RT benefit over modern perioperative FLOT SOC untested
- 🔗 Diverges from TOPGEAR (ph3), which found no survival benefit adding neoadj chemoRT to periop chemo
- ⚠️ G3+ hematologic toxicity higher with CRT: 14.6% (44/302) vs 10.3% (31/301)
- 📊 G3+ postoperative complications comparable: 9.0% (20/223) vs 7.6% (17/225)
