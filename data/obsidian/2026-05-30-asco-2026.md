# 2026-05-30 — ASCO Annual Meeting 2026

**ROADS: surgical bed recurrence 1% vs 12% for intraop GammaTile brachy vs post-op cavity SRS in resected brain mets >2cm — challenges the SRS standard.**

> [!summary] TL;DR
> RT drove the day's actionable news: ROADS (CNS) favors intraop brachytherapy over cavity SRS on local control, and Neo-CRAG (upper-GI) revives neoadjuvant chemoRT in gastric cancer (mOS 67.5 vs 37.6mo) where CRITICS/TOPGEAR were null. Prostate ran a genomic-classifier thread on an RT+ADT backbone.

## 💧 Bladder

MIBC bladder-preservation thread: durvalumab added to 55Gy/20fx chemoRT (RAD-IO) plus post-pCR perioperative management.

### RAD-IO

> 12-mo DFS 40/50 (80%, 95% CI 0.67-0.89) cleared the pre-set ≥75% GO bar for durvalumab added to 5FU/MMC chemoRT in MIBC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> RT is standard and transferable: 55Gy/20fx to bladder, plus 46Gy/20fx elective nodal coverage in the node-positive expansion, extending durvalumab-chemoRT preservation to N+ disease. 12-mo DFS 80% (40/50, 95% CI 0.67-0.89) cleared the pre-set ≥75% GO bar vs BC2001. Moves whether to layer IO onto a hypofractionated preservation backbone.

### Management of MIBC After Pathologic Complete Response

> Review of perioperative MIBC management after pCR: sandwich IO regimens continue adjuvant regardless of response, cisplatin-chemo alone moves to surveillance.

![Overall survival: HR 0.50.](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlnzOUWAAA9384.png)
*Overall survival: HR 0.50.*

- 📊 pCR (pT0N0) post-neoadjuvant chemo = strong prognostic marker: SWOG 8710 85% 5-yr OS if pT0
- 📐 Meta-analysis: pooled RR 0.19 for RFS with pCR
- 🔍 pCR used as primary endpoint in signal-seeking phase 2 trials
- 🔍 pCR co-primary (not standalone) endpoint in phase 3 registrational trials
- 🔍 High-risk residual disease (≥ypT2 or N+) post-neoadjuvant chemo → adjuvant therapy (CHECKMATE-274)
- 📊 Perioperative regimens across trials

  | Trial | Setting | Comparison | Result |
  |---|---|---|---|
  | NIAGARA | Cis-eligible | Gem/Cis + periop durvalumab | 24-mo OS 82.2% vs 75.2% |
  | VESPER | Cis-eligible | ddMVAC vs Gem/Cis | 5-yr OS 66% vs 57% |
  | KEYNOTE-905 | Cis-ineligible | Periop EVP vs cystectomy alone | OS HR 0.50 |
  | KEYNOTE-B15 | Cis-eligible | Periop EVP vs Gem/Cis | OS improved (under FDA review) |

- 💊 Post-pCR management by regimen
  - Sandwich IO (NIAGARA, EVP): continue planned adjuvant regardless of pathologic response
  - NIAGARA: resume durvalumab 8 mo post-cystectomy
  - Cisplatin-based chemo alone: surveillance after pCR is standard
- ⚠️ Not all pts in perioperative trials completed adjuvant therapy, often due to toxicity

## 🌰 Prostate

Genomic-classifier thread on a fixed RT+ADT/ARPI backbone: Decipher for intensification gating and docetaxel selection, mostly prognostic not predictive.

### Genomic Classifier + NCCN Risk Stratification (Abstract 5000)

> 22-gene GC independently prognostic (MFS/DM/OS, p<0.001); combined NCCN+GC score reclassifies ~¼ of high-risk pts to gate AAP intensification on an RT+ADT backbone.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> ~¼ of NCCN ≥HR pts are clinical/GC-discordant, so the 22-gene GC reclassifies who reaches CT-VHR (≥3 pts) and has AAP added onto the fixed RT+ADT backbone. The intensification it triggers carries STAMPEDE M0 magnitude (MFS HR 0.53, OS HR 0.60). It sharpens the intensification decision, not RT dose or target.

![Genomic Classifier + NCCN Risk Stratification (Abstract 5000)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmIhjTXsAYCPnx.jpg)

![Genomic Classifier + NCCN Risk Stratification (Abstract 5000)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl7tXrWYAQbvJX.jpg)

| Endpoint | HR (add AAP) | 95% CI | p |
|---|---|---|---|
| MFS | 0.53 | 0.44-0.64 | <0.0001 |
| OS | 0.60 | 0.48-0.73 | <0.0001 |

![Genomic Classifier + NCCN Risk Stratification (Abstract 5000)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl8SgDXMAUnzKN.png)

- 📊 22-gene GC (Decipher) independently improved prognostic estimates over clinical variables for MFS, DM, and OS (multivariable, p<0.001)
- 🔍 Population: NCCN ≥ high-risk localized prostate; RT+ADT is the fixed definitive backbone for all
- 🔍 Clinico-transcriptomic (CT) score = NCCN clinical points + 22-gene GC points

  | Component | Category | Points |
  |---|---|---|
  | NCCN clinical | High-risk | +1 |
  | NCCN clinical | Very-high-risk | +2 |
  | GC (Decipher) | < 0.6 | 0 |
  | GC (Decipher) | 0.6-0.85 | +1 |
  | GC (Decipher) | > 0.85 | +2 |

- 📊 Output rule: ≤2 pts = CT-HR → RT+ADT; ≥3 pts = CT-VHR → RT+ADT+AAP (abiraterone) intensification
- 🔗 ~¼ of NCCN ≥HR pts are clinical/GC-discordant, so GC moves the risk band (and the intensification call) in a meaningful fraction
- 💊 Intensification rationale is imported from STAMPEDE M0 (Attard, Lancet 2022) AAP benefit; MFS/OS HRs shown in figure
- ⚠️ Prognostic, not predictive: shows GC stratifies risk, not that CT-VHR pts derive MORE AAP benefit; no randomized biomarker×treatment interaction
- ⚠️ Retrospective GC validation on NRG cohort, externally benchmarked to STAMPEDE curves; not a prospective intensification RCT

### A-DREAM

> 41% remained treatment-free with eugonadal testosterone recovery at 18mo after interrupting ADT+ARPI in deep-responding mHSPC (1° EP met, one-sided p=0.0249).

![Median time to eugonadal T 9.0mo. T recovery by 18mo 52 (66.7%); treatment-free 18mo 45 (57.7%); primary endpoint (treatment-free + T recovery) 32 (41.0%), 80% CI 33.1-48.9%, one-sided p 0.0249](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmNOo6WIAEKMLx.jpg)
*Median time to eugonadal T 9.0mo. T recovery by 18mo 52 (66.7%); treatment-free 18mo 45 (57.7%); primary endpoint (treatment-free + T recovery) 32 (41.0%), 80% CI 33.1-48.9%, one-sided p 0.0249*

![A-DREAM](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmNOpKXsAIBSNg.jpg)

![A-DREAM](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmNOpSWAAI67Qq.jpg)

- 🔍 Phase 2 single-arm (Alliance), N=78 mHSPC deep responders interrupt ADT+ARPI; median FU 26.9mo (enrolled 07/2022-03/2024)
- 🔍 Entry: PSA<0.2 (stable/falling) after 18-24mo ADT + ≥12mo ARPI; 64.9% CHAARTED low-volume, median age 70
- 🔍 Re-initiation triggered by PSA ≥5 ng/ml, PCWG3 radiographic progression, or cancer-related symptoms
- 📊 Patient disposition at 26.9mo median FU
  - Continuing on interruption: 30 (38.5%)
  - Resumed ADT+ARPI per protocol: 29 (37.2%)
  - Resumed before meeting criteria: 5 (6.4%)
  - Started non-protocol treatment: 7 (9.0%)
  - Death before next tx: 1 (1.3%, MI)
- 📊 rPFS 15/78 events, median not reached (95% CI 32.5-NE)
- 📊 OS 4/78 events, median not reached (deaths: prostate cancer, MDS, influenza, MI)
- ⚠️ Primary gap is testosterone recovery, not progression: many stay treatment-free but recover T slowly (median 9.0mo) in this median-70yo cohort
- ⚠️ Single-arm, no randomised comparator vs continued therapy; survival cost of stopping is unquantified
- 🔗 Distinct from classic intermittent-ADT trials (SWOG 9346): interruption here follows a deep ADT+ARPI response, a more selected modern population

### ARACOG (AFT-47)

> MCCD -36.1 (enza) vs -15.8 (daro), P=0.009 at 24 wk: enzalutamide worse for cognition; randomized open-label phase 2, N=111 advanced prostate.

![ARACOG (AFT-47)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmQaM5XEAAV9a0.jpg)

| Arm (N) | Max-changed domain | Median % change | P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Darolutamide (48) | PALFAM (visual mem/exec) | -15.8 | 0.009 |
| Enzalutamide (47) | SWM (working mem/exec) | -36.1 | n/a |

![ARACOG (AFT-47)](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmQVOtW8AEcroz.jpg)

- 📊 1° EP met: greater cognitive decline on enzalutamide vs darolutamide at 24 wk (MCCD median change -36.1 vs -15.8, P=0.009)
- 🔍 Randomized open-label phase 2, N=111 (mHSPC, mCRPC, nmCRPC); DAR vs ENZ, stratified by age (<65, 65-80, >80)
- 🔍 1° EP = between-arm % change baseline to 24 wk in Maximally Changed Cognitive Domain (MCCD), from 5 CANTAB tests
- 🔍 Primary analysis N=95 (daro 48, enza 47); crossovers scored at time of crossover in their randomized arm
- ⚠️ Open-label: pts knew assignment; enza provided via standard of care (daro by study) may have skewed crossover
- ⚠️ CANTAB modules are research tools, not clinical diagnostics; MCCD picks each arm's worst domain (PALFAM daro, SWM enza)
- ⚠️ US academic centers only, predominantly White; most non-White pts randomized to enzalutamide (race-by-arm confound)
- 🔗 Direction fits darolutamide's low blood-brain-barrier penetration vs enzalutamide's CNS exposure (fatigue/cognitive AEs in prior enza trials)

### Precision Oncology: Applying New Biomarkers in GU Cancers

> Biomarker round-up: tissue HRR/tumor-suppressor testing is SOC, but genomic classifiers show 24% clinical-risk discordance and lack ARPI-era validation.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> For RT readers using genomic classifiers to gate ADT intensification with salvage or definitive RT, the session's caution transfers: Decipher was discordant with clinical risk in 24% of high-risk pts (tissue available in only 427 of 3816), and its validation cohorts predate ARPIs. Treat the score as hypothesis-generating, not a settled gate.

![Biomarker vs clinical-risk concordance in high-risk prostate: 24% discordant (tissue n=427 of 3816).](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmGFEnWUAEeQN4.png)
*Biomarker vs clinical-risk concordance in high-risk prostate: 24% discordant (tissue n=427 of 3816).*

![Precision Oncology: Applying New Biomarkers in GU Cancers](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJmHJahXYAMGSc4.png)

- 🔬 ASCO26 education session (J. Lang): a biomarker is useful only if it changes management; GU therapy selection stays biomarker-limited
- 📊 Tissue-panel testing for HRR + tumor-suppressor loss is called SOC in prostate
- 📊 Landscape-slide annotations, approx. eligible fraction: Lu-177 PSMA ~90%, PARP inhibitors ~10-20%, pembrolizumab ~3%
- 📊 Decipher genomic classifier discordant with clinical risk in 24% of high-risk events
- ⚠️ Discordance from tissue-available subset (n=427) of n=3816; representativeness of full trial population uncertain
- ⚠️ Source classifier trials predate ARPIs; applicability to current ARPI-based SOC questioned
- 🔍 ENZAMET biomarker cohort: 1,071 consented, GEP N=764, final Decipher cohort N=634 (56% of enrolled)
- 📊 Decipher DPMC ≤0.85 subgroup: ADT+enza+docetaxel had worse OS than ADT+enza (triplet cohort N=320)
- ⚠️ Confounded: docetaxel not randomized, with more high-volume disease + older pts → selection bias, not a validated predictor

## 🎀 Breast

Pre- vs sub-pectoral implant reconstruction: modest patient-reported chest well-being gain against more unplanned implant loss.

### PREPEC

> Pre-pectoral implant improved 2yr chest well-being +4.8 pts (BREAST-Q, p=0.01) vs sub-pectoral, but more unplanned implant loss (21% vs 15%).

![PREPEC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl3SaPXMAUSwwH.jpg)

| Endpoint (24mo) | Pre-pectoral (N=191) | Sub-pectoral (N=189) | Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| Physical well-being, chest (BREAST-Q) | 79.2 (75.5-82.8) | 74.3 (70.7-78.0) | 4.8 (1.0-8.7), p=0.01 |

![PREPEC](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJl3SaOXIAMhzBT.jpg)

| Unplanned implant loss/replacement (24mo) | Pre-pectoral | Sub-pectoral | Adj. difference (95% CI) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crude % (n/N) | 21.1% (41/194) | 14.5% (27/186) | 5.7% (-2.4 to 13.8) |

- 🔍 International randomized phase 3 (OPBC-02): pre- vs sub-pectoral implant after skin- or nipple-sparing mastectomy
- 🔍 Eligible in both therapeutic and risk-reduction mastectomy settings; primary QoL analysis N=191 vs 189
- ⚠️ 1° endpoint is patient-reported (BREAST-Q chest); open-label surgical trial, so PRO awareness bias is possible
- ⚠️ 4.8-pt gain is statistically significant but modest on the 0-100 BREAST-Q scale
- ⚠️ Main secondary safety endpoint failed non-inferiority: more unplanned implant loss with pre-pectoral
- 📐 Implant-loss adjusted difference favored sub-pectoral but its 95% CI crossed zero (not statistically significant)
- 🔍 PRO completion 83-95% across 6 post-randomization timepoints; multiple-imputation, linear mixed-model analysis
- 📊 Net trade-off: better patient-reported chest well-being with pre-pectoral vs fewer implant losses with sub-pectoral

Intraoperative brachytherapy challenges post-op cavity SRS for large resected brain mets, but watch the outsized OS gap for arm imbalance.

### ROADS

> Surgical bed recurrence 1% vs 12% and 2yr OS 62% vs 36% favoring intraoperative GammaTile brachytherapy over post-op SRS for resected brain mets >2cm.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology

|---|---|---|

## 🍽️ GI Upper

Neoadjuvant chemoRT is RT-attributable positive in high-risk gastric cancer, reopening a question CRITICS and TOPGEAR left null (though on a XELOX, not FLOT, backbone).

### Neo-CRAG

> Adding neoadjuvant chemoRT (45Gy/25fx) to periop XELOX improved mDFS 52.7 vs 24.4mo (HR 0.75) and mOS 67.5 vs 37.6mo in high-risk LAGC.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> The RT read is clean attribution: both arms share the XELOX backbone and the CRT arm got dose-reduced chemo during RT, yet locoregional recurrence halved (9.4% vs 18.3%) with more ypN0 and downstaging. On 45Gy/25fx, this revives neoadjuvant chemoRT for high-risk D2-resected gastric/EGJ, on a non-FLOT backbone.

![Neo-CRAG](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HJlIWxCWAAMF5px.jpg)

| Endpoint | CRT | CT | HR (95% CI), P |
|---|---|---|---|
| Median DFS | 52.7 mo | 24.4 mo | 0.750 (0.607-0.928), P=0.008 |
| 3-yr DFS | 55.6% | 42.4% | — |
| Median OS | 67.5 mo | 37.6 mo | 0.781 (0.628-0.970), P=0.025 |
| 5-yr OS | 50.1% | 44.2% | — |

- 📐 RT: 45 Gy/25 fx concurrent, started after chemo cycle 1, with dose-reduced XELOX (oxaliplatin 100, capecitabine 825) during RT
- 🔍 RT is the sole added variable: both arms get identical periop XELOX, and the CRT arm gets less chemo during RT, so the DFS/OS gain is RT-attributable
- 📊 Pathologic and locoregional outcomes (CRT vs CT)
- 🔍 Phase 3 RCT, N=620 (310/arm), 13 China centers, 2013-2022; 36.3% EGJ (Siewert II/III); all planned standardized D2 gastrectomy
- 🔗 vs CRITICS (2018) / TOPGEAR (2024): adding RT to a chemo backbone was negative in both; a positive Neo-CRAG keeps the neoadjuvant chemoRT question contested

- ⚠️ G3+ hematologic toxicity higher with CRT: 14.6% (44/302) vs 10.3% (31/301)
- 📊 G3+ postoperative complication rates comparable: 9.0% (20/223) vs 7.6% (17/225)

## 🤝 Supportive / QoL

Post-hoc selection score for celiac-plexus SRS in pancreatic pain; awaits external validation.

### SPIN Score (Celiac Plexus SRS) · [[NCT03323489]]

> 89% pain response at SPIN 2 vs 32% at SPIN 0 for celiac SRS; post-hoc selection score, n=90.

> [!info] Why it matters · Radiation oncology
> Response is threshold-gated on baseline pain (>6 → 65.8%, rising to 85.7% at >8), and the strongest predictor was neurotoxic-chemo exposure (multivariate OR 5.1). The RT decision this moves is timing: refer for celiac SRS before neurotoxic agents. SRS dose/fractionation not reported in source (parent Lancet Oncol trial).

- 🔍 Post-hoc analysis of 90 evaluable pts from the pivotal celiac SRS phase 2 ([[NCT03323489]], Lancet Oncol 2024)
- 📊 Pivotal-trial pain response 53% (95% CI 42-64%); no actionable selection tool existed before this
- 🔍 SPIN (Severe Pain + Intact Nerves) score, 0-2: baseline pain >6 (+1), no prior neurotoxic chemo (+1)
- 📊 Pain response by SPIN score

  | SPIN score | Pain response | n |
  |---|---|---|
  | 0 | 32% | 31 |
  | 1 | 53% | 40 |
  | 2 | 89% | 19 |

  | Predictor | Univariate OR | Multivariate OR |
  |---|---|---|

  | Age | 1.06, p=0.014 | dropped (collinearity) |
