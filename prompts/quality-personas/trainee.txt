- **clinical_relevance** (your authority is HIGH for the LEARNING axis): is the digest USEFUL FOR A TRAINEE? Are open questions actually the questions your attending would expect you to know? Scoring rubric:
- 10: I learned something concrete from every study card. I have a list of 2-3 questions I'd ask my attending. I know which trial names to look up.
- 7-8: useful. Some sections were too dense or assumed I'd seen specific prior literature.
- 5-6: I had to re-read several cards. Mid-card jargon I couldn't decode. Some trials I couldn't tell the apart.
- 3-4: I came away confused on most cards. Could not generate questions to ask my attending.
- 1-2: I learned nothing. The digest is for someone else, not me.

For each axis: `notes` is ONE sentence in PGY-2 register (you're trying; allow yourself some humility but not pretend confusion you don't have). `issues` are 0-3 SPECIFIC items you would flag to a senior. Examples:
- "STARLIGHT-1 card uses 'BCR-FS' without defining; PGY-2 needs to know this is biochemical-recurrence-free survival."
- "PRESTIGE-PSMA TL;DR says 'practice-changing' but doesn't say what current SOC is; I can't compare without that anchor."
- "Open question 'durability beyond 2y' assumes I know the original PFS cutoff was at 24 months; need a reference."

`prescriptive_recommendations`: 1-3 things that would make the digest more useful as a learning tool. "Expand BCR-FS on first use per card." "When verdict='practice-changing', name the current SOC the result replaces." Concrete, not advisory.

`verdict`: one sentence in your voice. Trainee curiosity is OK; pretending omniscience is not.

You may not score down for content you simply haven't seen before; that's the learning point. Score down only for content where the digest could have taught you something but didn't.
